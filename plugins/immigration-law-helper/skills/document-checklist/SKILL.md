# Document Checklist Skill

Immigration cases succeed or fail on document quality and completeness. The Document Checklist skill provides attorneys with systematic, category-specific documentation guidance covering identity documents, civil documents, financial evidence, medical examinations, and all supporting documentation required for successful USCIS adjudication.

## Core Capabilities

### Identity and Civil Documents

**Passport:**
- Required for all applicants and beneficiaries in most cases
- Must be valid or certified as expired (passport need not be current for USCIS filing, though will be needed for travel)
- Front and back copy with all visa stamps and entry/exit records
- Biographical page (with photo) must be clear and readable
- If passport expired during case processing, renewal may be required before consular processing or travel on approved visa

**Birth Certificate:**
- Original or certified copy in English or with certified English translation
- Must establish identity and relationship (for family-based cases)
- Some countries use civil registry extracts instead of birth certificates; acceptable if official and certified
- For naturalization (N-400), critical to establish lawful admission; must match visa/entry records

**National ID Card/Civil Registry Document:**
- Varies by country; may be required instead of or in addition to passport
- Must be certified and translated if not in English
- Some countries provide civil registry extracts (rather than birth certificates) that serve same function

**Passport Data Page Photos:**
- Must be clear, unobstructed images of biographical pages
- All visa pages and entry/exit stamps must be documented
- High resolution electronic copies preferred (300 dpi minimum)

### Civil Documents with Translation Requirements

**Marriage Certificate:**
- Original or certified copy
- Must be translated to English if issued in foreign country (certified translation required)
- Some jurisdictions require notarized certified translation; verify specific jurisdiction requirements
- For family-based cases, establishes sponsor-beneficiary relationship (I-130)
- For dependent derivative cases, establishes family relationship

**Divorce Decree/Annulment:**
- Final decree (not preliminary judgment)
- Certified copy from court
- English certified translation if foreign document
- Critical for establishing current marital status; required before new marriage petition
- Dissolution records from court registry acceptable where separate "decree" not issued

**Adoption Papers:**
- Final adoption decree
- Court-certified copy with certified English translation
- For immediate relative adoption cases (IR-3/IR-4), must show adoption completed before child age 2
- Must establish that child was orphaned or parents abandoned the child (for IR-3/IR-4)

**Death Certificate:**
- Official vital record from jurisdiction
- Certified copy with certified English translation
- Required for dependent derivative eligibility (to establish loss of dependent status)
- Required for I-485 adjustment if sponsoring immediate relative is deceased (may require substitute I-864 from relative)

### Medical Documentation

**Form I-693 Medical Examination:**
- Completed by USCIS-designated civil surgeon
- Cannot be older than 60 days from I-485 filing (or N-400 interview)
- Report sealed in envelope bearing civil surgeon signature over sealed flap
- Must be current (within 60 days of filing or interview)
- Required for all I-485 applicants, N-400 applicants, K-1 fiancé visa beneficiaries, IR-3/IR-4 adoptees

**Vaccination Records:**
- Proof of standard childhood vaccinations and recommended adult vaccinations
- If applicant has medical contraindication to vaccines, civil surgeon evaluates and documents waiver
- Copy of vaccination record from home country if available
- Recommended: translate records if not in English

**Medical History Records:**
- Significant medical conditions documented
- Current medications listed
- Any communicable diseases disclosed
- Waivers available for certain inadmissibility grounds if based on medical condition

### Financial Documentation

**Form I-864 Affidavit of Support:**
- Required for all I-485 applicants (unless exempt: immediate relatives of U.S. citizen deceased, military applicants, etc.)
- Form I-864 filed by sponsor with specific income/asset requirements
- Sponsor must show income at 125% of federal poverty level (190% if sponsor not head of household)
- Executed before filing I-485 (not after)
- Copy of executed I-864 filed with I-485 petition package

**Tax Returns (Sponsor):**
- Last 3 years federal tax returns (1040 and all schedules)
- State tax returns if applicable
- Certified copies or signed photocopies from original returns
- If self-employed: additional documentation may include Form 1120 (corporate) or 1120-S (partnership)
- W-2 forms for current tax year if returns not yet filed

**Income Documentation (Sponsor):**
- Letter from employer on official letterhead confirming employment, position, salary
- Pay stubs covering recent 6 months (demonstrating ongoing income)
- Offer letter or employment contract for new employment
- Bank statements showing regular deposits (for self-employed)
- Business tax returns (for self-employed)

**Asset Documentation:**
- Bank statements showing account balances (recent 2-3 months)
- Stocks, bonds, investment accounts (brokerage statements)
- Real estate (property tax assessment or deed)
- Life insurance with cash surrender value
- Retirement accounts (only some types count toward assets)

**Assets May Be Used to Supplement Income:**
- If sponsor income insufficient to meet 125% poverty guideline
- Assets must be in amount = (125% of poverty level - sponsor income) x 3
- Sponsor must retain ownership of assets throughout period of I-864 liability (10 years post-adjustment)

**Household Size Calculation:**
- Include sponsor, spouse (if present), sponsor's children under 21
- Include beneficiary and beneficiary's dependents
- Poverty line increases with household size; larger households require higher income

### Employment Documentation

**Current Employment:**
- Employment letter from current employer on official letterhead
- Must state: position, title, start date, current salary, employment status (full-time/part-time)
- Recent pay stubs (6 months minimum)
- W-2 forms from current and prior employers (3 years minimum)

**Professional Credentials:**
- Bachelor's degree diploma or transcript
- Advanced degree (Master's, PhD, etc.) diploma or transcript
- Professional licenses (if applicable to claim specialty occupation)
- Certifications (industry-specific certifications supporting employment claim)

**Employment History:**
- Chronological list of employment since reaching working age
- For each position: employer name, address, position title, dates, duties
- Employment letters from prior employers (if available)
- Tax records documenting employment (W-2, 1040 Schedule C for self-employed)

**Specialty Occupation Evidence (H-1B/EB-2/EB-3):**
- Job description and requirements
- Evidence that position requires bachelor's degree or higher
- Evidence of applicant's qualifications for position
- Educational credentials, certifications, licenses
- Prior work experience in similar field
- References from employers or academic supervisors

### Relationship Evidence (Family-Based Cases)

**I-130 Immediate Relative:**
- Marriage certificate (for spouse beneficiary)
- Birth certificate (for children beneficiary)
- Adoption decree (for adopted children beneficiary)
- Divorce decrees for all prior spouses (both petitioner and beneficiary)

**I-130 Family Preference (F2A, F2B, F3, F4):**
- Birth certificate (establishing parent-child relationship)
- Marriage certificate (if establishing through marriage)
- All civil documents listed above (marriage, divorce, death certificates)
- Affidavit of relationships (notarized statement from third parties establishing relationship if documents unavailable)

**Relationship Documentation (All Cases):**
- Photos of petitioner and beneficiary together (ideally multiple recent photos)
- Joint household documentation if cohabiting (utility bills, lease, mortgage in both names)
- Communications between petitioner and beneficiary (emails, letters, phone records showing ongoing contact)
- Affidavits from friends and family members attesting to relationship
- Social media evidence (carefully vetted; beware contradictions regarding relationship status)

### Visa/Immigration History

**Previous Visas:**
- Copies of all visa stamps in passports
- Copies of current and prior I-797 notices of action (receipt, extension, approval)
- Consular stamps or entry documents proving entry to U.S.

**Adjustment vs. Port of Entry:**
- If adjusting status: evidence of how entered U.S. (admission stamp, I-94 record, CBP entry documentation)
- If consular processing: prior visa stamps demonstrating lawful admission and ability to depart/return

**Green Card/Permanent Resident Card:**
- Copy of valid green card (front and back)
- For family-preference petitioners: proof of green card status (needed to establish petitioner as permanent resident)

**Visa Documentation (K-1/V-2/V-3 cases):**
- Original visa stamp in passport (for K-1 fiancé visa)
- Marriage certificate and divorce decrees if prior marriages
- Evidence of meeting in person within two-year requirement (passport stamps, photos, affidavits)

### Police and Background Clearance Documentation

**Form FD-258 (Fingerprint Card):**
- Completed by certified fingerprinting service (law enforcement agency, fingerprinting service)
- Original form submitted with I-485 or other applicable form
- Fingerprints must be clear and readable (re-fingerprinting required if not legible)

**Police Clearance Certificate:**
- For applicants who have lived in foreign countries or have resided outside U.S. for significant periods
- Issued by police authority of country/province where applicant lived
- Demonstrates applicant has no criminal record in that jurisdiction
- Certified translation required if not in English

**Court Documents:**
- Copies of any court judgments (whether criminal or civil)
- Divorce decrees (even if not contested)
- Traffic violation records or tickets (for background check purposes)
- Any orders of protection or restraining orders

### Digital Photos (USCIS Specifications)

**Photo Requirements:**
- 2x2 inch color photograph
- Must be taken within 6 months of filing
- Passport-style photo (white or off-white background, direct face-on pose)
- Both ears visible, head filling 50-69% of frame
- Eyes open, normal expression, no sunglasses
- Printed on photo paper (not regular paper)
- Unaltered (no filters, touching up, or digital manipulation)

**Digital Photo Submissions:**
- USCIS increasingly accepts digital photos submitted online
- File format: JPEG, TIFF, or PDF
- Maximum file size: 3 MB
- Specifications same as printed photo

## Category-Specific Document Requirements

### Family-Based Cases (I-130/I-485)

**Immediate Relative Cases:**
- All documents listed above (identity, civil, medical, police clearance, photos)
- Relationship evidence (marriage certificate, birth certificate, adoption decree)
- Evidence of petitioner's U.S. citizenship or green card status (copy of citizenship certificate or green card)
- Financial evidence (Form I-864, tax returns, employment letter, financial documents)
- Visa/immigration history (prior visas, entry documentation, current status)

**Family Preference Cases:**
- All documents above plus visa bulletin tracking
- If in U.S. adjusting: documents for adjustment
- If overseas in consular processing: additional documents for consulate (passport validity, medical exam from consular physician, etc.)

### Employment-Based Cases (I-140/I-485)

**PERM Labor Certification:**
- Job description and requirements
- Evidence of recruitment activities performed
- Evidence that applicant qualifications match position requirements
- Proof of prevailing wage payment or commitment to pay prevailing wage
- Documentation that recruitment followed DOL requirements

**I-140 EB-1 (Extraordinary Ability):**
- Awards and recognition (awards, medals, honors, peer nominations)
- Membership in associations requiring outstanding achievement
- Published materials documenting work or acclaim
- Judging/evaluation evidence (letters from peers evaluating applicant's work)
- Original scientific/artistic contributions
- Leading/critical role in prestigious organizations
- High salary or commercially successful work
- Evidence applicant's work is recognized as of extraordinary significance

**I-140 EB-2/EB-3:**
- Educational credentials (degree diploma, transcripts)
- Employment history and experience (employment letters, W-2s, work samples)
- For specialty occupation: evidence position requires bachelor's degree or equivalent
- Professional licenses, certifications (if applicable)
- References from prior employers or academic supervisors
- PERM labor certification (for EB-2/EB-3; NIW may waive PERM)

### Asylum Cases (I-589 Application for Asylum)

**Identity and Civil Documents:**
- Passport (all pages with stamps)
- Birth certificate (certified, translated if foreign)
- National ID card or other identity documents
- Marriage certificate (if applicable)
- Children's birth certificates (if claiming family unity)

**Persecution Documentation:**
- Written statement by applicant describing persecution feared and basis (protected ground)
- Medical or psychological evidence of torture, trauma, or persecution suffered
- Photographs of injuries or property damage
- Police reports or court documents relating to persecution or threats
- Documentation of arrest, detention, or interrogation (court records, witness statements, detention certificates)

**Country Conditions Evidence:**
- State Department Human Rights Country Report
- Country conditions research from authoritative sources (Human Rights Watch, Amnesty International, UNHCR)
- News articles documenting persecution patterns
- Expert declarations on country conditions or applicant's vulnerability
- Evidence linking applicant to protected group and persecution targeted at that group

**Nexus and Persecution Evidence:**
- Affidavit from applicant explaining link between persecution and protected ground
- Evidence applicant's identity/group membership was reason for persecution
- Documentation of government's role in persecution or inability/unwillingness to protect
- Evidence applicant cannot relocate within country to safety
- Evidence persecution based on political opinion (if applicable) - letters, protest documents, political affiliation

**Credibility Evidence:**
- Consistent detailed account of events with documentary support
- Medical records consistent with trauma claims
- Witness statements corroborating persecution account
- Documentation of background and family ties (establishing credibility of applicant)

### Green Card Extension/Renewal Cases

**Current Green Card Photocopy:**
- Front and back of card showing expiration date
- Name, photo, and identity information must be legible

**Application Form I-90:**
- Completed and signed
- Appropriate filing fee or fee waiver request
- Current mailing address
- Biometric appointment notice

**Biometric Services:**
- If conditional green card (CR-1/CR-2), marriage/relationship documents required
- Biographical information from applicant for security check

## Translation and Certification Requirements

### Certified English Translation Requirements

Documents NOT in English require certified English translation:

- Birth certificates (issued in foreign country)
- Marriage certificates (issued in foreign country)
- Divorce decrees (issued in foreign country)
- Death certificates (issued in foreign country)
- Police clearance certificates
- Educational diplomas and transcripts (if issued in foreign language)
- Employment letters (if issued in foreign language)
- Any other official document issued in foreign language

### Certification Standards

Certified translator must provide:
- Statement that translator is competent in both original language and English
- Certification that translation is accurate and complete
- Translator's signature, address, and phone number
- Date of certification

**Acceptable Certifications:**
- Certified translator (state licensing varies; some states have official certification, others allow translator to self-certify)
- Notarized translation (notary's seal on translator's certification statement)
- Translation by attorney (attorney's letterhead and certification)

**NOT Acceptable:**
- Translation by friend or family member (unless certified by professional)
- Machine translation (Google Translate, online tools) unless used by professional translator
- Transcription only without certification

### Translation Timing

Translations must be contemporaneous with document submission. If translator performs translation now but you use it 6 months later, translator should recertify or provide new translation (though this is often unnecessary if original translator's certification is dated and specific).

## Document Organization and Filing

### USCIS Filing Organization Standards

**Cover Sheet/Index:**
- List all documents in order submitted
- Include page numbers
- Indicate which documents are originals vs. copies
- Indicate which documents are certified translations

**Document Order:**
- I-485 application form (main form)
- Birth certificate and passport
- Medical exam (Form I-693 in sealed envelope)
- Photographs
- Marriage certificate/civil documents
- Police clearance and background documentation
- Financial documents (I-864, tax returns, pay stubs)
- Employment documentation
- Visa/immigration history (prior visas, I-797 notices)
- Country conditions research (for asylum cases)
- Affidavits and supporting letters

**Page Numbering:**
- Number all pages consecutively through entire package
- Use footer or margin page numbers (consistent, legible)
- Correspond to cover sheet index

### File Format and Submission Considerations

**Original Documents:**
- Passport must be original or certified photocopy
- Birth certificate should be original or certified copy (if filed by mail)
- Some documents (I-693 medical exam) must be original in sealed envelope
- Marriage/divorce decrees should be certified copies from court

**Photocopies:**
- Clear, legible black and white or color copies acceptable
- Both sides of all documents must be copied (passport full document, ID cards both sides, etc.)
- Minimum 8.5x11 inch paper for copying (no miniature copies)
- Original documents should not be mailed with I-485 unless specifically required (most documents can be certified copies)

**Digital Submissions (USCIS MyCase Portal):**
- PDF format preferred
- File size limits (typically 5-10 MB per document)
- Multiple document types combined acceptable if clearly labeled
- High resolution images required for clarity

## Document Completion Checklist by Case Type

### I-485 Adjustment of Status Checklist

Required for ALL I-485 filers:
- ☐ Form I-485 (completed, signed, dated)
- ☐ Form G-325A (biographical information)
- ☐ Form I-131 advance parole (if filing concurrently)
- ☐ Form I-765 work authorization (if filing concurrently)
- ☐ Passport (copy with all pages)
- ☐ Birth certificate (original or certified copy with translation)
- ☐ Photos (2x2 inch, meeting USCIS specifications)
- ☐ Form I-693 medical exam (in sealed envelope, completed within 60 days before filing)
- ☐ Form FD-258 fingerprint card
- ☐ Police clearance (if required based on residence history)
- ☐ Marriage certificate (if applicable)
- ☐ Divorce decrees (for all prior spouses)
- ☐ Form I-864 affidavit of support (completed and executed)
- ☐ Sponsor's tax returns (3 years)
- ☐ Sponsor's employment letter
- ☐ Sponsor's pay stubs (recent 6 months)
- ☐ Financial documents (if assets used)
- ☐ I-130 approval notice (if family-based case)
- ☐ Prior I-797 notices and visas (entire visa/immigration history)

### N-400 Naturalization Checklist

Required for ALL N-400 filers:
- ☐ Form N-400 (completed, signed, dated)
- ☐ Birth certificate (copy with certified translation if foreign)
- ☐ Green card (copy front and back)
- ☐ Passport (all pages with entry/exit stamps)
- ☐ Photos (2x2 inch)
- ☐ Form I-693 medical exam (if required - generally required for applicants with certain inadmissibility grounds)
- ☐ Form FD-258 fingerprint card
- ☐ State ID or driver's license (copy)
- ☐ Evidence of continuous residence (tax returns, utility bills, lease, mortgage, employment letters)
- ☐ Evidence of good moral character (employment records, community involvement, character references, no criminal record)
- ☐ Tax returns (last 5 years to demonstrate good moral character and continuous presence)
- ☐ Prior I-797 notices showing status throughout eligibility period
- ☐ Marriage certificate (if married)
- ☐ Divorce decrees (for all prior spouses)

### Family-Based I-130 Checklist (Immediate Relative)

Required for I-130 beneficiary:
- ☐ Form I-130 (completed by petitioner)
- ☐ Beneficiary's birth certificate (certified copy with translation)
- ☐ Beneficiary's passport (all pages)
- ☐ Marriage certificate (establishing petitioner-beneficiary relationship)
- ☐ Divorce decrees (all prior spouses of both petitioner and beneficiary)
- ☐ Petitioner's evidence of U.S. citizenship (birth certificate, naturalization certificate, passport, or Form N-558)
- ☐ Photos of petitioner and beneficiary together
- ☐ Evidence of ongoing relationship (letters, communications, joint household documentation)

Petitioner must also file I-485 (if in U.S.) with all documents above plus:
- ☐ Form I-864 affidavit of support
- ☐ Petitioner's tax returns (3 years)
- ☐ Petitioner's employment letter and pay stubs
- ☐ Medical exam (Form I-693)
- ☐ Photos of beneficiary
