# Case Tracker Skill

Immigration practitioners face constant pressure to manage multiple cases at various stages of adjudication. Each visa category has distinct processing timelines, filing requirements, and critical deadlines. The Case Tracker skill provides immigration attorneys with systematic case management capability that incorporates real-world USCIS processing data, service center assignments, and visa category-specific milestones.

## Core Capabilities

### Visa Type Timeline Management

Different immigration benefits have fundamentally different processing pathways and timelines:

**Nonimmigrant Visa Categories:**

- **F-1 Student Visa and OPT (Optional Practical Training):** F-1 visa processing typically takes 4-6 weeks after SEVIS record creation. OPT applications (Form I-765 with EAD category designation) average 2-5 months from submission to card receipt. The critical deadline is the OPT application submission within 14 calendar days after degree conferment.

- **H-1B Work Visa:** Annual cap-subject H-1B petitions filed during the registration and filing windows (typically March-October) face queue-based processing. Current processing timelines range 2-6 months depending on service center. Premium processing (15 calendar days) is available. Extensions beyond initial authorization require timely recapture of periods worked out of status.

- **L-1 Intracompany Transfer:** Initial L-1 petitions (Form I-129 with L-1A/L-1B classification) process in 3-6 months; expedited processing available. Extensions and new office L-1A managers face additional requirements. One-year engagement in foreign company before L-1A petition is mandatory.

- **O-1 Extraordinary Ability:** O-1 petitions require intensive evidence of extraordinary ability and timely event scheduling. Processing averages 2-4 months. Critical considerations include the events triggering the need for O-1 status and whether premium processing is available for the specific service center.

- **K-1 Fiancé Visa:** Fiancé petitions (Form I-129F) submitted at USCIS require approval before NVC processing can begin, typically 3-6 months. Following approval, NVC processing adds 6-12 months depending on country and visa availability. The two-year conditional period following marriage is critical for future adjustment planning.

**Immigrant Visa Categories:**

- **I-130 Immediate Relative Petitions:** Processing varies by service center but averages 3-6 months for immediate relatives (I-R classification). Priority dates are immediately available for immediate relatives. Concurrent I-485 filing accelerates processing when applicant is in the United States.

- **I-130 Family-Preference Petitions:** Family preference petitions (F2A, F2B, F3, F4) face visa bulletin backlogs. Processing times for petition approval typically 18-36 months, but visa availability determines when beneficiary can proceed to consular processing or I-485 adjustment. Current visa bulletin priority date gaps mean applicant may wait years between petition approval and eligibility for adjustment/consular processing.

- **PERM Labor Certification:** PERM applications filed at Department of Labor average 6-12 months from filing to certification (absent complications). Recruitment process preceding filing adds 3-6 months. Audit rates and Request for Information (RFI) responses can extend timelines to 18+ months. Once certified, Form I-140 EB petition must follow within specified timeframe.

- **I-140 EB-1/EB-2/EB-3 Petitions:** I-140 petitions filed at USCIS service center processing times vary dramatically: EB-1 (extraordinary ability) may process in 6-18 months; EB-2 (advanced degree/national interest waiver) 12-24 months; EB-3 (skilled worker) facing significant visa bulletin backlogs, 18-36+ months. EB-2 NIW petitions have distinct requirements including self-petitioner eligibility in certain circumstances.

- **I-485 Adjustment of Status:** I-485 adjustment applications concurrent with I-140 may process in 12-24 months from filing; dependent on visa availability and security/background checks. Non-concurrent I-485 cases waiting for visa availability may face extended processing. Advance parole (Form I-131) and work authorization (Form I-765) concurrent filing provides critical benefits during adjudication.

- **Naturalization (N-400):** N-400 naturalization applications process in 8-18 months depending on service center. Applicant must maintain continuous residence, physical presence, and good moral character during the processing period. Timely filing by applicant approaching eligibility is critical.

### USCIS Processing Time Tracking

Processing times published by USCIS are benchmarks, but real-world outcomes vary significantly:

**Service Center Variation:** The four USCIS service centers (Texas, Nebraska, Vermont, California) handle different petition types and have different processing capacity and backlogs. An I-485 at Nebraska Service Center may process significantly faster than the same petition at Texas Service Center. Attorneys should track service center assignment when possible and consult current processing times specific to the assigned center.

**Adjustment vs. Consular Processing:** Adjustment of status (I-485) at USCIS service center and adjustment overseas at U.S. embassy/consulate have different timelines. Consular processing may take 6-12 months overseas after visa becomes available, depending on country, embassy workload, and case complexity.

**Premium Processing and Expedite Requests:** Forms I-129 (for certain visa categories), I-140, and I-765 are eligible for premium processing (15 calendar days for decision). Certain circumstances (medical conditions, inadmissibility issues, hardship) may warrant expedite request (generally 30 days). Both mechanisms require Form I-907 (premium) or written expedite request with supporting documentation.

### Receipt Notice Tracking and Document Management

USCIS issues Form I-797 receipt notice within 5-7 days of receipt of mailed application or within 1-2 days of online filing. Receipt notice includes critical information: case number, filing date (not receipt date), receipt date, and service center assignment. Applicant should preserve receipt notice as proof of timely filing.

Biometric services notice (Form I-797) may be issued 2-4 weeks after receipt notice for applicable cases (I-485, I-765 if fingerprints needed). Applicant must appear for biometric appointment on the scheduled date to avoid abandonment of application.

Interview notice (Form I-797) typically issued 4-16 weeks after biometric services depending on case type and service center. Interview date should be noted and attorney should prepare applicant with full interview preparation package.

### Priority Date Monitoring and Visa Bulletin Strategy

For family-preference and employment-based immigrant petitions, the visa bulletin determines when a beneficiary can proceed from petition approval to adjustment of status or consular processing. The State Department issues monthly visa bulletins with current priority dates for each visa category and country.

**Immediate Relatives:** I-R beneficiaries have immediately available priority dates upon beneficiary's birth and have no wait.

**Family Preference:** F2A immediate relatives of green card holders (spouse, child under 21) currently have immediate availability; F2A elderly beneficiaries face longer waits. F2B adult sons/daughters wait 10+ years in high-demand countries (Philippines, Mexico). F3 and F4 wait 15-30+ years depending on country. Attorneys must monitor visa bulletin to assess when applicant will reach priority date, enabling advancement to next processing stage.

**Employment-Based:** EB-1 extraordinary ability has current priority dates; EB-2 and EB-3 face backlogs. For EB-2/EB-3 petitioners, visa bulletin movement determines when visa becomes available, which is critical for timing of I-485 filing or consular processing. Some beneficiaries have been waiting 5-10+ years for visa availability in oversubscribed countries.

**Visa Recapture and Movement:** Attorneys should track visa bulletin movement to anticipate when applicant's priority date becomes current and prepare for next processing stage. Beneficiaries may be able to file concurrent I-485 upon visa availability or should prepare for interview scheduling and consular processing documents.

### Aging-Out Risk Management

Children born to visa applicants and immigrants face critical aging-out deadlines as they turn 21 (or 25 for EB-2/EB-3 petitions with derivative status). Aging-out occurs when a beneficiary of an I-130 petition or derivative of an I-140 petition exceeds maximum age eligibility.

**IR Classification Aging-Out:** Immediate relatives of U.S. citizens age out at 21 if the beneficiary has not adjusted status or obtained an immigrant visa. IR-4 (orphans adopted abroad) must have adoption completed before age 2 (or 4 if sibling group). K-2 children (fiancé visa derivatives) must marry the I-R2 beneficiary or face aging-out at 21.

**Dependent Derivative Aging-Out:** Children deriving visa eligibility from parent's employment-based petition age out at 21 if adjustment has not been completed or if they have not been included in the parent's petition. Beneficiary must be under 21 at time of parent's adjustment/visa issuance to derive benefits.

**F-2A Aging-Out:** F-2A spouses of permanent residents face aging-out at 21 if not yet adjusted. F-2A children age out at 21 regardless of marital status.

Case tracker must flag aging-out risks: children approaching age 21 should trigger urgent action to complete adjustment or file derivative petitions. For EB-2/EB-3 cases, children approaching 21 may require beneficiary's spouse to file separate petition if aging-out is imminent.

### Strategic Deadline Management

Immigration cases have cascading critical deadlines:

- **Filing Deadlines:** Applicant must file before visa eligibility expires (visa bulletins for preference cases), before aging-out occurs, or before expiration of current visa status.
- **Response Deadlines:** USCIS RFE responses typically due within 12 months of RFE date (USCIS may extend up to 24 months if applicant requests additional time with good cause explanation).
- **Expiration Deadlines:** Passport expiration does not necessarily affect visa validity, but expired passport must be renewed before travel. Driver's license and state ID expiration may trigger background checks.
- **Status Expiration:** Applicant must maintain continuous status in current visa classification or face unlawful presence. Grace periods vary (H-1B has 10-day grace period; F-1 has 60-day grace period for students transferring schools).

### Case Status Check Recommendations

For applications pending adjudication:

- **Initial Phase (0-4 weeks):** No status check needed; USCIS is processing receipt.
- **Mid-Phase (4-16 weeks):** Contact USCIS if no biometric or interview notice received; verify application receipt by calling service center with case number.
- **Extended Processing (16+ weeks):** Request case status through USCIS portal or phone (1-800-375-5283); consider congressional inquiry if significantly delayed.
- **Priority Date Tracking:** Monitor visa bulletin monthly if case depends on priority date availability.

## Implementation in Case Management

Case tracker should maintain:

1. **Case master file** with beneficiary information, visa category, filing date, and receipt notice
2. **Processing timeline** specific to visa category, service center, and filing date
3. **Deadline tracking** with alerts for upcoming response deadlines, status check dates, and age-out risks
4. **Service center processing metrics** for predictive timeline modeling
5. **Visa bulletin tracking** for priority date cases, with monthly bulletin review
