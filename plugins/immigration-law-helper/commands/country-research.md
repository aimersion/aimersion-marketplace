# Country Research Command

Request from the client:

1. **Country of concern** (where persecution is claimed)
2. **Protected ground** (race, religion, nationality, political opinion, particular social group)
3. **Specific persecution claim** (what form the harm takes, by what group/entity)
4. **Timeframe** (when did/does persecution occur)
5. **Applicant's specific role or characteristics** (membership in group, visibility, etc.)

## Execute country conditions research:

### Identify and search for evidence from authoritative sources:

- **U.S. State Department Country Reports on Human Rights Practices**
- **UNHCR Position Papers** on claims from the specific country
- **U.S. Department of State International Religious Freedom Reports** (for religion-based claims)
- **Country Reports by Human Rights Watch and Amnesty International**
- **Congressional Research Service (CRS) reports** on specific countries
- **EASO (European Asylum Support Office) Country of Origin Information** (EUAA under updated structure)
- **Recent news sources** from reputable outlets covering country conditions

### Compile a country conditions research summary that includes:

### Executive Summary:

- Overview of general country conditions
- Relevance to the specific claim and protected ground
- Current political/security situation

### Protected Ground Analysis:

- Evidence of persecution targeting the specific protected ground
- Patterns and prevalence
- Government responsibility or acquiescence

### Specific Persecution Evidence:

- Documentation of harm to members of the claimant's group
- Patterns of persecution
- Timing and current applicability
- Severity levels of documented harm

### Credibility and Nexus Factors:

- Evidence linking persecution to the protected ground (not personal vendetta)
- Government ability/willingness to protect
- Alternative routes to safety (relocation feasibility)

### Source Citations and Evidence Organization:

- Full citations for all sources
- URL links to original documents
- Direct quotes with page numbers
- Organization of evidence by relevance to claimant's specific claim

Link to country-conditions-researcher skill (research-sources-guide.md and asylum-framework.md) for framework guidance.

Deliver as a formatted research memo with organized evidence sections, clear citations, and analysis connecting general country conditions to the applicant's specific claim.
