# Sales Enabler

Create compelling technology assessments and managed services proposals that win MSP clients.

## The MSP Sales Framework

### Discovery → Assessment → Proposal → Close

1. **Discovery**: Understand the prospect's business, pain points, and current IT situation
2. **Assessment**: Evaluate their environment and identify gaps and risks
3. **Proposal**: Present a solution with clear value and pricing
4. **Close**: Address objections and finalize terms

### What Wins MSP Deals

- **Fear reduction**: Show them what could go wrong (ransomware, downtime, compliance fines)
- **Value demonstration**: Calculate the cost of their current approach vs. managed services
- **Trust building**: Reference similar clients, share relevant case studies
- **Quick wins**: Identify immediate improvements to demonstrate value during evaluation

### Managed Services Packaging

**Basic (Monitoring + Break-Fix Priority)**
- Network and server monitoring
- Patch management
- Backup monitoring
- Priority support access
- Monthly health report

**Standard (Proactive Management)**
- Everything in Basic +
- Unlimited helpdesk support
- Proactive maintenance
- Security stack (AV/EDR + email security)
- Quarterly business reviews

**Premium (Strategic IT Partner)**
- Everything in Standard +
- vCIO/strategic advisory
- Advanced security (SIEM, SOC)
- Compliance management
- Project hours included
- 24/7 support

## Pricing Strategies

### Per-User Pricing
- Most common and predictable model
- Includes: device management, helpdesk, security, cloud services
- Typical range: $100-250/user/month depending on tier and market

### Per-Device Pricing
- Better for environments with more devices than users
- Separate pricing for workstations, servers, and network devices
- Typical: Workstation $50-80/mo, Server $200-500/mo

### Tiered Flat-Rate
- Fixed monthly fee based on company size band
- Simpler for the client to understand
- Less precise margin management

## Output Guidelines

When creating MSP sales materials:
- Lead with business value, not technical features
- Include risk quantification (cost of downtime, breach probability)
- Show total cost of ownership comparison (current vs. managed)
- Make pricing transparent and easy to understand
- Include implementation timeline and onboarding process
- Address common objections (cost, control, responsiveness)
