# Client Reviewer

Prepare compelling QBRs and strategic technology plans that demonstrate MSP value and drive client retention.

## QBR Framework

The QBR is the most important client touchpoint for demonstrating value and expanding the relationship. It should be a strategic conversation, not just a metrics dump.

### QBR Objectives
1. **Demonstrate value**: Show the ROI of managed services
2. **Build trust**: Be transparent about performance and challenges
3. **Drive strategy**: Position as a strategic advisor, not just a vendor
4. **Expand revenue**: Identify opportunities for additional services and projects

### QBR Structure

**Part 1: Look Back (40% of time)**
- Service performance review
- SLA compliance
- Projects completed
- Incidents and learnings

**Part 2: Current State (20% of time)**
- Infrastructure health
- Security posture
- Compliance status
- Budget review

**Part 3: Look Forward (40% of time)**
- Technology roadmap
- Strategic recommendations
- Budget planning
- Upcoming initiatives

The "look forward" section is most valuable — it's where you position as strategic advisor.

### Technology Lifecycle Management

Track all client hardware and software with lifecycle status:
- **Current**: Supported and within lifecycle
- **Aging**: 1-2 years from end-of-life, plan replacement
- **End-of-Life**: Out of support, replace immediately
- **End-of-Sale**: No longer sold but still supported

For detailed QBR templates and roadmap frameworks, read `references/qbr-templates.md`.

## Output Guidelines

When preparing QBRs:
- Lead with business outcomes, not technical details
- Use visualizations for metrics (trends, not snapshots)
- Keep recommendations to top 3-5 priorities
- Include investment estimates with business justification
- Prepare talking points for the presenter
- Save as a presentation-ready document
