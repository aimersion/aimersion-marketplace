# Color Consultant

Guide paint color selection with trend awareness, client needs assessment, and room-specific recommendations.

## Color Consultation Workflow

1. **Client questionnaire** — gather preferences, style, existing elements
2. **Assess the space** — lighting, size, function, architectural features, fixed finishes
3. **Identify color direction** — warm, cool, neutral, bold, muted
4. **Recommend palette** — 3-5 colors: primary wall, accent, trim, ceiling, accent wall (if desired)
5. **Present options** — with reasoning for each recommendation
6. **Sample guidance** — recommend testing samples on walls before committing

## Client Discovery Questions

Ask the client:
1. What is the room used for? (bedroom, living room, office, kitchen, etc.)
2. What mood do you want to create? (calm, energizing, cozy, sophisticated, playful)
3. What colors do you love? What colors do you hate?
4. What existing elements must stay? (flooring, countertops, furniture, artwork)
5. How much natural light does the room get? Which direction do the windows face?
6. Do you prefer bold or subtle? Warm or cool tones?
7. Any colors from the rest of the home to coordinate with?
8. How long do you plan to stay? (Resale-friendly vs. personal preference)

## Room-by-Room Guidelines

### Living Room / Family Room
- **Warm and inviting**: Greiges, warm whites, soft taupes, muted sage
- **Sophisticated**: Deep navy, charcoal, rich greens
- **Light and airy**: Pale blue, soft white, light gray
- **Accent walls**: Behind fireplace or TV wall

### Bedroom
- **Restful**: Soft blues, sage green, lavender, warm gray
- **Romantic**: Dusty rose, deep plum, warm neutrals
- **Modern**: Crisp white, slate blue, charcoal accent
- **Avoid**: Bright reds or oranges (too stimulating for sleep)

### Kitchen
- **Classic**: White cabinets with warm-toned walls
- **Modern farmhouse**: Sage green, navy, or black cabinets with white walls
- **Bold**: Colored cabinets (emerald, navy, terracotta) with neutral walls
- **Consider**: Backsplash, countertop, and floor colors as part of the palette

### Bathroom
- **Spa-like**: Soft blue-greens, warm whites, pale gray
- **Bold**: Deep teal, black accent wall, jewel tones
- **Classic**: White with contrasting vanity color
- **Consider**: Tile color, fixture finishes, and lighting

### Home Office
- **Focus**: Blues and greens (proven to aid concentration)
- **Creativity**: Soft yellow, warm orange accents
- **Calm authority**: Deep green, navy, charcoal
- **Avoid**: All-white (eye fatigue) or very dark walls without good lighting

### Exterior
- **Traditional**: Earth tones, classic whites, colonial blues
- **Modern**: Dark charcoal, black, deep green, mixed materials
- **Craftsman**: Warm browns, greens, deep reds
- **Consider**: Roof color, stone/brick, neighborhood context, HOA rules

## Color Trend Guidance

When asked about current trends, research using web search for the latest:
- Sherwin-Williams Color of the Year
- Benjamin Moore Color of the Year
- PPG Color of the Year
- Designer trends from Architectural Digest, House Beautiful, etc.

## Light Direction Impact

| Window Direction | Light Quality | Color Advice |
|-----------------|--------------|--------------|
| North-facing | Cool, blue-tinted | Use warmer colors to compensate |
| South-facing | Warm, bright | Any color works; cool colors stay balanced |
| East-facing | Warm morning, cool afternoon | Test colors at multiple times of day |
| West-facing | Cool morning, warm/golden afternoon | Colors will appear dramatically different AM vs. PM |

## Output Formats
- **Color consultation summary** — recommendations with reasoning
- **Color palette card** — primary, secondary, trim, ceiling, accent colors
- **Client questionnaire** — printable form for initial consultation
- **Shopping list** — specific paint brand/color codes and quantities
