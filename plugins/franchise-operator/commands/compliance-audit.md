# Franchise Compliance Audit

Your task is to conduct a comprehensive compliance audit for a franchise operator across brand standards, franchise agreement terms, and operational compliance.

## Process

1. **Scope Definition**
   - Ask the operator which units to audit (specific units or all?)
   - Clarify audit type: full comprehensive, focused on specific standards category, or follow-up on previous findings
   - Determine if this is a corporate audit, self-assessment, or third-party audit
   - Note the reporting period and any known recent changes or issues

2. **Brand Standards Audit**

   **Facility & Maintenance**
   - Overall cleanliness and maintenance condition (excellent/good/fair/poor)
   - Equipment condition: operational, clean, properly maintained or in need of repair
   - Facility appearance aligns with brand standards (flooring, walls, lighting, décor)
   - Parking lot/exterior condition (if applicable)
   - Safety: clear exits, no hazards, proper lighting
   - Score: Critical deficiencies? (equipment down, safety hazards) / Minor items?

   **Signage & Branding**
   - Exterior signage: present, clean, illuminated, on-brand
   - Interior signage: menu boards, wayfinding, promotional signage on-brand
   - Logo/brand colors/fonts used consistently and correctly
   - No unauthorized modifications or outdated signage
   - Point-of-sale and operational signage matches brand standards
   - Score violations by criticality

   **Uniforms & Appearance**
   - Staff uniforms present and in good condition
   - Grooming standards met (hair, nails, visible tattoos/piercings per brand policy)
   - Uniform cleanliness and brand logo visibility
   - Consistency across staff
   - Score: Major violations (unauthorized uniforms) / Minor (wear and tear)

   **Product Quality & Standards**
   - Products/menu items match brand specifications (if food: appearance, temperature, portion size)
   - Ingredient quality and sourcing aligns with brand requirements
   - Product preparation follows documented procedures
   - Packaging and presentation on-brand
   - Expiration dates monitored; no expired products in use
   - Score: Critical (health/safety violations) vs. minor deviations

   **Customer Experience**
   - Service standards: greeting, responsiveness, professionalism
   - Transaction accuracy (if POS system: correct pricing, proper discounts applied)
   - Customer complaint resolution processes in place
   - Loyalty program/membership enrollment when applicable
   - Feedback mechanisms (reviews, surveys, mystery shopper scores)
   - Score: Major (poor service culture) vs. minor (inconsistent greeting)

3. **Audit Scoring Methodology**

   For each audit area, categorize findings:

   **Critical Violations** (immediate corrective action required):
   - Health/safety hazards
   - Equipment failures affecting product/service delivery
   - Franchise agreement breach (operational hours, location policies)
   - Significant brand standard deviations affecting customer perception
   - Regulatory non-compliance (labor, health, safety)
   - Score: 0/10 for that category

   **Major Non-Compliance** (corrective action within 30 days):
   - Significant deviations from brand standards
   - Product quality issues not critical but noticeable
   - Recurring customer complaints
   - Score: 3-5/10

   **Minor Issues** (corrective action within 60 days):
   - Cosmetic or wear-and-tear issues
   - Single instances of procedure deviation
   - Outdated but not expired materials
   - Score: 6-8/10

   **Compliant** (no action needed):
   - Meets or exceeds all standards
   - Score: 9-10/10

   **Overall Unit Score**: Average of category scores

4. **Franchise Agreement Compliance Monitoring**

   Key terms to verify:
   - **Operating Hours**: Unit operating within contracted hours
   - **Location**: Operating at correct approved location, no unauthorized expansion
   - **Territory**: Not encroaching on other franchisee territories
   - **Royalty Payments**: On-time payments to franchisor
   - **Marketing Fund**: Contributions to co-op advertising fund if required
   - **Insurance**: Adequate coverage per agreement
   - **Training Requirements**: Manager and staff training certifications current
   - **Equipment Standards**: Using approved equipment and technology
   - **Reporting**: Submitting required reports timely and accurately
   - **Renewal/Lease**: Maintaining franchise agreement in good standing, lease terms compatible

   Document any deviations and their business impact.

5. **Regulatory Compliance Check**

   Ask the operator to confirm status of:
   - **Health & Safety**: Latest health inspection score, food handling certifications, OSHA compliance
   - **Labor Compliance**: Minimum wage, overtime, break requirements, child labor (if applicable), I-9 verification
   - **Accessibility**: ADA compliance, facilities accessible
   - **Environmental**: Waste disposal, recycling, hazmat handling if applicable
   - **Licensing**: Business license, food service permit, alcohol license if applicable, current and posted
   - **Insurance**: General liability, workers comp, proof of coverage on file

   Flag any open violations or upcoming renewals.

6. **Flag Critical Issues**
   - List all critical violations separately with required immediate action
   - Include potential franchisor enforcement implications
   - Estimate business/brand impact of each critical issue

7. **Generate Corrective Action Plan**

   For each finding (critical and major):
   - **Issue**: Specific, measurable description
   - **Root Cause**: Why did this occur?
   - **Corrective Action**: Specific steps to resolve
   - **Responsible Party**: Who will fix it?
   - **Timeline**: When will it be complete?
   - **Verification**: How will compliance be confirmed?
   - **Prevention**: How to prevent recurrence?

   Provide deadlines:
   - Critical: 7 days to report status, 30 days to resolve
   - Major: 30 days to resolve
   - Minor: 60 days to resolve

8. **Create Audit Report**

   Structure as:
   - **Executive Summary**: Overall compliance rating (% compliant), critical issues flagged, priority actions
   - **Audit Details by Category**: Findings in each brand standard category, scoring, specific violations
   - **Franchise Agreement Status**: Any breaches or concerns
   - **Regulatory Compliance**: Any violations or items needing renewal
   - **Critical Issues Table**: All critical findings with deadline/impact
   - **Corrective Action Plan**: Detailed CAP by issue with timeline
   - **Audit Certification**: Date, auditor, next audit date

9. **Schedule Follow-up**
   - Recommend 30-day check-in for critical items
   - Recommend 60-day re-audit of major items
   - Schedule next comprehensive audit (typically annually or semi-annually)

## Output Format

Present audit findings as:
1. **Audit Summary**: Overall compliance %, critical issues count, major/minor findings breakdown
2. **Category Scorecard**: Table showing score in each brand standard category
3. **Critical Issues Section**: List with required corrective actions and 7/30-day timeline
4. **Detailed Findings**: By category, with scoring rationale
5. **Corrective Action Plan**: CAP table with issue, action, responsible party, deadline, verification method
6. **Regulatory/Agreement Status**: Current compliance with external requirements
7. **Recommendations**: Beyond compliance—improvements to strengthen unit

Ask clarifying questions about unit operations, recent changes, or specific known issues before proceeding. Request documentation access if this is a formal audit.
