# Unit Financial Analysis and Economics

Your task is to help a franchise operator analyze financial performance of a single unit or portfolio of units, identify profit improvement opportunities, and benchmark against industry standards.

## Process

1. **Gather Financial Data**
   - Ask operator to provide or reference financial statements for the unit(s):
     - Period covered (month/quarter/year)
     - Total Revenue (top line)
     - Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) or Product Cost
     - Labor costs (wages + payroll taxes + benefits)
     - Occupancy costs (rent/lease, utilities, property taxes, insurance)
     - Operating expenses (marketing, supplies, repairs/maintenance, other)
     - Capital expenditures (if any during period)
     - Royalty payments to franchisor
     - Marketing/co-op advertising fund contributions
   - Ask for comparable prior periods (prior year same period, prior quarter) for trend analysis
   - Request unit information: size (sqft), location type, unit age, format/concept

2. **Build 4-Wall P&L Analysis**

   Construct a unit-level P&L with standard franchise structure:

   ```
   REVENUE                                      [Amount]    [% of Revenue]
   =========================================================================
   Total Revenue                                [XXX]       100.0%

   COST OF GOODS SOLD
   - Food/Product Cost                          [XXX]       [XX]%
   - Packaging Materials                        [XXX]       [X]%
   ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
   Gross COGS                                   [XXX]       [XX]%

   Gross Profit (Revenue - COGS)                [XXX]       [XX]%

   OPERATING EXPENSES

   LABOR (most important cost)
   - Manager Salary                             [XXX]       [X]%
   - Assistant Manager Salary                   [XXX]       [X]%
   - Crew Wages (hourly)                        [XXX]       [XX]%
   - Payroll Taxes & Benefits (est. 12-15%)    [XXX]       [X]%
   ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
   Total Labor Cost                             [XXX]       [XX]% (typically 25-35%)

   OCCUPANCY
   - Rent/Lease Payment                         [XXX]       [X]%
   - Utilities (electric, water, gas)           [XXX]       [X]%
   - Property Taxes & Insurance                 [XXX]       [X]%
   - Maintenance & Repairs                      [XXX]       [X]%
   ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
   Total Occupancy Cost                         [XXX]       [X]% (typically 8-12%)

   MARKETING & ADVERTISING
   - Local Marketing Spend                      [XXX]       [X]%
   - Royalty-Based Ad Fund Contribution         [XXX]       [X]%
   ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
   Total Marketing                              [XXX]       [X]% (typically 3-5%)

   OTHER OPERATING EXPENSES
   - Supplies (cleaning, paper, etc.)           [XXX]       [X]%
   - Equipment Leases                           [XXX]       [X]%
   - Technology/POS/Phone/Internet              [XXX]       [X]%
   - Professional Services (accounting, legal)  [XXX]       [X]%
   - Credit Card Processing Fees                [XXX]       [X]%
   - Licenses, Permits, Subscriptions           [XXX]       [X]%
   - Other Miscellaneous                        [XXX]       [X]%
   ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
   Total Other Operating Expenses               [XXX]       [X]% (typically 5-8%)

   ROYALTY & AD FUND (if not included above)
   - Franchise Royalty (typically 5-6%)         [XXX]       [X]%
   ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

   =========================================================================
   EBITDA (Operating Profit)                    [XXX]       [XX]%
   (Gross Profit - Operating Expenses - Royalties)

   CAPITAL EXPENDITURES (Non-Operating)
   - Equipment, Remodeling, Technology          [XXX]       [X]%
   ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

   NET OPERATING PROFIT (before taxes/debt)     [XXX]       [XX]%
   ```

3. **Calculate Key Ratios and Metrics**

   **Profitability Ratios**:
   - Gross Margin % = (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue × 100
   - EBITDA Margin % = EBITDA / Revenue × 100
   - Operating Margin % = Operating Profit / Revenue × 100
   - Net Margin % = Net Profit / Revenue × 100

   **Efficiency Ratios**:
   - Food/Product Cost % = COGS / Revenue × 100 (benchmark: 25-35% depending on concept)
   - Labor Cost % = Total Labor / Revenue × 100 (benchmark: 25-35%)
   - Occupancy Cost % = Rent & Utilities / Revenue × 100 (benchmark: 8-12%)
   - Operating Expense Ratio = Total OpEx / Revenue × 100

   **Productivity Metrics** (if data available):
   - Revenue per Sqft = Total Revenue / Unit Size
   - Revenue per Labor Hour = Total Revenue / Total Labor Hours
   - Labor Hours per $100 Revenue = (Total Labor Hours / Revenue) × 100
   - Transactions per Labor Hour (if transaction count available)
   - Average Ticket = Revenue / Number of Customers or Transactions

   **Cash Flow Indicators**:
   - EBITDA in dollars (operating cash generation)
   - Days Sales Outstanding (if credit sales component)
   - Capital Expenditure Run Rate (annual maintenance + growth CapEx)

4. **Contribution Margin Analysis** (if detailed cost structure available)

   For units with product mix data:
   - Calculate contribution margin for product categories
   - Identify which product lines drive profit vs. which are loss leaders
   - Analyze customer mix: high-ticket customers vs. low-ticket
   - Recommend product mix optimization to improve margins

5. **Break-Even Analysis**

   Calculate the unit's financial runway:
   - Break-Even Revenue (monthly) = Fixed Costs / Contribution Margin %
     - Fixed Costs: rent, manager salary, minimum utilities, insurance
     - Variable Costs: COGS, hourly wages proportional to volume
   - Break-Even Transaction Count = Break-Even Revenue / Average Ticket
   - Months to Break-Even (if unit not yet profitable)
   - Safety Margin = (Current Revenue - Break-Even Revenue) / Current Revenue

6. **Compare to Benchmarks**

   Benchmark unit metrics against:
   - **Prior Period Comparison**: Same quarter/month last year (YoY growth)
   - **Portfolio Average**: If operator has multiple units, show average performance
   - **Peer Units**: Similar size, location type, unit age
   - **Franchise Average**: If operator has franchise system benchmark data
   - **Industry Standards**: Generic QSR/retail benchmarks if applicable

   Show variance analysis: actual vs. benchmark, variance amount, % variance.
   Highlight: units significantly above/below benchmark, deteriorating trends.

7. **Identify Profit Improvement Opportunities**

   Analyze each P&L line for opportunities:

   **COGS Improvements**:
   - Is product cost higher than benchmark? Renegotiate supplier contracts?
   - Waste/spoilage issues? Inventory management improvement?
   - Portion size/recipe adherence issues? Retraining needed?
   - Product mix shift to higher margin items?
   - Supplier consolidation or sourcing optimization?

   **Labor Cost Reduction**:
   - Labor % higher than benchmark? Understaffing/overtime issue?
   - Scheduling optimization: reduce payroll during slow periods?
   - Productivity improvement: more transactions per labor hour?
   - High turnover? Retention improvement could reduce training costs?
   - Wage/benefit structure alignment with market and franchise benchmarks?

   **Revenue Growth Opportunities**:
   - Is traffic (customer count) below benchmark? Marketing/promotions needed?
   - Is average ticket below benchmark? Upselling training? Menu pricing?
   - Day-part mix: weak periods? Daypart-specific promotions?
   - Customer acquisition cost: how efficient is marketing spend?
   - Repeat customer rate: can loyalty programs drive more visits?

   **Occupancy Cost Optimization**:
   - Is rent % of revenue high? Consider renegotiation, relocation, or format change?
   - Utilities high? Energy efficiency improvements, equipment upgrades?
   - Insurance costs aligned with other units?

   **Operating Expense Control**:
   - Discretionary spend (marketing, supplies, professional services) aligned with plan?
   - Credit card fees high? Explore alternative payment processors?
   - Technology/subscription costs justified and optimized?

8. **Build Unit Economics Model** (if projecting)

   If analyzing a new unit or turnaround scenario:
   - Develop pro forma 5-year financial projection
   - Sensitivity analysis: how do changes in traffic/ticket/costs affect profitability?
   - Break-even timing and path to target profitability
   - Capital required vs. expected returns
   - Payback period on initial investment

9. **Create Multi-Unit Portfolio Analysis** (if operator has multiple units)

   If analyzing multiple units:
   - Portfolio summary: total revenue, total EBITDA, blended margins
   - Unit-by-unit performance ranking
   - Identify top profit contributors vs. marginal units
   - Identify scaling opportunities (best practices from top performers)
   - Aggregate cash generation vs. CapEx needs
   - Recommend reallocation of resources to highest-return units

10. **Develop Action Plan for Improvement**

    Prioritize opportunities by:
    - Impact on profitability (high impact first)
    - Ease of implementation (quick wins first)
    - Time to realization (immediate vs. 6-month)

    For top 3-5 opportunities:
    - **Specific Action**: What will you do?
    - **Owner**: Who's responsible?
    - **Timeline**: When will it happen?
    - **Expected Benefit**: $ impact on EBITDA
    - **Risks/Constraints**: What could prevent success?
    - **Success Metrics**: How will you measure it?

11. **Present Financial Analysis**

## Output Format

Structure your response as:
1. **Executive Summary**: EBITDA, key margin %, YoY growth, top 3 improvement opportunities
2. **Unit P&L Statement**: 4-wall detail with prior period comparison
3. **Key Metrics Dashboard**: Profitability ratios, productivity metrics, efficiency ratios
4. **Benchmark Comparison**: How unit stacks up vs. prior period, peers, franchise average
5. **Detailed Cost Analysis**: Breakdown of each major cost category, identifies top opportunities
6. **Improvement Opportunity Summary**: Top 5-10 opportunities ranked by impact
7. **Action Plan**: Specific improvements with owner, timeline, expected benefit
8. **Pro Forma/Sensitivity Analysis**: (if applicable) Financial projections with scenarios

Ask for clarifying questions about unit operations, known challenges, management priorities, and any constraints before proceeding. Request access to detailed financial statements or sales records if analysis will be more valuable with more granular data.
