# Staff Training and Development Plan

Your task is to create a comprehensive training and development plan for a franchise unit or multi-unit operation.

## Process

1. **Gather Current State Information**
   - Ask about current organizational structure: number of staff, roles (manager, assistant manager, crew), average tenure
   - Describe current training programs: what exists today, how structured, who conducts training
   - Identify specific skill gaps: where are staff underperforming, what feedback have managers given
   - Ask about turnover: annual turnover rate, which roles have highest turnover, exit reasons if known
   - Clarify business model/operational complexity to understand training scope needed
   - Understand any known brand training requirements or compliance training needs

2. **Assess Current Team Capabilities**

   For each role level:
   - **Current Competency**: What skills do staff currently have?
   - **Proficiency Assessment**: On scale 1-5 (1=beginner, 5=expert), where do staff perform in:
     - Technical skills (product knowledge, equipment operation, procedures)
     - Customer service/interaction
     - Problem-solving and decision-making
     - Time management and productivity
     - Safety and compliance awareness
     - Team collaboration and communication
     - Leadership (for managers): delegation, coaching, performance management

   Ask operator to rate current state: emerging (scores avg 1-2), developing (2-3), proficient (3-4), advanced (4-5).

3. **Identify Capability Gaps**

   Compare current state to desired state:
   - **Strategic Gaps**: Where does the business need to improve to hit growth targets?
     - If unit is underperforming, are gaps in execution, motivation, systems?
     - If scaling to new units, what training will new managers/teams need?
   - **Operational Gaps**: What procedures do staff not follow consistently?
   - **Role-Specific Gaps**: Technical, customer service, leadership gaps by role
   - **Compliance Gaps**: Certifications, legal training needs (food safety, labor law, safety)
   - **Development Gaps**: Which staff have promotion potential needing advanced skills?

   Prioritize gaps by: impact on customer experience, impact on profitability, frequency of occurrence, ease to address.

4. **Define Competency Framework by Role**

   For each role (Manager, Assistant Manager, Crew/Staff), define 5-7 core competencies:

   **Manager Competencies** (example):
   1. Strategic Planning & Execution: Set unit goals, allocate resources, track progress
   2. Operational Excellence: Ensure consistent execution of systems, quality, service
   3. People Leadership: Hire, coach, develop, motivate, manage performance
   4. Financial Management: Build/manage P&L, control costs, drive revenue
   5. Brand Advocacy: Ensure brand standards, customer experience, compliance
   6. Problem-Solving: Diagnose issues, implement solutions, prevent recurrence
   7. Self-Development: Continuous learning, seek feedback, model growth mindset

   **Assistant Manager Competencies** (example):
   1. Operational Leadership: Execute systems, coach crew, maintain standards
   2. Customer Focus: Deliver service excellence, resolve issues
   3. Staff Development: Train, observe, provide feedback
   4. Team Collaboration: Support manager, mentor crew, build team
   5. Compliance & Safety: Maintain safe environment, follow procedures
   6. Productivity: Efficient operation, time management, multi-tasking

   **Crew/Staff Competencies** (example):
   1. Product/Service Excellence: Quality work, correct procedures, attention to detail
   2. Customer Service: Friendly, responsive, problem-solving
   3. Safety & Compliance: Follow safety procedures, food handling if applicable
   4. Teamwork: Collaboration, communication, positive attitude
   5. Reliability: Attendance, punctuality, consistency
   6. Initiative: Identify problems, suggest improvements, go above and beyond

   For each competency, define proficiency levels (1-5) with observable behaviors.

5. **Design Training Curriculum**

   Structure training into phases:

   **Phase 1: Onboarding (Week 1)**
   - Welcome orientation: company values, culture, expectations
   - Role-specific overview: responsibilities, key processes, success metrics
   - Compliance training: safety, health codes, labor laws, sexual harassment
   - Meet the team: manager intro, peer introductions, team culture
   - First-day tasks: workspace setup, paperwork, system access
   - Duration: 1-2 days, completion by end of week 1

   **Phase 2: Foundational Training (Weeks 2-4)**
   - Product/Service Knowledge: deep dive on products, menu, standards
   - Core Procedures: step-by-step operational procedures (POS, service flow, etc.)
   - Safety & Compliance: detailed training on food safety, hazard awareness, procedures
   - Customer Service Standards: greeting, upselling, problem-resolution
   - Equipment Operation: hands-on training on all equipment used
   - Practical shadowing: 10-20 hours observing experienced staff
   - Duration: 2-4 weeks depending on role complexity
   - Completion milestone: procedural knowledge assessment, signed-off competency checklist

   **Phase 3: Skill Development (Weeks 5-12)**
   - Advanced procedures: handling edge cases, high-stress situations
   - Speed and efficiency: practice to build speed while maintaining quality
   - Leadership/coaching (for assistant managers): how to observe and give feedback
   - Problem-solving: diagnostic skills, when to escalate
   - Customer service recovery: handling complaints, retaining upset customers
   - Duration: 4-8 weeks
   - Completion milestone: observed competency demonstration, manager sign-off

   **Phase 4: Ongoing Development (Months 4+)**
   - Monthly coaching: 1-on-1 feedback, progress against competencies
   - Cross-training: learn other roles for flexibility
   - Advanced topics: handling complex situations, mentoring others
   - Leadership development (for managers): strategic thinking, P&L management, team development
   - Compliance refresher: annual food safety, safety, harassment training
   - Cadence: ongoing; monthly 1-on-1s, quarterly competency reviews, annual planning

   **Phase 5: Management/Leadership Development (for promotion track)**
   - Strategic planning: unit P&L, goal-setting, forecasting
   - People management: hiring, performance management, compensation
   - Financial analysis: P&L deep dive, cost control, profitability improvement
   - Change management: implementing changes, managing resistance
   - Multi-unit oversight (if applicable): scaling practices, consistency
   - Duration: 8-12 weeks structured, then ongoing quarterly sessions
   - Completion: readiness assessment for promotion

6. **Identify Training Delivery Methods**

   For each training module, select delivery method:
   - **Instructor-Led**: Hands-on training for procedures, equipment, customer service
   - **On-the-Job Training (OJT)**: Shadowing, then doing with observation
   - **Documentation/Self-Study**: Written procedures, videos, online modules
   - **Group Training**: Quarterly team meetings covering policy changes, new products
   - **Mentoring/Coaching**: 1-on-1 ongoing feedback from manager
   - **External Training**: Brand-provided courses, third-party certifications
   - **Peer Training**: Experienced staff train newer staff

   Recommend blended approach: initial instruction, followed by OJT, supported by documentation.

7. **Create Implementation Timeline**

   Build out a 90-day launch plan:
   - **Week 1**: Announce plan, assign trainers, prepare materials
   - **Week 2-3**: Onboarding and foundational training for any new hires or struggling staff
   - **Week 4-6**: Skill development phase continues, monitor progress
   - **Week 7-8**: Assessments, competency reviews, gap identification
   - **Week 9-12**: Advanced training, finalize development, prepare for ongoing phase

   Then ongoing:
   - Monthly 1-on-1 coaching by manager
   - Quarterly skill refreshers and progress reviews
   - Annual competency assessments and planning
   - Quarterly team training on policy/product updates

8. **Define Success Metrics & Accountability**

   Measure training effectiveness:
   - **Completion**: % of staff completing each training phase on time
   - **Competency Achievement**: % of staff demonstrating proficiency in core competencies
   - **Assessment Scores**: Training assessments, knowledge tests, observation scores
   - **Customer Impact**: NPS/satisfaction scores, complaint reduction
   - **Operational Impact**: Consistency, error rates, speed/efficiency improvements
   - **Retention**: Staff retention rate in 6 months post-training
   - **Promotion**: Staff ready for promotion/advancement

   Assign accountability:
   - Manager: responsible for ensuring staff complete training, coaching, progress tracking
   - Trainer/Assistant Manager: conduct training, maintain training records
   - Staff: complete training, demonstrate competency, participate in development

9. **Build the Training Plan Document**

   Include:
   - **Current State Assessment**: Organizational structure, current capabilities by role, key gaps
   - **Competency Framework**: Definitions and proficiency levels for each role
   - **Training Curriculum**: By phase with topics, duration, methods, success criteria
   - **Implementation Timeline**: 90-day launch + ongoing cadence
   - **Training Materials Needed**: What needs to be created/sourced
   - **Trainer Requirements**: Who will conduct training, training needed for trainers
   - **Success Metrics**: How you'll measure effectiveness
   - **Resource Plan**: Budget, time commitment, scheduling
   - **Risk Mitigation**: How to handle disruptions, scaling to new units
   - **Review/Revision Schedule**: When to assess and update plan (quarterly/annually)

## Output Format

Present the training plan as:
1. **Executive Summary**: Current state, key gaps, training approach, expected outcomes
2. **Organizational Analysis**: Current structure, tenure, turnover, current training programs
3. **Competency Framework**: By role with proficiency levels and behavioral indicators
4. **Training Curriculum**: By phase with topics, timeline, delivery method, completion criteria
5. **Implementation Timeline**: Gantt-style month-by-month plan for first 90 days and beyond
6. **Training Materials Checklist**: What exists, what needs to be created
7. **Success Metrics & Accountability**: How you'll track effectiveness and who's responsible
8. **Budget & Resource Plan**: Estimated hours, tools, materials, budget if any
9. **Risk Plan**: How to handle coaching challenges, scaling, retention issues

Ask clarifying questions about current staff skills, specific operational challenges, and business priorities before building the plan. Tailor the framework and curriculum to the specific business model and unit needs.
