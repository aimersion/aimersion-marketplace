# Application Timeline and Deadlines

Strategic timing throughout the college admissions process significantly impacts your success. This timeline provides month-by-month guidance from sophomore year through senior year, covering testing, applications, financial aid, and decision deadlines.

## Sophomore Year

**Fall**
- Take the PSAT if available (practice for SAT)
- Begin researching colleges informally
- Assess academic trajectory and discuss future goals with counselor
- Explore potential majors and career interests

**Spring**
- Continue building college research list
- Take SAT or ACT if you want to establish baseline
- Consider summer programs or college camps for exploration
- Meet with college counselor to discuss timeline

**Summer**
- Attend college visits and campus tours if possible
- Consider SAT/ACT prep if standardized testing needed
- Engage in meaningful summer activities (internships, volunteering, research, leadership)
- Read widely and explore intellectual interests

## Junior Year

**Fall**
- **October/November:** Take PSAT for National Merit eligibility
- Finalize SAT and ACT test schedule (most students test in spring/fall senior year)
- Begin or continue college research systematically
- Request initial college information from schools
- Meet with college counselor; review college list framework

**Winter/January**
- **January:** Take first SAT or ACT if planning spring testing
- Refine college list based on research (reach/target/safety framework)
- Start documenting extracurricular activities, leadership, community service
- Meet with teachers to gauge rec letter suitability

**Spring**
- **March/April:** Additional SAT/ACT testing (last spring test before applications)
- Attend college representative visits and college fairs
- Campus visits if feasible
- Finalize college list (12-15 schools for research, narrow to 7-12 final)
- Request applications and review requirements for each school

**Summer**
- **June/July:** Final SAT/ACT testing if needed (not recommended; earlier is better)
- Request or confirm teacher recommendations
- Prepare brag sheets for recommenders
- Create a spreadsheet with all deadlines and requirements for each school
- Draft personal statements and essays
- Summer activities that strengthen profile

## Senior Year: Early Fall (Critical Period)

**August**
- **Register for September/October SAT or ACT** if you haven't finalized scores
- Create master spreadsheet with all college deadlines and requirements
- Create individual checklists per school with:
  - Application portal login and requirements
  - Essay prompts and word counts
  - Recommendation letter requirements
  - Test score submission details
  - Application deadlines and decision timeline
- Request official transcripts from high school
- Begin writing personal statements and supplemental essays

**September**
- **SAT/ACT testing** (if registered)
- Finalize personal statement draft
- Begin supplemental essay writing
- Request teacher recommendations (give 4+ weeks notice)
- Request school counselor recommendation
- Contact schools if you plan Early Decision (application opens Sept 1)

**October**
- **November SAT/ACT registration closes**
- Apply Early Decision (if chosen) by November 1
- Complete and submit Early Action applications (if offered)
- Obtain official test scores and arrange Score Choice if applicable
- Continue supplemental essay writing

## Senior Year: Fall Deadlines

**November 1 (Common Early Decision Deadline)**
- Early Decision applications due
- Some schools use November 15 or other November dates

**November 15 (Secondary Early Decision/Action Dates)**
- Some schools' ED/EA applications due

**Early to Mid-November**
- Continue applications to other schools on your list

## Senior Year: Winter Deadlines

**December 31 / January 1 (Most Common Regular Decision Deadline)**
- Majority of schools' applications due
- Some use December 15 or January 15

**Late December / Early January**
- Complete remaining applications
- Ensure all required materials submitted (essays, transcripts, test scores, recommendations)
- FAFSA submission (opens October 1, priority deadline December 1)

**January 10 (CSS Profile Priority Deadline)**
- CSS Profile due at schools requiring it
- Some schools have different dates

## Spring Senior Year

**January through April**
- Monitor application portals for portal deadlines different from application deadlines
- Some schools require additional forms or information by different dates
- Appeal for financial aid if needed

**March 1 - April 30**
- College admission decisions released
- Financial aid award letters arrive (within 2 weeks of admission typically)
- Compare award letters and net costs across schools

**May 1**
- National College Decision Day
- All admitted students must commit by May 1
- Submit enrollment deposits

**May - June**
- Submit housing deposits and preferences
- Register for orientation
- Notify schools you've declined

## Testing Timeline Strategy

**SAT/ACT Timing Recommendations**

Take your first test in **spring of junior year** (March-May). This provides:
- Time to retake if needed before senior year
- Fall senior year for final retakes without rushing
- Score by fall to include in applications
- Reduces stress during senior year application period

**Typical Testing Schedule:**
- Junior Spring (March, April, or May): First attempt
- Junior Summer or Fall Senior Year: Retake if needed
- No later than October senior year: Final score submission

**Plan to test 1-2 times ideally.** Three tests is acceptable; four or more suggests insufficient prep rather than improvement.

## Financial Aid Timeline

**October 1** — FAFSA available for 2024-2025 award year

**December 1** — FAFSA priority deadline (some state/school aid tied to this date)

**January 10** — CSS Profile priority deadline for schools requiring it

**February - March** — Submit any additional financial aid documents requested

**Mid-March through April** — Colleges mail financial aid award letters

**May 1** — Enrollment deposit due (confirms enrollment and locks in financial aid package)

## Early Decision vs. Early Action vs. Regular Decision

**Early Decision (ED)**
- Binding commitment—you agree to attend if admitted
- Single ED application only per cycle
- Applications due: November 1 or 15 (varies)
- Decisions released: December 15-January 15
- Use only if you have a clear first-choice school and can afford it

**Early Action (EA)**
- Non-binding admission—you can apply to multiple schools, delay decision
- Some schools restrict whether you can apply regular decision to other schools (Single-Choice EA)
- Applications due: November 1 or 15
- Decisions released: December 15-January 31
- Use if you're ready and a school offers non-binding early action

**Regular Decision (RD)**
- Standard application timeline
- No advantage or disadvantage from timing (submit by deadline)
- Applications due: January 1 (most common)
- Decisions released: March 1-April 30
- Use for most schools on your list

**Rolling Admissions**
- Schools evaluate and admit students on rolling basis as applications arrive
- No fixed deadline (continues until class fills)
- Earlier applications reviewed first
- Apply early in fall if schools use rolling admissions

## Master Checklist

**Before Junior Year Ends:**
- [ ] College list drafted (15-20 schools researched)
- [ ] SAT/ACT testing plan finalized
- [ ] Extracurricular profile documented
- [ ] Initial college visits completed (if feasible)

**By August Senior Year:**
- [ ] Final college list confirmed (7-12 schools)
- [ ] Master deadline spreadsheet created
- [ ] Test scores finalized (or test date scheduled)
- [ ] Personal statement drafted

**By November Senior Year:**
- [ ] All early action/decision applications submitted
- [ ] Teacher recommendations submitted
- [ ] FAFSA in progress or completed
- [ ] Test scores sent to colleges

**By January 1 Senior Year:**
- [ ] All regular decision applications submitted
- [ ] All supplemental essays completed
- [ ] CSS Profile submitted (if required)
- [ ] FAFSA completed

**By May 1 Senior Year:**
- [ ] College decisions reviewed
- [ ] Financial aid award letters compared
- [ ] Final school chosen and enrollment deposit submitted
