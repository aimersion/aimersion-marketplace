# Weekly Intelligence Report

Turn multi-channel marketing data into one clear view of what's working and what to do about it.

## The Purpose

A weekly marketing report is a decision tool, not a data dump. The person reading it should know in under 10 minutes: what happened, what it means, and what we're doing about it.

If a data point in the report doesn't drive a decision, cut it.

## Report Structure

### Section 1: The Headline (30 seconds)

```
MARKETING INTELLIGENCE — WEEK OF [DATE]

THE HEADLINE: [One sentence on the most important thing this week]

Examples:
"LinkedIn drove 14 MQLs this week — best week of Q2. Scaling budget 30% starting Monday."
"Lead volume dropped 40% — tracked to email deliverability issue. Fixed Thursday."
"Webinar series week 3: 89 registrations, 42% show rate. 23 hot leads handed to sales."

STATUS: [On Track / Needs Attention / Off Track] vs [Monthly MQL goal]
MQL progress: [X] of [Y] goal for month ([Z]% with [X] weeks remaining)
```

## Reporting Rules

1. **One page maximum** — if it doesn't fit, cut data not a decision
2. **Every number has a "so what"** — don't just report, interpret
3. **Red/yellow/green** — status indicators faster than paragraphs
4. **Clear asks at the end** — never let leadership wonder what you need
5. **Same template every week** — familiarity makes it faster to read and act on
6. **Send Monday morning** — when decisions for the week are being made, not Friday afternoon

## Cadence Structure

```
WEEKLY: Full report above
  Audience: CMO/VP, CEO, VP Sales

MONTHLY (first Monday of month): Weekly report + monthly rollup
  Add: Channel ROI analysis, MQL attribution by source, goal vs actual, plan for next month

QUARTERLY: Full performance review
  Add: ICP and messaging review, channel portfolio review, budget reallocation, next quarter goals
```
