# Conversion Optimizer

Find and fix the friction points that prevent visitors from becoming leads.

## The CRO Mindset

Conversion optimization is not about making pages prettier. It's about removing every obstacle between a motivated visitor and the action they want to take. Every element either helps or hurts that journey.

## The 5-Second Test

A visitor should answer these within 5 seconds of landing:
1. What is this?
2. Who is it for?
3. What do I get if I act?
4. Why should I trust this?
5. What do I do next?

If any answer is unclear or slow, that's a conversion problem. Start there.

## Element-by-Element Audit

### Headline (single highest-impact element)

```
Strong headline patterns:
"[Specific outcome] for [specific ICP]"
"How [similar company type] [achieved specific result]"
"[ICP pain stated directly] — here's how to fix it"

Weak headline patterns (rewrite these):
"The Leading Platform for [Category]" — meaningless, feature-led
"Welcome to [Company Name]" — visitor-irrelevant
"Improve Your [Function] Process" — too vague to convert
"[Product Name]: Now Available" — no one cares
```

### Form Field Analysis

Ask about every field: "Is this essential to qualify or route this lead?"

```
Essential (keep):
- Email — always
- First name — personalization
- Company name — for B2B routing and scoring

Add only with a specific use case:
- Phone — only if sales calls immediately (adds significant friction)
- Job title — if used for routing/scoring (use dropdown to reduce friction)
- Company size — if used for scoring (use dropdown)

Remove immediately:
- Address — never needed at lead stage
- "How did you hear about us?" — use UTMs instead
- Multiple optional fields — they still communicate friction
- Any field that will never be used in CRM
```

Each field removed typically lifts form completion by 8-15%.

### CTA Button

```
High-converting text:
"Get my [specific thing]" — ownership framing
"Book my demo" — not "Submit"
"Start free trial"
"Download the [specific asset name]"
"Show me how it works"

Avoid:
"Submit" — impersonal and vague
"Click here" — non-specific
"Learn more" — weak commitment
"Request information" — passive

Design requirements:
- High contrast with background
- Large enough to tap on mobile (44px minimum height)
- White space around it — no competing elements
- One button per page section (not 3 competing CTAs)
```

### Social Proof Placement

```
Most effective for B2B:
1. Customer logos — recognizable brands your ICP knows (above fold)
2. Specific result quote — "We reduced ramp time by 40% in 90 days" — Name, Title, Company
3. Review scores — G2 or Capterra with number of reviews
4. Usage stat — "Trusted by 500+ sales teams at companies like X, Y, Z"

Placement priority:
- Above fold: logo strip (authority signal immediately)
- Mid-page: testimonial with specific result (near form or CTA)
- Near CTA: review score + quote (reduces risk at decision moment)
```

## A/B Test Priority Queue

Run in this order (highest expected impact first):

1. **Headline** — biggest lever, highest variance, test first
2. **CTA text** — high impact, low effort to change
3. **Form length** — removing one field can lift 10-20%
4. **Offer type** — guide vs webinar vs assessment vs demo
5. **Social proof format** — logos vs testimonial vs stats
6. **Hero image/video** — person vs product vs abstract

**Test standards:**
- One variable at a time
- Minimum 250 conversions per variant (not visits)
- Minimum 2-week runtime
- 95% statistical confidence before declaring winner

## Conversion Rate Benchmarks

| Page Type | Low | Average | Strong |
|---|---|---|---|
| B2B demo request | 1-2% | 3-5% | 6-10%+ |
| Content download | 5-10% | 15-25% | 30%+ |
| Webinar registration | 10-15% | 20-30% | 35%+ |
| Free trial | 2-5% | 5-10% | 12%+ |

If below average: fix headline and form first.
If at average: test CTA text and social proof.
If above average: focus on lead quality, not just volume.
