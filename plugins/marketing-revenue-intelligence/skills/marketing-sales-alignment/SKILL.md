# Marketing-Sales Alignment

Build the systems that make marketing and sales one revenue function instead of two teams blaming each other.

## Why Alignment Breaks Down

The root cause is different success metrics:
- Marketing is measured on: leads, MQLs, pipeline generated
- Sales is measured on: revenue closed, quota attainment

When deals don't close, marketing says "We gave you 50 MQLs."
Sales says "None of them were real."

Neither team is lying. Both are optimizing for their own metric. The fix: shared definitions, shared data, and shared accountability for the same outcomes.

## The Five Alignment Systems

### System 1: Shared ICP Definition

Both teams must agree on exactly who they're targeting.

```
Joint ICP document (review quarterly):
- Firmographic criteria: company size, industry, geography, stage
- Technographic criteria: tools they use that indicate fit or disqualify
- Title criteria: who buys, who uses, who blocks, who champions
- Behavioral criteria: what actions signal readiness to buy
- Exclusion criteria: who is definitively NOT a fit

Signed off by: Head of Marketing + VP Sales
Updated: Quarterly using win/loss data
```

### System 2: Explicit MQL Definition

An MQL is not a judgment call — it's a lead that meets specific, agreed-upon criteria that marketing and sales both signed off on.

```
Document the exact criteria:
- What score threshold = MQL?
- Which actions auto-qualify regardless of score?
- What firmographic data disqualifies regardless of behavior?

Review trigger: If MQL → SQL rate drops below [X]%, revisit definition.
Sales rejection requires a documented reason — "bad lead" is not acceptable.
```

### System 3: Lead Handoff SLA

```
Marketing commits to:
- Lead meets documented MQL criteria before passing to sales
- Lead enriched with: company, title, behavior summary, source
- Sales notified within [X] hours of MQL trigger (target: 1 hour)

Sales commits to:
- First contact attempt within [X] hours of MQL receipt (target: 24 hours)
- Minimum 5 contact attempts before marking unresponsive
- Feedback on lead quality within [X] days (thumbs up/down + reason)
- Rejection requires a documented reason tied to ICP criteria

Tracking: Both teams see the same dashboard showing SLA compliance.
```

### System 4: The Revenue Feedback Loop

The most underbuilt system in most B2B companies. Without it, marketing keeps generating the same quality leads and sales keeps complaining.

```
Weekly (15-min sync or async Slack):
Sales → Marketing: "The [channel/campaign] leads this week were [quality]. Here's why."
Marketing → Sales: "Here's what content is converting to MQLs. Anything useful for calls?"

Monthly revenue review:
Marketing shares: MQLs by source, channel ROI, funnel conversion rates
Sales shares: Closed-won by source, top objections, deal velocity
Together: What should we do more of? Less of? What content is missing?

Quarterly ICP review:
Pull closed-won data: firmographic/psychographic profile of best customers
Pull closed-lost data: where we consistently lose and why
Update ICP, scoring, and targeting based on actual outcomes
```

### System 5: Shared Revenue KPIs

```
Joint KPIs (both teams accountable):
1. Pipeline generated by marketing ($, not # of MQLs)
2. MQL → Closed-Won rate (marketing owns quality, sales owns conversion)
3. Marketing-sourced revenue as % of total revenue
4. Time from first marketing touch to closed-won deal

Supporting metrics each team owns:
Marketing: CPL, MQL volume, channel ROI, content output
Sales: MQL response time, SQL → opportunity rate, close rate
```

## Fixes for Common Alignment Failures

**"Sales isn't working our leads"**
→ Implement the SLA with tracking: 5 contacts in 5 days
→ Measure attempt rate in CRM (are they even trying?)
→ If attempts happening but no responses: ICP targeting issue
→ If attempts not happening: management + process issue

**"Marketing's leads are garbage"**
→ Pull rejection report with documented reasons
→ If "wrong company size/industry": fix targeting
→ If "not in buying mode": tighten behavioral scoring requirements
→ If "we can never reach them": fix lead data quality or add phone field

**"We don't know which marketing activities drive revenue"**
→ Implement UTM structure immediately
→ Add "Original Lead Source" field in CRM — captured at form fill, never overwritten
→ Monthly closed-won report by original source — share with both teams

**"Marketing creates content but sales never uses it"**
→ Have sales tell marketing their top 3 objections monthly
→ Marketing creates content specifically addressing those objections
→ Sales gets content in CRM at the deal stage where the objection appears
→ Track: which content pieces correlate with deals that progress faster
