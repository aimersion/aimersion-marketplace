Transform data findings into a compelling executive narrative and presentation structure.

Context: $ARGUMENTS (use this as the topic/findings to build the story around, or create a template if empty)

Create a complete data story including:

**1. The Headline**
- Lead with the single most important insight (one sentence)
- Frame it in business impact terms, not data terms

**2. The Context (Why This Matters)**
- 2-3 sentences establishing why stakeholders should care
- Connect to business strategy or current priorities

**3. What the Data Shows**
- 3-5 key findings, each with:
  - Finding statement (plain English)
  - Supporting evidence (specific numbers)
  - Visualization recommendation
  - So-what implication

**4. The Narrative Arc**
- Situation → Complication → Resolution structure
- Written in flowing prose, not bullet points
- 150-200 words, boardroom-ready

**5. Recommendations**
- 2-4 specific, actionable recommendations
- Each with: action, expected impact, owner, timeline

**6. Presentation Slide Outline**
- Slide-by-slide breakdown (10-12 slides max)
- Each slide: title, key message, visual type, speaker notes

**7. Anticipated Questions & Answers**
- 5-7 questions executives are likely to ask
- Prepared answers with supporting data points

Write in confident, business-fluent language. Avoid jargon. Lead with insight, not methodology.
