# Lead Generator

Build systematic lead generation campaigns for roofing companies through canvassing, neighborhood marketing, and referral programs.

## Lead Generation Channels

### 1. Door-to-Door Canvassing (Highest ROI for storm damage)

**Canvasser Script Framework:**
1. **Introduction** — Name, company, reason for visit (brief)
2. **Establish relevance** — "We noticed storm damage in the neighborhood" or "We just completed work on [neighbor's] roof"
3. **Create awareness** — Most homeowners do not know they have damage
4. **Offer value** — Free, no-obligation roof inspection
5. **Handle objections** — prepared responses for common pushbacks
6. **Set the appointment** — specific day and time, not "we will call you"
7. **Leave behind** — business card, door hanger, inspection report

**Key Objection Responses:**
- "I already have a roofer" → "That is great! We are just offering free inspections for the neighborhood. No obligation."
- "My roof is fine" → "It may be! But hail damage is not always visible from the ground. A free inspection gives you peace of mind."
- "I am not interested" → "I understand. Here is my card in case you notice anything. We are in the area helping your neighbors."
- "Is this a scam?" → "Great question. Here is my license number and our local office address. We have been serving [area] for [X] years."

### 2. Neighborhood Campaigns (After Completing a Nearby Job)

**Strategy:**
- Target 20-40 homes surrounding a completed job
- Door hangers: "We just replaced your neighbor's roof at [address]"
- Before/after photos of the completed job (with permission)
- Limited-time neighborhood discount
- Offer to match existing color/style

### 3. Referral Program
- Offer $250-500 referral bonus for signed contracts
- Provide referral cards with tracking codes
- Follow up with referrers on outcome
- Thank referrers promptly with payment/gift

### 4. Digital Lead Generation
- Google Local Services Ads (pay per lead)
- Facebook/Instagram ads targeting homeowners in service area
- NextDoor posts and recommendations
- Google Business Profile optimization (reviews, photos, posts)

### 5. Insurance Agent Partnerships
- Build relationships with local insurance agents
- Offer to be a preferred vendor for storm claims
- Provide agents with materials they can share with policyholders
- Reciprocal referral arrangements

## Canvassing Route Planning

Organize canvassing by:
1. **Storm path** — follow confirmed hail/wind damage reports
2. **Age of roofs** — target neighborhoods where roofs are 15-25+ years old
3. **Proximity to completed jobs** — leverage social proof
4. **Permit data** — neighborhoods with recent re-roofing permits (competitors are active = market is buying)
5. **HOA neighborhoods** — one approved contractor can lead to multiple jobs

## Tracking and Follow-Up

For every door knocked:
- Address
- Contact name (if given)
- Result: not home, no interest, appointment set, inspection completed
- Follow-up needed: yes/no and date
- Notes

**Follow-up cadence:**
- Day 1: Leave door hanger if not home
- Day 3: Return visit if not home on first attempt
- Day 7: Phone/text follow-up for inspection appointments
- Day 14: Final follow-up for undecided prospects

## Output Formats
- **Canvassing scripts** — complete door-to-door scripts with objection handling
- **Door hanger content** — text and layout for print materials
- **Neighborhood campaign plan** — targeted approach after completing a job
- **Lead tracking spreadsheet** — organize prospects by status and follow-up date
- **Referral program materials** — program details, referral cards, tracking
