# Warranty Tracker

Manage manufacturer warranty requirements, registration, and maintenance compliance for roofing installations.

## Warranty Types

### Manufacturer's Material Warranty (Standard)
- Covers manufacturing defects in the roofing material
- Typically 20-50 years or "lifetime" depending on product
- Usually limited and prorated after initial period
- Requires proper installation per manufacturer specifications
- No registration often required for standard warranty

### Manufacturer's System Warranty (Enhanced)
- Covers materials AND labor for repair
- Requires use of the manufacturer's complete system (all components same brand)
- Must be installed by a certified/credentialed contractor
- Registration required within a specific timeframe (usually 30-90 days)
- Typically 25-50 years, often non-prorated for initial period
- Regular maintenance inspections may be required

### Workmanship Warranty (Contractor)
- Covers installation defects
- Provided by the roofing contractor
- Typically 2-10 years
- Should specify what is covered and exclusions

## Warranty Registration Process

For each completed job, track:

1. **Manufacturer and product** — brand, product line, color
2. **Installation date**
3. **Registration deadline** — typically 30-90 days from install
4. **Registration method** — online portal, mail-in form, or through contractor portal
5. **Certificate number** — once registered
6. **Customer delivery** — provide warranty certificate to homeowner

## Installation Requirements for Warranty Compliance

Most manufacturers require:

- [ ] Approved underlayment type and installation
- [ ] Proper nailing pattern (4 or 6 nails per shingle, in the nailing zone)
- [ ] Correct exposure (typically 5" or 5-5/8" for architectural shingles)
- [ ] Ice and water shield in required areas
- [ ] Adequate ventilation (balanced intake and exhaust, 1:150 or 1:300 ratio)
- [ ] Starter strip at eaves and rakes
- [ ] Hip and ridge cap from same manufacturer
- [ ] Proper flashing at all penetrations, walls, and valleys
- [ ] Slope requirements met (minimum pitch for product type)

**Ventilation is the #1 reason warranty claims are denied.** Always document ventilation calculations.

## Maintenance Requirements

### Annual Maintenance Checklist (to maintain warranty)
- [ ] Visual inspection of shingles, flashing, and penetrations
- [ ] Clear debris from roof surface and valleys
- [ ] Clean gutters and downspouts
- [ ] Trim overhanging tree branches (minimum 6 feet clearance)
- [ ] Check attic for proper ventilation and signs of leaks
- [ ] Inspect pipe boots, vent flashings, and sealants
- [ ] Check ridge cap and hip shingles
- [ ] Document inspection with photos and date

### Maintenance Schedule Reminders
Set up reminders for each customer:
- **Annual inspection** — anniversary of installation
- **Post-storm check** — after any significant weather event
- **5-year deep inspection** — more thorough assessment
- **Warranty renewal** — if applicable

## Output Formats
- **Warranty registration tracker** — spreadsheet of all jobs with registration status
- **Customer warranty packet** — professional document with warranty details and maintenance guide
- **Maintenance reminder** — email/text template for annual inspection outreach
- **Warranty claim documentation** — supporting materials for filing a claim
