# Storm Damage Responder

Mobilize quickly after severe weather events with organized response plans, marketing campaigns, and insurance claim guidance.

## Storm Response Workflow

### Immediate (0-24 hours after event)
1. **Monitor storm reports** — check NWS alerts, local news, hail size reports, wind speed data
2. **Assess impact area** — identify affected neighborhoods by zip code or subdivision
3. **Activate response team** — assign canvassers, inspectors, and office support
4. **Launch initial outreach** — social media posts, email/text to existing customers in area
5. **Prepare materials** — door hangers, business cards, inspection forms, tarp supplies

### Short-Term (1-7 days)
6. **Door-to-door canvassing** — systematic neighborhood coverage in affected areas
7. **Free roof inspections** — scheduled assessments for homeowners
8. **Damage documentation** — photos, measurements, detailed inspection reports
9. **Insurance claim filing support** — help homeowners start the process
10. **Emergency repairs** — tarp and temporary fix services

### Follow-Up (1-4 weeks)
11. **Insurance adjuster coordination** — attend adjuster meetings, supplement as needed
12. **Estimate delivery** — provide detailed repair/replacement estimates
13. **Contract signing** — assignment of benefits or direct contracts
14. **Material ordering** — based on approved scope
15. **Production scheduling** — crew assignments and material delivery coordination

## Damage Assessment Guide

### Hail Damage Indicators
**Asphalt Shingles:**
- Bruising (soft spots when pressed)
- Granule loss (dark spots exposing mat)
- Cracking or fractures
- Dents in metal flashing, vents, gutters

**Metal Roofing:**
- Dents and dimples
- Paint chipping at impact points
- Seam damage

**Other Indicators:**
- Damaged outdoor AC units, mailboxes, cars
- Broken window screens
- Dented siding or fascia
- Damaged fencing

**Hail Size Reference:**
| Size | Diameter | Typical Damage |
|------|----------|---------------|
| Pea | 0.25" | Usually cosmetic only |
| Marble | 0.50" | Possible granule loss |
| Dime | 0.75" | Likely granule loss |
| Quarter | 1.00" | Probable shingle damage |
| Golf ball | 1.75" | Significant damage likely |
| Tennis ball | 2.50" | Severe damage expected |
| Baseball | 2.75"+ | Major damage, possible penetration |

### Wind Damage Indicators
- Lifted, curled, or missing shingles
- Exposed underlayment or decking
- Debris impact damage
- Ridge cap damage
- Fallen tree limb damage

## Insurance Claim Support

### Homeowner Guidance
- File the claim promptly (most policies have time limits)
- Document everything with photos and video before any repairs
- Do not sign any documents from the insurance company without understanding them
- The homeowner has the right to choose their own contractor
- Understand the difference between ACV (Actual Cash Value) and RCV (Replacement Cost Value)
- Deductible responsibility — explain clearly

### Supplement Process
When the insurance company's initial scope is insufficient:
1. Compare adjuster's line items to your inspection findings
2. Document discrepancies with photos and measurements
3. Prepare a detailed supplement with Xactimate line items or equivalent
4. Submit to adjuster with supporting documentation
5. Follow up systematically until resolved
