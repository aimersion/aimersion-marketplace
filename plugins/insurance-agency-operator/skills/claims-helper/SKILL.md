# Claims Helper Skill

Guide policyholders through the insurance claims process with step-by-step instructions, documentation checklists, and advocacy support.

## Claims Filing Fundamentals

Every claim follows these core steps:
1. **Notification**: Report loss to insurer within required timeframe (typically 30-60 days)
2. **Documentation**: Gather evidence and supporting documentation
3. **Claim Assignment**: Insurer assigns adjuster or processes claim
4. **Evaluation**: Adjuster investigates, inspects, and evaluates
5. **Settlement**: Insurer makes settlement offer or denial
6. **Resolution**: Claim approved, paid, or appealed

## Auto Insurance Claims

**Immediate Actions:**
- Check for injuries; call 911 if needed
- Move vehicles to safe location if possible
- Document scene with photos/video (vehicles, damage, scene, road conditions)
- Get contact/insurance info from other drivers involved
- Report to police (required in many states for injury/significant damage)
- Report to insurer within 24-48 hours

**Documentation Checklist:**
- Police report number and contact info
- Photos of all vehicle damage (wide and close-up)
- Photos of accident scene (road conditions, signals, debris)
- Contact info for other driver, passengers, witnesses
- Your statement of how accident occurred
- Medical records if injury-related
- Repair estimates (get 2-3 for significant claims)
- Rental car receipts (keep all documentation)
- Lost wages documentation if applicable

**Claims Status Updates:**
- Ask adjuster for direct contact number and claim number
- Request email for written communication
- Agree on inspection appointment timing
- Follow up in 3-5 business days if no contact
- Request written explanation if liability is contested

## Property/Homeowners Claims

**Immediate Actions:**
- Ensure family safety; evacuate if necessary
- Document the loss with photos/video before cleanup
- If weather-related, take steps to prevent further damage
- Report to insurance within 24-48 hours
- Do NOT throw away damaged items until adjuster documents

**Documentation Checklist:**
- Photos/video of all damage (interior and exterior)
- List of damaged items with descriptions and values
- Receipts/proof of ownership where available
- Proof of value (credit card statements, appraisals, warranty docs)
- Replacement cost estimates from contractors
- Building permits or inspection records (if relevant)
- Credit card statements showing prior purchases
- Tax returns (for business property claims)
- Proof of maintenance history (roof inspections, etc.)

**Water Damage Specific:**
- Determine if water damage is covered under policy
- Identify water source (sudden/accidental vs. gradual)
- Document all affected areas with photos
- Get multiple quotes for remediation
- Request emergency mitigation support if available

## Liability Claims

**Incident Documentation:**
- Detailed written account of what happened
- Photos of accident scene from multiple angles
- Names and contact info for all witnesses
- Medical records for injured parties
- Police report if applicable
- Any communications with injured party

**Policyholder Responsibilities:**
- Do NOT admit fault at scene; provide only basic information
- Cooperate with insurer's investigation
- Do NOT settle claims without insurer approval
- Notify insurer of any lawsuits or legal threats immediately
- Maintain records of all conversations with claimant

## Life Insurance Claims

**Notification Requirements:**
- Report death to insurer within 30 days
- Provide death certificate (certified copy)
- Provide beneficiary claim form completed by beneficiary
- Note: No time limit on claims, but faster reporting speeds payment

**Documentation Checklist:**
- Death certificate (certified copy from vital records)
- Proof of age/identity documents
- Beneficiary identification and Social Security number
- List of all policies to be claimed
- Proof of premium payment (final bill)
- Marriage certificate (if beneficiary name changed)
- Court orders (if beneficiary is minor or incompetent)

**Claim Processing:**
- Life claims typically process within 30-45 days
- Insurer will request any missing documentation
- Payment method options (check, EFT, settlement option)
- Beneficiary can request proof of payment

## Disability Insurance Claims

**Claim Initiation:**
- Notify insurer within 30 days of disability
- Provide physician statement of disability
- Document work cessation date and reason
- Provide proof of income (recent tax returns, W-2s, pay stubs)
- Complete claim form and physician statement

**Ongoing Documentation:**
- Monthly updates on disability status
- Medical reports (typically required quarterly)
- Proof of ongoing treatment
- Earnings reports if on own-occupation or partial disability
- Notification if returning to work (even part-time)

**Definition of Disability:**
- Own-occupation: Cannot perform own job
- Any-occupation: Cannot perform any job
- Partial disability: Earning reduced income
- Presumptive disability: Specific conditions automatically qualify

## Claims Advocacy Strategies

**If Claim is Denied:**
1. Request written explanation with policy language reference
2. Review policy for coverage definition and exclusions
3. Obtain legal review if significant amount
4. File appeal with additional documentation
5. Request appraisal if valuation is disputed (property claims)
6. File complaint with state insurance commissioner if unresolved

**If Settlement Offer Seems Low:**
- Get independent valuation/repair estimates
- Review how adjuster calculated damages
- Request itemized breakdown of settlement
- Ask for reconsideration with new documentation
- Request appraisal for property damages (available in most policies)

**Documentation Throughout:**
- Keep all correspondence with insurer
- Document all phone conversations (date, time, person, topics)
- Send follow-ups via email to create written record
- Never destroy original receipts or documentation
- Maintain timeline of all claim activities

Refer to references/ directory for claim type-specific forms, communication templates, and appeal guidelines.
