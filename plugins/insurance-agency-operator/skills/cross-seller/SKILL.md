# Cross-Seller Skill

Analyze client data to identify missing insurance lines, life events triggering new needs, and strategies to convert mono-line to multi-line clients.

## Account Analysis Framework

Review each client across multiple dimensions:

**Current Coverage Inventory:**
- Document all existing policies by line (auto, home, umbrella, life, disability, business)
- Note policy effective dates, limits, and premium amounts
- Identify coverage gaps by comparing against recommended benchmarks

**Client Profile Factors:**
- Age, family status, number of dependents
- Home ownership and mortgage status
- Vehicle ownership (number, types, ages)
- Employment status, income level
- Business ownership or involvement
- Lifestyle factors (pools, memberships, assets)

## Life Events as Triggers

**High-Priority Life Events:**
- **Marriage**: New household liability exposure; estate planning need; spousal coverage review
- **New Baby**: Increased life insurance need; disability coverage gap; education savings
- **Home Purchase**: New property insurance need; increased liability exposure; mortgage payoff life insurance
- **Business Start**: New business liability, property, and workers' comp needs
- **Promotion/Income Increase**: Umbrella opportunity; disability/life increase; business planning
- **Retirement**: Life insurance review; disability phase-out; asset protection review

## Mono-Line to Multi-Line Conversion Strategy

**For Auto-Only Clients:**
- Identify home ownership (public records, zip code analysis)
- Present bundled auto/home quote with discount impact
- Emphasize liability protection coordinating across policies
- Provide liability gap analysis comparing separate vs. bundled

**For Home-Only Clients:**
- Survey for vehicle ownership and commute patterns
- Cross-sell auto with emphasis on coordinated liability
- Add umbrella if home value + net assets warrant
- Explore life insurance fit based on family structure

**For Business Owner Clients:**
- Ensure complete business insurance package (GL, property, workers' comp)
- Identify business owners' personal coverage gaps
- Explore key person and buy-sell life insurance needs
- Present business interruption and cyber coverage

## Opportunity Prioritization Scoring

Score each opportunity on 3 factors (rate 1-5 scale):

**Fit**: How well does the missing coverage address client's actual exposure?
- 5: Critical gap given client's situation
- 1: Nice-to-have or marginal fit

**Receptivity**: How likely is client to buy based on prior engagement?
- 5: Recently engaged, positive relationship, previous acceptance of recommendations
- 1: Cold prospect, limited engagement history

**Revenue Impact**: What is the annual premium potential?
- 5: $500+ annual premium opportunity
- 1: Under $100 annual premium

**Implementation**: Opportunities scoring 12+ are high priority; 9-11 medium priority; under 9 lower priority.

## Cross-Sell Bundling Calculations

Present bundling value in simple terms:
- Auto only: Current premium = $XXX
- Home only: Current premium = $XXX
- Bundled rate: $XXX (Y% discount)
- **Annual Savings: $XXX**

Emphasize non-price benefits:
- Single renewal date and payment
- Coordinated claims handling
- Simplified policy management
- Loyalty benefits and service enhancements

## Account Rounding Methodology

Quarterly analysis of book of business:
1. Segment clients by current coverage type
2. Run life event scan (anniversaries, public records)
3. Identify top 20-30 opportunities by priority score
4. Schedule 15-minute review calls to present opportunities
5. Track conversion rate and revenue impact per segment

Refer to references/ directory for client analysis templates, life event triggers by age group, and bundling discount strategies.
