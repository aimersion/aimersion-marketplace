# Retention Manager Skill

Develop and execute proactive client retention programs including renewal outreach sequences, rate increase management, win-back campaigns for lapsed clients, and annual service reviews.

## Retention Framework

Every client represents recurring revenue and growth opportunity. A comprehensive retention program addresses three segments:

1. **At-Risk Clients**: Those receiving rate increases, shopping other carriers, or exhibiting low engagement
2. **Active Clients**: Renewing regularly with good payment history; prime candidates for cross-sell
3. **Lapsed Clients**: Cancelled or non-renewed within past 24 months; potential win-back candidates

## Proactive Renewal Outreach Sequence

Start renewal outreach 90 days before policy expiration.

**Day 90 Before Expiration: Initial Renewal Notice**
- Send renewal declaration with current coverage details
- Include summary of any coverage changes since last year
- Attach customer service contact information
- Request confirmation of coverage details (address, vehicles, occupants)
- Set expectation: "We'll send your updated premium quote 60 days before expiration"

**Day 60 Before Expiration: Premium Quote and Value Proposition**
- Send renewal quote with current premium
- Highlight any premium changes with explanation
- Emphasize coverage enhancements added at no cost (endorsements, riders)
- Include customer testimonial or success story related to similar coverage
- Call-to-action: "Reply to confirm coverage or call with questions"
- Include link to online customer portal for easy renewal

**Day 45 Before Expiration: Service Appreciation Outreach**
- Personal note (email or handwritten card) thanking them for being a customer
- Recount key milestone: "Thank you for 5 years of trust"
- Reference any significant events they experienced: "So glad we were there after your claim"
- Include service number for immediate assistance with renewal questions
- Subtle offer: "If you've had any coverage gaps or changes, now is the perfect time to discuss"

**Day 30 Before Expiration: Competitive Value Messaging**
- If active customer showing engagement: Focus on loyalty benefits, multi-policy discount value, and service advantages
- Outline what they save by bundling with you vs. separate carriers
- Share security benefits: "Dedicated agent familiar with your situation" vs. customer service lines
- Call-to-action: "Lock in your renewal today to ensure no coverage gaps"

**Day 14 Before Expiration: Final Reminder (Multi-channel)**
- Email reminder: "Your renewal is due in two weeks"
- If phone number available: Brief courtesy call "Just want to make sure you saw the renewal quote"
- Physical reminder postcard (if direct mail utilized)
- LinkedIn message (if high-net-worth client and you're connected)
- Simple call-to-action: "Reply or call to process your renewal"

**Day 1 Before Expiration: Last Call Alert**
- Final urgent message: "Your policy expires tomorrow!"
- Emphasis on coverage lapse risk and liability exposure
- Direct phone or chat contact option
- Emergency renewal process instructions

**Day 1-7 After Expiration (If Not Renewed): Win-Back Outreach**
- Assumes client has lapsed; begins win-back campaign
- Personal call: "I noticed your auto policy lapsed—I'm concerned about coverage gaps. Can we get you reinstated?"
- Offer grace period reinstatement (many carriers allow 30-day grace)
- Provide expedited quote if they're comparing with other carriers
- Acknowledge reason for lapse (price shopping, other life events) without judgment

## Rate Increase Management

When renewal involves a rate increase, proactive communication determines retention or loss.

**Rate Increase Communication Strategy**

For increases under 5%:
- Frame as inflation/carrier adjustment
- Emphasize coverage value and service benefits
- Highlight bundling discounts that offset increases
- Offer coverage review to identify potential savings areas

For increases 5-10%:
- Immediate outreach before client sees quote
- Explain drivers: claims history, inflation, coverage changes, carrier adjustments
- Provide comparison: "Your rate is still competitive with alternatives"
- Offer value-add: Endorsement review, cross-sell opportunity, policy optimization
- Suggest alternatives: Coverage adjustments that reduce premium without material risk

For increases over 10%:
- Urgent personal outreach (phone call, not email)
- Investigate root cause: Claims impact, rating changes, market conditions
- Propose solutions: Explore different carriers, adjust coverage strategically, optimize bundling
- Acknowledge concern: "I understand this increase is frustrating. Here's what we're doing about it"
- Offer to shop coverage if current carrier cannot reduce rate

**Rate Increase Talking Points:**
- "Your claims history drives a lot of our premium—let's review if you can reduce future risk"
- "With 3 years claim-free, you should be eligible for claim-free discounts—let me verify"
- "Your home/vehicle value may have increased, which legitimately increases replacement cost"
- "These inflation-driven increases are industry-wide; let me show you how we're managing this"

## Annual Service Review Framework

Conduct comprehensive review 30-60 days before renewal to identify gaps and build relationship value.

**Service Review Meeting Agenda (30 minutes)**

**Part 1: Life Changes Review (5 minutes)**
- "Tell me what's changed since we last reviewed your coverage"
- Listen for: Recent home purchases, vehicle additions, family changes, business changes, income changes
- Document: Any new exposures or coverage needs
- Identify: Immediate gap opportunities

**Part 2: Current Coverage Deep Dive (10 minutes)**
- "Let's walk through each policy you have with us"
- Auto: Vehicles, drivers, annual mileage, commute changes
- Home: Recent improvements, rental income, home business
- Life: Beneficiaries, coverage adequacy
- Other: Umbrella, disability, business insurance status
- Ask: "Is this coverage still serving your needs?"

**Part 3: Gap and Cross-Sell Identification (8 minutes)**
- "Based on what you've shared, I see some opportunities"
- Present top 2-3 opportunities with clear rationale
- Use benchmarking language: "For someone with your assets, industry best practice is..."
- Focus on risk mitigation, not just premium increases
- Present options with pricing: "For just $X/month more, here's what we'd add"

**Part 4: Service Assessment and Feedback (7 minutes)**
- "How's the experience been with us?"
- Listen for satisfaction, pain points, needs
- Address concerns: "I hear you about X—let me make that better"
- Share service improvements made in past year
- Reinforce: Service team availability, claims support, digital tools

## Win-Back Campaign for Lapsed Clients

For clients who cancelled within the past 24 months, deploy targeted win-back sequence.

**Win-Back Campaign Timeline**

**Month 1 (After Cancellation Discovery):**
- Personal outreach call: "I noticed you're no longer with us. I wanted to check in"
- Listen for reason: Price, service, found different carrier, life change
- Do NOT be defensive about rate; understand true reason
- Offer: Immediate re-quote with current rates, or opportunity to discuss what they're shopping
- Propose: "Let me see if I can match or beat the alternative quote"
- Soften: Acknowledge difficulty of decision without pressure

**Month 2-3 (Value Reinforcement):**
- Email with "We miss you" messaging
- Share recent agency success story or testimonial from similar client
- Highlight service improvements or new benefits introduced since they left
- Offer: Limited-time loyalty returning customer discount
- Provide: Easy path to return with simple link or call

**Month 4-6 (Life Events Trigger Outreach):**
- Monitor for public records indicating life events (home purchase, marriage, etc.)
- If event identified: "Congratulations on your new home! Thinking of you and would love to help protect it"
- Frame as personal concern, not sales pitch
- Provide new client incentive if they return for new coverage

**Month 6-12 (Seasonal Re-engagement):**
- Seasonal campaign inclusion (hurricane prep, winter driving, etc.)
- If open rate high: Progress to higher-touch outreach
- If low engagement: Switch to quarterly gentle reminder

**When NOT to pursue win-back:**
- Accounts with consistent claims and premium losses
- Clients with poor payment history
- Situations where they found significantly better value legitimately

## Loyalty Programs and Acknowledgments

**Anniversary Recognition Program:**
- Year 1: Thank you call/message on policy anniversary
- Year 3: Recognition of 3-year loyalty with small gesture (gift, handwritten note, discount token)
- Year 5: Significant recognition (gift, special event invitation, service tier upgrade)
- Year 10+: VIP treatment acknowledgment with special pricing or perks

**Service Recovery Moments:**
- When claim occurs: Assign account coordinator for duration of claim
- After claim closes: "Glad we were there for you" message with outcome
- If rate increase needed due to claim: Gentle explanation and support for recovery plan
- Document: Build case for future loyalty discounts as claims-free years accumulate

## Retention Metrics and Tracking

Monitor these KPIs monthly:

- **Renewal Retention Rate**: % of renewals kept vs. cancelled (target: 95%+)
- **Premium Retention**: Total premium renewed vs. baseline
- **Rate Increase Retention**: % retained when faced with premium increase by tier
- **Cross-Sell Capture Rate**: % of renewals that added new line within 12 months
- **Lapsed Client Win-Back Rate**: % of win-back attempts that result in return
- **Net Promoter Score**: Track client satisfaction and referral likelihood
- **Cancellation Reasons**: Categorize reasons for non-renewal to identify trends

**Retention Improvement Targets:**
- Reduce cancellations under 5% annually
- Achieve 60%+ cross-sell rate on renewal touchpoints
- Win back 20%+ of lapsed clients within 12 months
- Maintain 90%+ satisfaction scores
