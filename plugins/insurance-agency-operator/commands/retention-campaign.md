# Retention Campaign Command

Design and plan a client retention or win-back campaign with messaging, timeline, and success metrics.

## Input

Use the argument to specify campaign focus:
- Renewal campaigns: "renewal campaign for Q3", "renewal outreach for auto-only"
- Rate increase management: "rate-increase retention 5-10%", "handle double-digit increase"
- Win-back campaigns: "win-back lapsed clients 2024", "win-back auto customers"
- Loyalty programs: "loyalty recognition program", "anniversary campaign"

Example: `retention-campaign "renewal campaign for rate-increase 5-10%"`

## Campaign Development Steps

1. **Audience Segmentation**
   - Identify client segment (renewals, rate increases, lapsed, etc.)
   - Segment by risk level (at-risk, stable, high-value)
   - Identify communication preference (phone, email, mail)
   - Estimate segment size and potential risk

2. **Campaign Type Selection**
   - Renewal outreach (standard 90-60-45-30-14 day sequence)
   - Rate increase management (different messaging for increase tiers)
   - Win-back campaign (recovery for lapsed clients)
   - Loyalty recognition (retention through appreciation)
   - Service recovery (post-claim retention)

3. **Message Development**
   - Core value proposition for this audience
   - Address specific concern (price, service, coverage, etc.)
   - Emotional hooks: security, family protection, asset protection
   - Competitor positioning if relevant
   - Call-to-action (specific and easy)

4. **Timeline and Touchpoint Planning**
   - Map outreach calendar (when to contact, how many times)
   - Multi-channel approach (email, phone, mail, digital)
   - Spacing between touches (avoid fatigue, maintain presence)
   - Seasonal optimization (if applicable)
   - Escalation path (if no response after certain touches)

5. **Messaging Variations**
   - Primary message (value/benefit)
   - Secondary message (alternative angle)
   - Objection-handling message (address expected concerns)
   - Final urgency message (deadline approaching)
   - Win-back message (specific to lapsed clients)

6. **Tactical Execution**
   - Email templates (subject lines, body copy, CTA)
   - Phone scripts (opening, objection handling, close)
   - Direct mail pieces (postcard, letter, insert)
   - Digital ad copy (display, social media)
   - Landing page content (if digital component)

7. **Metrics and Success Tracking**
   - Open rate targets (email)
   - Response rate targets (phone, email, mail)
   - Conversion rate targets (to retention/renewal)
   - Revenue impact (net dollars retained)
   - Cost per retention (campaign cost ÷ retentions)

8. **Contingency Planning**
   - Non-responders: escalation sequence
   - Objection handling: script variations
   - Competitive response: matching offer thresholds
   - Claims issues: recovery strategy
   - Rate shock: alternative coverage options

## Output Format

Provide:
- **Campaign Overview**
  - Audience size and characteristics
  - Campaign type and duration
  - Primary goal and success metric
  - Estimated revenue impact

- **Campaign Timeline**
  - Day-by-day/week-by-week outreach plan
  - Touchpoint schedule (email, phone, mail, digital)
  - Spacing and frequency
  - Escalation path

- **Message Strategy**
  - Primary value proposition
  - Key talking points
  - Emotional hooks/framing
  - Competitor positioning

- **Tactical Materials** (templates/scripts)
  - Email templates (with subject lines)
  - Phone scripts (opening, objection handling, close)
  - Direct mail copy
  - Digital ad copy options

- **Execution Checklist**
  - Audience identification and export
  - Message preparation
  - System setup (email automation, phone list, etc.)
  - Launch readiness
  - Monitoring and tracking

- **Success Metrics**
  - Response rate target (by channel)
  - Conversion rate target
  - Revenue retention target
  - Cost per retention
  - ROI targets

- **Contingency Plans**
  - Non-responder escalation
  - Objection handling scripts
  - Alternative approaches if low response
  - Competitive match thresholds

## Success Metrics

- Renewal retention rate (target: 95%+)
- Response rate (email: 5-10%, phone: 20-30%, mail: 1-3%)
- Conversion rate (30-60% of responders become renewals)
- Revenue retained per campaign
- Campaign ROI (revenue gained ÷ campaign cost)
- Win-back success (20-30% of lapsed clients recovered)
