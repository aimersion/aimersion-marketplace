# Launch Content Planner

Generate comprehensive launch content calendars that coordinate messaging across email, social, PR, and paid channels. Content is the bridge between your product and your audience — getting the timing, sequence, and messaging right across channels is what separates memorable launches from forgettable ones.

## Gathering Context

Before creating a content plan, understand:

1. **Product and launch date** — what's launching and when?
2. **Target audience** — who are we talking to, and where do they consume content?
3. **Key messaging** — what's the core value proposition and 3 key benefits?
4. **Channels available** — which platforms does the user actively use? (email list size, social accounts, PR contacts)
5. **Content resources** — can they create video? Do they have a designer? Budget for paid?
6. **Launch type** — soft launch (small audience), hard launch (big push), or rolling launch?

## Content Strategy Framework

### The Launch Content Arc

Every launch follows a narrative arc across 4 stages:

**Stage 1: Tease (4-6 weeks before)** — Create curiosity without revealing everything
**Stage 2: Reveal (2-3 weeks before)** — Show the product, build anticipation
**Stage 3: Launch (Launch week)** — Maximum energy, all channels firing
**Stage 4: Sustain (2-8 weeks after)** — Maintain momentum, nurture, optimize

### Channel Priority by Product Type

| Product Type | Primary Channel | Secondary | Tertiary |
|-------------|----------------|-----------|----------|
| SaaS / B2B | Email + LinkedIn | Blog + PR | Twitter/X |
| DTC / E-commerce | Instagram + Email | Facebook + Influencers | PR |
| Amazon | Email + External Traffic | Social + Influencers | PPC |
| Crowdfunding | Email + Social | PR + Community | Paid Ads |
| Digital Product | Email + Webinars | Social + Affiliates | Blog |

## Email Content Plan

### Pre-Launch Email Sequence (5-7 emails over 2-4 weeks)

| Email | Timing | Type | Subject Line Formula |
|-------|--------|------|---------------------|
| 1 | T-14 days | Teaser | "Something big is coming..." / "Can you keep a secret?" |
| 2 | T-10 days | Story | "Why we built [product]..." / "The problem that kept us up at night" |
| 3 | T-7 days | Sneak Peek | "First look at [product]..." / "You're getting early access to this" |
| 4 | T-3 days | Pre-order/Early Bird | "Pre-orders open NOW" / "Your exclusive early pricing" |
| 5 | T-0 | Launch Announcement | "It's here! [Product] is officially live" / "We just launched" |
| 6 | T+2 days | Social Proof | "Here's what early customers are saying..." |
| 7 | T+5 days | Last Chance | "Launch pricing ends Sunday" / "48 hours left" |

### Email Copy Structure (Problem → Solution → Proof → Urgency → Action)

1. Open with the reader's pain point or aspiration (2-3 sentences)
2. Introduce the product as the solution (2-3 sentences)
3. Social proof: testimonial, stat, or early result (1-2 sentences)
4. Urgency element: time limit, quantity limit, or exclusive offer
5. Single, clear CTA button

**Best send times:** 8 PM (59% open rate — highest), 5-6 PM (peak clicks)
**Avoid:** Monday mornings, late night

## Social Media Content Plan

### Launch Day Posting Schedule

| Time | Post Type | Platform Focus | Goal |
|------|-----------|---------------|------|
| 9-10 AM | Launch announcement (primary visual) | All platforms | Establish the news |
| 12-1 PM | Product demo / benefits carousel | Instagram, LinkedIn | Show value |
| 3-4 PM | Founder story / behind-the-scenes | Twitter/X, LinkedIn | Build connection |
| 5-6 PM | Customer testimonial / early review | All platforms | Social proof |
| 7-8 PM | Engagement post / Q&A / giveaway | Instagram, Twitter/X | Extend momentum |

### Content Types by Launch Phase

**Tease Phase (Weeks -6 to -3):**
- Mystery/pixelated product teasers
- "Coming soon" countdown graphics
- Behind-the-scenes development content
- Audience polls: "Would you rather X or Y?"
- Problem-focused content (not product-focused)

**Reveal Phase (Weeks -2 to -1):**
- Product reveals with high-quality visuals
- Feature highlight carousels (one feature per slide)
- Short demo videos (30-60 seconds)
- Countdown posts with specific date
- "Tag someone who needs this" engagement posts

**Launch Phase (Week 0):**
- Launch announcement across all platforms
- Customer testimonial graphics
- Live video / AMA on primary platform
- Real-time milestone updates ("1,000 customers in 24 hours!")
- User-generated content reshares

**Sustain Phase (Weeks +1 to +8):**
- Customer success stories and case studies
- Feature deep-dive tutorials
- Comparison posts (before/after, vs. alternatives)
- FAQ content addressing common questions
- Industry thought leadership content

### Platform-Specific Guidelines

**LinkedIn:** Professional tone, industry insights, founder journey posts perform best. Long-form posts (1,300+ characters) get 3x more engagement. Best times: Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM.

**Twitter/X:** Conversational, concise, thread-friendly. Launch day thread telling the full story works well. Engage with every reply in first 24 hours. Best times: 9 AM-12 PM weekdays.

**Instagram:** Visual-first, carousel posts for features, Stories for behind-the-scenes, Reels for demos. Use 5-10 targeted hashtags per post. Best times: 11 AM-1 PM, 7-9 PM.

**Facebook:** Community-focused, longer posts OK, group engagement. Best for DTC/consumer brands. Share to relevant groups (with permission). Best times: 1-4 PM weekdays.

## PR Content Plan

### Press Release Structure (400-600 words)
1. **Headline:** [Company] + [Product] + [Specific Benefit]
2. **Sub-headline:** Elaborates with additional hook
3. **Dateline:** CITY, STATE — Date
4. **Opening paragraph:** What, who, when, where, why, how (the essential facts)
5. **Body:** Key features/benefits, market context, differentiation
6. **Quote:** 1-2 quotes (CEO/founder + customer/partner)
7. **Availability:** Pricing, where to buy, launch date
8. **Boilerplate:** Company background paragraph
9. **Media contact:** Name, email, phone

### PR Timeline
- **T-4 weeks:** Build media list (20-50 relevant journalists/publications)
- **T-3 weeks:** Send embargoed press release to top 5-10 outlets
- **T-2 weeks:** Follow up with additional story angles
- **T-1 week:** Confirm coverage commitments, send final assets
- **T-0:** Publish press release, notify all media contacts embargo is lifted

### Media Kit Contents
- [ ] Press release (PDF and text)
- [ ] High-resolution product photos (3-5 images, 300 DPI)
- [ ] Founder headshot and bio
- [ ] Company logo files (PNG, SVG)
- [ ] Product fact sheet (one page)
- [ ] Key statistics and data points
