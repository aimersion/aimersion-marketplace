Build a pricing strategy for a product launch using psychological pricing frameworks and competitive analysis.

## Steps

1. **Gather Context** — Ask the user:
   - What's the product and who is it for?
   - What do competitors charge?
   - What are the unit costs / COGS?
   - What's the launch goal? (revenue, adoption, market share)
   - Any existing customer research on willingness to pay?

2. **Competitive Pricing Analysis** — Research competitors' pricing using web search. Build a comparison matrix showing features vs. price for the top 5-10 alternatives.

3. **Strategy Selection** — Based on the product type, market position, and goals, recommend the optimal pricing strategy (penetration, skimming, freemium, tiered, early bird).

4. **Apply Psychological Tactics** — Layer in charm pricing, anchoring, decoy pricing, or tiered structures as appropriate.

5. **Design Early Bird Structure** — If applicable, create a multi-tier launch pricing structure with specific discount percentages and durations.

6. **Build Pricing Page Recommendation** — Recommend how to present pricing (3-tier layout, annual vs. monthly, feature comparison).

7. **Validate with Unit Economics** — Check that recommended pricing supports healthy LTV:CAC ratio (>3:1) and gross margins.

## Output

Deliver a pricing strategy document with specific prices, not just frameworks.
