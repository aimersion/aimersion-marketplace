# Draft Professional Legal Letter

Your task: Create a professional legal letter tailored to the user's specific need and situation.

**Step 1: Determine letter type and purpose**
Ask the user to specify:
- Letter type from: engagement letter, demand letter, representation letter, settlement demand, termination of representation, referral letter, status update, opposing counsel communication, court filing cover letter, client advice letter, document request, or other
- Recipient (client, opposing party, opposing counsel, court, third party)
- Primary purpose and desired outcome
- Tone (formal, firm, collaborative, threatening if demand)
- Any specific legal claims or issues to address

**Step 2: Gather substantive details**
Based on letter type, collect:

For demand letters:
- Claimant name and address
- Recipient/defendant name and address
- Date of incident/breach
- Factual summary (what happened)
- Legal basis (breach of contract, personal injury, property damage, etc.)
- Damages calculation (special damages with detail, general damages estimate)
- Demand amount
- Deadline for response/settlement (typically 30 days)
- Consequence if not paid (litigation threat, collection action)

For engagement letters:
- Client name and contact info
- Matter description
- Scope of representation (what's included)
- Limitations (what's excluded)
- Fee structure (hourly rate, flat fee, contingency)
- Retainer amount and billing practices
- Estimated budget if available
- Conflict of interest disclosures if any
- Confidentiality and work product acknowledgment

For representation letters:
- Client authorization
- Scope of representation
- Fee arrangement summary
- Client responsibilities
- Next steps

For status updates:
- Case timeline to date
- Recent developments
- Actions taken this period
- Next scheduled steps
- Any decisions needed from client

**Step 3: Draft the letter**
Structure as professional business letter with:
- [FIRM LETTERHEAD] placeholder at top
- Date
- Recipient address block
- "Re: [Client Name] v. [Opposing Party] / [Matter Description]" or subject line
- Salutation (Dear [Recipient]:)
- Opening paragraph (purpose of letter)
- Body paragraphs (facts, law, analysis, as appropriate)
- Closing paragraph (what you want them to do, deadline)
- Closing ("Sincerely," "Respectfully," etc.)
- Signature block with attorney name, title, bar number
- Reference initials (JD/abc for attorney/typist)
- "cc:" line if copied to others

**Step 4: Apply legal language and templates**
Use the letter-templates.md reference for:
- Proper legal letter structure and formatting
- Boilerplate language appropriate to letter type
- Citation format if legal authorities cited
- Demand letter calculated damage statements
- Proper threat language (litigation without harassment)
- Professional closing phrases

**Step 5: Ensure compliance**
- Verify letter complies with state ethics rules
- Include required disclosures (whether you're an attorney, representation status)
- Ensure settlement demand letters include demand amount clearly
- Check that any threats or language are ethically proper
- Add "Privileged and Confidential" or "Attorney-Client Privileged" header if appropriate
- Include any required "demand letter" formatting per state rules

**Step 6: Provide output**
- Save as markdown file: `letter-[type]-[recipient]-[date].md`
- Offer conversion to Word or Google Docs format
- Provide email-ready version with subject line
- Include editing notes in [brackets] for customization points
- Flag any aggressive language for review before sending

**Special considerations:**
- Do NOT send threatening language that could constitute extortion
- Avoid "good faith" language that contradicts a litigation demand
- For demand letters, specify deadline clearly (30 days is standard)
- For termination letters, review state rules on notice requirements
- For opposing counsel, maintain professional tone regardless of dispute nature
