# Draft Motion Command

Generate a professionally formatted criminal defense motion with proper legal structure, constitutional framework, factual background, and persuasive argument.

## Process

1. **Gather Motion Details**
   - Ask what type of motion is needed (suppress evidence, dismiss charges, compel discovery, continuance, bail reduction, etc.)
   - Request the factual basis and specific incidents giving rise to the motion
   - Identify the legal theory or constitutional basis
   - Ask for any existing case law or precedent
   - Determine filing deadline and court

2. **Determine Motion Type and Framework**
   - **Motion to Suppress**: Fourth Amendment search/seizure, Miranda violations, fruit of poisonous tree
   - **Motion to Dismiss**: speedy trial violation, insufficient evidence, prosecutorial misconduct, jurisdictional defects
   - **Motion to Compel**: discovery violations, expert witness disclosure, evidence inspection
   - **Motion for Continuance**: inadequate preparation time, discovery delays, witness unavailability
   - **Motion for Bail Reduction**: changed circumstances, excessive bail, new information
   - **Other Motions**: severance, change of venue, appointment of counsel, expert funding

3. **Research Legal Framework**
   - Identify controlling constitutional provisions or statutes
   - Gather relevant case law precedent
   - Identify factors courts consider for the motion type
   - Review local court rules and formatting requirements
   - Assess burden of proof standards

4. **Organize Factual Background**
   - Present facts chronologically
   - Cite to police reports, discovery, and evidentiary support
   - Highlight facts favorable to the motion
   - Distinguish unfavorable facts
   - Use precise language without argument

5. **Develop Legal Argument**
   - State the applicable legal standard clearly
   - Apply facts to legal framework
   - Address prosecution counterarguments
   - Distinguish unfavorable precedent
   - Cite controlling authority
   - Build persuasive narrative

6. **Format for Filing**
   - Proper caption with case name, docket number, judge
   - Clear and concise title
   - Numbered paragraphs (not required but clear)
   - Subheadings for readability
   - Conclusion requesting specific relief
   - Proposed order

## Output Format

Deliver a motion document with:
- Case caption and motion title
- Introduction summarizing legal claim
- Factual background with evidentiary support
- Legal argument section with framework and application
- Conclusion with specific relief requested
- Proposed order for judge signature
- Certificates of service and compliance
- File formatted for immediate court filing
