# Sentencing Mitigation Packet Command

Construct a comprehensive sentencing mitigation package with narrative development, character references, treatment recommendations, and persuasive sentencing memorandum to advocate for the most lenient sentence.

## Process

1. **Gather Client and Sentencing Information**
   - Request client's full background (age, family history, education, employment)
   - Ask about the conviction charges and guilty plea/verdict facts
   - Obtain sentencing guideline calculation and judge's likely range
   - Identify any mandatory minimum sentences
   - Ask about substance abuse, mental health, or trauma history
   - Request prior criminal history details

2. **Investigate Mitigation Themes**
   - **Childhood and Family**: adverse childhood experiences, abuse, neglect, poverty, parental substance abuse
   - **Education and Employment**: school performance, vocational training, job history, work ethic
   - **Mental Health**: diagnosed conditions, treatment history, medication management, symptoms related to offense
   - **Substance Abuse**: addiction history, recovery efforts, sobriety time, treatment participation
   - **Community Contributions**: volunteer work, family support, religious involvement, mentoring
   - **Personal Growth**: demonstrated change, rehabilitation, insight into wrongdoing, remorse
   - **Collateral Consequences**: immigration status, custody of children, caregiver responsibilities
   - **Health Issues**: serious illness, disability, chronic pain, medical treatment needs

3. **Organize Mitigation Chronologically**
   - Create timeline of client's life showing trajectory
   - Highlight turning points and progress
   - Connect past hardships to current strengths
   - Show pattern of rehabilitation attempts
   - Document recovery milestones

4. **Coordinate Character References**
   - Identify potential reference writers (employers, clergy, family, treatment providers, community leaders)
   - Request specific letters addressing character, remorse, community ties, and rehabilitation
   - Provide guidance on letter content and formatting
   - Collect letters emphasizing positive attributes and community support

5. **Identify Treatment and Program Recommendations**
   - Research appropriate programming for client's issues (substance abuse, mental health, education, employment training)
   - Identify specific programs with openings and sentences
   - Calculate cost and duration
   - Explain benefits and expected outcomes

6. **Draft Sentencing Memorandum**
   - Weave mitigation narrative into persuasive structure
   - Address aggravating factors honestly but contextualize
   - Compare to similar cases with lower sentences
   - Emphasize rehabilitation potential
   - Propose alternative sentencing (probation, halfway house, day reporting)
   - Explain how imprisonment serves no purpose given mitigation

## Output Format

Deliver a complete mitigation package with:
- Client background summary and mitigation themes
- Life chronology with turning points and progress
- Character reference request letters (templates and finalized)
- Treatment provider letters documenting participation
- Employer and educational institution letters
- Family impact statement (if applicable)
- Detailed sentencing memorandum with legal and factual argument
- Proposed sentencing order
- Appendices with supporting documentation
- Organization guide for judge's review
