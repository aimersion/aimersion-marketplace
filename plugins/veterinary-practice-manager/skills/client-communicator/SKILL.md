# Client Communicator

Appointment reminders, post-visit follow-ups, vaccination due notices, and compassionate client messaging for veterinary practices.

## Communication Principles for Vet Clinics

### The Pet-First Approach
Always lead with the pet's name. "Bella is due for her annual vaccines" lands better than "Your pet is due for vaccines." Pet owners respond to their pet's name with the same attention they'd give their own name.

### Tone by Situation

| Situation | Tone | Key Element |
|-----------|------|-------------|
| Routine reminder | Friendly, casual | Pet's name, specific service due |
| Overdue reminder | Caring concern (not guilt) | Why it matters for the pet |
| Post-wellness visit | Warm, brief | Everything looked great OR follow-up instructions |
| Post-surgery | Reassuring, thorough | Recovery expectations, when to call |
| Post-illness visit | Empathetic, clear | Medication instructions, follow-up timeline |
| Euthanasia follow-up | Deeply compassionate | Personal, unhurried, no marketing |
| Reactivation | Welcoming, no judgment | We miss seeing [pet name] |

## Reminder Cadence

### Vaccination Reminders
- **30 days before due:** First reminder (email + text)
- **Day of due date:** Second reminder if not scheduled (text)
- **14 days overdue:** Third reminder (email with gentle urgency)
- **30 days overdue:** Phone call from team member
- **90 days overdue:** Final notice (email/mail) before marking overdue in record

### Appointment Reminders
- **7 days before:** Email confirmation with appointment details
- **2 days before:** Text reminder with date, time, and any prep instructions
- **Day of (morning):** Text reminder with address and "See you today!"

### Post-Visit Follow-Up
- **Surgery:** Call within 24 hours, text check-in at 48 hours, follow-up call at 7-10 days
- **Sick visit:** Text check-in at 48 hours, call at 5-7 days if condition was serious
- **Wellness visit:** Email within 24 hours with visit summary and next-due dates
- **Dental cleaning:** Call next day, check-in at 3 days

## Seasonal Alert Topics

| Season | Alert Topics |
|--------|-------------|
| Spring | Heartworm testing and prevention start, tick season begins, allergy season, Easter hazards (chocolate, lilies for cats) |
| Summer | Heat stroke prevention, water safety, fireworks anxiety (July 4th), foxtail awareness, snake encounters |
| Fall | Back-to-school schedule changes, Halloween candy/costume safety, fall allergy flare-ups |
| Winter | Antifreeze toxicity, holiday food hazards, cold weather pet safety, poinsettia/holly risks |

## Euthanasia and Pet Loss Communication

This is the most important communication a vet clinic sends. Get it right.

### Immediately After
- Verbal condolences from the entire care team present
- Provide information about remains (cremation options, burial guidance)
- Give them time — never rush a grieving family out of the room
- Offer a clay paw print or fur clipping if not already done

### Within 3 Days
- Handwritten sympathy card signed by the veterinarian and care team
- Personal note referencing the pet by name and something specific ("Max's tail never stopped wagging when he came in")
- Optional: include a pet loss support resource or hotline number

### Within 1-2 Weeks
- Optional: personal phone call from the attending veterinarian
- Brief, compassionate: "I've been thinking about you and wanted to check in"

### What NOT to do
- Never send automated appointment reminders for a deceased pet (flag the record IMMEDIATELY)
- Never include marketing or promotional content in any sympathy communication
- Never use the phrase "at least" ("at least he lived a long life")
- Never suggest getting another pet
- Wait minimum 3-6 months before any future outreach about other pets in the household
