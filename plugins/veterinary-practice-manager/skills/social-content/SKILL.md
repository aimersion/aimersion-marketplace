# Social Content

Pet health tips, clinic updates, seasonal alerts, and engagement content for veterinary practice social media.

## Content Strategy for Vet Clinics

### Why Social Media Matters for Vet Practices
- 60% of pet owners choose a vet clinic based on online presence and reviews
- Social media is the #1 way new pet owners (especially millennials and Gen Z) find a vet
- Consistent posting builds trust and top-of-mind awareness
- Engagement creates emotional connection that drives loyalty and referrals

### Posting Frequency Recommendations
- **Facebook:** 3-5 posts per week
- **Instagram:** 4-7 posts per week (including Stories)
- **TikTok:** 2-3 videos per week (short, authentic, don't need to be polished)

### Content Pillars (Rotate These)

1. **Educate** (30%) — Pet health tips, seasonal warnings, nutrition advice, common misconceptions
2. **Connect** (30%) — Patient photos (with consent), team introductions, behind-the-scenes, community events
3. **Engage** (25%) — Polls, questions, contests, "this or that," pet photo submissions
4. **Promote** (15%) — Services, wellness plans, new offerings, special events

## Pet Health Content Calendar by Season

### Spring (March-May)
- Heartworm prevention: why, when, how
- Tick and flea season prep
- Spring allergy symptoms in pets
- Easter hazards: chocolate, lilies, plastic grass
- Heartworm awareness month (April)
- National Pet Month (May)

### Summer (June-August)
- Heat stroke signs and prevention
- Never leave pets in cars (temperature infographic)
- Water safety for dogs
- Fireworks anxiety prep (early June for July 4th)
- Hot pavement check (hand test)
- Summer grooming tips
- Travel safety with pets

### Fall (September-November)
- Back-to-school: pets adjusting to changed routines
- Halloween safety: costumes, candy, decorations
- Thanksgiving hazards: turkey bones, fatty foods, xylitol
- Fall mushroom toxicity awareness
- Prep indoor pets for heating season (dry air, space heaters)

### Winter (December-February)
- Holiday hazards roundup (plants, food, ornaments, tinsel)
- Antifreeze toxicity warning
- Cold weather safety: frostbite, hypothermia, ice melt on paws
- New Year fireworks anxiety
- Winter weight management
- Valentine's Day: chocolate toxicity reminder
- National Pet Dental Health Month (February)

## Content That Gets High Engagement

**What works:**
- Before/after grooming photos
- New patient "first visit" photos with welcome sign
- Staff holding cute animals
- "Guess the breed" posts
- Weight loss success stories (pets)
- "Happy tails" adoption stories for rescue clinic patients
- Quick educational videos (30-60 seconds)
- Day-in-the-life content
- Memes and relatable pet humor (keep it on-brand)

**What doesn't work:**
- Clinical/graphic surgical content
- Dry, text-heavy educational posts with no images
- Too many promotional posts in a row
- Generic stock photos of animals
- Posting the same content across all platforms without adaptation
