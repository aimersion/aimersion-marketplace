# Staff Trainer

Create training materials, standard operating procedures, and competency checklists for veterinary practice staff.

## Training Material Categories

### Clinical Staff (DVMs, Techs, Assistants)

**Procedure Protocols** — Step-by-step SOPs for:
- Patient intake and triage
- Physical examination workflow
- Vaccine administration (route, site, documentation, reaction monitoring)
- Blood draw (venipuncture technique, sample handling, labeling)
- IV catheter placement
- Anesthesia induction, monitoring, and recovery
- Surgical prep (patient and room)
- Dental cleaning and charting
- Radiology positioning and safety
- In-house lab procedures (running CBC, chemistry, urinalysis, cytology)
- Emergency protocols (CPR, anaphylaxis, GDV stabilization, toxicosis)
- Euthanasia protocol (preparation, procedure, client support, aftercare)
- Controlled substance handling and documentation

**For each procedure, include:**
1. Purpose and indications
2. Required equipment and supplies
3. Step-by-step instructions
4. Safety precautions
5. Documentation requirements
6. Common complications and how to handle them
7. Competency sign-off checklist

### Front Desk / Client Service Staff

**Key training areas:**
- Phone etiquette and call handling (scheduling, triage questions, price inquiries)
- Client check-in and check-out workflow
- Payment processing and financial discussions
- Appointment scheduling optimization
- Handling upset or emotional clients
- Insurance and payment plan explanations
- Medical record basics (what to document, what not to)
- OSHA and safety awareness
- Emergency call recognition (when to interrupt the doctor)

### All Staff

- OSHA safety training (sharps, radiation, anesthesia gases, animal handling)
- HIPAA-equivalent confidentiality (client information privacy)
- Workplace safety and animal bite/scratch protocol
- Controlled substance awareness and reporting
- Compassion fatigue awareness and self-care
- Customer service standards and practice values

## Training Material Format

When creating training materials, structure them for practical use:

### SOPs (Standard Operating Procedures)
- Title and SOP number
- Purpose (one sentence)
- Applies to (which roles)
- Equipment needed
- Step-by-step procedure (numbered, clear, actionable)
- Safety notes (highlighted/bold)
- Quality check points
- Documentation requirements
- Review date and approved by

### Competency Checklists
- Task description
- Demonstrated to trainee (date, trainer initials)
- Performed under supervision (date, trainer initials)
- Performed independently (date, trainer initials)
- Verified competent (supervisor signature, date)

### Quick Reference Cards
- Laminated card format (for posting in treatment area)
- Drug dose charts by weight
- Emergency drug doses
- Lab reference ranges
- Triage criteria (when to call the vet to the phone)
- Vaccine protocols by species and age

## New Employee Onboarding Framework

### Week 1: Orientation
- Practice tour and safety walkthrough
- Team introductions and role explanations
- Review employee handbook and policies
- Technology training (practice management software, phone system)
- Shadow experienced staff in their role

### Week 2-3: Hands-On Training
- Start performing tasks under direct supervision
- Begin competency checklist sign-offs
- Review practice protocols and SOPs
- Practice client communication scenarios (role-play)

### Week 4: Supervised Independence
- Perform tasks with supervision available but not constant
- Receive daily feedback
- Complete remaining competency sign-offs
- Identify areas needing additional training

### 90-Day Check-In
- Formal review of competency completion
- Feedback session (bidirectional)
- Goal setting for continued development
- Address any gaps
