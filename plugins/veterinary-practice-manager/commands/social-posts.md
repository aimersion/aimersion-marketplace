Generate social media content for the veterinary practice. Theme or quantity: $ARGUMENTS

## Process

1. **Context** — Ask the user for (skip what's already provided):
   - Practice name and any branding notes
   - Platforms used (Facebook, Instagram, TikTok)
   - Any current promotions or events to highlight
   - Seasonal focus or specific topic requests
   - Whether they want posts for a week, two weeks, or a month

2. **Generate a Content Mix** following the 80/20 rule (80% value/engagement, 20% promotional):

   **Educational Posts (2-3 per week):**
   - Pet health tips by season (tick prevention spring/summer, holiday hazards winter, etc.)
   - Breed-specific health awareness
   - Common symptoms that need a vet visit vs. can wait
   - Nutrition myths debunked
   - Dental health for pets
   - Senior pet care tips
   - Puppy/kitten milestone guides

   **Engagement Posts (2-3 per week):**
   - "Pet of the week" (with client consent)
   - Photo contests ("Show us your pet's best trick")
   - This-or-that polls ("Dogs or cats — which team are you?")
   - "Meet our team" spotlight
   - Behind-the-scenes clinic life
   - Adorable patient photos (with consent)
   - Pet holiday celebrations (National Dog Day, Cat Day, etc.)

   **Promotional Posts (1-2 per week):**
   - Wellness plan promotion
   - Seasonal service highlights (dental month, vaccine clinics)
   - New services or technology announcements
   - Referral program reminders
   - Online pharmacy / food delivery promotion

3. **For Each Post, Provide:**
   - Platform-specific caption (character limits: FB unlimited but keep under 250 for engagement, IG up to 2200 with most important info first, TikTok for video concepts)
   - Image/video suggestion (what to photograph or film)
   - Relevant hashtags (10-15 for Instagram, 2-3 for Facebook)
   - Best posting time recommendation
   - Engagement prompt (question or CTA to drive comments)

4. **Content Calendar** — Organize into a weekly grid showing which post goes live on which day and platform.

Tone: warm, approachable, occasionally funny. Vet clinics that show personality on social media build the strongest client loyalty. Pet puns are encouraged.
