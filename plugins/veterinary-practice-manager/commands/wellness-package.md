Design a wellness plan package. Target: $ARGUMENTS (e.g., "puppy", "adult cat", "senior dog")

## Process

1. **Determine the Target** — Ask the user for:
   - Species (canine, feline, or both)
   - Life stage (puppy/kitten, adult, senior)
   - Practice location (affects parasite prevalence and lifestyle risks)
   - Services currently offered and their fee schedule
   - Pricing goal (cost-neutral, profit center, or loss-leader for retention)

2. **Build the Package** including:

   **Included Services:**
   - Wellness exams (how many per year — typically 1 for adults, 2 for seniors, series for puppies/kittens)
   - Vaccinations (core and recommended non-core for the area)
   - Parasite screening (fecal, heartworm/tick-borne disease)
   - Diagnostic bloodwork (CBC, chemistry, T4 for seniors)
   - Dental assessment
   - Nail trims
   - Parasite prevention (heartworm, flea/tick — # months included)
   - Nutrition consultation

   **Optional Add-Ons:**
   - Dental cleaning (at a discount)
   - Spay/neuter (for puppy/kitten plans)
   - Microchipping
   - Additional bloodwork panels
   - Behavioral consultation
   - Unlimited office visits (for sick visits — powerful differentiator)

3. **Pricing Structure:**
   - Calculate total à la carte value of included services
   - Set plan price at 15-25% discount from à la carte
   - Divide into monthly payments (12 monthly installments)
   - Show the client: monthly cost, total annual cost, total savings vs. à la carte
   - Example format: "$49/month — saves you $185/year compared to paying as you go"

4. **Client-Facing Materials:**
   - Plan name (something memorable, not just "Wellness Plan Level 1")
   - One-page comparison sheet (what's included at each tier if multiple tiers)
   - FAQ document for front desk team
   - Enrollment form template
   - Welcome packet content for new enrollees

5. **Terms and Conditions Guidance:**
   - Minimum enrollment period (typically 12 months)
   - Cancellation policy (remaining balance due)
   - What happens if the pet passes away
   - Transferability between pets
   - What's NOT included (emergencies, illness, surgery beyond spay/neuter if included)

Tone: the wellness plan should feel like a smart financial decision and a commitment to the pet's health, not a sales pitch.
