# Lesson Writer

Create compelling lesson content for online courses — video scripts, text lessons, worksheets, exercises, and supporting materials. Every lesson must teach effectively while maintaining student engagement.

## Video Script Writing

### Script Structure (Per Video Segment: 5-10 minutes)
1. **Hook** (15-30 seconds): Open with a question, surprising fact, story, or relatable problem
2. **Roadmap** (15 seconds): "In this lesson, you'll learn [outcome 1], [outcome 2], and [outcome 3]"
3. **Core Teaching** (3-7 minutes): Deliver content in 2-3 clear segments
4. **Example/Demo** (1-3 minutes): Show a real-world application or walkthrough
5. **Summary** (30 seconds): Recap the key takeaways
6. **Call to Action** (15 seconds): Tell the student exactly what to do next

### Script Formatting
```
[TALKING HEAD]
"Welcome back! Today we're tackling [topic]. If you've ever struggled with [pain point], this lesson is going to change everything."

[SCREEN SHARE / SLIDES]
"Let me show you exactly how this works..."
[Show step 1 with annotation]
[Pause for emphasis] "This is the part most people get wrong."

[TALKING HEAD]
"Now it's your turn. Pause this video and try [exercise]. I'll wait."
[Beat] "Great. Let's look at what you should have gotten..."
```

### Video Script Best Practices
- Write conversationally — read scripts aloud to check naturalness
- Use "you" and "we" language to create connection
- Signal transitions clearly: "Now that we've covered X, let's move to Y"
- Include B-roll notes, screen share cues, and visual overlay instructions
- Target 130-150 words per minute for comfortable pacing
- Mark emphasis points, pauses, and tone shifts

## Text Lesson Writing

### Lesson Layout
- **Title**: Clear, benefit-oriented ("How to Write Headlines That Convert")
- **Estimated reading time**: [X minutes]
- **Learning objectives**: 2-3 bullet points
- **Content body**: Structured with headers, examples, and visuals
- **Key takeaways**: Boxed or highlighted summary
- **Next steps**: Link to exercise or next lesson

### Writing Guidelines
- Keep paragraphs to 3-4 sentences maximum
- Use one idea per paragraph
- Include a visual, example, or interactive element every 300-400 words
- Use analogies to connect new concepts to familiar ones
- Bold key terms on first use and define them inline
- Create "Pro Tip" callout boxes for advanced insights

## Worksheet & Exercise Design

### Exercise Types
- **Guided practice**: Step-by-step worksheet applying the lesson concept
- **Reflection prompts**: Open-ended questions for personal application
- **Case study analysis**: Real-world scenario with structured questions
- **Skill drill**: Repetitive practice for technique mastery
- **Creative application**: Open-ended project applying the concept freely

### Worksheet Structure
1. **Context**: Brief reminder of the concept (2-3 sentences)
2. **Instructions**: Clear, numbered steps
3. **Work space**: Structured fields or prompts for the student to complete
4. **Self-check**: Criteria or example answers for the student to compare
5. **Extension**: Optional advanced challenge for fast learners

## Engagement Techniques

- **Pattern interrupt**: Change the format or energy every 3-5 minutes
- **Prediction prompts**: "Before I reveal the answer, what do you think?"
- **Relatable stories**: Open lessons with a personal experience or student example
- **Curiosity gaps**: Tease upcoming content to maintain forward momentum
- **Social proof**: Reference other students' results or common struggles
- **Micro-commitments**: Small actions that build investment ("Type your answer in the comments")

## Content Quality Checklist

Before finalizing any lesson:
- [ ] Learning objectives are clearly stated
- [ ] Content directly supports the objectives (no tangents)
- [ ] At least one concrete example per concept
- [ ] Appropriate for the target audience's level
- [ ] Engaging hook in the first 30 seconds
- [ ] Clear call to action at the end
- [ ] Accessible language (no unexplained jargon)
- [ ] Visual/interactive elements included
