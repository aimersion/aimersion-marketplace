# Marketing Launcher

Plan and execute online course launches — from pre-launch audience building through post-launch optimization. Design email sequences, webinar funnels, social media campaigns, and sales page copy that convert.

## Launch Timeline

### Pre-Launch Phase (4-8 weeks before)
- **Audience building**: Grow email list with lead magnets related to course topic
- **Content seeding**: Publish free content that establishes authority and creates demand
- **Waitlist creation**: Build anticipation with an early access or discount offer
- **Social proof**: Collect beta tester testimonials and transformation stories
- **Partnerships**: Recruit affiliates, guest speakers, or cross-promotion partners

### Launch Phase (5-14 days)
- **Day 1**: Open cart — announce availability to full list
- **Days 2-3**: Value-driven content — share wins, testimonials, case studies
- **Days 4-7**: Objection handling — address common concerns via email and social
- **Days 8-10**: Urgency building — deadline reminders, bonus expiration
- **Final 48 hours**: Last chance — scarcity messaging, final testimonials
- **Close**: Cart closes — thank you + waitlist for next cohort

### Post-Launch Phase (1-2 weeks after)
- **Onboarding**: Welcome sequence for new students
- **Feedback collection**: Survey new buyers on purchase decision
- **Downsell**: Offer alternative to non-buyers (payment plan, mini-course, waitlist)
- **Analysis**: Review metrics, identify what worked, document learnings

## Email Launch Sequence

### Pre-Launch Emails (Waitlist/Leads)
1. **The Story** — Share your personal connection to the topic and why you created the course
2. **The Problem** — Agitate the pain point your course solves
3. **The Solution Preview** — Reveal the framework or approach (without giving everything away)
4. **Social Proof** — Share beta results, testimonials, or case studies
5. **Early Access Offer** — Exclusive discount or bonus for waitlist members

### Launch Emails (7-Day Open Cart)
1. **The Doors Are Open** — Announce the course, link to sales page, highlight key benefits
2. **The Curriculum** — Walk through what's inside module by module
3. **FAQ & Objections** — Address "Is this right for me?" questions
4. **Student Story** — Feature a transformation story
5. **Bonus Stack** — Reveal limited-time bonuses
6. **48-Hour Warning** — Urgency + recap of everything included
7. **Final Hours** — Last call, emotional close, deadline reminder

## Webinar Funnel Structure

### Registration Page
- Compelling headline focused on the transformation
- 3 bullet points of what attendees will learn
- Date, time, and "Save My Spot" CTA
- Social proof (attendee count, testimonials)

### Webinar Content (60-90 minutes)
1. **Introduction & Promise** (5 min): Set expectations, build credibility
2. **Story** (10 min): Personal journey and why this matters
3. **Teaching Content** (25-30 min): Deliver genuine value — teach a framework or strategy
4. **The Gap** (5 min): Show what's possible but hard to do alone
5. **The Offer** (10 min): Present the course as the bridge
6. **Bonuses & Urgency** (5 min): Stack value, create time pressure
7. **Q&A** (15-20 min): Handle objections live

### Post-Webinar Sequence
- Replay email (sent 1 hour after)
- Recap email with key takeaways (next day)
- FAQ email addressing common questions
- Final deadline email

## Sales Page Copy Framework

### Above the Fold
- **Headline**: Transformation promise ("Go from [current state] to [desired state] in [timeframe]")
- **Subheadline**: Who it's for and the key benefit
- **CTA button**: Action-oriented ("Enroll Now" or "Start Learning Today")

### Body Sections (in order)
1. Pain point agitation
2. Introduce the solution (your course)
3. Curriculum overview with module descriptions
4. Instructor bio and credibility
5. Testimonials and results
6. Bonus stack
7. Pricing and guarantee
8. FAQ section
9. Final CTA with urgency

## Testimonial Strategy

### Collecting Testimonials
- Ask specific questions: "What was your biggest takeaway?" "What result did you achieve?"
- Request before-and-after context
- Ask for permission to use with full name and photo
- Capture video testimonials when possible

### Using Testimonials
- Match testimonials to objections they address
- Place near pricing sections and CTAs
- Feature diverse voices (different backgrounds, experience levels)
- Include specific results and numbers where possible
