# Student Engager

Drive student engagement, community participation, and course completion through strategic communication, community management, and motivational nudges.

## Onboarding Sequence

### Welcome Sequence (First 7 Days)
1. **Welcome email** (Immediate): Celebrate their decision, provide login instructions, set expectations
2. **Quick win** (Day 1): Direct them to an easy first lesson that delivers immediate value
3. **Community intro** (Day 2): Guide them to introduce themselves in the community
4. **Study plan** (Day 3): Help them create a realistic completion schedule
5. **Check-in** (Day 7): Ask how it's going, offer help, encourage progress

### First Lesson Experience
The first lesson must deliver a "quick win" — a tangible result within 15 minutes. This early success creates momentum and reduces refund requests.

## Engagement Strategies

### Active Engagement Triggers
- **Celebration emails**: Automated congrats at 25%, 50%, 75%, 100% completion
- **Weekly digest**: Summary of community highlights and new content
- **Challenge prompts**: Weekly or daily challenges that encourage action
- **Live office hours**: Scheduled Q&A sessions for real-time interaction
- **Accountability partners**: Pair students for mutual support

### Re-Engagement for Inactive Students
Trigger when a student hasn't logged in for 7+ days:
1. **Gentle nudge** (Day 7): "We miss you! Here's what you're working on next..."
2. **Value reminder** (Day 14): Share a testimonial or result from an active student
3. **Support offer** (Day 21): "Is something blocking you? Reply and let me help."
4. **Fresh start** (Day 30): "It's never too late — here's how to jump back in"

### Reducing Drop-Off Points
Common drop-off points and solutions:
- **After Module 1**: Ensure quick win; send personal check-in
- **Mid-course plateau**: Introduce a bonus or live event
- **Before final project**: Break the project into small, dated milestones
- **Technical confusion**: Create a FAQ or troubleshooting guide

## Community Management

### Discussion Prompt Templates
- **Reflection**: "What's one thing from this week's lesson you'll implement immediately?"
- **Show your work**: "Share your [exercise/project] and give feedback to one other student"
- **Debate**: "Do you agree with [concept]? Share your perspective and why"
- **Wins**: "What's one win you've had this week, big or small?"
- **Help thread**: "What's your #1 question right now? Let's solve them together"

### Responding to Student Questions
- Acknowledge the question and validate the student's thinking
- Answer clearly and concisely — link to relevant lesson if applicable
- Turn individual questions into community learning moments
- Tag other students who might benefit from or contribute to the answer
- If the question reveals a content gap, note it for course improvement

### Community Guidelines
- Set clear expectations from day one (posted and pinned)
- Model the behavior you want to see (post regularly, be supportive)
- Address negativity quickly and privately
- Celebrate contributions publicly
- Create a "wins" channel or thread for positive reinforcement

## Completion Rate Optimization

### Benchmarks
- Average online course completion: 5-15%
- Good completion rate: 30-40%
- Excellent completion rate: 50%+
- Cohort-based courses: 70-85%

### Strategies to Improve Completion
- Keep lessons under 10 minutes
- End every lesson with a clear next action
- Use progress bars and completion badges
- Send automated progress emails
- Create a completion certificate with social sharing
- Offer a "graduation" bonus or community recognition
- Build accountability through cohort deadlines
