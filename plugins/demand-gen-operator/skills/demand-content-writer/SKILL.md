# Demand Content Writer

Generate high-quality, pain-led B2B content that builds awareness, drives engagement, and converts to pipeline.

## The Demand Content Philosophy

Most B2B content educates. Demand content *disturbs* — it makes the reader uncomfortable enough with their current situation to want to change it. The best content:
1. Names the pain they feel but haven't articulated
2. Shows why their current approach is making it worse
3. Reframes the problem in a new way
4. Points to a better path

## Required Inputs Before Writing

Establish before producing any content:
- **ICP:** Who specifically is this for (title, company type, pain level)
- **Funnel stage:** TOFU / MOFU / BOFU and why
- **Topic/angle:** The specific insight, pain, or story this piece leads with
- **Format:** LinkedIn post / Blog / Email / Ad copy / Video script
- **CTA:** What should the reader do after consuming this?

## LinkedIn Posts

### Format Options

**Hook-first (for engagement and reach):**
```
[Provocative opening line — 1 sentence, stops the scroll]

[Build the tension — 2-3 sentences expanding the problem]

[The insight/reframe — your unique perspective]

[Practical takeaway — 3-5 bullets or numbered points]

[CTA — question, link, or call to action]
```

**Story-led (for authentic reach):**
```
[Personal story opener — specific, real, relatable moment]
[The lesson embedded in the story]
[The broader implication for the reader]
[What to do with this insight]
```

**Data-led (for authority and shares):**
```
[Surprising stat or data point]
[Why this matters more than people realize]
[What it means for the reader's specific role]
[The counterintuitive action this implies]
```

**Rules:**
- First line must stop the scroll — never start with "I" or a generic observation
- Maximum 3 sentences before a line break
- Use specific numbers over vague claims ("40% of reps" not "many reps")
- End with a question or clear CTA
- 150-300 words optimal for engagement

## Blog Posts

**Structure:**
```
Title: [Primary keyword + compelling angle — not just the keyword]
Meta description: [Search intent match, 155 chars, CTA implicit]

Opening (2-3 sentences): Pain-first hook — make the reader feel seen

H2: [The problem in depth]
  What's actually happening (name the specific situation)
  Why conventional solutions fail (the insight most miss)
  The cost of inaction (make the stakes real)

H2: [The framework / approach]
  Step 1 with specifics and example
  Step 2 with specifics and example
  Step 3 with specifics and example

H2: [Proof it works]
  Case study, customer result, or external data
  Specific metric or outcome

H2: [How to apply this starting now]
  Practical immediate actions (not just theory)

CTA: [Specific next action relevant to what they just read]
```

**Rules:**
- 1,200-2,000 words for thought leadership pieces
- 2,000-3,000 words for SEO-targeted guides
- Every H2 should be a standalone takeaway if scanned
- Include at least one original insight — not just curated information
- CTA must match funnel stage (don't ask for demo after TOFU post)

## Email Campaigns

**Structure:**
```
Subject line: [Specific, curiosity-driven, under 50 chars]
Preview text: [Completes or extends the subject line — don't repeat it]

Opening: [1-sentence hook — pain acknowledgment or provocative insight]

Body paragraphs (3-4, each one point):
  P1: Validate the pain — they're not alone in this
  P2: The insight — why it's happening / what most people miss
  P3: The implication — what happens if this doesn't change
  P4: The path forward — what you recommend

Single CTA: [One specific, clear action]

Signature: [Name, role, company — no wall of links]
```

**Rules:**
- One CTA only — multiple CTAs kill conversion
- Under 200 words for nurture sequence emails
- Subject lines: specific beats vague ("3 reasons your QBRs kill deals" beats "Important update")

## Ad Copy

**LinkedIn Sponsored Content:**
```
Intro text (150 chars — most important): [Pain statement or bold claim]
Body (up to 600 chars): [Pain expanded → insight → payoff → CTA]
Headline (70 chars): [Outcome, ICP pain solved, or specific asset name]
CTA button: [Match to funnel stage — "Download" / "Learn More" / "Book Demo"]
```

**Google Search Ads:**
```
Headline 1 (30 chars): [Primary keyword or pain]
Headline 2 (30 chars): [Key benefit or differentiator]
Headline 3 (30 chars): [CTA or social proof]
Description 1 (90 chars): [Pain + solution — specific]
Description 2 (90 chars): [Social proof + CTA]
```

## Quality Check

Before finalizing any piece:
- [ ] Opening makes the ICP feel immediately understood?
- [ ] Says something they haven't heard before?
- [ ] Specific enough that vague readers won't click (B2B ad copy should repel non-ICP)?
- [ ] Free of corporate jargon and feature-speak?
- [ ] Single, clear CTA that matches what they just read?
- [ ] Would someone forward this to a colleague because it's genuinely useful?
