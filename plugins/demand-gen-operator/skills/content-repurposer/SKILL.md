# Content Repurposer

Extract maximum value from every long-form asset — turn one piece into a full week of content.

## The Core Principle

Most B2B teams create content in isolation. A blog gets published, gets some traffic, and is forgotten. A webinar happens, gets recorded, and sits in a folder. This skill turns every major asset into a multi-week distribution engine.

Rule: Every long-form asset should produce at least 5x its creation cost in distribution value.

## Repurposing by Source Type

### From a Blog Post (1,500+ words)

**Extract:**
1. **3-5 LinkedIn posts** — One insight or section per post
2. **1 LinkedIn carousel** — The framework or process as slides
3. **1 email** — The most provocative insight + link to full post
4. **3 shareable quotes** — For graphics or pull-quote posts
5. **1 short-form video script** — The opening hook expanded to 60-90 seconds

**Process:**
```
Step 1: Identify the 5 most quotable/shareable sentences
Step 2: Extract the core framework or process (if any)
Step 3: Find the most surprising data point or counterintuitive claim
Step 4: Isolate the strongest story or example
Step 5: Build each output format from these raw materials
```

### From a Podcast Episode (30-60 min)

**Extract:**
1. **5-7 LinkedIn posts** — One per major talking point or insight
2. **1 long-form blog post** — The episode's core argument with added context
3. **3-5 email snippets** — Best quotes or insights for nurture sequences
4. **10+ short clip concepts** — 30-90 second moments with specific insights
5. **1 episode summary** — For show notes and SEO
6. **Guest-specific content** — Personalized posts the guest can share

### From a Webinar (45-90 min)

**Extract:**
1. **5-7 LinkedIn posts** — Key takeaways, frameworks, memorable audience questions
2. **1 blog post** — The main framework or methodology presented
3. **Post-webinar email sequence** — Immediate recap, key insight, follow-up offer (3 emails)
4. **3-5 short clip concepts** — For LinkedIn video and potential ad creative
5. **1 carousel** — Key slides reimagined as a LinkedIn document post
6. **Sales enablement doc** — Objections raised and how they were answered

### From a Case Study

**Extract:**
1. **3 LinkedIn posts** — Problem, process, and result (one each)
2. **1 nurture email** — Story-led version for email sequences
3. **2 ad creative concepts** — One pain-led, one outcome-led
4. **1 sales one-pager** — Summary format for sales to share in deals
5. **1 testimonial pull quote** — For website, proposals, and ads

## Creative Angle Rotation

When producing multiple LinkedIn posts from one asset, rotate angles to avoid repetition:
- Post 1: The insight (what most people miss)
- Post 2: The framework (step-by-step how-to)
- Post 3: The story (specific example or case)
- Post 4: The data (stat or number that surprises)
- Post 5: The question (engagement post that invites debate)

Each post should feel like it stands alone — a reader who saw all 5 shouldn't feel like they saw the same post five times.
