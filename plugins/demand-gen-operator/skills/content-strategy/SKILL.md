# Content Strategy

Build a systematic content engine that maps every piece to a funnel stage, ICP pain level, and business outcome.

## The Core Problem This Solves

Most B2B content is created by gut feel. The result: tons of top-of-funnel awareness content that generates traffic but no pipeline. This skill creates a demand-first content strategy — everything maps to buyer behavior, pain level, and conversion intent.

## Funnel-Stage Content Matrix

### Stage 1: Problem Aware (TOFU)
Buyer knows they have a problem but hasn't defined it as a category need.
- **Content types:** Opinion pieces, industry trends, "why X is broken" posts, podcast episodes, original research
- **KPI:** Impressions, reach, new audience, social engagement
- **Tone:** Provocative, insight-led, challenges status quo
- **Budget of total content effort:** 30%

### Stage 2: Solution Aware (MOFU-Upper)
Buyer is researching how to solve the problem.
- **Content types:** Frameworks, how-to guides, comparison posts, webinars, detailed LinkedIn posts
- **KPI:** Engagement, time on page, email subscribers, content downloads
- **Tone:** Educational, authoritative, practical
- **Budget of total content effort:** 35%

### Stage 3: Vendor Aware (MOFU-Lower)
Buyer is evaluating specific vendors/solutions.
- **Content types:** Case studies, ROI calculators, competitive comparisons, demo content, testimonial-led posts
- **KPI:** Demo requests, content downloads, direct engagement
- **Tone:** Proof-led, specific outcomes, reduces risk
- **Budget of total content effort:** 25%

### Stage 4: Decision (BOFU)
Buyer is close to deciding.
- **Content types:** Objection-handling content, implementation guides, customer success stories, pricing/value justification
- **KPI:** MQLs, demo completions, proposal requests
- **Tone:** Direct, confidence-building, removes last objections
- **Budget of total content effort:** 10%

## Weekly Content Cadence Formula

For a B2B company targeting 15+ pieces per week:

```
DAILY (LinkedIn):
  1 opinion/insight post (TOFU/MOFU)
  1 repurposed piece from long-form

WEEKLY:
  1 long-form blog post (1,500+ words, SEO-targeted, MOFU)
  1 email to list (curated insight or content roundup)
  2-3 short-form video scripts or clip concepts (TOFU)
  1 deep-dive: case study OR framework OR data piece (MOFU/BOFU)

MONTHLY:
  1 webinar or live event
  1 research piece or original data
  1 customer story (written or video)
```

## Content Opportunity Scoring

Score each potential content topic before committing to create it:

| Factor | Score (1-5) | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| ICP pain alignment | 1-5 | 30% |
| Search volume (if SEO) | 1-5 | 20% |
| Competitive content gap | 1-5 | 20% |
| Proof/data available | 1-5 | 15% |
| Time to create (inverse) | 5-1 | 15% |

Priority Score = weighted average. Build the highest-scoring topics first.

For every piece in the calendar:
```

Channel: [Where it will be distributed]
CTA: [What the reader should do next]
SEO target: [Primary keyword if applicable]

```

## Content-to-Pipeline Attribution Plan

Build the measurement model before creating content:

1. **UTM structure** — Every piece gets UTM parameters (source/medium/campaign/content)
2. **Content touchpoints** — Track which pieces appear in closed-won customer journeys
3. **MQL source mapping** — Which content channels drive MQLs (not just traffic)
4. **Quarterly audit** — Which content pieces earned their creation cost in pipeline?

## Quarterly Planning Output

Deliver per quarter:
1. **3 Themes** — Quarterly narrative that aligns to business priorities and ICP pain cycles
2. **12-week calendar** — Topics, formats, owners, publish dates
3. **Pillar content** — 1-2 major pieces (research, guide, framework) that anchor the quarter and get repurposed
4. **Distribution amplification plan** — How each major piece gets promoted beyond initial publish
5. **Content retirement plan** — What outdated content to update or retire
