# Outreach Engine

Generate job opportunities through proactive outreach — not just inbound applications. Direct outreach to recruiters, hiring managers, and network contacts gets responses at 3-5x the rate of cold ATS applications. Most job seekers skip this. Don't.

## Outreach Philosophy

The best jobs are often:
- Not yet posted (companies thinking about hiring but not committed)
- Already filled internally (but similar roles opening soon)
- Posted but finding the right candidates through referrals

Outreach creates pipeline that the ATS alone never will.

## Outreach Types

### Type 1: Recruiter at Target Company

**Best for**: Companies you want to work for, even if no current opening

**LinkedIn message (under 300 chars):**
```
Hi [Name] — I'm a [title] with [X years] in [specific area], currently 
exploring new opportunities. [Company] is at the top of my list. 
Open to hearing about any current or upcoming roles?
```

**Email version (if you have it):**
```
Subject: [Your Role] exploring opportunities at [Company]

Hi [Name],

I'm a [title] with [X years] building [specific thing] at [previous company]. 
[Company] has been on my radar because of [specific reason — product, 
market position, recent news].

I'd love to learn if there are any current or upcoming openings on the 
[team/function] side. Happy to share my resume or context on my background.

[Name] | [LinkedIn] | [Phone]
```

---

### Type 2: Hiring Manager — Proactive Reach

**Best for**: When you know who you'd report to before a role is posted

**LinkedIn message:**
```
Hi [Name] — I've followed your work at [Company] for a while, 
particularly [specific thing they've written/built/said]. I'm a 
[role] with [specific relevant experience]. If you're ever building 
out your team, I'd welcome a conversation.
```

**Key**: Do NOT ask for a job directly. Ask for a conversation. Let them bring up roles.

---

### Type 3: Warm Network — 1st Degree Contacts

**Best for**: People you know well who work at target companies

**Message:**
```
Hey [Name] — hope things are great! Quick ask — I'm actively looking 
for my next [role type] role and [Company] is high on my list. 

Would you be open to (a) chatting for 20 min about your experience there, 
or (b) passing my resume along if there are relevant openings?

No pressure either way — I know you're busy. Here's a quick snapshot of 
what I'm looking for: [2 sentences].
```

**Always attach**: A short, updated resume or link to LinkedIn profile.

---

### Type 4: Cold Network — 2nd Degree or Alumni

**Best for**: Weaker ties — same school, same previous employer, mutual connections

**Connection request note:**
```
Hi [Name] — we're both [alumni of X / former [Company] people / both in 
[Slack community]]. I'm exploring [role type] opportunities and [Company] 
is on my list. Would love to connect and potentially ask a few questions.
```

**Follow-up message (after connecting):**
```
Thanks for connecting [Name]! I'm currently looking for my next [role] 
opportunity — [Company] is on my short list. 

I'd genuinely value 15-20 minutes to learn about your experience there 
and whether my background might be a fit. Happy to return the favor 
however useful — always glad to share what I know about [their industry/role].
```

---

### Type 5: Informational Interview Request

**Best for**: Learning about a company before applying, building relationship before a role opens

**Message:**
```
Hi [Name] — I've been following [Company]'s work in [space] and 
would love to learn more about the [function] team from someone 
doing it. Would you be open to a 20-minute call?

I'm exploring [role] opportunities and trying to understand which 
companies are the best fits — not asking for a job referral, 
just genuine learning.
```

**After the call:**
- Send a thank-you note within 24 hours
- Reference something specific from the conversation
- Stay in touch — this is a long-term relationship, not a one-time ask

---

### Type 6: Recruiter Agency / Executive Search Outreach

**Best for**: Senior roles ($150k+), specialized functions

**Email:**
```
Subject: [Your Title/Function] — Open to Confidential Conversations

Hi [Recruiter name],

I'm a [title] with [X years] specializing in [specific area], 
currently exploring new opportunities on a confidential basis.

My background in brief:
- [Achievement 1 with number]
- [Achievement 2 with number]  
- [Companies/industries relevant to their typical placements]

I'm targeting [role level/type] in [industry/function] with 
compensation expectations around [$X-Y base].

If you're working on searches that might be a fit, I'd welcome 
a brief call. Happy to send a full resume.

[Name] | [Phone] | [LinkedIn]
```

## Personalization at Scale

For high-volume outreach, use this template system to maintain quality:

**Base template + 3 personalization slots:**

```
[Hook — PERSONALIZED]: 1 sentence specific to this person/company
[Credential — TEMPLATE]: Your standard 1-sentence pitch
[Ask — PERSONALIZED]: Specific to what you want from this person
[Easy Yes — TEMPLATE]: Make it low-friction to say yes
```

This lets you send 20+ personalized messages per hour without each one feeling generic.

## LinkedIn Profile Optimization for Inbound

Make recruiters come to you by optimizing your profile to appear in searches:

**Headline formula (not just job title):**
```
[Current title] | [Key skill] | [Industry] | [What you're looking for/known for]
Example: "Product Manager | PLG & Monetization | B2B SaaS | Helping Teams Ship Less, Win More"
```

**"Open to Work" settings:**
- Use the "Open to Work" feature set to recruiter-only (not public green banner)
- List multiple target job titles — use all common variations
- Be specific about location preferences
- This puts you in recruiter search results immediately

**Profile keyword optimization:**
- Your headline is the most-searched field — load it with relevant keywords
- Skills section: Add all skills from your target job descriptions
- About section: Write in first person, include keywords naturally
- Experience bullets: Mirror the language of your target role JDs

## Outreach Volume & Tracking

**Weekly outreach targets by job search intensity:**

| Mode | Recruiter Messages | HM Messages | Network Outreach |
|------|-------------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Active (primary job) | 15-20/week | 5-10/week | 10-15/week |
| Passive (employed) | 5-10/week | 2-3/week | 5/week |
| Networking only | 3-5/week | 1-2/week | 10-15/week |

**Track all outreach in pipeline-tracker with:**
- Date sent
- Person name + title
- Company
- Message type
- Response (Yes / No / No response)
- Follow-up date

## Follow-Up Cadence

```
Day 0:  Initial message
Day 5:  One follow-up if no response (50% of responses come after follow-up)
Day 12: Final follow-up if still no response, then archive
```

**Follow-up message:**
```
Hi [Name] — following up on my note from [X days ago]. 
Still very interested in connecting if the timing works.
```

Never follow up more than twice. Move on and stay positive.

## Response Handling

**When they say "we don't have anything right now":**
```
"Totally understood — I appreciate you checking. Would it be okay 
to stay in touch? Happy to reconnect in a few months or if 
something relevant comes up."
```

**When they ask for more information:**
- Respond within 1 hour during business hours
- Send resume immediately
- Suggest a specific time to talk (not "let me know when you're free")

**When they ask for salary requirements:**
- State your range, not a single number
- Lead with "I'm targeting $X-Y based on my experience, though I'm focused on finding the right fit"
- Never give a floor — give a range where the floor is actually your target
