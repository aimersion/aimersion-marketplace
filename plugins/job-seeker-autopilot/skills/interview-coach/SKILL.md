# Interview Coach

Prepare candidates to perform at their best in any interview format — recruiter screens, hiring manager interviews, panel interviews, case studies, and final rounds. The goal is not memorization. It's building a library of strong, honest stories and the frameworks to deploy them confidently.

## Interview Prep Framework

### Phase 1: Understand the Interview

Ask the user:
1. **What stage?** Recruiter phone screen / HM first round / Panel / Case / Final / Offer negotiation
2. **Who are they meeting?** Recruiter / HM / Peers / Executives / Cross-functional
3. **What format?** Behavioral / Technical / Case / Portfolio review / Presentation
4. **What role?** (To understand what matters most in this interview)
5. **Company research done?** (If not, redirect to company-researcher first)

Different stages require different preparation — don't over-prep a recruiter screen or under-prep a final round.

---

### Phase 2: Build the Story Bank

The most important interview prep is building 8-10 strong career stories that can answer almost any question. Use the STAR+ format:

```
STAR+ Format:
S - Situation:  Set the context (1-2 sentences max)
T - Task:       What was your specific responsibility?  
A - Action:     What YOU did (not "we") — be specific about your choices
R - Result:     Quantified outcome — what changed because of you?
+ - Learning:   What would you do differently? (Shows self-awareness)
```

**The Universal Story Bank — build these 10 stories:**

1. **Biggest achievement** — Your most impressive career result
2. **Leadership example** — Led a team or project under pressure
3. **Conflict resolution** — Navigated disagreement with colleague/manager
4. **Failure** — Something that didn't work and what you learned
5. **Influence without authority** — Got buy-in with no direct power
6. **Prioritization under pressure** — Competing demands, hard choices
7. **Ambiguous problem** — No clear answer, you figured it out
8. **Data-driven decision** — Used analysis to make a call
9. **Cross-functional collaboration** — Worked across teams/departments
10. **Adapting to change** — Pivot, reorganization, new direction

For each story, help the user build it out fully in STAR+ format. These 10 stories, well-prepared, will handle 90% of behavioral questions.

### Phase 3: Question Preparation by Role Type

#### Universal Behavioral Questions

**"Tell me about yourself"** — Most important answer of the interview.

Formula:
```
1. Where you are now (current role/most recent role — 1 sentence)
2. How you got here (1-2 sentences of relevant career arc)
3. What you're known for / what you do best (most relevant to this role)
4. Why you're here today (why this role, this company — genuine, specific)

Total: 90 seconds maximum. Practice until it's smooth, not memorized-sounding.
```

**"Why are you leaving your current role?"**
Never say anything negative about current employer. Frame as:
- You've accomplished what you set out to do
- Ready for the next level of challenge
- This specific opportunity is uniquely compelling because [specific reason]

**"Why do you want to work here?"**
This must be specific. Generic answers ("I love your culture and values") are disqualifying.
Format: [Specific thing about company] + [How it connects to your experience] + [What you'd contribute]

**"What's your biggest weakness?"**
Three rules: Real (not a humble-brag), Past (you've worked on it), Resolved (what you did about it)
```
Example: "Earlier in my career I struggled with delegating — I'd take things back 
rather than invest time developing others. I got feedback on this at [Company] 
and have actively worked on it. Now I'm proud of the managers I've developed."
```

**"Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"**
Tie to this role's growth path. Show ambition without implying you'll leave:
"I'd want to have mastered this role and taken on broader scope — ideally growing 
into [next level] as the team and company scale."

**"What's your salary expectation?"**
- Research: Levels.fyi, Glassdoor, LinkedIn Salary, Payscale
- Format: "I'm targeting $X-Y based on my research for this level in [location/industry]. 
  I'm primarily focused on finding the right fit — is that in the range for this role?"

#### Role-Specific Question Banks

**Product Manager interviews:**
- "How would you prioritize our roadmap?"
- "Tell me about a product you shipped and the metrics it moved"
- "How would you improve [Company's product]?"
- "Describe how you work with engineering"
- Prep: Have a 3-minute product critique of their product ready

**Marketing / Demand Gen:**
- "Walk me through a campaign you built from scratch"
- "How do you think about attribution?"
- "What channels have you found most effective for [their customer type]?"
- "If you joined and owned pipeline, what would you change in 90 days?"

**Sales:**
- "Walk me through your sales process"
- "Tell me about the biggest deal you closed — how did you do it?"
- "How do you handle objections?"
- "Role play: sell me [their product]"
- Prep: Know their product, ICP, and competitive positioning cold

**Engineering:**
- System design questions (for senior roles)
- "Tell me about the most complex technical challenge you've solved"
- "How do you approach code review?"
- "Walk me through a time you had to balance speed vs quality"
- Prep: LeetCode for pure coding roles; system design study for senior

**Operations / Consulting:**
- Case study interviews — structured problem solving
- "How would you improve this process?"
- "Walk me through how you'd diagnose [problem]"
- Prep: Case interview frameworks (MECE, issue trees)

### Phase 4: Mock Interview

Conduct a practice interview session:

1. Tell the user: "I'm going to ask you real interview questions. Answer out loud (or in writing). After each answer, I'll give you specific feedback."

2. Ask questions appropriate to their stage and role — mix of:
   - 3 behavioral questions from the story bank areas
   - 1 role-specific technical/functional question
   - "Do you have any questions for me?" (always prep this)

3. After each answer, give feedback on:
   - Structure (did they use STAR? Was it clear?)
   - Specificity (did they quantify? Be concrete?)
   - Length (too long, too short, rambling?)
   - Tone (confident, not arrogant?)
   - Missed opportunities (what should they have added?)

---

### Phase 5: Questions to Ask Them

"Do you have any questions for us?" is NOT a formality. It's your final chance to stand out.

**Questions that impress:**
- "What does success look like for this role in the first 90 days?"
- "What's the hardest part of this role that you haven't mentioned yet?"
- "How did the last person in this role do well / where did they struggle?"
- "What's the biggest challenge the [team] is facing right now?"
- "[Reference specific company news] — how is the team thinking about that?"
- "What do you personally love about working here that outsiders don't know?"

**Questions that hurt you:**
- "What does the company do?" (shows zero research)
- "What are the benefits?" (save for offer stage)
- "How many vacation days?" (save for offer stage)
- Nothing (shows no curiosity)

Always have 5 questions ready — they may answer some during the interview, so you need backups.

## Post-Interview Protocol

**Within 24 hours:**
- Send thank-you email to each interviewer (get names during the interview if needed)
- Keep it brief: 3-4 sentences, reference something specific from YOUR conversation with them, restate interest

**Thank-you template:**
```
Subject: Thank you — [Role Title] interview

Hi [Name],

Thank you for the time today. I really enjoyed [specific thing from the 
conversation — a challenge they mentioned, an insight you shared, etc.].

It reinforced my excitement about [specific aspect of the role/team/mission]. 
I'd love to be part of what you're building.

Please let me know if there's anything else I can provide.

[Name]
```

**If they ask for references:**
Contact your references immediately before giving their names. Brief them on:
- The company and role
- What you'd like them to emphasize
- Any specific questions they might get

## Interview Day Checklist

```
BEFORE (night before):
  - Company brief reviewed (company-researcher output)
  - Story bank reviewed — not memorized, internalized
  - 5 questions for them ready
  - Outfit ready, tech tested (for video)
  - Logistics confirmed (address, parking, Zoom link)

DAY OF (1 hour before):
  - Review job description one more time
  - Review hiring manager's LinkedIn
  - Arrive/log in 5-10 min early
  - Water nearby, notes allowed in video interviews

DURING:
  - Pause before answering — it's okay to think
  - Ask for clarification if question is unclear
  - Take notes on their answers (shows engagement)
  - Match their energy level and formality

AFTER (within 1 hour):
  - Write down every question you were asked
  - Note what you'd answer differently
  - Send thank-you notes
  - Update pipeline tracker
```
