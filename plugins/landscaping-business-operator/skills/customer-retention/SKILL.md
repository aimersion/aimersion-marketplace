# Customer Retention

Build systematic retention programs that reduce churn and generate referrals.

## Retention Touchpoints

### Contract Renewal (60-90 days before expiration)
1. **Early outreach** (90 days) — "We are planning next season and want to make sure you are on the schedule"
2. **Formal renewal** (60 days) — Renewal letter with pricing, any changes, loyalty benefits
3. **Follow-up** (30 days) — Personal call or text if no response
4. **Final notice** (15 days) — "Last chance to secure your spot" with urgency

### Pricing Strategy for Renewals
- **Loyal customer discount** — 5-10% off for multi-year customers
- **Early-bird pricing** — lower rate for renewing before deadline
- **Price increase communication** — if raising prices, explain why (fuel, labor, materials) and what additional value they receive
- **Bundle incentive** — add a service for free or discounted when renewing (one-time aeration, mulch application)

### Satisfaction Surveys
Send after:
- First month of service (early issue detection)
- Mid-season (July/August)
- End of season (before renewal push)

**Survey Questions (keep to 5 or fewer):**
1. How satisfied are you with the quality of our work? (1-5)
2. How would you rate our communication and reliability? (1-5)
3. Is there anything we could do better?
4. Would you recommend us to a neighbor or friend? (1-5)
5. Any additional services you would be interested in?

### At-Risk Customer Identification
Watch for these signals:
- Calls to complain (even minor issues)
- Requests to skip visits or reduce service
- No response to renewal outreach
- Negative survey responses
- Payment delays (new pattern)

**Intervention for at-risk customers:**
- Personal call from owner/manager (not a technician)
- On-site visit to assess and address concerns
- Offer a service recovery gesture (free enhancement, upgraded service for a month)
- Document and fix the root cause

## Referral Program

### Structure
- **Reward**: $50-100 credit or cash per referred customer who signs a contract
- **Timing**: Pay when the referral signs, not when they are first referred
- **Tracking**: Unique referral codes or simple "who referred you" tracking
- **Communication**: Mention the program at every touchpoint (invoices, emails, on-site visits)

### Referral Request Timing
Best times to ask for referrals:
- After completing a visible improvement (new install, seasonal color)
- After receiving positive feedback or compliment
- After a positive survey response
- With the renewal package
- Never after a complaint or service issue

## Win-Back Campaign
For customers who did not renew:
1. **30 days after lapse** — "We miss you" message, ask for feedback on why they left
2. **Spring (if lapsed in fall)** — "Ready for spring? We saved your profile"
3. **Special offer** — one-time discount or added service to come back
4. **No more than 3 attempts** — respect their decision

## Output Formats
- **Renewal letter/email** — professional with pricing and loyalty benefits
- **Satisfaction survey** — formatted for email or print
- **Referral program materials** — cards, email templates, tracking sheet
- **Win-back campaign** — 3-touch email/text sequence
- **Retention dashboard** — customer status tracking by renewal date
