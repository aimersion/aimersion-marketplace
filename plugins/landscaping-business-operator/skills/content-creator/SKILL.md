# Content Creator

Create engaging social media and marketing content that showcases landscaping work and attracts new customers.

## Content Pillars

### 1. Before/After Transformations
The most engaging content type for landscaping businesses.

**Format:**
- Side-by-side or slider comparison photos
- Brief description of the project scope
- Timeframe ("Completed in 2 days")
- Call to action ("Ready for your transformation? Free estimate in bio")

**Caption Framework:**
"[Attention-grabbing opener about the transformation] + [Brief project description] + [Result/benefit to homeowner] + [Call to action]"

**Post types:**
- Weekly lawn transformation
- Landscape installation reveal
- Seasonal cleanup before/after
- Hardscape project completion
- Overgrown-to-manicured dramatic reveals

### 2. Seasonal Tips (Educational Content)
Position the company as the local expert.

**Spring Tips:**
- When to start mowing (and at what height)
- Pre-emergent timing for weed prevention
- How to assess winter damage
- Preparing irrigation systems

**Summer Tips:**
- Proper mowing height during heat
- Watering best practices (time of day, frequency, duration)
- How to identify common lawn diseases
- Heat stress vs. drought stress

**Fall Tips:**
- Why fall fertilization matters
- Aeration and overseeding timing
- Leaf management strategies
- Winterizing irrigation systems

**Winter Tips:**
- Ice melt and salt damage prevention
- Planning spring projects during winter
- Equipment maintenance
- Cold-weather plant protection

### 3. Project Showcases
In-depth posts about significant projects.

**Content to include:**
- Project challenge and client goals
- Design approach and material choices
- Construction/installation process (progress photos)
- Finished result with professional photos
- Client testimonial (with permission)

### 4. Behind-the-Scenes
Build trust and humanize the brand.

- Crew at work (action shots)
- Equipment and truck fleet
- Team introductions and milestones
- Training and skill development
- Community involvement and events

### 5. Community Engagement
- Local event sponsorship highlights
- Neighborhood beautification projects
- Seasonal yard-of-the-month features
- Local garden center partnerships

## Platform-Specific Guidelines

### Facebook
- Longer captions OK (3-5 sentences)
- Before/after albums perform well
- Local community groups for engagement
- Reviews and recommendations leverage
- Facebook Marketplace for seasonal services

### Instagram
- Visual-first — high-quality photos essential
- Reels for transformation videos (15-60 seconds)
- Stories for daily behind-the-scenes
- Hashtags: mix of broad (#landscaping) and local (#DallasLandscaping)
- Carousel posts for project showcases

### NextDoor
- Neighborhood-specific recommendations
- Seasonal service announcements
- Respond to "looking for a landscaper" posts
- Share tips specific to local conditions

### Google Business Profile
- Regular posts with photos keep profile active
- Seasonal offers and promotions
- Before/after photos in the photo gallery
- Respond to every review (positive and negative)

## Content Calendar Framework

Post 3-5 times per week across platforms:
- **Monday**: Motivation/behind-the-scenes (crew, equipment, shop)
- **Tuesday**: Tip Tuesday (seasonal educational content)
- **Wednesday**: Work-in-progress or project showcase
- **Thursday**: Throwback/before-after transformation
- **Friday**: Fun/community content or weekend tip

## Output Formats
- **Social media posts** — platform-specific with hashtags and CTAs
- **Content calendar** — weekly or monthly posting plan
- **Project showcase** — detailed post series for a completed project
- **Seasonal tip series** — 4-8 tips for the current season
- **Blog post** — long-form SEO content for website
