# Battlecard Builder

Create battlecards that sales reps actually use. The #1 reason battlecards fail is they're built for product marketers, not for reps in the middle of a deal. Every element should be scannable in under 2 minutes and immediately actionable in a live conversation.

## Design Principles

1. **2-Minute Rule**: A rep should be able to scan the entire card and extract what they need in under 2 minutes between meetings. If it takes longer, it won't get used.
2. **Conversation-ready**: Every talking point should work as actual words a rep can say to a prospect. Not marketing copy — real conversation.
3. **Honest about strengths**: A battlecard that says "we're better at everything" destroys credibility. Acknowledge where the competitor is strong, then pivot to where you win.
4. **Evidence-backed**: Every claim needs a proof point — a customer quote, a metric, a case study reference. Unsupported claims get challenged by prospects.
5. **Governance metadata**: Include source dates and verification status. The moment a rep finds something outdated, they stop trusting the entire card.

## Battlecard Creation Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Context

Before building the card, understand:
- **Which competitor?** (one card per competitor — don't combine)
- **Deal stage focus?** Early/discovery, mid-deal/evaluation, or late-stage/negotiation?
- **Top 3-5 deals where this competitor appeared** — what happened? What did reps say?
- **Existing intel** — any win/loss data, customer feedback, or previous battlecards?

### Step 2: Research the Competitor

Use web search and connected tools to gather current intelligence:
- Current website messaging and positioning
- Product capabilities, pricing, and packaging
- Recent customer reviews (G2, Capterra, TrustRadius)
- Recent product announcements and roadmap signals
- Common praise and complaints from customers
- Their sales approach (self-serve, demo-required, etc.)

### Step 3: Build the Battlecard

Use the **FIA Framework** for every competitive insight:
- **Fact**: What is objectively true about the competitor?
- **Impact**: Why does this matter to the prospect?
- **Act**: What should the rep say or do with this information?

[Questions for the rep to ask the PROSPECT that expose the competitor's

     heavily. Let me show you [specific feature/capability]."

2. **"Have you asked [competitor] about [known limitation]?"**
   - Why this works: [explanation of the limitation]
   - Follow-up: [suggested talking point]

3. **"What does your team need in terms of [area of competitor weakness]?"**
   - Why this works: [explanation]
   - Follow-up: [suggested talking point]

## Battlecard Maintenance

Stale battlecards are worse than no battlecards — they erode trust. Include this guidance:

- **Review cadence**: Every 30-60 days, or immediately when a major competitor move happens
- **Staleness indicators**: If the "Recent Moves" section is empty or >90 days old, the card needs updating
- **Feedback loop**: Ask reps what worked and what didn't after competitive deals — feed this back into the card
- **Version control**: Date every update and note what changed

## Multiple Battlecard Variants

For mature CI programs, consider creating stage-specific variants:

- **Discovery Card** (1 page): Quick dismiss + trap questions. For early-stage conversations.
- **Evaluation Card** (2-3 pages): Full comparison + objection handling. For active competitive evaluations.
- **Negotiation Card** (1 page): Pricing intel + win stories + loss prevention. For late-stage deals.
