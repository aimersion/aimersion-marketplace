# Retargeting Planner

Design retargeting sequences that stay relevant, avoid fatigue, and guide prospects toward conversion.

## Why Most Retargeting Fails

Most retargeting shows the same ad to everyone who visited the site, regardless of what they did or when. The result: the same CPO as cold traffic, with wasted budget on uninterested visitors. Effective retargeting segments by behavior and time, then shows progressively stronger content as intent signals increase.

## Audience Segmentation

### By Behavior (highest impact segmentation)

```
Tier 1 — Highest intent (show direct response):
- Visited pricing page
- Started but abandoned demo/trial form
- Visited 2+ case study or customer pages
- Watched 75%+ of a product video

Tier 2 — High interest (show proof and consideration content):
- Visited 3+ pages in one session
- Read a blog post (scroll depth 80%+)
- Downloaded content or registered for webinar
- Returned to site more than once in 30 days

Tier 3 — Casual (show awareness/brand content):
- Visited homepage only
- Single blog post visit, short session
- Quick bounce — likely not ICP
```

### By Recency

```
0-3 days:   Show direct CTA (demo, trial, assessment)
4-14 days:  Show proof and social proof content
15-30 days: Show new insight or content they haven't seen
31-60 days: Show fresh angle — something changed (new content, offer)
60+ days:   Archive from active retargeting (stop spending)
```

## 30-Day Sequence Design

```
Days 1-3: Strike while warm
  Ad: Direct response — "Ready to see how it works? Book 20 min."
  Frequency: Up to 3x/day for Tier 1; 1x/day for Tier 2
  Creative: Strong CTA, specific outcome promise

Days 4-10: Build confidence
  Ad: Social proof — customer quote with specific result, review score
  Frequency: 1-2x/day
  Rotate: 3 creative variants minimum (avoid fatigue)

Days 11-20: Different angle
  Ad: New content piece — insight, framework, or guide they haven't seen
  Frequency: 1x/day
  Note: If they downloaded something, skip to next stage

Days 21-30: Final sequence
  Ad: Scarcity or urgency (if genuine) OR best-performing earlier ad
  Frequency: 1x/day

Day 31+: Archive
  Remove from active retargeting
  Add to monthly low-frequency brand campaign ($1-2 CPM, 1-2x/month)
  Reactivate only when they return to site
```

## Platform-Specific Setup

### LinkedIn Retargeting

```
Audiences to create:
1. All site visitors, last 30 days (exclude customers)
2. Pricing page visitors, last 14 days (highest intent — smaller, more aggressive)
3. Video viewers 75%+, last 30 days (self-qualified interest)
4. Lead gen form openers who didn't submit, last 30 days

Sequence:
  Tier 1: Pricing/demo page → InMail (personal, high-intent message)
  Tier 2: Content pages → Lead gen form (lower-friction offer)
  Tier 3: Homepage/blog → Sponsored content (awareness/brand)
```

### Google Display and RLSA

```
Display retargeting audiences:
- All visitors, 30 days
- High-intent pages, 14 days (separate campaign, higher bids)

RLSA (Remarketing Lists for Search Ads):
- Bid 50% higher for any branded searches from past visitors
- Bid 30% higher for category searches from pricing page visitors
- These are people who know you AND are actively searching — close them

Frequency caps:
- Display: Maximum 7x/week per person
- Above this threshold: rotate creative or pause
```

## Creative Rotation Schedule

```
Weeks 1-2: Launch with 3 variants (test angles)
Week 3: Refresh with winner + 1 new creative
Month 2+: Monthly creative refresh minimum

Fatigue signals (refresh immediately):
- CTR drops >30% from week 1 baseline
- Negative feedback (hide ad) rate increases
- Frequency >8 with declining CTR
```

## Measuring Retargeting Effectiveness

```
Primary metrics:
- View-through conversion rate (saw ad → converted later)
- Assisted conversion rate (retargeting in path of other conversions)
- CPL vs cold traffic CPL (retargeting should be 30-50% lower)

Segment performance analysis:
- Compare conversion rate by behavioral tier (pricing >> homepage)
- Compare conversion rate by recency (0-3 days >> 15-30 days)
- Use this data to shift budget toward highest-converting combinations
```
