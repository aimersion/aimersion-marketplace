# Ad Copy Engine

Write B2B ad copy that attracts the right buyers and converts them to pipeline.

## The B2B Ad Copy Principle

The goal of B2B ad copy is not mass appeal. It's to be instantly recognizable and compelling to your ICP while being completely ignorable to everyone else. Specificity is both the targeting mechanism and the conversion mechanism.

## LinkedIn Ad Copy

### Sponsored Content

**Intro text (150 chars — shown before "see more" — most important):**
```
Formula options:
- [Provocative pain statement] + [specific number]
- [Bold claim about their situation]
- [Counterintuitive insight about their category]

Strong examples:
"Most B2B sales teams are generating pipeline that will never close. Here's the pattern."
"Your reps are spending 65% of their time on accounts that won't buy this year."
"The reason AEs miss quota isn't effort. It's which accounts they're chasing."

Weak examples (avoid):
"Introducing [Product] — the leading platform for [category]"
"Learn how [Company] can help you achieve [vague outcome]"
```

**Body (up to 600 chars):**
```
P1: Expand the pain — 2-3 sentences that make ICP feel understood
P2: The insight — what most people miss about this problem
P3: The payoff — what changes when this is solved
CTA: Single, specific action
```

**Headline (70 chars):**
```
Strong: specific outcome, ICP pain named, or asset title
"How 200-person SaaS teams cut ramp time by 40%"
"The Sales Efficiency Framework for B2B VPs"
"Download: Q2 Pipeline Health Report"

Weak: vague, feature-led, or generic
"Improve Your Sales Process Today"
"Learn About Our Platform"
```

### LinkedIn Lead Gen Form Ads

```
Offer headline: [Specific asset — not "Free Guide" but the actual title]
Offer description: "In [X pages/minutes], you'll understand [specific outcome]. Used by [social proof stat]."
Form intro: "Get instant access to [asset name]"
Thank you message: "Check your email — [specific next step explained]"
```

### LinkedIn Message Ads (InMail)

```
Subject (50 chars): Role-specific, not sales-y
Good: "Your Q3 pipeline — a thought"
Good: "Quick question about [their function]"
Bad: "Are you interested in [product]?"

Body structure:
- Line 1: Reference something specific (their content, company news, mutual connection)
- Lines 2-3: The pain they likely have — validate it, don't pitch
- Lines 4-5: Who you've helped and the specific outcome
- Close: Specific, low-commitment ask
```

## Google Search Ad Copy

### Headline Formulas (30 chars each)

**Category keywords ("B2B sales software"):**
```
H1: [Category you own] — "AI Sales Intelligence"
H2: [Key differentiator] — "Cut Ramp Time by 40%"
H3: [Proof or CTA] — "Trusted by 500+ Teams"
```

**Competitor keywords ("[competitor] alternative"):**
```
H1: "[Competitor] Alternative"
H2: "[Your key advantage over them]"
H3: "See Why Teams Switch"
```

**Pain keywords ("reduce sales ramp time"):**
```
H1: "Cut Sales Ramp By 40%"
H2: "AI-Powered Rep Readiness"
H3: "Free Trial — See Results Fast"
```

### Description Lines (90 chars each)
```
D1: [Pain + solution — specific numbers preferred]
"Stop losing 6 months to ramp lag. Our platform gets new AEs to quota in 60 days, not 180."

D2: [Social proof + CTA]
"500+ B2B teams use [product] to build predictable pipeline. Book a 20-minute demo today."
```

## A/B Testing Framework

Test one variable at a time, in priority order:
1. **Hook/angle** — pain-led vs outcome-led vs insight-led
2. **Offer** — guide vs webinar vs assessment vs demo
3. **Headline** — different ICP pain points for same audience
4. **CTA** — "Book demo" vs "See how it works" vs "Get the guide"

**Test duration:** Minimum 2 weeks, minimum 200 impressions per variant
**Winning criteria:** CTR AND conversion rate — high CTR with low CVR means attracting wrong audience

## Quality Check

Before any ad goes live:
- [ ] First line would stop an ICP from scrolling past?
- [ ] Specific enough that non-ICP won't click (this saves budget)?
- [ ] Zero corporate jargon or feature-speak?
- [ ] Single CTA only?
- [ ] Numbers are specific, not vague ("40%" not "significant improvement")?
- [ ] Message matches what's on the landing page?
