# Apprentice Trainer

Structure apprentice training programs with progressive skill building and NEC code education.

## Apprenticeship Year Structure

### Year 1 — Fundamentals
**On-the-job focus:**
- Tool identification and proper use
- Job site safety and PPE
- Material identification (wire types, boxes, devices, conduit)
- Basic hand bending of EMT (90s, offsets, back-to-back)
- Wire pulling and cable routing
- Box and bracket installation
- Basic device installation (outlets, switches)
- Reading blueprints (basics)

**Code focus:**
- NEC Article 90 — Introduction
- NEC Article 100 — Definitions
- NEC Article 110 — Requirements for electrical installations
- Basic safety rules (working clearances, LOTO concepts)

### Year 2 — Residential Proficiency
**On-the-job focus:**
- Complete residential rough-in (layout, drill, pull, secure)
- Device and fixture trim-out
- Panel terminations (under supervision)
- GFCI and AFCI installation and testing
- Basic troubleshooting with a meter
- Conduit bending (intermediate — kick 90s, saddles)

**Code focus:**
- NEC Article 210 — Branch circuits
- NEC Article 220 — Load calculations (basic)
- NEC Article 250 — Grounding and bonding (fundamentals)
- NEC Article 334 — NM cable

### Year 3 — Commercial Introduction
**On-the-job focus:**
- Commercial conduit installation (EMT, rigid, PVC)
- MC cable installation
- Wire pulling in conduit systems
- Commercial device and fixture installation
- Fire alarm device installation (under supervision)
- Motor connections (basic)
- 3-phase concepts

**Code focus:**
- NEC Article 300 — General requirements for wiring methods
- NEC Article 310 — Conductors (ampacity tables)
- NEC Article 358-392 — Conduit and raceway articles
- NEC Article 430 — Motors (introduction)

### Year 4 — Journey-Level Preparation
**On-the-job focus:**
- Independent residential work (minimal supervision)
- Service installations and upgrades
- Sub-panel installation and load balancing
- Generator and transfer switch installation
- Troubleshooting complex circuits
- Job layout from blueprints
- Material ordering and takeoffs
- Supervising helpers

**Code focus:**
- NEC Article 220 — Load calculations (complete)
- NEC Article 230 — Services
- NEC Article 240 — Overcurrent protection
- NEC Article 250 — Grounding (complete)
- Journeyman exam preparation

## Training Task Assignment

When assigning tasks, specify:
- **Task description** — what to do, step by step
- **Required tools and materials**
- **NEC references** — applicable code sections
- **Safety considerations** — specific hazards for this task
- **Quality standards** — what a passing result looks like
- **Time expectation** — reasonable completion time
- **Supervision level** — direct, general, or independent
