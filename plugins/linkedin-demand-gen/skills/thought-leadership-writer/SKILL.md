# Thought Leadership Writer

Write LinkedIn posts that build genuine authority and attract ICP attention before they're ready to buy.

## What Thought Leadership Actually Is

Thought leadership is not bragging, sharing wins, or writing announcements. It's consistently articulating a point of view on how the world works in your domain — and being right often enough that your ICP trusts your judgment before they ever need your product.

Best thought leadership makes the reader think: "This person really gets what I'm dealing with."

## Voice Framework

Before writing, establish the person's voice profile:
```
Tone: [Analytical / Provocative / Empathetic / Mentor / Challenger]
Style: [Data-driven / Story-led / Framework-builder / Contrarian]
Perspective: [Practitioner / Executive / Founder / Analyst]
Audience: [Who reads this — their titles, industries, pain points]
Topics they own: [3-5 areas of genuine expertise]
What feels inauthentic: [Topics or positions they wouldn't actually say]
```

## Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: Category Point of View
"Here's how I see this space differently from everyone else"
- Challenges conventional wisdom in the category
- Proposes a better framework or way of thinking
- Positions them as thinking ahead of the market

### Pillar 2: Behind-the-Scenes Reality
"Here's what actually happens inside companies like yours"
- Insider perspective non-practitioners don't have
- Makes the reader feel like they're getting privileged access
- Builds trust through transparency and specificity

### Pillar 3: Lessons From Experience
"Here's what I learned the hard way about [topic]"
- Story-led, specific, personal
- Failure stories often outperform success stories (more relatable)
- Only works if it's actually true — authenticity is the mechanism

### Pillar 4: Unpopular Opinion
"The [commonly accepted thing] is wrong"
- High engagement because it provokes reaction
- Must have a real argument, not just a contrarian position for its own sake
- Use once every 5-7 posts — not every post can be controversial

### Pillar 5: Pattern Recognition
"Here's what I'm noticing across the [X] companies I talk to"
- Positions them as having valuable market-level pattern recognition
- Data-forward without requiring formal research
- Highly relevant when the observation matches ICP pain

## Post Formats

### The Observation Post
```
[Specific observation from real experience — not generic]

What I keep seeing:
[The pattern or data point]

Why this matters more than people think:
[The implication for their situation]

What the best [role] do about it:
[Specific actionable insight]

[Optional: question to invite engagement]
```

### The Failure/Lesson Post
```
[Opening: specific mistake or false belief they held]

For [time period], I believed [wrong thing].

Then [specific moment/event that changed it].

What I actually learned: [the real insight]

What I'd tell myself earlier: [practical lesson in one sentence]
```

### The Contrarian Take
```
[State the conventional wisdom clearly]

Most [role] believe this. I think it's wrong.

Here's why:
1. [Counter-argument with specific evidence]
2. [Counter-argument with specific evidence]
3. [Counter-argument with specific evidence]

A better approach: [their actual position]
```

### The Pattern Post
```
I talk to [X] [type of person] every [week/month].

Almost all of them tell me: [common pain or situation]

The pattern I keep seeing: [insight about why this happens]

What the best ones do differently:
- [Behavior 1]
- [Behavior 2]
- [Behavior 3]

The real difference isn't [what most people think]. It's [actual differentiator].
```

## Formatting Rules

- Post 3-5x per week minimum for algorithm traction
- Engage with every comment within the first hour — this dramatically increases reach
- Vary between text posts, carousel posts, and occasional polls
- Never post company news as thought leadership — it destroys reach and credibility
- Reposts with added commentary build relationships while filling the calendar
