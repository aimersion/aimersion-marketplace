# ICP Engagement

Build systematic familiarity with target accounts through strategic LinkedIn engagement — before any sales conversation starts.

## The Psychology Behind This

People buy from people they know, like, and trust. In B2B, "knowing" someone means they've seen your name and found your perspective valuable at least 3-7 times before engaging back. Systematic engagement creates that familiarity at scale without cold outreach feeling cold.

## Target Identification

### Engagement Tier Structure

**Tier 1 — Direct ICP (engage daily):**
- Decision-makers at Tier A target accounts
- Champions/influencers your buyers follow
- Active creators who post about ICP-relevant topics

**Tier 2 — ICP Adjacent (3x/week):**
- Decision-makers at Tier B accounts
- Practitioners who influence your buyers

**Tier 3 — Ecosystem (1x/week):**
- Industry hashtag conversations
- Relevant publication posts
- High-reach content in your category

### Finding Engagement Targets

```
Search criteria on LinkedIn:
- Job title: [ICP buyer titles]
- Company size: [ICP firmographic range]
- Posted in last 30 days (active creators convert better)

Additional signals to prioritize:
- Following your competitors
- Engaging with category content
- Recently promoted or changed roles (high receptivity period)
- Asking questions publicly that your product answers
```

## Comment Writing: The VCAT Framework

Every comment should be one of:
- **V**alidate — Affirm the insight and add supporting context
- **C**hallenge — Respectfully push back with a different perspective
- **A**dd — Extend their point with something they didn't mention
- **T**ransfer — Connect their point to your experience or a different context

### Strong vs Weak Comments

**Strong (use this):**
```
Reference their specific point + your experience/data that extends it +
optional question that invites them to respond back

Example: "The 6-month ramp figure tracks with what we see across similar-sized 
teams. The part that surprises most people is how much of that delay is onboarding 
structure vs. rep quality. What's been the biggest lever for you when you've 
shortened that cycle?"
```

**Weak (never use):**
- "Great post!"
- "So true!"
- "Love this perspective!"
- "Couldn't agree more."
- Anything a bot could generate without reading the post

### Comment Rules
- Never mention your company or product in engagement comments
- Always be specific to what they actually wrote
- Add genuine insight — not just agreement
- Ask questions that invite them to respond
- 2-4 sentences — substantive without being a wall of text

## Weekly Engagement Schedule

```
DAILY (20-30 min):
- Comment on 5-7 Tier 1 posts
- Reply to anyone who replied to your comments
- Note posts that mention pain your product solves (potential outreach trigger)

3X PER WEEK:
- Comment on 3-4 Tier 2 posts
- Engage in 1-2 relevant hashtag or industry discussions

WEEKLY REVIEW (15 min):
- Who engaged with your comments? (warm outreach candidates)
- Update target list (remove inactive, add newly active creators)
- Track: conversations started from commenting
```

## Engagement-to-Outreach Bridge

When a target has:
- Engaged with your comments 2+ times, OR
- You've commented on their content 3+ times across different posts

They're warm enough for a connection request:

```
Connection request (300 char limit):
"[Name], I've been following your posts on [topic]. Your take on 
[specific thing they wrote] was sharp — wanted to connect and 
keep the conversation going."
```

Never pitch in the connection request. The goal is the connection.

## Measuring Results

Track weekly:
- Comments posted
- Reply rate (engagement back)
- Connection requests sent (from warm targets)
- Acceptance rate
- Conversations started
- Accounts that moved from engagement to outreach
