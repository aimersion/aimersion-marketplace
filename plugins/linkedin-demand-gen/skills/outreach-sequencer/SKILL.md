# Outreach Sequencer

Build behavior-triggered, personalized LinkedIn outreach that books meetings without feeling like spam.

## Why Most LinkedIn Outreach Fails

1. Sent to cold accounts who've never seen the sender before
2. Immediately pitching a product in message 1
3. Generic — could be sent to anyone
4. About the sender, not the recipient

Effective outreach is: triggered by behavior, personalized to context, focused on opening a conversation rather than closing a deal.

## Trigger-Based Sequences

### Trigger 1: Content Engagement (Warmest)
They liked, commented on, or shared your content.

```
Connection request:
"[Name], you engaged with my post on [topic] — glad it landed. 
Your work in [their space] is interesting. Would love to connect."

Message 2 (after accepted, 2-3 days):
"Thanks for connecting. Your comment on [specific thing] 
caught my attention — especially [the specific point they made].

[Your genuine reaction/extension of their point — 1-2 sentences]

Are you actively working on [related challenge] right now?"

Message 3 (if reply, or 5 days after M2):
[Based on their reply, or:]
"For [their company type], [pain-point question specific to context]. 
We've been helping [similar companies] with [specific outcome]. 
Worth a quick call to see if there's a fit?"
```

### Trigger 2: Job Change
New leaders have 90-day windows of budget and mandate.

```
Message 1:
"[Name], congrats on the new role at [company]. Starting as [title] 
is a significant moment — especially with [market condition/challenge 
relevant to their function].

What's the top thing you're focused on solving in the first 90 days?"

Message 2 (no reply, 5 days):
"Quick follow-up — most [title] we talk to right now are navigating 
[ICP pain]. Is that on your radar at [company]?"
```

### Trigger 3: Funding Announcement
Company raised funding — building team, spending budget.

```
Message 1:
"[Name], saw [company] raised [round] — congrats. That kind of 
growth usually means [relevant function/pain] becomes a real 
priority fast.

How are you thinking about [relevant area] as you scale?"
```

### Trigger 4: Cold Outreach (ICP fit only)
Use sparingly. Must be highly personalized.

```
Connection request:
"[Name], I work with [title/company type] on [specific outcome]. 
Noticed [specific thing about their company/profile]. 
Would love to connect."

Message 2 (5 days after connection):
"Thanks for connecting. Noticed [specific company signal — 
recent hire, product launch, news]. 

[Connecting their signal to pain your solution addresses — 1 sentence]

[Question: is this relevant to them right now?]"

Message 3 (7 days, final):
"[Name] — last message on this.

[One-line what you do and for who]

If [pain you solve] ever becomes a priority, happy to share 
what's been working for [similar companies]. No pressure either way."
```

## Sequence Rules

1. Never pitch in message 1 — ever. Connection message is not a pitch.
2. Maximum 3 messages per cold sequence, then stop completely.
3. Always personalize to something real — their post, company news, their role change.
4. One question per message — don't interrogate.
5. Under 150 words per message — brevity signals respect.
6. If they say no: "Understood — I'll leave you alone. Best of luck with [their stated priority]."

## Message Quality Standards

**Before sending any message, check:**
- Could this message only be sent to this specific person? If it could go to 1,000 people unchanged, rewrite it.
- Does it ask for something reasonable given the relationship stage?
- Does it put their interest ahead of your need to book a meeting?
- Is there a clear, single question they can answer in one sentence?

## Tracking

Weekly metrics:
- Connection acceptance rate (target: 30-40%)
- Reply rate on message 2 (target: 15-25%)
- Meeting booked rate (target: 5-10% of conversations)
- Which triggers convert best (adjust focus accordingly)
