# Sales Trigger Monitor

Find the moments when buying probability spikes — and act before competitors do.

## Why Timing Beats Targeting

The right message to the right person at the wrong time gets ignored. The mediocre message at the exact right moment books a meeting. Trigger monitoring is about systematically finding those moments.

## Signal Tiers

### Tier 1: Act Within 24-48 Hours

**Leadership change:**
- New C-suite, VP, or director hire at a target account
- Why it matters: New leaders have budget, mandate, and urgency in their first 90 days

**Funding announcement:**
- Series A-D or meaningful revenue milestone
- Why it matters: Funding means approved headcount and technology budgets

**Rapid hiring in target function:**
- 15%+ headcount growth in a relevant department in 90 days
- Job postings for roles that use your product
- Why it matters: Scaling means they're actively buying tools to support growth

**Active technology evaluation signal:**
- Job postings explicitly mentioning your category or competitors
- "Currently evaluating" or "looking to replace" language in posts or job descriptions
- Why it matters: Active evaluation mode = they're ready to have conversations now

### Tier 2: Act Within 1 Week

**Engagement with your content:**
- Target account personnel liked, commented on, or shared your posts
- Why it matters: Self-identified interest in your category

**ICP pain stated publicly:**
- Someone posts about the exact problem you solve with no clear solution
- Why it matters: Pain acknowledged + no solution = open door

**Company milestone:**
- New product launch, market expansion, acquisition announcement
- Why it matters: Growth events create new technology and operational needs

### Tier 3: Add to Nurture (revisit in 30 days)

- Profile view from a target account
- Following your company page
- Connection acceptance from cold outreach
- Engagement with competitor content or company pages

## Monitoring System

### Daily (15 minutes)

```
LinkedIn:
1. Check Sales Navigator alerts for target account changes
2. Review who engaged with your last 3 posts
3. Scan connections' activity for pain signals

Google Alerts (set up these searches):
"[company name] raises" — for funding
"[company name] appoints" — for leadership changes
"[company name] expands" — for growth signals
"[company name] acquires" — for M&A signals
```

### Weekly Signal Log

Maintain a table:
| Date | Account | Contact | Signal Type | Tier | Action Taken | Response |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

### Signal-to-Action Matrix

| Signal | Response | Message Angle | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| New VP hired | Direct outreach | "New role" template | Within 48 hours |
| Funding raised | Direct outreach | "Funding" template | Within 24 hours |
| Engaged with your content | Comment reply + DM | Content engagement template | Same day |
| Hiring for your function | Outreach to hiring manager | "Building the team" template | Within 3 days |
| Pain stated publicly | Comment with insight + DM | Pain-acknowledgment template | Same day |
| Job change at existing contact | Warm congratulations | Check-in message | Within 48 hours |

## Priority Account Scoring

Combine signal strength with ICP fit:

```
High priority (act today):
- Tier A ICP account + Tier 1 signal

High priority (act this week):
- Tier A ICP + Tier 2 signal
- Tier B ICP + Tier 1 signal

Medium priority (add to nurture):
- Tier B ICP + Tier 2 signal
- Any ICP + Tier 3 signal

Low priority (ignore for now):
- Non-ICP + any signal
```

## Building Your Signal Library

Step 1: Define signal criteria for each ICP segment
Step 2: Set up monitoring (Sales Navigator, Google Alerts, manual LinkedIn checks)
Step 3: Build response templates for each signal type (see outreach-sequencer skill)
Step 4: Assign weekly monitoring ownership
Step 5: Track which signals actually convert to pipeline — focus there

## Monthly Signal Review

- Which signal types generated the most outreach?
- Which signal types had the highest reply rate?
- Which signal types produced the most pipeline?
- Adjust monitoring focus based on what's actually converting.
