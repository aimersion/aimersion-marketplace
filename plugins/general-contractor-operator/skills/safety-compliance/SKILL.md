# Safety Compliance

Generate OSHA-compliant construction safety documentation including checklists, toolbox talks, and incident reports.

## Core Safety Documents

### 1. Daily Safety Checklist
Generate site-specific daily checklists covering:

- **PPE verification** — hard hats, safety glasses, hi-vis vests, steel-toe boots, gloves
- **Fall protection** — guardrails, safety nets, personal fall arrest systems for work above 6 feet
- **Scaffold safety** — proper erection, planking, access, guardrails
- **Ladder safety** — condition, setup angle (4:1 ratio), securing
- **Electrical safety** — GFCI protection, cord condition, lockout/tagout
- **Excavation safety** — shoring, sloping, benching, competent person
- **Housekeeping** — clear walkways, debris removal, material storage
- **Fire prevention** — extinguisher locations, hot work permits
- **Tool condition** — guards in place, cord condition, proper use
- **First aid** — kit stocked and accessible, emergency numbers posted

### 2. Toolbox Talks (5-15 minute safety meetings)
Generate talk outlines for common construction hazards:

**High-Priority Topics:**
- Fall protection (OSHA 1926 Subpart M)
- Scaffold safety (OSHA 1926 Subpart L)
- Electrical safety and GFCI use (OSHA 1926 Subpart K)
- Excavation and trenching (OSHA 1926 Subpart P)
- Personal protective equipment
- Heat illness prevention
- Struck-by hazard awareness (Focus Four)
- Caught-in/between hazard awareness (Focus Four)

**Seasonal Topics:**
- Heat stress prevention (summer)
- Cold stress and hypothermia (winter)
- Lightning safety (storm season)
- Slip/trip hazards in wet/icy conditions

**Task-Specific Topics:**
- Concrete pour safety
- Crane and rigging safety
- Confined space entry
- Hot work and welding
- Silica dust exposure (OSHA Table 1)
- Lead paint and asbestos awareness

### Toolbox Talk Format
Each talk should include:
1. Topic and date
2. Applicable OSHA standard reference
3. Key hazards (3-5 bullet points)
4. Required controls and PPE
5. Real-world scenario or discussion question
6. Attendee sign-in section

### 3. Incident Reports
Document workplace incidents with:

- Date, time, and exact location on site
- Injured party information (name, trade, employer)
- Description of incident (what happened, sequence of events)
- Nature of injury/illness
- Witness statements
- Root cause analysis (immediate and contributing causes)
- Corrective actions taken immediately
- Preventive actions to avoid recurrence
- OSHA recordkeeping determination (recordable vs. first aid)
- Photos of incident scene

### 4. Job Hazard Analysis (JHA)
For each task, document:

| Step | Hazard | Control Measure |
|------|--------|----------------|
| [Task step] | [What could go wrong] | [How to prevent it] |

## OSHA Recordkeeping Requirements

- Maintain OSHA 300 Log for recordable injuries/illnesses
- Post OSHA 300A Summary February 1 - April 30 annually
- Retain records for 5 years
- Report fatalities within 8 hours
- Report hospitalizations, amputations, eye loss within 24 hours

## Key OSHA Standards for Construction (1926)

- **Subpart C** — General safety and health provisions
- **Subpart E** — Personal protective equipment
- **Subpart K** — Electrical
- **Subpart L** — Scaffolds
- **Subpart M** — Fall protection (6-foot trigger height)
- **Subpart N** — Cranes and derricks
- **Subpart O** — Motor vehicles and mechanized equipment
- **Subpart P** — Excavations
- **Subpart Q** — Concrete and masonry
- **Subpart X** — Stairways and ladders

## Output Formats

- **Daily checklist** — printable PDF or markdown
- **Toolbox talk** — single-page outline with sign-in sheet
- **Incident report** — structured form document
- **JHA** — table format per task
- **Site-specific safety plan** — comprehensive document for project start
