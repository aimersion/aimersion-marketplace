# Client Communicator

Draft professional, clear client communications for construction projects.

## Communication Types

### 1. Progress Updates
Weekly or milestone-based updates covering:

- **Work completed this period** — specific tasks and areas
- **Work in progress** — current activities and trades on site
- **Upcoming work** — next 1-2 weeks preview
- **Schedule status** — on track, ahead, or behind (with explanation)
- **Budget status** — approved change orders, pending changes
- **Issues or concerns** — anything requiring client awareness or decision
- **Photos** — remind user to attach progress photos

**Tone**: Professional, confident, transparent. Lead with accomplishments, address issues directly with proposed solutions.

### 2. Schedule Change Notifications
When the schedule shifts, communicate:

- What changed and why (weather, inspection delays, material delivery, scope change)
- Impact on completion date
- Mitigation steps being taken
- Revised milestone dates
- Whether client action is needed

### 3. Payment Requests / Draw Requests
Professional payment applications including:

- Pay period dates
- Work completed to date (by line item or phase)
- Percentage complete per line item
- Amount previously billed
- Amount this draw
- Retainage withheld
- Net amount due
- Lien waiver status

### 4. Decision Requests
When client input is needed:

- Clearly state what decision is needed
- Provide options with pros/cons and cost implications
- State the deadline for the decision (and why)
- Explain impact of delayed decision on schedule

### 5. Punch List Communication
At project closeout:

- List of items to be addressed
- Proposed timeline for completion
- Request for walkthrough scheduling
- Final payment and retainage release timeline

## Communication Best Practices

- **Be specific** — "Framing is 75% complete" beats "making good progress"
- **Lead with solutions** — when reporting problems, include the plan to resolve
- **Use plain language** — avoid construction jargon unless the client is savvy
- **Document everything** — every communication should be in writing
- **Timely responses** — acknowledge client messages within 24 hours
- **Photo documentation** — suggest including progress photos with updates

## Output Formats

- **Email draft** — ready to send via email tools
- **Formal letter** — letterhead-style document (.docx)
- **Draw request** — formatted payment application (.xlsx or .pdf)
- **Meeting minutes** — structured notes from owner/architect meetings
