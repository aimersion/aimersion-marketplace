# Court Filing Helper Skill

Court filings are the foundation of family law litigation. Well-drafted, procedurally compliant filings establish legal positions, advance client interests, and set tone for case. Poorly drafted filings may result in dismissal, waiver of claims, default judgments, or unfavorable outcomes.

This skill helps attorneys prepare comprehensive, compliant court filings across common family law situations.

## Understanding Jurisdictional Requirements

Family law is primarily state law. Each state has distinct procedural requirements, substantive law, and court practices.

**Critical First Step**: Identify applicable jurisdiction (state and county) and confirm:
- Correct court for filing (family court, district court, superior court varies by state)
- Venue requirements (typically where respondent resides or where parties married)
- Personal jurisdiction basis (typically where respondent resides or parties agreed)
- Residency requirements for divorce (typically 6 months in state; varies)
- Correct caption format and naming conventions
- Required forms (many states have mandatory forms)
- Filing fee requirements
- Service requirements (personal service, certified mail, publication)
- Court rules and local rules

**Jurisdiction Selection**:
When multiple states have connections to parties/children, strategic jurisdiction selection matters. Family court jurisdiction determined by:
- **Divorce**: Where defendant resides; can be where parties last lived together; can be where plaintiff resides if defendant consents
- **Custody**: Typically where child has lived for past 6 months (Uniform Child Custody Jurisdiction and Enforcement Act—UCCJEA)
- **Child Support**: Federal child support enforcement statutes apply

## Types of Family Law Filings

### Initial Pleadings

**Petition for Divorce/Dissolution**:
- Initiates divorce case
- States grounds for divorce (in fault states) or dissolution (no-fault states)
- Describes marriage date, separation date, children
- Identifies assets and debts (preliminary)
- States requested relief (custody, support, property division)
- Must comply with jurisdictional requirements

**Petition for Custody Modification**:
- Requests change to existing custody arrangement
- Must show substantial change in circumstances
- Details changed circumstances
- Proposes new custody arrangement
- Explains why change in child's best interest

**Petition for Paternity**:
- Establishes biological relationship between parent and child
- Required before child support or custody can be determined
- States facts supporting paternity
- May request DNA testing
- Requests custody and child support determination

**Petition for Domestic Violence Protective Order**:
- Requests court order protecting petitioner from abuse
- Describes acts of abuse and timeline
- Requests temporary protection pending hearing
- Requests final protective order prohibiting contact, requiring distance
- May request custody of children, possession of home, attorney fees

### Motions and Temporary Relief

**Motion for Temporary Orders** (also called "Motion for Temporary Relief"):
- Filed early in case seeking immediate relief pending final resolution
- Addresses most pressing issues (custody, support, asset possession)
- Used when party needs protection or support can't wait for trial

**Motion to Modify Custody**:
- Requests change to existing custody
- Accompanied by affidavits explaining changed circumstances
- Proposes new arrangement
- Requests temporary modification pending trial

**Motion for Temporary Child/Spousal Support**:
- Requests support pending final order
- Includes financial affidavit
- References support guidelines
- Proposes amount and duration

**Motion for Possession of Home**:
- Requests right to remain in/possess family home pending trial
- Important for housing stability
- Often tied to custody arrangement
- May address mortgage/property tax obligations

**Motion to Compel Discovery**:
- Requests court order requiring other party to comply with discovery
- Used when party fails to respond to interrogatories, produce documents, or submit to examination
- Includes certification that parties attempted to resolve dispute
- Requests sanctions if necessary

**Motion for Attorney Fees**:
- Requests other party pay attorney costs
- Statutory basis typically required
- Shows other party's ability to pay and requesting party's financial need
- Attached affidavit detailing attorney time and costs

**Motion to Stay Proceedings**:
- Requests court pause case
- Used when mediation ongoing, parties negotiating, or other grounds
- Must show good cause

### Responses and Answers

**Response to Petition for Divorce**:
- Answers allegations in petition
- Admits or denies each claim
- Asserts own claims (countersuit)
- States defenses if applicable
- Proposes own relief if seeking different outcome

**Response to Motion for Temporary Orders**:
- Opposes requested relief with argument
- Proposes alternative relief
- Includes affidavit supporting position
- Addresses legal standard for temporary orders

### Declarations and Affidavits

**Affidavit Supporting Motion**:
- Sworn statement supporting motion
- Includes factual information supporting legal argument
- Personal knowledge standard (not hearsay)
- Detailed and specific (not vague or conclusory)
- Sworn under oath

**Declaration Regarding Assets and Debts**:
- Under oath accounting of all assets and liabilities
- Often mandatory in family law cases
- Detailed inventory with values and characterization
- Declaration of accuracy and completeness

**Declaration Regarding Parenting**:
- Parent's statement regarding parenting capacity, relationship with child
- Describes daily involvement, special relationship, contribution to child's wellbeing
- Addresses custody factors
- Supports custody request

## Drafting Comprehensive Petitions

### Petition for Dissolution of Marriage Structure

**Caption** (Heading):
```
STATE OF [STATE]
COUNTY OF [COUNTY]
DISTRICT COURT

In the Matter of the Marriage of:

[PETITIONER'S FULL NAME],
                                    Petitioner,

v.                                   Case No.: [If known]

[RESPONDENT'S FULL NAME],
                                    Respondent.
_________________________________

                              PETITION FOR DISSOLUTION OF MARRIAGE
```

**Introductory Paragraphs**:

1. Petitioner [name] brings this Petition for Dissolution of Marriage against Respondent [name], and in support thereof, alleges as follows:

2. Jurisdiction and Venue:
   - Petitioner is a resident of [county, state] and has resided here for at least [6 months/as required by state law]
   - Petitioner was married to Respondent on [date] in [city, state]
   - The parties separated on [date]
   - Respondent is currently a resident of [county, state]
   - The parties have [number] child/children of the marriage
   - This Court has personal jurisdiction over both parties and subject matter jurisdiction to hear this action

3. Grounds for Dissolution:
   - [In fault states: State specific ground—infidelity, cruelty, abandonment, etc.]
   - [In no-fault states: "The marriage is irretrievably broken" or "The parties have irreconcilable differences"]

**Marriage and Separation Information**:

4. Marriage Information:
   - Date of Marriage: [Date]
   - Location of Marriage: [City, State]
   - Length of Marriage: [Years and months]
   - Reason for Separation: [Brief statement of cause if relevant]
   - Date of Separation: [Date when parties ceased cohabitation]

**Children Information** (if applicable):

5. Children of the Marriage:
   - Child #1: [Full name], born [date], age [X]
   - Child #2: [Full name], born [date], age [X]
   - [Continue for all children]
   - The children have resided with Petitioner since [date of separation/as detailed]
   - Child #1 attends [school/daycare]
   - All children are in good health with no serious health issues [unless otherwise true]

**Financial Information**:

6. Assets and Debts (Preliminary—subject to full disclosure):
   - The parties own a home located at [address], valued at approximately $[value] with mortgage balance of approximately $[balance]
   - The parties own [describe vehicles, business interests, retirement accounts, etc.]
   - The parties owe [credit cards, loans, etc.]
   - A detailed financial disclosure will be provided in compliance with court requirements

**Requested Relief**:

7. Petitioner respectfully requests the Court to:

   a) **Dissolution**: Dissolve the marriage between Petitioner and Respondent;

   b) **Custody of Children**: Award Petitioner primary custody of the children, with Respondent to have parenting time [specify as requested], in the best interests of the children, considering:
      - The children have primarily resided with Petitioner throughout the marriage and separation
      - Petitioner has been the primary caregiver
      - The children are well-adjusted to their residence and school
      - [Other relevant factors]

   c) **Child Support**: Order Respondent to pay child support in an amount at least equal to the guideline amount of $[amount] per month, plus [insurance, childcare, extraordinary expenses], commencing [date];

   d) **Spousal Support**: [If applicable]: Order Respondent to pay spousal support in an amount of $[amount] per month for a period of [duration], commencing [date];

   e) **Property Division**: Award Petitioner [describe property requested] and order Respondent to pay/transfer [other property], such that property is divided [fairly/equitably/50-50 as applicable to state];

   f) **Debt Allocation**: Assign each party responsibility for specific debts incurred during the marriage;

   g) **Attorney Fees**: Order Respondent to pay Petitioner's reasonable attorney fees, expert costs, and court costs;

   h) **Other Relief**: Such other and further relief as the Court deems just and proper.

**Signature Block**:

```
Respectfully submitted,

[Date]

_____________________________
[Attorney Name]
[Attorney License Number]
[Address]
[Phone]
[Email]

Attorney for Petitioner
```

## Motion for Temporary Orders Structure

### Motion for Temporary Custody and Child Support

**Caption**: [Same as petition above]

### Motion Heading:

MOTION FOR TEMPORARY ORDERS—CUSTODY AND CHILD SUPPORT

---

### Body:

Petitioner requests that this Court issue a temporary order awarding temporary custody to Petitioner and ordering Respondent to pay temporary child support, pending the parties' final hearing in this case.

In support of this Motion, Petitioner alleges:

**I. FACTUAL BACKGROUND**

1. The parties separated on [date], and Petitioner has had primary custody of the children since separation.

2. The children have maintained their residence, school, and community connections throughout this period.

3. Disrupting the current arrangement would be harmful to the children's stability and wellbeing.

4. The children require financial support for their basic needs.

5. Respondent's income is [detail income and source].

**II. LEGAL STANDARD**

A Court should award temporary custody based on the following factors:
- Current arrangement and stability
- Children's best interests
- Relationship with each parent
- No final determination of permanent custody required

Child support should be at least the guideline amount pending final determination.

**III. ARGUMENT**

A. **Temporary Custody is Appropriate**

The children should remain in their current placement with Petitioner because:

- Children have resided with Petitioner continuously since separation
- Children attend established school and participate in activities
- Disrupting placement would cause emotional harm
- Petitioner has been primary caregiver throughout marriage
- Changing placement would harm children's relationship with peers and community
- No evidence suggests Respondent should have primary custody

B. **Child Support is Appropriate**

Temporary child support should be ordered because:

- Guideline calculation: [Detail guideline calculation]
- Income: [Detail each parent's income]
- Child support amount: $[amount] monthly
- Respondent has ability to pay
- Children have basic needs requiring support
- Respondent shares responsibility for financial support

**IV. CONCLUSION**

Petitioner respectfully requests that the Court issue an order:

1. Awarding temporary primary custody to Petitioner, with Respondent to have parenting time [specify];

2. Requiring Respondent to pay temporary child support in the amount of $[amount] per month, commencing [date], plus [insurance/childcare/other add-ons];

3. Granting such other and further relief as the Court deems just and proper.

## Checklists for Family Law Filings

### Petition for Dissolution Checklist

Before filing petition for divorce/dissolution:

**Jurisdictional Requirements**:
- [ ] Confirm correct court (family court, district court, etc.)
- [ ] Verify state residency requirements met
- [ ] Confirm venue (defendant's residence or agreed venue)
- [ ] Verify personal jurisdiction basis
- [ ] Confirm state's no-fault vs. fault grounds
- [ ] Check state-specific requirements (waiting period, mandatory forms)

**Content Requirements**:
- [ ] Caption with correct court and parties
- [ ] Jurisdiction paragraph with facts supporting jurisdiction
- [ ] Grounds for divorce properly stated
- [ ] Marriage date and location
- [ ] Separation date if applicable
- [ ] All children identified with birthdates
- [ ] Brief statement of assets/debts
- [ ] Requested relief clearly stated
- [ ] Specific requests (custody, support, property division, attorney fees)
- [ ] Proper signature block with attorney information

**Procedural Requirements**:
- [ ] Correct number of copies (typically original + 2-3 copies)
- [ ] Filing fee calculated and prepared
- [ ] Proof of service prepared if concurrent service required
- [ ] Any mandatory forms completed
- [ ] Case information sheet/civil cover sheet completed
- [ ] Any local rule requirements verified

**Compliance Check**:
- [ ] Font size and type meet requirements
- [ ] Margins meet requirements
- [ ] Line numbering correct (usually on left side)
- [ ] Caption format correct
- [ ] Signature block correctly formatted
- [ ] All pages numbered
- [ ] Certificate of service included (if mailed)

---

### Motion for Temporary Orders Checklist

**Content Elements**:
- [ ] Clear identification of relief sought (custody, support, possession)
- [ ] Factual basis for each request
- [ ] Legal standard for temporary orders cited
- [ ] Explanation of why requirements met
- [ ] Specific relief requested (with amounts and dates)
- [ ] Urgency and need for temporary order
- [ ] Proposed temporary arrangement details

**Supporting Documents**:
- [ ] Affidavit of Petitioner with specific facts
- [ ] Financial affidavit showing income and expenses
- [ ] Proposed parenting plan or custody schedule
- [ ] Child support calculation (if requesting support)
- [ ] Any evidence of abuse, safety concerns, or other justification

**Procedural Preparation**:
- [ ] Certificate of service (proof that respondent was served)
- [ ] Proposed order prepared
- [ ] Notice of hearing prepared (if required)
- [ ] Any local form requirements completed
- [ ] Court calendar checked for available hearing dates

### Court Order Structure

Courts expect orders in specific format. Typical structure:

**ORDER**

[STATE OF [STATE]
COUNTY OF [COUNTY]
DISTRICT COURT

In the Matter of the Marriage of:

[PETITIONER'S NAME],

Petitioner,

v.

[RESPONDENT'S NAME],

Respondent.]

**HAVING CONSIDERED** the [motion/petition/evidence] and having heard [argument/nothing in opposition], the Court hereby enters the following ORDER:

**NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED:**

1. [Specific directive—"Petitioner shall have temporary primary custody of the minor children..."]

2. [Specific directive—"Respondent shall pay temporary child support in the amount of $X per month, commencing [date]..."]

3. [Any conditions or modifications]

4. [Any deadlines for compliance]

**FURTHER ORDERED** that:

[Any additional provisions]

**This Order shall be effective** [date] and remain in effect [until modified/until final judgment/unless modified].

[Signature of Judge]

[Date]

[Judge Name and Title]

## Key Elements for Effective Family Law Filings

### Clarity and Specificity

- Use plain language
- Avoid legalese when possible
- Be specific (not vague or conclusory)
- Include dates, amounts, and details
- State facts, not conclusions ("Respondent violated the order by failing to pay support on March 1" not "Respondent is irresponsible")

### Logical Organization

- Organize by topic (jurisdiction, children, assets, requested relief)
- Use headings for clarity
- Progress logically through issues
- Separate different claims/requests

### Complete Information

- Include all necessary facts
- Don't omit material information
- Anticipate counter-arguments
- Address unfavorable facts proactively

### Proper Legal Citations

- Cite applicable statutes
- Cite case law if relevant
- Explain how law applies to facts
- Provide statutory references for support calculations

### Accuracy and Consistency

- Ensure names spelled consistently
- Verify dates and numbers
- Consistent terminology throughout
- Proofread carefully for errors

### Professional Presentation

- Proper formatting
- Clean appearance
- No handwritten annotations
- Professional font
- Proper margins and spacing

## Filing and Service Procedures

### Electronic Filing

- Many courts now require electronic filing
- Verify filing procedures and requirements
- Create online account if required
- Upload documents correctly
- Verify filing confirmation
- Maintain receipt documentation

### Paper Filing

- Verify number of copies required
- Bring original plus copies
- Bring filing fee (cash, check, credit card—verify)
- Complete any required cover sheets
- Ask for file-stamped copies

### Service Requirements

- Service on opposing party required (rules vary)
- Methods: personal service, certified mail, service of attorney
- Proof of service required
- Document proof in case file

## Common Drafting Pitfalls to Avoid

**Overstatement**: Don't exaggerate. Judges notice and credibility suffers.

**Ambiguity**: Be clear about what you're requesting. Vague language invites disputes.

**Inconsistency**: Be consistent in facts, dates, terminology. Inconsistency suggests carelessness or dishonesty.

**Emotionalism**: Focus on facts and law. Emotional language undermines credibility.

**Conclusory Statements**: "Respondent is unfit" is not enough. Detail facts: "Respondent admitted to using methamphetamine during parenting time..."

**Missing Details**: Include specific amounts, dates, locations. "Support" is vague; "$1,500 monthly beginning June 1" is clear.

**Poor Organization**: Confusing organization frustrates judges. Use clear structure and headings.

**Legal Errors**: Wrong citations, wrong legal standard, procedurally improper requests—all harm your case.
