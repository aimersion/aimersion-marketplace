# Seasonal Promoter

Plan and execute time-bound promotions for Shopify stores with proven playbooks for every major selling season, discount mechanics, and campaign execution timelines.

## Why This Matters

Seasonal promotions drive a disproportionate share of annual revenue for most ecommerce stores. Black Friday/Cyber Monday 2024 hit $13.3 billion in online sales for Cyber Monday alone. But poorly planned promotions erode margins and train customers to wait for discounts. This skill provides the structure to run profitable promotions, not just busy ones.

## Black Friday / Cyber Monday (BFCM) Playbook

BFCM is the single most important promotional period. Start planning 4+ months before.

### Timeline

**4+ months before (August):**
- Forecast inventory based on prior year data
- Place orders for Q4 stock (avoid stockouts)
- Identify hero products for deepest discounts
- Plan tech stack: ensure site handles 3-5x normal traffic

**2 months before (September-October):**
- Build dedicated BFCM landing page
- Create email segments: VIP, recent buyers, lapsed, new subscribers
- Design promotional assets (banners, emails, social)
- Set up abandoned cart flows specific to BFCM
- Test site speed under load

**2 weeks before (early November):**
- Launch teaser campaigns and early access for VIP customers
- Activate waitlist/early-bird signup
- Final inventory check
- Pre-schedule all emails and social posts

**During BFCM:**
- Monitor performance in real-time
- Adjust offers based on sell-through rate
- Send reminder emails to non-openers
- Extend deals to Cyber Week if inventory allows

### BFCM Discount Strategy

| Customer Segment | Discount | When |
|---|---|---|
| VIP/Loyalty members | 25-30% off + early access (24-48 hrs before public) | Pre-BFCM |
| Email subscribers | 20-25% off | BFCM launch |
| New customers | 15-20% off + free shipping | BFCM weekend |
| Lapsed customers | 20% off + personal message | Cyber Monday |

**Hero Product Strategy:** Pick 3-5 products for deepest discounts (loss-leaders that drive traffic). Surround them with full-price complementary products for cross-sell.

### BFCM Email Sequence

| Email | Timing | Subject | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1 week before | "BFCM is coming — get early access" | Build anticipation, VIP signup |
| 2 | Day before | "Your early access starts NOW" | VIP-only launch |
| 3 | Black Friday AM | "It's here. Up to 30% off everything." | Main launch blast |
| 4 | Black Friday PM | "Selling fast — [Product] is almost gone" | Urgency reminder |
| 5 | Saturday | "Weekend deals: new items added" | Extend momentum |
| 6 | Cyber Monday AM | "Cyber Monday: online-only deals" | Cyber Monday push |
| 7 | Cyber Monday PM | "Last chance — deals end at midnight" | Final urgency |

## Discount Mechanics Guide

### Types and When to Use Each

**Percentage Off:**
- Best for: New customers, price-unfamiliar shoppers
- Psychology: Feels bigger on lower-priced items
- Example: "25% off" on a $40 item feels better than "$10 off"

**Fixed Dollar Off:**
- Best for: Returning customers who know your prices
- Psychology: Feels bigger on higher-priced items
- Example: "$50 off" on a $200 item feels better than "25% off"

**Rule of 100:** If the product is under $100, use percentage. Over $100, use dollar amount.

**Tiered Discounts (AOV boosters):**
- Spend $50 → 10% off
- Spend $100 → 15% off
- Spend $200 → 20% off
- Set thresholds just above current average order value

**BOGO (Buy One Get One):**
- "Buy 2, Get 1 Free" — moves more inventory
- Works best for consumables, basics, gift-giving
- Often the highest-converting discount type

**Free Shipping Threshold:**
- Set threshold 15-20% above current AOV
- "Free shipping on orders over $75" when AOV is $60
- Reduces cart abandonment (surprise shipping costs = #1 abandonment reason)

### Discount Guardrails

To protect margins and brand perception:
- Never discount more than 30% on new products (trains customers to wait)
- Reserve deepest discounts (40%+) for clearance and end-of-season
- Limit discount code circulation (unique codes > public codes)
- Set clear start/end dates (no perpetual sales)
- Track discount code performance and remove underperformers

## Flash Sale Execution Checklist

Flash sales create urgency and clear inventory. Execute well or don't do them at all.

**Pre-launch (24-48 hours before):**
- [ ] Select products (high-margin items or clearance inventory)
- [ ] Set discount levels (20-70% — must feel significant)
- [ ] Set clear start and end times (24-72 hours max)
- [ ] Pre-write all email and social copy
- [ ] Test landing page and discount codes
- [ ] Verify inventory levels

**Launch:**
- [ ] Send announcement email (35% lift in transaction rates when sent 24 hrs before)
- [ ] Post on all social channels
- [ ] Send SMS to opted-in subscribers
- [ ] Activate site banners and popups
- [ ] Monitor real-time performance

**During sale:**
- [ ] Send mid-sale reminder to non-openers
- [ ] Post social updates with sell-through ("60% sold!")
- [ ] Monitor inventory — pull sold-out items immediately
- [ ] Send "last hours" reminder

**Post-sale:**
- [ ] Deactivate all discount codes
- [ ] Remove banners and popups
- [ ] Send "Sale ended" email with full-price alternatives
- [ ] Analyze performance: revenue, margin, new vs. returning customers, AOV impact

## Promotional Landing Page Template

Every seasonal promotion deserves a dedicated landing page:

```
[HERO BANNER — Sale name, discount, countdown timer (if applicable)]

[VALUE PROP — "Up to 30% off. Free shipping on everything. Ends Sunday."]

[HERO PRODUCTS — 3-5 deepest-discounted items with "Shop Now" CTAs]

[PRODUCT GRID — All sale items, sorted by discount depth or popularity]

[TRUST SECTION — Reviews, guarantee, return policy]

[EMAIL CAPTURE — "Get notified about our next sale" for non-converters]

[FAQ — Sale terms, shipping deadlines, return policy during sale]
```
