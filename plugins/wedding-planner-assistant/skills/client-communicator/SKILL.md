# Wedding Planner Client Communications

Build trust and manage expectations with polished, professional communications at every stage of the planning journey.

## Communication Cadence

### Planning Phase Timeline

| Months Out | Communication | Purpose |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| 12+ | Welcome packet + kickoff meeting | Set expectations, gather vision, establish process |
| 10-12 | Monthly planning updates | Venue secured, vendor shortlists, budget check |
| 8-10 | Vendor introductions | Connect couple with recommended vendors |
| 6-8 | Design concept presentation | Mood boards, color palette, aesthetic direction |
| 4-6 | Bi-weekly updates | Menu tasting, floral mock-ups, stationery, rentals |
| 2-3 | Detailed timeline draft | Day-of flow, vendor schedules, logistics |
| 1 month | Final details meeting | Confirm all vendors, finalize timeline, walkthrough |
| 2 weeks | Final confirmation email | Everything locked, last changes deadline |
| 1 week | Wedding week guide | Rehearsal details, emergency contacts, relax reminders |
| Day after | Thank you + next steps | Gratitude, vendor tips, final invoice details |

### Communication Principles

1. **Proactive, not reactive** — send updates before clients ask; control the narrative
2. **Visual when possible** — use mood boards, Pinterest links, photo examples over text-heavy emails
3. **Decision-focused** — every update should either inform or request a specific decision
4. **Emotionally aware** — planning is stressful; balance professionalism with warmth and reassurance
5. **Boundary-setting** — clear response times, revision limits, and communication channels

## Client Communication Templates

### Welcome Packet Contents

Include in the initial onboarding:
- Welcome letter with planner bio and planning philosophy
- Planning process overview with key milestones
- Communication guidelines (response times, preferred channels, office hours)
- Questionnaire: vision, priorities, must-haves, non-negotiables, style references
- Budget worksheet template
- Vendor category overview with typical booking timelines
- Next steps and first planning meeting date

### Planning Update Structure

Every update email should follow this format:
1. **Celebration** — what's been accomplished since last update (bookings, decisions, wins)
2. **In progress** — what's actively being worked on
3. **Decisions needed** — specific choices the couple needs to make with options and deadline
4. **Upcoming** — what's coming next in the planning timeline
5. **Budget snapshot** — quick summary of spent, committed, and remaining

### Vendor Introduction Email

When connecting the couple with a vendor:
- Brief introduction of who the vendor is and why they're recommended
- Specific reasons this vendor is a great fit for their wedding (style, venue experience, personality match)
- What to expect in the first meeting
- Questions to prepare (reference vendor-manager skill question lists)
- Clear next step (scheduling a meeting or call)

### Final Details Email (2 Weeks Before)

Comprehensive confirmation covering:
- Confirmed guest count and final headcount deadline
- Complete vendor contact list with day-of arrival times
- Detailed timeline (attach as PDF)
- Layout/floor plans
- Outstanding balance due dates
- Emergency contact information
- Weather contingency plan
- Any last-minute decisions needed (final song choices, seating chart, etc.)
