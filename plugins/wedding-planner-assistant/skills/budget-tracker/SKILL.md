# Wedding Budget Management

Create realistic budgets, track expenses in real time, and find smart ways to maximize value without sacrificing the couple's vision.

### Hidden Costs Checklist

Costs couples often forget:

- Gratuities for all vendors (15-20% for caterer, bartender; $100-300 for DJ, photographer, officiant)
- Vendor meals at reception ($25-75 per vendor)
- Marriage license fee ($25-100 depending on state/county)
- Dress alterations ($200-600)
- Wedding party gifts ($50-150 per person)
- Welcome bags for out-of-town guests ($10-25 per bag)
- Day-of emergency kit supplies
- Rental delivery/pickup fees
- Cake cutting fee (some venues charge $2-5 per slice)
- Corkage fee for BYO alcohol ($10-25 per bottle)
- Overtime charges for venue and vendors
- Insurance (event liability policy: $100-300)
- Hotel room blocks (room attrition penalties)
- Postage for invitations (can be $200+ for 100 invitations)

## Cost-Saving Strategies

### High-Impact Savings (Won't Compromise Quality)

- **Off-peak timing**: Friday or Sunday weddings save 20-40% on venue
- **Seasonal flowers**: Use what's in bloom instead of imported varieties
- **Brunch or lunch reception**: Lower food and alcohol costs vs. evening
- **Shorter bar hours**: Open bar during cocktail and dinner, cash bar for late night
- **Digital RSVPs**: Skip RSVP postage, use a wedding website
- **Limit plus-ones**: Define clear guidelines (married/engaged couples only, or all guests get a +1)
- **All-inclusive venues**: Bundled pricing often beats a la carte

### Medium-Impact Savings

- Ceremony and reception in same location (no transportation)
- Buffet or family-style instead of plated (15-25% savings)
- Limit floral to key areas (altar, head table, cocktail) — use candles and greenery elsewhere
- Digital invitations for save-the-dates, printed for formal invitations only
- Repurpose ceremony florals at reception
