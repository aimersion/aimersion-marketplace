# Hotel Guest Communications

Craft compelling, brand-appropriate guest communications for every touchpoint in the guest journey — from booking confirmation through post-stay loyalty nurturing.

## Communication Principles

1. **Warm and personal** — use the guest's name, reference their specific stay details, avoid robotic templates
2. **Anticipatory** — address needs before guests ask; proactively share relevant information
3. **Solution-oriented** — when addressing issues, lead with what you CAN do, not what you can't
4. **Brand-consistent** — match the property's positioning (luxury = refined, boutique = playful, business = efficient)
5. **Action-clear** — every message should have a clear next step or call to action

## Guest Journey Communication Map

### Pre-Arrival (Booking to Check-in)

**Booking confirmation** (immediate):
- Confirm dates, room type, rate, special requests
- Set expectations about the property experience
- Include practical information (parking, directions, check-in time)

**Pre-arrival message** (3-7 days before arrival):
- Build excitement about their upcoming stay
- Offer upgrades, dining reservations, spa bookings, experiences
- Request arrival time and transportation needs
- Flag any special occasions (anniversary, birthday from profile)

**Day-of arrival** (morning of check-in):
- Confirm readiness and welcome
- Provide mobile check-in link if available
- Share weather and local event highlights

### In-Stay

**Welcome message** (within 1 hour of check-in):
- Thank them for choosing the property
- Introduce key contacts (concierge, guest services)
- Highlight amenities, dining hours, Wi-Fi details

**Mid-stay check-in** (day 2 for 3+ night stays):
- Ask if everything meets expectations
- Offer assistance with plans, reservations, requests
- This is the critical moment to catch and resolve issues before checkout

**Service recovery** (when issues arise):
- Acknowledge the specific issue immediately — never minimize or deflect
- Explain what you're doing to resolve it with a timeline
- Offer appropriate compensation proportional to the inconvenience
- Follow up to confirm resolution

### Post-Stay

**Thank-you message** (within 24 hours of checkout):
- Express genuine gratitude referencing specific stay elements
- Invite them to share feedback (direct survey link)
- Encourage a review on TripAdvisor/Google (only if stay was positive)

**Review response** (within 24-48 hours of review posting):
- Positive reviews: Thank specifically, reference details, invite return
- Negative reviews: Acknowledge, apologize, explain resolution, take conversation offline
- Mixed reviews: Thank for positives, address concerns, demonstrate improvement

**Re-engagement** (30-60 days post-stay):
- Share upcoming events, seasonal packages, or loyalty offers
- Reference their past preferences to personalize the pitch
- Create urgency with limited-time offers or availability alerts

## Review Response Framework

When responding to online reviews:

1. **Thank** the guest by name for taking time to share feedback
2. **Acknowledge** specific points they raised (shows you read carefully)
3. **Address** any concerns with what was done or what's changed
4. **Invite** them back with a forward-looking positive statement
5. **Sign** with your name and title (personal accountability)

Never be defensive, never blame the guest, never reveal confidential stay details publicly.
