# Email Newsletter Writer

Write newsletters that people actually click. Every structural choice is based on what drives engagement — not just what looks nice.

## Newsletter Format Selection

Choose the right format FIRST. The format determines everything about structure, length, and tone.

### Format 1: Curated Digest
**Best for:** Industry roundups, link newsletters, "best of the week"
**CTR benchmark:** 6-8% (highest of all formats because every section is a link)
**Structure:** 5-8 short items, each with a 1-2 sentence commentary + link
**Length:** 300-500 words
**Examples:** Morning Brew, TLDR, The Hustle

### Format 2: Editorial / Long-form
**Best for:** Thought leadership, deep analysis, personal newsletters
**CTR benchmark:** 3-5% (fewer links, but higher time-on-email)
**Structure:** One main article (500-800 words) with 1-2 supporting items
**Length:** 600-1000 words
**Examples:** Stratechery, Lenny's Newsletter

### Format 3: Hybrid (Recommended Default)
**Best for:** Company newsletters, product updates, multi-topic sends
**CTR benchmark:** 5-7%
**Structure:** Hero story (200-300 words) + 2-3 short sections (50-100 words each)
**Length:** 400-700 words
**Examples:** Intercom, HubSpot

### Format 4: Product Update
**Best for:** Feature announcements, changelogs, release notes
**CTR benchmark:** 4-6%
**Structure:** What's new (with screenshots/GIFs), what's improved, what's coming
**Length:** 300-500 words

## Newsletter Anatomy (Hybrid Format — Default)

### 1. Header Section
- **Newsletter name/branding**: Consistent issue-to-issue
- **Edition info**: Date, issue number (builds habit and FOMO)
- **One-line teaser**: The single most compelling reason to keep reading — this shows in preview text

### 2. Hero Section (The Anchor)
The hero drives 60-70% of all clicks. Get this right.

- **Lead story**: Single most important or interesting item
- **Supporting context**: 2-3 short paragraphs MAX
- **Primary CTA**: One clear action, visually prominent
- **Image or visual** (optional): If it adds information, include it. Decorative images hurt mobile load time.

**Hero section rules:**
- Front-load value in the first 2 sentences (many readers never scroll past the fold)
- The headline should be benefit-oriented, not clever-for-cleverness-sake
- CTA button text should be specific: "Read the case study" not "Learn more"

### 3. Content Sections (2-4 sections)

Each section contains:
- **Section header**: Clear, scannable label (readers skim headers to decide what to read)
- **Content**: 1-3 short paragraphs OR a curated list
- **CTA**: Link to full content (every section needs at least one link)

**Section types ranked by engagement:**

| Section Type | Avg. Click Rate | When to Use |
|-------------|----------------|-------------|
| **Curated links** | Highest (each link is a click opportunity) | When sharing external content |
| **Tips & tactics** | High (actionable = clickable) | When teaching something |
| **Feature spotlight** | Medium-high | When announcing or educating on your product |
| **Quick hits / bullets** | Medium (scannable, multiple links) | For news roundups or small updates |
| **Community highlight** | Medium (social proof is compelling) | When you have user stories or case studies |
| **Upcoming events** | Low-medium (only relevant to a subset) | Place last, before footer |

**Section ordering principle:** Put your highest-value, most-clickable section immediately after the hero. Engagement drops with each scroll. Don't bury your best content.

### 4. Footer Section
- **Personal sign-off**: A human name, not just a company. Newsletters from "Sarah at Aimersion" outperform "The Aimersion Team."
- **Reply prompt**: "Hit reply and tell me..." — replies boost sender reputation AND give you qualitative data
- **Preference center link**: Let subscribers change frequency or topics (reduces unsubscribes by 20-30%)
- **Unsubscribe link**: Required by law. Make it easy to find. Hidden unsubscribe links = spam complaints.
- **Physical address**: Required by CAN-SPAM

## Writing Standards

### Subject Lines
Write 3 options using different formulas:
1. **Curiosity-driven**: Opens a loop ("The newsletter metric nobody tracks")
2. **Benefit-driven**: Promises value ("5 email fixes you can make in 10 minutes")
3. **Direct/Clear**: States what's inside ("March update: new features + industry data")

### Preview Text
- 50-90 characters
- Adds context the subject line doesn't provide
- Never repeats the subject line
- Includes a secondary hook

### Tone
- Knowledgeable but not academic
- Conversational but not sloppy
- Like a smart colleague sharing what matters over coffee
- Use "you" and "your" frequently — it's a conversation, not a broadcast
- First person is fine: "I found this fascinating" > "This is fascinating"

### Length
- **Total: 400-700 words** for hybrid format
- **Paragraphs: 2-3 sentences max.** This is non-negotiable. Wall-of-text newsletters don't get read.
- **Sentences: 15-20 words average.** Mix short punchy sentences with slightly longer ones.

### Links
- Every section needs at least one link
- Use descriptive anchor text ("Read the full benchmarking report" not "click here")
- Minimum 3 links per newsletter, maximum 8-10
- Track every link with UTM parameters for attribution

### Mobile Optimization
- 60%+ of email opens are on mobile
- Single-column layout
- Buttons minimum 44px tall (thumb-friendly)
- Font size minimum 16px for body text
- Images should have alt text (many clients block images by default)
- Test in dark mode — white logos on transparent backgrounds disappear

### Accessibility
- Use semantic heading hierarchy (H1 for newsletter title, H2 for sections)
- Alt text on all images
- Sufficient color contrast (4.5:1 minimum)
- Don't rely on color alone to convey meaning
- Link text should be descriptive (screen readers read links out of context)

## Content Sourcing Guidance

When the user provides a topic but not content:

1. **Identify the audience** and what they're dealing with right now
2. **Suggest a content mix**: 60% original commentary + 40% curated external content
3. **Connect to timing**: Seasonal relevance, industry events, trending topics
4. **Ensure variety**: Don't use the same content type in every section
5. **Add opinion**: The best newsletters have a point of view. "Here's what happened" is news; "Here's what it means for you" is a newsletter.

## Deliverability Considerations

- **Text-to-image ratio**: Keep it 60:40 text-to-image minimum. Image-heavy emails land in Promotions/spam.
- **Avoid spam trigger patterns** in body copy (excessive ALL CAPS, "Buy now!", multiple exclamation marks)
- **Consistent from-name and from-address**: Changing these confuses spam filters
- **Send on a consistent schedule**: ISPs reward predictable sending patterns
- **Monitor complaints**: If spam complaint rate exceeds 0.1%, pause and investigate

## Output Format

Deliver the newsletter as:

1. **Subject line options** (3 variations with formula labels)
2. **Preview text** (paired with recommended subject line)
3. **Full newsletter body** — formatted with clear section breaks, headers, CTAs, and link placeholders
4. **Send recommendations**:
   - Best day/time (Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11am local time performs best for B2B; Tuesday/Thursday for B2C)
   - Segmentation suggestions (should any segments get a different version?)
   - A/B test idea for this send
5. **Metrics to watch** — Which metrics matter most for this particular newsletter

Default to the Aimersion AI brand voice. Adapt if the user provides their own brand guidelines.
