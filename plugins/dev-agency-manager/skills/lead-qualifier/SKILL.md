# Lead Qualifier

Qualify inbound leads, prepare for discovery calls, and select portfolio case studies for software development agency sales.

## Qualification Framework

### The Agency BANT+ Model

Standard BANT adapted for agency sales:

**Budget**
- Can they afford your services? (Minimum engagement > your cost floor)
- Are they aware of market rates for software development?
- Have they budgeted for this project, or is it exploratory?
- Budget signals: company size, funding stage, prior tech investments

**Authority**
- Who is the decision maker? (CTO, VP Engineering, CEO, Procurement?)
- Is the person you're talking to the buyer, influencer, or user?
- What's the approval process? (Single decision maker vs. committee)
- Organizational complexity: how many stakeholders need to agree?

**Need**
- Is the need real and urgent, or nice-to-have?
- What happens if they don't solve this problem?
- Have they tried other solutions before?
- Is this a defined project or a vague idea?

**Timeline**
- When do they need to start? When do they need to deliver?
- Is the timeline driven by a real deadline (launch date, regulatory, competitive)?
- Is the timeline realistic for the scope described?

**+Fit** (agency-specific additions)
- Technical fit: Can your team actually build this?
- Cultural fit: Will this be a pleasant client relationship?
- Strategic fit: Does this project advance your agency's goals?
- Portfolio fit: Will this make a good case study?

### Lead Scoring Model

Score each dimension 0-25:

| Dimension | Score | Criteria |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| Budget (0-25) | 25 | Budget matches or exceeds estimate |
| | 15 | Budget exists but may be tight |
| | 5 | No budget defined yet |
| | 0 | Budget significantly below market |
| Fit (0-25) | 25 | Perfect tech, cultural, and strategic fit |
| | 15 | Good tech fit, acceptable culture |
| | 5 | Marginal fit, would need to stretch |
| | 0 | Poor fit on multiple dimensions |
| Need (0-25) | 25 | Urgent, well-defined, critical to business |
| | 15 | Real need, reasonable timeline |
| | 5 | Nice-to-have, exploratory |
| | 0 | Vague, no clear business driver |
| Engagement Quality (0-25) | 25 | Decision maker engaged, responsive, clear process |
| | 15 | Good communication, multiple stakeholders |
| | 5 | Slow response, unclear decision process |
| | 0 | Unresponsive, RFP-only, no direct contact |

**Total Score Interpretation:**
- 75-100: Hot lead — prioritize, fast-track proposal
- 50-74: Warm lead — pursue with standard process
- 25-49: Cool lead — qualify further before investing significant effort
- 0-24: Poor fit — politely decline or refer

## Discovery Call Preparation

Prepare for discovery calls with:
1. Company research (funding, team size, tech stack, recent news)
2. Key questions mapped to BANT+ dimensions
3. Relevant case studies from portfolio (2-3 max)
4. Preliminary estimate range (not a commitment)
5. Clear next steps to propose at end of call

## Red Flags to Watch For

- "We need this by next week" (unless very small scope)
- "Our last agency couldn't deliver" (may be the client, not the agency)
- "We just need someone to code our Figma designs" (commodity view of development)
- "Can you give us a fixed price before we define requirements?"
- RFP sent to >5 agencies (low win probability, high effort)
- No access to technical decision maker
- "We'll pay on delivery" or unusual payment terms

## Output Guidelines

When qualifying leads:
- Provide a clear score with justification for each dimension
- Highlight both opportunities and red flags
- Include discovery call questions tailored to the specific lead
- Recommend 2-3 relevant case studies
- End with a clear Pursue / Pursue with Conditions / Pass recommendation
