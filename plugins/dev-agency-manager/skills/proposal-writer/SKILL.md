# Proposal Writer

Create winning software development proposals and SOWs for agency client engagements.

## Proposal Structure Framework

Every winning proposal tells a story: "We understand your problem, here's how we'll solve it, here's why we're the right team, and here's what it will cost."

### The Winning Formula

1. **Lead with understanding, not capabilities.** Show you understand the client's business problem before talking about technology.
2. **Be specific.** Vague proposals lose. Name technologies, estimate hours, define deliverables concretely.
3. **Address risk proactively.** Clients fear project failure. Show you've thought about what could go wrong.
4. **Differentiate on process, not just price.** Every agency can write code. Show your methodology, communication rhythm, and quality practices.

### Engagement Models

**Fixed Price**
- Best for: Well-defined scope, MVP builds, redesigns
- Pros: Client knows total cost, simple budgeting
- Cons: Agency bears scope risk, requires detailed requirements
- Pricing approach: Estimate hours × rate × 1.3 (30% buffer for unknowns)

**Time & Materials (T&M)**
- Best for: Ongoing development, evolving requirements, R&D
- Pros: Flexibility, pay for actual work, easy to adjust scope
- Cons: Client has less cost predictability
- Pricing approach: Blended rate × estimated monthly hours, with ranges

**Retainer**
- Best for: Maintenance, fractional CTO, ongoing support
- Pros: Predictable revenue, long-term relationship
- Cons: Must demonstrate ongoing value
- Pricing approach: Monthly fee for guaranteed availability + hourly overage

### Estimation Best Practices

For detailed estimation templates and frameworks, read `references/proposal-templates.md`.

**Three-point estimation:**
- Optimistic: Everything goes perfectly
- Most likely: Normal course with minor issues
- Pessimistic: Significant challenges encountered
- Use: (Optimistic + 4 × Most Likely + Pessimistic) / 6

**Breaking down estimates:**
- Never estimate in units larger than 2 weeks
- Include: development, code review, QA, deployment, documentation
- Add 15-20% for project management overhead
- Add 10-15% for meetings and communication
- Add 20-30% contingency for unknowns

### Technology Stack Recommendations

When proposing a tech stack:
- Justify each choice (don't just list technologies)
- Consider client's team capabilities for future maintenance
- Align with industry standards for their domain
- Address scalability and performance requirements
- Note any licensing costs

## Output Guidelines

When writing proposals:
- Professional but not stuffy tone
- Use the client's industry language
- Include visuals: timeline diagrams, architecture diagrams, team org charts
- Keep executive summary to one page
- Make pricing section crystal clear with no ambiguity
- Include clear next steps and call to action
