# Resource Allocator

Optimize team allocation, utilization, and capacity planning for software development agencies.

## Utilization Framework

### Target Utilization Rates
- **Individual contributors**: 75-85% billable (allows for learning, internal, admin)
- **Tech leads**: 60-70% billable (code review, mentoring, architecture)
- **Project managers**: 70-80% billable (some admin and BD support)
- **Overall agency target**: 70-80% billable utilization

### Danger Zones
- **>90% utilization**: Burnout risk, no slack for emergencies, quality drops
- **<60% utilization**: Revenue risk, may indicate sales pipeline problem
- **Single project dependency**: Any team member on 100% one client is a risk

### Utilization Calculation
Billable Utilization = Billable Hours / Available Hours × 100

Available Hours = Working Days × 8 - PTO - Holidays - Training Days

## Capacity Planning Process

1. **Inventory current capacity** — list every team member, their skills, current allocation
2. **Map project demand** — hours needed per role for each project, current and upcoming
3. **Identify gaps** — where demand exceeds supply by role/skill
4. **Plan solutions** — reassign, hire, contract, defer, or decline
5. **Monitor weekly** — adjust as projects evolve

### Skill Matching Matrix

Build a matrix of team members vs. skills, rated 1-5:
- 5: Expert, can lead and mentor
- 4: Proficient, independent
- 3: Competent, needs occasional guidance
- 2: Basic, needs supervision
- 1: Beginner, learning

Use for:
- Optimal project staffing
- Identifying single points of failure (only one person at level 4+ for a critical skill)
- Planning cross-training
- Hiring prioritization

For detailed allocation frameworks and templates, read `references/allocation-frameworks.md`.

## Financial Impact

### Revenue Per Employee
- Target: $15K-25K/month revenue per billable employee
- Calculation: Hourly rate × billable hours per month
- Track monthly and compare to salary + overhead

### Bench Cost
- Cost of unbillable time: (Salary + Benefits) × (1 - Utilization %)
- Acceptable bench: 1-2 people rotating through training and internal projects
- Extended bench (>4 weeks): trigger sales outreach or consider reductions

## Output Guidelines

When creating resource plans:
- Use clear tables showing people, projects, and percentages
- Flag risks with severity ratings
- Provide actionable recommendations, not just data
- Consider team morale and growth alongside utilization numbers
- Include 90-day forward view minimum
