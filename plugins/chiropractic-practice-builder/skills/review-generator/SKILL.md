# Review Generator

Build a systematic, ethical review generation strategy for chiropractic practices that turns happy patients into vocal advocates.

## Timing Strategy — The "Golden Moments"

Identify the best moments to ask, ranked by conversion likelihood:

1. **Milestone moment** — Patient hits a care plan milestone (pain reduced by 50%, returned to activity)
2. **Wow moment** — Patient expresses excitement about results verbally in-office
3. **Gratitude moment** — Patient thanks the doctor or staff unprompted
4. **Referral moment** — Patient mentions they told someone about the practice
5. **Discharge/maintenance transition** — Patient "graduates" from corrective to wellness care

Never ask after: a difficult visit, billing discussion, insurance denial, or when the patient seems rushed.

## Ask Methods

### In-Office (Highest Conversion)
- Front desk script after a positive visit
- Doctor's personal ask during the visit (most powerful)
- Tablet or QR code at checkout station
- Printed card with QR code handed with receipt

### Digital Follow-Up
- Text message 1–2 hours after a positive visit
- Email with direct link to Google review page
- Follow-up for patients who said "yes" but haven't posted yet (gentle, one-time nudge)

## Message Templates

Create templates for each channel:
- Keep them short (under 50 words for text, under 100 for email)
- Make the review link one tap/click away
- Reference the specific positive experience when possible
- Never offer incentives for reviews (violates Google and FTC guidelines)
- Include the practice name and doctor name for personal touch

## Review Platform Priority

1. **Google Business Profile** — Highest visibility and SEO impact
2. **Yelp** — Important for local discovery (but never ask directly per Yelp policy — share general link)
3. **Facebook** — Good for social proof and community
4. **Healthgrades/Vitals** — Healthcare-specific credibility

## Responding to Reviews

Draft response templates for:
- **5-star reviews** — Thank specifically, reinforce their health journey
- **4-star reviews** — Thank warmly, note openness to feedback
- **Negative reviews** — Acknowledge concern, invite offline conversation, never disclose patient information (HIPAA)

## Ethical Guidelines

- Never fabricate, incentivize, or purchase reviews
- Never ask patients to remove negative reviews
- Never share patient health information in review responses
- Respect patients who decline — never pressure or follow up more than once
- Comply with HIPAA in all review responses (never confirm someone is a patient)
