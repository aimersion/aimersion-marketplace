# Patient Reactivation

Identify inactive chiropractic patients and build multi-channel re-engagement campaigns that feel personal, not salesy.

## Defining Inactive Patients

Segment patients by inactivity window to tailor messaging urgency and tone:

| Segment | Last Visit | Priority | Approach |
|---------|-----------|----------|----------|
| Cooling Off | 30–60 days | High | Gentle check-in, schedule next adjustment |
| Lapsed | 61–120 days | Medium | Wellness reminder, share progress they've made |
| Dormant | 121–365 days | Lower | Re-introduction, special incentive offer |
| Lost | 365+ days | Lowest | "We miss you" with compelling reason to return |

## Campaign Creation Process

1. **Gather patient data** — Ask the user for their list of inactive patients (or help them define criteria). Determine what information is available: name, last visit date, treatment history, contact method preferences.

2. **Segment the list** — Categorize patients into the tiers above. Prioritize the Cooling Off and Lapsed segments for highest ROI.

3. **Craft personalized messages** — For each segment, create outreach that:
   - References their specific treatment history when available (e.g., "How's your lower back feeling since your last adjustment?")
   - Provides a clear reason to return (seasonal wellness, new services, maintenance benefits)
   - Includes a specific call-to-action with easy booking options
   - Feels like it comes from their doctor, not a marketing department

4. **Multi-channel sequences** — Build a 3-touch sequence per segment:
   - Touch 1: Personal text/SMS or email (highest open rates)
   - Touch 2: Follow-up email with educational content (value-first)
   - Touch 3: Phone call script or handwritten note prompt (for high-value patients)

5. **Track and optimize** — Suggest a simple tracking method: who was contacted, who responded, who booked, who showed up.

## Message Tone Guidelines

- Warm and caring, never pushy or guilt-inducing
- Reference specific benefits of continued chiropractic care
- Acknowledge life gets busy — no judgment for gaps in care
- Position return visits as self-care, not obligation
- Use the doctor's voice and name, not generic clinic branding

## Common Re-engagement Hooks

- Seasonal: "Spring is a great time to reset your spine after winter hibernation"
- Life events: "Starting a new workout routine? Let's make sure your spine is aligned"
- New services: "We've added [massage therapy/decompression/dry needling] since your last visit"
- Wellness maintenance: "Just like dental cleanings, regular adjustments prevent bigger issues"
- Insurance: "Your insurance benefits reset — use them before they expire"

## Incentive Strategy

Offer value without devaluing the service:
- Complimentary posture assessment or re-evaluation (not free adjustments)
- Bring-a-friend first visit special
- Wellness workshop invitation (positions them as VIP, not discount-seeker)
- Priority scheduling for returning patients
