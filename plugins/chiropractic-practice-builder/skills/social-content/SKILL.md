# Chiropractic Social Content

Generate educational, engaging social media content that positions the chiropractor as a trusted wellness authority while attracting new patients.

## Content Pillars

Rotate through these categories for variety:

1. **Education** (40%) — Explain conditions, bust myths, teach self-care
   - "What actually happens when you hear that 'crack'"
   - "3 stretches for desk workers that take under 2 minutes"
   - "Why your headaches might be coming from your neck"

2. **Social Proof** (25%) — Patient success stories, reviews, transformations
   - Before/after posture photos (with consent)
   - Patient testimonial quotes (with permission)
   - "This patient came in unable to turn their head. After 6 weeks..."

3. **Behind the Scenes** (20%) — Humanize the practice and team
   - Doctor's morning routine, continuing education, why they became a chiropractor
   - Staff spotlights, office culture moments
   - New equipment or technique demonstrations

4. **Community & Engagement** (15%) — Polls, questions, local involvement
   - "What's your biggest posture challenge? Comment below"
   - Local event sponsorships, charity involvement
   - Seasonal wellness challenges

## Platform-Specific Formatting

### Instagram / Facebook
- Short-form video scripts (30–60 seconds) for Reels
- Carousel post outlines (5–7 slides with hook → education → CTA)
- Single-image posts with caption (under 150 words, line breaks for readability)

### X (Twitter)
- Concise tips and myth-busters (under 280 characters)
- Thread format for deeper education topics
- Engagement questions and polls

### LinkedIn
- Professional wellness tips for corporate audiences
- Workplace ergonomics and productivity angles
- Longer-form thought leadership

## Content Creation Process

1. Ask the user about their practice's specialty focus, patient demographics, and brand voice
2. Generate a week's worth of content (5–7 posts) across selected platforms
3. Include hashtag suggestions relevant to chiropractic and local reach
4. Provide posting time recommendations based on platform best practices
5. Suggest visual concepts for each post (photo ideas, graphic text overlays)

## Compliance Guardrails

- Never make claims that chiropractic cures specific diseases
- Use language like "may help," "many patients experience," "research suggests"
- Always recommend patients consult their provider for specific medical advice
- Include appropriate disclaimers on health claims
- Ensure all patient photos/stories have written consent
- Follow platform-specific healthcare advertising guidelines
