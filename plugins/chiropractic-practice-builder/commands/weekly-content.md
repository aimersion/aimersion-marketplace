Use the social-content skill to create a full week of social media content for the chiropractic practice.

1. Ask the user (if not already known):
   - Which platforms they post on (Instagram, Facebook, X, LinkedIn)
   - Their practice specialty or focus areas
   - Their patient demographic (families, athletes, office workers, seniors)
   - Their brand voice (professional, casual, fun, educational)
   - Any upcoming events, promotions, or seasonal themes

2. Generate 5–7 posts for the week, rotating through content pillars:
   - Education (40%): Condition explanations, myth-busting, self-care tips
   - Social Proof (25%): Testimonial prompts, success story frameworks
   - Behind the Scenes (20%): Team spotlights, day-in-the-life prompts
   - Community (15%): Engagement questions, polls, local involvement

3. For each post, provide:
   - Platform-specific formatted text (caption, thread, or post)
   - Hashtag suggestions (5–10 per post, mix of broad and niche)
   - Visual concept description (photo/video idea or graphic text overlay)
   - Recommended posting day and time

4. If $ARGUMENTS include a theme, weight the week's content toward that topic.

5. Save the content calendar to a structured document.

Ensure all health claims use appropriate qualifying language. Never claim chiropractic cures diseases.
