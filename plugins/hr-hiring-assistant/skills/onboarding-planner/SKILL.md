# Onboarding Planner

Create onboarding plans that get new hires productive fast and make them feel welcome from Day 0. Research shows that structured onboarding improves new hire retention by 82% and productivity by 70%. At a 50-person company where every person matters, this is non-negotiable.

## The Onboarding Timeline

### Phase 0: Pre-Start (1-2 weeks before Day 1)

This is where most small companies drop the ball. The offer is signed, everyone relaxes, and the new hire shows up to a half-set-up laptop and confusion.

**Universal Pre-Start Checklist:**
- [ ] Send welcome email: start date, time, location (or login details for remote), dress code, parking, what to bring
- [ ] Order and ship equipment (laptop, monitor, peripherals) — for remote, ship 3+ business days early
- [ ] Create accounts: email, Slack/Teams, calendar, core tools
- [ ] Add to relevant Slack channels and email groups
- [ ] Assign onboarding buddy (not their manager — someone they can ask "dumb" questions to)
- [ ] Create Day 1 calendar with invites
- [ ] Prepare a "welcome kit" document: org chart, team norms, key links, glossary of company acronyms
- [ ] Hiring manager preps the team: "Here's who's starting, their background, and how to welcome them"
- [ ] Set up payroll, benefits enrollment (auto-send enrollment links on Day 1)

### Phase 1: Day 1 — Belonging and Basics

Goal: Make them feel like they made the right decision. No deep work on Day 1.

**Universal Day 1 Template:**
| Time | Activity | With | Notes |
|------|----------|------|-------|
| 9:00 | Welcome and "How are you feeling?" | Manager | Warm, not transactional |
| 9:30 | IT setup and tool walkthrough | IT or Buddy | Screen share for remote |
| 10:30 | Team introductions (round-robin) | Team | Each person shares name, role, one fun fact |
| 11:30 | Company overview: mission, values, how we work | Manager or Founder | For <50 people, founder time is powerful |
| 12:00 | Welcome lunch | Manager + 2-3 team members | Virtual lunch for remote (send a meal delivery) |
| 1:30 | Role expectations and 30/60/90 preview | Manager | Set the frame, don't overwhelm |
| 2:30 | Buddy coffee chat | Onboarding buddy | Informal — "ask me anything" |
| 3:30 | Self-guided: explore tools, read docs | Self | Give them breathing room |
| 4:30 | End-of-day check-in | Manager | "What questions do you have?" |

### Phase 2: Week 1 — Context and Connections

- [ ] Complete compliance training (harassment prevention, security, data handling)
- [ ] Read team documentation and recent project context
- [ ] 1:1 meetings with each team member (15-20 min)
- [ ] Shadow 2-3 key meetings to understand team rhythms
- [ ] Complete one small, well-scoped task (first "win")
- [ ] Daily 15-min check-in with manager (fade to weekly after Month 1)
- [ ] Friday: Week 1 retrospective with manager — "What's clear? What's confusing?"

### Phase 3: Month 1 (30-Day Goals)
- Understand team processes and own 1-2 workstreams
- Build relationships with cross-functional partners
- Deliver first meaningful contribution
- Complete all compliance and training requirements
- Manager check-in: "Are you getting what you need?"

### Phase 4: Month 2 (60-Day Goals)
- Operating independently on core responsibilities
- Identified first improvement or initiative to propose
- Contributing to team planning and discussions
- Building institutional knowledge

### Phase 5: Month 3 (90-Day Goals)
- Fully productive in the role
- Completed a significant project or milestone
- Manager review: formal 90-day check-in with two-way feedback
- Decision point: "Is this working for both of us?"

## Department-Specific Additions

Read the relevant department guide from `references/onboarding-by-dept.md` for additional context on what each department needs. Key differences:

**Engineering:**
- Dev environment setup guide (expect this takes half of Day 1)
- Codebase walkthrough with a senior engineer
- First PR within Week 1 (something small — a typo fix or minor bug)
- Intro to CI/CD, testing practices, code review norms
- On-call rotation introduction (not on-call until Month 2+)

**Sales:**
- CRM training and pipeline walkthrough
- Shadow 3-5 sales calls in Week 1
- Product deep-dive with a product manager
- First discovery call solo by Week 3 (with manager listening)
- Quota ramp: 0% Month 1, 50% Month 2, 100% Month 3

**Customer Success:**
- Product certification/training in Week 1
- Shadow 5+ customer calls
- Intro to book of business by Week 2
- First customer meeting solo by Week 3
- Full book ownership by Day 60

**Marketing:**
- Brand guidelines, tone of voice, and messaging deep-dive
- Analytics access and dashboards walkthrough
- Content calendar and campaign overview
- First content piece or campaign contribution by Week 2

**Finance:**
- Systems access: ERP, expense management, banking
- Chart of accounts walkthrough
- Close process overview with timeline
- Shadow a close cycle before participating

**Executive/Leadership:**
- 1:1 with every direct report in Week 1
- Skip-level 1:1s in Week 2
- Meet key cross-functional partners
- 90-day strategic assessment due at end of Month 3

## Buddy Program Guidelines

The onboarding buddy makes or breaks the experience. Here's how to run it:

**Who makes a good buddy:**
- Same or adjacent team (not the new hire's manager)
- 6+ months tenure (knows the ropes)
- Genuinely friendly and patient
- Willing to be interrupted with questions

**Buddy responsibilities:**
- Daily check-in for first two weeks (can be async in Slack)
- Available for "no stupid questions" conversations
- Lunch/coffee companion (virtual or in-person)
- Help navigate unwritten rules and cultural norms
- Duration: 60 days (then transition to normal team relationships)

## Key Contacts Matrix

```
| Person | Role | Contact For |
|--------|------|-------------|
| [Manager] | Direct manager | Day-to-day guidance, goals, feedback |
| [Buddy] | Onboarding buddy | Culture, navigation, "dumb" questions |
| [IT Contact] | IT | Tool access, equipment, tech issues |
| [HR/People Ops] | HR | Benefits, policies, payroll |
| [Admin] | Office admin | Facilities, supplies, logistics |
```
