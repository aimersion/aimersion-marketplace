# Remote Hiring

Hire and onboard remote employees effectively. Remote hiring introduces specific challenges around timezone coordination, virtual assessment, compliance across jurisdictions, and building culture without physical proximity. This skill addresses all of them.

## Remote Job Description Additions

When writing a JD for a remote role, include these beyond the standard JD framework:

- **Remote work details:** Fully remote, hybrid (how many days), or "remote with occasional travel"
- **Timezone requirements:** "Core hours 10am-3pm ET" or "Any timezone" or "Must overlap 4+ hours with US Pacific"
- **Travel expectations:** "Quarterly team offsites" or "Annual company retreat" or "No travel required"
- **Equipment policy:** Company-provided laptop, stipend amount, home office budget
- **Location restrictions:** "US-based" or "US/Canada/UK" or "Anywhere" — this matters for payroll and compliance

## Remote Interview Best Practices

### Video Interview Setup
- Send calendar invites with video link, expected duration, and who they'll meet
- Test your own audio/video before the call
- Have a backup plan (phone number) if tech fails
- Allow 5-minute buffer between back-to-back interviews

### Evaluating Remote-Specific Skills
Add these competencies to your interview loop for remote roles:

| Competency | Why It Matters | Sample Question |
|-----------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Written communication** | Remote teams live in Slack/email | "Walk me through how you'd communicate a project delay to your team and stakeholders" |
| **Self-direction** | No one's watching over their shoulder | "Tell me about a time you had to manage your own priorities with competing deadlines and no clear direction" |
| **Async collaboration** | Not everyone is online at the same time | "How do you handle decisions when key stakeholders are in different timezones?" |
| **Proactive communication** | Over-communication is a feature, not a bug | "How do you keep your team informed about your progress day-to-day?" |
| **Home office setup** | Practical readiness | "Do you have a dedicated workspace? What's your internet reliability like?" (ask matter-of-factly, not as a gatekeeper) |

### Technical Assessment for Remote Roles
- Prefer async take-home exercises over live coding (better signal, less anxiety)
- Set clear time expectations ("This should take 2-3 hours; don't spend more than 4")
- Use collaborative tools (Figma, CodeSandbox, Google Docs) for live exercises
- Record sessions (with consent) so other interviewers can review

## Remote Onboarding Checklist

### Pre-Start
- [ ] Ship laptop and equipment (arrive 2+ days before start date)
- [ ] Send welcome package (company swag, handwritten note from manager)
- [ ] Set up all accounts: email, Slack, GitHub, Notion, etc.
- [ ] Create a "Getting Started" doc with links to everything they need
- [ ] Assign an onboarding buddy (ideally in a similar timezone)
- [ ] Schedule Week 1 meetings (don't overwhelm — max 4 hours of meetings per day)

### Day 1 Agenda (Remote)
| Time | Activity | Format |
|------|----------|--------|
| 9:00 | Welcome call with manager | Video call |
| 9:30 | IT setup help (screen share) | Video call |
| 10:30 | Team introductions | Group video call |
| 11:30 | Break and self-guided reading | Async |
| 1:00 | Buddy lunch chat | Video call |
| 2:00 | Role expectations and 30/60/90 | Video call with manager |
| 3:00 | Explore tools and documentation | Async |
| 4:00 | End-of-day check-in | Video or Slack |

### Week 1 Priorities
- [ ] Complete security and compliance training
- [ ] Read team documentation and recent project context
- [ ] 1:1 video calls with each team member (15-20 min each)
- [ ] Shadow a team meeting or standup
- [ ] Complete one small, well-defined task
- [ ] Daily check-in with manager (move to weekly after Month 1)

## Multi-Jurisdiction Compliance

When hiring remote employees across states or countries:

### US Multi-State Considerations
- **Payroll:** Must register for payroll in each state where you have employees
- **Tax withholding:** Varies by state; some have reciprocity agreements
- **Benefits:** Some states mandate specific benefits (CA paid family leave, NY disability)
- **Employment law:** Minimum wage, overtime, meal breaks vary by state
- **Workers' comp:** Required in nearly all states; state-specific

### International Considerations
- **Employer of Record (EOR):** Consider Deel, Remote, Oyster for international hires without a local entity
- **Contractor vs. employee:** Classification rules vary dramatically by country
- **Data privacy:** GDPR applies to EU-based employees regardless of company location
- **Working hours:** Many countries have strict working time directives

Always recommend consulting employment counsel for multi-jurisdiction compliance.

## Remote Work Agreement Template

Include these sections in your remote work agreement:
1. Work location and any location restrictions
2. Core working hours and timezone expectations
3. Equipment and home office stipend details
4. Internet and workspace requirements
5. Data security and confidentiality obligations
6. Communication expectations (response times, availability)
7. Expense reimbursement policy
8. Return of equipment upon termination
