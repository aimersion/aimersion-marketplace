# Org Planning

Plan organizational structure for growing teams. At 50 people, every new hire changes the org — and every structural decision (add a manager? create a new team? promote from within?) has outsized impact.

## Planning Framework

### Key Questions
1. **What roles do we need?** (Gap analysis against business goals)
2. **In what order?** (Sequencing — what unlocks the most value first?)
3. **Build or buy?** (Hire externally or promote/develop internally?)
4. **At what level?** (Senior hire who can lead, or junior hire to grow?)
5. **What's the budget?** (Fully-loaded cost modeling)

### Healthy Org Benchmarks

| Metric | Healthy Range | Warning Sign |
|--------|---------------|--------------|
| Span of control | 5-8 direct reports | <3 (too hierarchical) or >12 (too flat, manager overwhelmed) |
| Management layers | 3-4 for 50 people | >4 = decisions too slow |
| IC-to-manager ratio | 6:1 to 10:1 | <4:1 = top-heavy (expensive, slow) |
| Team size | 5-9 people | <4 = isolated, >12 = hard to manage |
| Single points of failure | 0 | Anyone who, if they left, would cause a crisis |

### Internal Promotion Assessment

Before hiring externally, evaluate internal candidates:

| Factor | Internal Promotion | External Hire |
|--------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Ramp time** | Already knows the business | 3-6 months to full productivity |
| **Cultural risk** | Known quantity | Unknown |
| **Cost** | Usually 10-20% raise | Market rate (often higher) |
| **Team morale** | Signals growth path exists | Can demotivate if internal candidates were overlooked |
| **Skills gap** | May need development support | Brings new skills and perspectives |
| **Speed** | Immediate (with transition plan) | 2-4 months to hire and start |

**Promotion readiness checklist:**
- [ ] Already performing at next-level expectations for 6+ months
- [ ] Peers and skip-level would support the promotion
- [ ] Has the skills (or a clear development plan for gaps)
- [ ] Role exists at the next level (don't create titles just to retain people)
- [ ] Compensation can be adjusted to match the new level

## Headcount Planning

### Cost Modeling

For each proposed hire, calculate fully-loaded cost:

```
Base salary: $[X]
+ Benefits (typically 20-30% of base): $[Y]
+ Equity (annual value): $[Z]
+ Equipment and setup: $[A]
+ Recruiting costs (agency fee or internal time): $[B]
= Fully-loaded annual cost: $[TOTAL]
```

### Hiring Sequencing Template

| Priority | Role | Rationale | Fully-Loaded Cost | Hire By | Dependency |
|----------|------|-----------|-------------------|---------|------------|
| 1 | [Role] | [Why now] | $[X] | Q[X] | None |
| 2 | [Role] | [Why after #1] | $[X] | Q[X] | [Depends on...] |
| 3 | [Role] | [Why this order] | $[X] | Q[X] | [Depends on...] |

## Output

Produce text-based org charts, headcount plans with cost modeling, hiring roadmaps, and structural health assessments. Flag single points of failure, excessive management overhead, and roles that should be promoted from within versus hired externally.
