# Case Support Prep

Prepare medical device sales reps to provide excellent in-case support during surgical procedures.

## Pre-Case Preparation Checklist

### Surgeon Intelligence
- Review the surgeon's preferences and past cases (implant sizes used, technique variations)
- Note any specific requests or idiosyncrasies from previous cases
- Check if this is a new surgeon (requires more detailed preparation and teaching mindset)
- Review the surgeon's preferred approach and any known challenges

### Device and Instrumentation
- Confirm exact product inventory needed (sizes, styles, backup options)
- Verify all instruments and trays are available, sterile, and in working order
- Prepare backup/contingency products for unexpected intraoperative findings
- Know the step-by-step implantation technique cold — reps should be able to talk through any step
- Review device-specific IFU (Instructions for Use) for any nuances

### Case-Specific Details
- Patient demographics relevant to device selection (if shared by surgeon)
- Planned procedure and approach
- Pre-operative imaging review points (what the surgeon will reference)
- Anticipated challenges based on case complexity
- Complementary devices or add-ons that may be needed

### Logistics
- OR time and location confirmed
- Check-in procedures for the facility
- Vendor credentialing current and up to date
- Loaner/consignment set delivery confirmed
- Post-case implant documentation requirements (lot numbers, serial numbers, stickers)

## In-Case Support Guide

Structure advice for different phases:
- **Before incision** — Instrument layout, imaging display, initial setup
- **During procedure** — Anticipate needs, have next instruments ready, sizing recommendations
- **Critical decision points** — When the surgeon may need device options or technique guidance
- **Closing** — Document implants used, collect unused inventory, capture lot/serial numbers

## Post-Case Follow-Up

- Same-day thank-you message or check-in
- Document case details for territory records (device used, any issues, surgeon feedback)
- Report any device concerns through proper channels
- Schedule follow-up on patient outcomes if appropriate
- Identify cross-selling or upselling opportunities revealed during the case
