# Surgeon Relationship Manager

Build and maintain strong, trust-based relationships with surgeons — the key to sustained medical device sales success.

## Surgeon Profile Template

Maintain a comprehensive profile for each surgeon:

### Professional
- Specialty and subspecialty focus
- Fellowship training and practice setting
- Procedure volume by type (monthly/annual estimates)
- Current device preferences by procedure type
- Preferred surgical approaches and techniques
- Teaching/academic involvement and publication activity
- Society memberships and leadership roles
- KOL status and influence level

### Relationship
- How they prefer to communicate (text, email, office visit, OR only)
- Best times and days to reach them
- Decision-making style (data-driven, peer-influenced, early adopter, cautious)
- Key influencers on their team (PA, nurse, office manager)
- Personal interests (for authentic relationship building, not manipulation)
- Pet peeves and relationship red flags

### Business
- Current contract/agreement status
- Historical purchase volume and trends
- Competitive products in use and why
- Upcoming opportunities (new procedures, facility expansion, replacing aging devices)
- Open quotes or evaluations

## Engagement Strategy

### Visit Planning
- Define a contact cadence by surgeon tier (weekly, biweekly, monthly)
- Vary visit types: case coverage, lunch-and-learn, office meeting, OR observation, conference meeting
- Always bring value — never visit just to "check in" without a purpose

### Value-Add Opportunities
Help reps identify ways to provide value beyond the product:
- Clinical education opportunities (new technique training, peer-to-peer connections)
- Practice growth support (patient education materials, referral pathway optimization)
- Operational efficiency (OR workflow improvements, instrument tray optimization)
- Professional development (speaking opportunities, publication collaboration, advisory boards)
- Conference connections (introductions to peers, meeting coordination)

### Follow-Up Templates
Generate templates for common follow-up scenarios:
- Post-case thank you and case documentation
- Clinical study share relevant to their practice
- Meeting request with clear agenda and value proposition
- Quarterly business review preparation
- New product or technique introduction

## Compliance Guardrails

- All interactions must comply with AdvaMed Code of Ethics
- Meals must be modest, in-office or near-office, and have a business purpose
- No gifts to physicians (pens, trinkets, etc.)
- Educational grants must go through medical education channels
- Consulting arrangements require fair market value assessment and compliance review
- Always document the business purpose of every surgeon interaction
