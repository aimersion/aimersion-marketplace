# Competitor Tracker

Monitor competitive activity in the medical device landscape and arm reps with intelligence to win against every competitor in their territory.

## Competitive Intelligence Framework

### What to Track

1. **Product launches and updates** — New devices, line extensions, recalls, 510(k) clearances, PMA approvals
2. **Clinical evidence** — New studies published, clinical trial results, registry data
3. **Pricing and contracting** — GPO contract wins/losses, pricing rumors, bundling strategies
4. **Personnel changes** — New reps in territory, manager changes, key hires/departures
5. **Surgeon adoption** — Which surgeons are evaluating or switching to competitor products
6. **Marketing activity** — Conference presence, sponsored educational events, digital campaigns

### Intelligence Sources
- FDA device databases (510(k), PMA, recalls, MAUDE reports)
- PubMed and clinical journals for new studies
- Company press releases and investor calls
- Conference proceedings and exhibit hall observations
- Field intelligence from distributor partners and OR staff
- Surgeon conversations and feedback

## Competitor Profile Template

For each major competitor, maintain:
- **Company overview** — Size, focus areas, recent financial health
- **Product portfolio** — Key products, differentiators, known limitations
- **Clinical evidence** — Strongest and weakest clinical claims
- **Pricing position** — Premium, value, or aggressive pricing strategy
- **Sales approach** — Team size, coverage model, key relationships in territory
- **Vulnerabilities** — Service gaps, product limitations, surgeon complaints

## Competitive Response Playbook

Help reps prepare responses to common competitive scenarios:
- Competitor undercuts on price → value selling framework
- Competitor publishes favorable study → evidence analysis and response talking points
- Surgeon evaluating competitor device → trial/evaluation strategy
- Competitor wins a key account → win-back timeline and approach
- Competitor launches next-gen product → differentiation messaging

## Battlecard Creation

Generate concise, usable battlecards for each competitor:
- One page maximum, printable or mobile-friendly
- "Us vs. Them" feature comparison (factual, not disparaging)
- Top 3 selling points against this competitor
- Top 3 objections surgeons raise (with responses)
- Clinical evidence comparison
- Compliance note: never disparage competitors — compare factually and let the data speak
