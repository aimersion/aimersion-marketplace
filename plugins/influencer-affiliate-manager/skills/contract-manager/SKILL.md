# Contract Manager

Generate and review influencer partnership agreements that protect both brand and
creator. Every contract should be clear enough that neither party is surprised by
any term, while being enforceable enough to protect the brand's investment.

## Why Contracts Matter More Than Ever

FTC penalties can exceed $53,000 per violation. Content usage disputes are the
fastest-growing category of influencer-brand litigation. A creator posting competitor
content during an undocumented exclusivity expectation has no contractual obligation
to stop. The contract is the only protection.

**Important:** Claude is not a lawyer. These templates provide a strong starting point
based on industry best practices, but all contracts should be reviewed by legal counsel
before execution.

## Essential Contract Clauses

Every influencer agreement needs these sections. Read `references/contract-clauses.md`
for detailed clause language and variations.

### 1. Scope of Work & Deliverables

Specify with precision:
- Exact number and type of deliverables (posts, Reels, Stories, TikToks, YouTube videos)
- Platform(s) for each deliverable
- Content specifications (minimum length, format, orientation, resolution)
- Key messages, required mentions, and hashtags
- Required and prohibited visual elements
- Caption requirements and mandatory links
- Draft submission and revision process (typically 2 rounds included)
- Content approval workflow and response SLAs (brand responds within X business days)
- Posting schedule with specific dates or windows

### 2. Compensation & Payment Terms

Define clearly:
- Total compensation amount and currency
- Payment structure: flat fee, per-deliverable, performance bonus, or hybrid
- Payment schedule: upon signing, upon posting, net-30 after campaign end
- Performance bonus criteria and calculation method
- Product seeding details (what the creator receives and whether it's compensation)
- Expense reimbursement policy (if applicable)
- Late payment terms (interest rate, grace period)
- Kill fee: what the creator receives if the brand cancels after work begins (typically 25-50% of contracted amount)

### 3. Content Usage Rights & Licensing

This is where most disputes occur. Be explicit about:
- **Organic usage:** Can the brand repost on its own social channels? Duration?
- **Paid amplification:** Can the brand run the content as paid ads? On which platforms?
- **Whitelisting:** Can the brand run ads from the creator's account?
- **Extended usage:** Website, email marketing, print, OOH, retail displays?
- **Duration:** 30 days, 90 days, 1 year, perpetual?
- **Exclusivity of usage rights:** Exclusive to the brand, or can the creator re-license?
- **Content ownership:** Creator retains ownership with license to brand (most common)
  vs. work-for-hire where brand owns outright (rare, costs more)
- **Pricing tiers:** If paid usage costs extra, specify rates

**Common structure:**
- Organic repost rights: included in base fee, 12 months
- Paid amplification: +25-50% of base fee per 30-day window
- Whitelisting: +30-50% of base fee per 30-day window
- Extended usage (web, email, print): negotiate separately

### 4. FTC Disclosure Requirements

Non-negotiable. The contract must require:
- Clear disclosure in every piece of sponsored content
- Approved disclosure language: #ad, #sponsored, "Paid partnership with [Brand]"
- Platform-specific requirements (verbal + overlay for video, above-fold for captions)
- Creator acknowledges responsibility for FTC compliance
- Brand has the right to request corrections for non-compliant disclosures
- Indemnification: creator holds brand harmless for disclosure violations

### 5. Exclusivity Clause

Define precisely:
- **Category exclusivity:** The specific product categories or competitors covered
- **Named competitors:** List specific competitor brands (don't leave ambiguous)
- **Duration:** Campaign period + post-campaign blackout (typically 30-90 days)
- **Platform scope:** All platforms or specific platforms?
- **Compensation for exclusivity:** Longer or broader exclusivity warrants higher pay
- **Exceptions:** Any pre-existing relationships grandfathered in?

### 6. Morality / Conduct Clause

Protects the brand's reputation:
- Termination triggers: illegal activity, hate speech, discrimination, harassment
- Social media conduct during campaign period
- Obligation to disclose any pending legal issues or controversies
- Notification requirements (creator must inform brand of potential issues)
- Consequences: immediate termination, return of unused fees, content takedown
- Grace period: distinguish between "immediate termination" offenses and
  "opportunity to cure" offenses

### 7. Termination & Cancellation

Cover all exit scenarios:
- **Brand cancellation before content creation:** Full kill fee or partial payment
- **Brand cancellation after content creation:** Full payment for completed work
- **Creator cancellation:** Return of advance payments, timeline for replacement
- **For-cause termination:** Morality clause violations, missed deadlines (with cure period)
- **Content removal rights:** Can the brand require content takedown after termination?
- **Notice period:** Typically 7-14 days written notice for without-cause termination

### 8. Confidentiality

- Campaign details are confidential until public launch
- Compensation terms are confidential indefinitely
- Creator may not share brand strategy or internal communications
- Survival period: confidentiality outlasts the contract (typically 2 years)

### 9. Representations & Warranties

Both parties confirm:
- Creator has authority to enter the agreement
- Content will be original (no copyright infringement)
- Creator's audience metrics are not artificially inflated
- Creator will comply with all applicable laws and platform terms
- Brand has rights to the products and trademarks being promoted

### 10. Dispute Resolution

- Governing law (state/country)
- Mediation before litigation
- Arbitration clause (optional, often preferred for speed and cost)
- Venue for any legal proceedings

## Contract Templates by Partnership Type

### One-Off Sponsored Post
Lightweight, 3-5 pages. Focus on deliverables, compensation, usage rights, and
FTC compliance.

### Campaign Ambassador (3-6 months)
More comprehensive, 6-10 pages. Add exclusivity, morality clause, content calendar
with flexibility, and performance bonus structure.

### Long-Term Brand Ambassador (6-12+ months)
Full agreement, 8-15 pages. Include all clauses above plus content bank provisions,
monthly reporting requirements, event appearance terms, and renewal options.

### Affiliate-Only Partnership
Lighter on content requirements, heavier on commission terms, tracking methodology,
attribution windows, and payout schedules.

## Negotiation Guidelines

When the user is negotiating terms with a creator:

**Common creator pushback and recommended responses:**
- "I want perpetual usage rights included" → Offer 6-12 month license with option to extend
- "Exclusivity is too broad" → Narrow to named competitors only, or reduce duration
- "I need to approve how my content is used in ads" → Grant approval right for paid usage with 48-hour response window
- "Payment net-60 is too long" → Offer 50% upon posting, 50% net-30
- "Two revision rounds isn't enough" → Three rounds with clear brief reduces the need

**Rate negotiation leverage points:**
- Offer whitelisting rights at a discount if bundled with the original deal
- Long-term commitments (3+ months) justify 10-20% volume discounts
- Performance bonuses reduce upfront risk for the brand
- Content repurposing rights are valuable to the brand — price them in
