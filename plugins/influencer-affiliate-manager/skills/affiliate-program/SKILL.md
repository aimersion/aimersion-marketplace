# Affiliate Program Design

Build affiliate programs that align creator incentives with business outcomes.
The best programs make it easy for creators to earn and easy for brands to measure
— with commission structures that reward real performance, not just clicks.

## Why Affiliate + Influencer = The Future

The line between influencer and affiliate marketing has blurred. The hybrid model
— where creators earn a base fee for content plus commissions on sales — delivers
32% higher click-to-order conversion than either approach alone. In 2025, 61% of
US brands use tiered commission structures for top-performing partners.

## Commission Structure Models

### 1. Flat Percentage Commission

The simplest model. Every sale earns the same commission rate.

**Setup:**
- Commission rate: typically 10-20% for DTC, 5-15% for marketplace
- Attribution window: 30 days click (industry standard)
- Cookie/tracking: via affiliate platform link

**Best for:** Getting started, simple product catalogs, testing new creators.
**Limitation:** No incentive to outperform.

### 2. Tiered Performance Commission

Commissions increase as the creator hits volume thresholds. The most effective
model for motivating growth — brands using tiered structures see 27% higher
retention among top affiliates.

**Example Structure:**

| Monthly Sales Volume | Commission Rate | Example Payout |
|---------------------|----------------|----------------|
| $0 - $2,000        | 10%            | $200           |
| $2,001 - $5,000    | 15%            | $750           |
| $5,001 - $10,000   | 18%            | $1,800         |
| $10,001+           | 22%            | $2,200+        |

**Design principles:**
- Tier 1 should be achievable by casual affiliates (low barrier to earn)
- Tier 2 should be achievable by engaged micro-influencers
- Top tiers should require genuine commitment and quality audience
- Consider whether tiers apply to the full amount or only incremental sales
  (full-amount tiers are simpler and more motivating)

### 3. Hybrid: Flat Fee + Commission

Combines guaranteed payment with performance upside. The smartest brands use
this for their core creator roster.

**Typical structure:**
- Flat fee: covers content creation time and creative value
- Commission: rewards actual sales performance
- Fee range: 30-60% base fee vs. commission-only equivalent

**Example:**
Instead of $3,000 flat per post, offer $1,500 flat + 15% commission on sales.
If the creator drives $15,000 in sales, they earn $1,500 + $2,250 = $3,750 —
more than the flat fee, and the brand only pays extra for results.

**Best for:** Mid-tier creators with proven audiences, ongoing partnerships.

### 4. Product Seeding + Commission

Zero upfront cost for the brand (beyond product). Creator receives free
product and earns commission on sales.

**When it works:**
- Product has strong visual appeal and natural social shareability
- Creator genuinely loves the product (authentic enthusiasm is obvious)
- Product cost is meaningful enough to be perceived as compensation
- Typically for nano and micro creators building their affiliate income

**When it doesn't:**
- High-value creators who expect flat fees
- Products with low AOV (a 10% commission on a $15 product = $1.50 per sale)
- Creators who receive dozens of seeding offers weekly

### 5. Revenue Share / Recurring Commission

For subscription businesses — commission on each billing cycle.

**Structure:**
- First-purchase commission: 20-30%
- Recurring commission: 5-15% per renewal
- Duration: 3-12 months, or lifetime of customer

**Best for:** SaaS, subscription boxes, membership programs where LTV is high.

## Program Design Checklist

### Before Launch

1. **Choose an affiliate platform**
   - Read `references/affiliate-platforms.md` for platform comparison
   - Key considerations: tracking reliability, payment automation, creator
     dashboard quality, integration with your ecommerce stack

2. **Set commission structure**
   - Start with flat percentage while you gather data
   - Move to tiered after 3-6 months with performance data

3. **Define attribution rules**
   - Attribution window (click and view-through)
   - Last-click vs. first-click vs. multi-touch
   - How to handle cross-device conversions
   - Return/chargeback policy for commissions

4. **Create affiliate onboarding materials**
   - Brand guidelines and approved messaging
   - Product information and key selling points
   - Content examples and best practices
   - Tracking link and promo code instructions
   - FTC disclosure requirements and examples

5. **Set program policies**
   - Prohibited promotion methods (spam, misleading claims, brand bidding)
   - Content approval requirements (if any)
   - Cookie/tracking window
   - Payment terms and minimum payout threshold
   - Grounds for termination

### After Launch

6. **Recruit creators strategically**
   - Prioritize quality over quantity in early stages
   - Start with 20-30 creators, learn, then scale
   - Use the influencer-vetting skill for each candidate

7. **Monitor and optimize**
   - Weekly: check for fraud signals (unusual conversion patterns)
   - Monthly: review top/bottom performers, adjust commissions if needed
   - Quarterly: evaluate program economics and tier effectiveness

## Scaling the Program

### Phase 1: Foundation (0-50 affiliates)
- Manual recruitment and relationship management
- Simple flat-rate commission
- Focus on finding product-market fit for the affiliate channel

### Phase 2: Growth (50-200 affiliates)
- Introduce tiered commissions
- Build self-serve onboarding
- Add seasonal promotions and bonus campaigns
- Segment affiliates by type (content creators, review sites, coupon sites)

### Phase 3: Scale (200+ affiliates)
- Automated fraud detection and compliance monitoring
- Custom commission rates for top performers
- Dedicated affiliate manager or agency
- Integration with broader marketing mix modeling
