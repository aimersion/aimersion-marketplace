# Compliance Monitor

Ensure every piece of influencer content meets FTC, platform, and international
regulatory requirements. Non-compliance isn't just a legal risk — it erodes
consumer trust and can result in fines exceeding $53,000 per violation.

## The Stakes

In 2025, the FTC issued over 150 warning letters to influencers about inadequate
disclosures. Penalties range from $5,000 to $43,792 per violation depending on
severity and reach. Every single post, story, or video that fails to disclose a
material connection can be counted as a separate violation. Brands are liable too —
the FTC holds companies responsible for ensuring their influencers comply.

## What Requires Disclosure

Any "material connection" between the creator and the brand must be disclosed.
Material connections include:

- Cash payment (any amount)
- Free products or services
- Commission or affiliate earnings
- Discounts or special pricing not available to the public
- Gifted trips, event access, or experiences
- Employment or consulting relationships
- Family or personal relationships with brand representatives
- Equity, stock options, or financial interest in the brand

**Key principle:** If the relationship could affect the weight a viewer gives
to the endorsement, it must be disclosed. When in doubt, disclose.

## Disclosure Standards

### Required Characteristics

Every disclosure must be:
1. **Clear:** Uses unambiguous language a reasonable consumer understands immediately
2. **Conspicuous:** Placed where the audience will see it without searching
3. **Unavoidable:** Cannot be hidden by scrolling, clicking "more", or platform UI

### Approved Disclosure Language

**Strong (always acceptable):**
- #ad
- #sponsored
- "Paid partnership with [Brand]"
- "Ad" or "Advertisement"
- "[Brand] gifted me this product"
- "I earn a commission on purchases through my link"

**Insufficient (do NOT use alone):**
- #collab
- #ambassador
- #partner
- #spon
- #sp
- "Thanks to [Brand]" (without stating commercial relationship)
- "Gifted" alone (ambiguous — gifted by a friend? by the brand?)

### Platform-Specific Requirements

Read `references/platform-compliance.md` for detailed platform-by-platform guidance.

**Quick reference:**

**Instagram Feed Posts:**
- #ad or #sponsored must appear before the "more" fold (first 2-3 lines)
- Use platform "Paid Partnership" label AND caption disclosure
- Platform tools supplement but do NOT replace caption disclosure

**Instagram Stories:**
- Text overlay on each Story frame: "#ad" or "Paid partnership"
- Placement: visible, not obscured by platform UI elements
- Cannot be a tiny font — must be readable at a glance

**Instagram Reels / TikTok:**
- Verbal disclosure within the first 30 seconds of the video
- On-screen text overlay visible for at least 3 seconds
- Caption disclosure alone is NOT sufficient for video content
- Use platform-native branded content tools in addition

**YouTube:**
- Verbal disclosure at the start of the video (first 30 seconds)
- Activate YouTube's "includes paid promotion" checkbox
- Description must include written disclosure
- Mid-roll disclosures needed for long-form content (>10 min) if the
  sponsorship segment is distinct

**Podcasts:**
- Verbal disclosure at the beginning of the episode
- Written disclosure in episode description
- Repeat verbal disclosure before ad-read segments

**Livestreams:**
- Verbal disclosure at the start and at regular intervals (every 15-20 min)
- On-screen text overlay throughout the stream
- Pin a comment with disclosure if the platform supports it

**Blog / Website:**
- Disclosure at the top of the article, before the content
- Affiliate link disclosures near the links themselves
- Not in the footer — it must be above any product mentions

## Emerging Compliance Areas (2025-2026)

### AI-Generated Content Disclosure
If influencers use AI tools to create or enhance sponsored content, they must
disclose both the sponsorship AND the AI involvement. This is a rapidly evolving
area — monitor FTC guidance quarterly.

### Content Targeting Minors
The FTC applies stricter requirements to content aimed at younger viewers:
- No subtle product placements
- Enhanced monitoring of unboxing-style promotions
- Disclosures must be age-appropriate and extra-clear

### International Requirements
If your campaign has international reach, additional regulations apply:
- **UK (ASA):** Requires #ad on all paid content, strict enforcement
- **EU:** GDPR implications for tracking, unfair commercial practices directive
- **Canada:** Competition Act requirements similar to FTC but with distinct rules
- **Australia:** AANA Code of Ethics, TGA rules for health products

## Training Creators on Compliance

Provide every creator partner with a compliance briefing that covers:

1. What constitutes a material connection (with examples)
2. Platform-specific disclosure requirements for their content types
3. Approved disclosure language (provide a cheat sheet)
4. Common mistakes and how to avoid them
5. The brand's content review and correction process
6. Consequences of non-compliance (contractual and legal)

## Compliance Monitoring Workflow

### During Campaign
1. Review every piece of content within 24 hours of posting
2. Flag non-compliant content immediately
3. Send correction request to creator with specific instructions
4. Verify correction was made within the required timeframe
5. Log all compliance issues and corrections for records

### Post-Campaign Audit
1. Audit 100% of campaign content for disclosure compliance
2. Document any violations and corrective actions taken
3. Update creator compliance scores for future partnership decisions
4. Report systemic issues and update training materials accordingly
