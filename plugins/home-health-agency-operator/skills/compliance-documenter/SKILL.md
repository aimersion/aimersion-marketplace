# Compliance Documenter

Generate and review regulatory documentation for Medicare-certified home health agencies, including OASIS assessments, plans of care, and Conditions of Participation compliance.

## Core Documentation Types

### OASIS Assessment Support
- Guide clinicians through OASIS-E item completion with plain-language explanations
- Flag commonly missed or incorrectly coded items
- Provide coding tips for accurate case-mix and PDGM classification
- Generate assessment timing reminders (SOC, ROC, Recertification, Transfer, Discharge)

### Plan of Care (CMS-485)
- Generate plan of care templates based on patient diagnoses and OASIS findings
- Include required elements: diagnoses, medications, DME, functional limitations, activities permitted, safety measures, nutritional requirements, orders for disciplines and treatments
- Ensure physician signature and dating compliance
- Track recertification timelines (60-day episodes)

### Visit Documentation Templates
- Skilled nursing visit notes with required clinical elements
- Therapy visit notes (PT, OT, SLP) with functional goal documentation
- Home health aide supervisory visit templates
- Coordination of care notes

## Medicare Compliance Checklist

For each patient, verify:
- Homebound status documented and justified
- Skilled need clearly established
- Physician face-to-face encounter documented (within required timeframe)
- Plan of care signed and dated by physician
- OASIS transmitted within required timeframe
- Visit frequency matches ordered plan of care
- Aide supervisory visits completed every 14 days (or per state requirement)

## Survey Readiness

Help prepare for state surveys and accreditation:
- Generate a mock survey checklist based on current Conditions of Participation (CoPs)
- Identify common deficiency areas and remediation steps
- Create staff competency documentation templates
- Infection control and emergency preparedness policy templates
- QAPI (Quality Assurance and Performance Improvement) program documentation

## Important Disclaimers

- This tool provides documentation templates and guidance — it does not replace clinical judgment
- Always verify current CMS regulations as requirements change with annual updates
- Recommend the user consult their compliance officer or legal counsel for complex situations
- OASIS coding should be validated by a certified OASIS specialist
