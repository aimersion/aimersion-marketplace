# Marketing Generator

Create marketing materials for tutoring businesses — local SEO content, referral programs, seasonal campaigns, social media, and community outreach.

## Local SEO Content

### Google Business Profile Optimization
- Business name, address, phone number (NAP) — consistent everywhere
- Primary category: "Tutoring Service" or "Academic Tutoring"
- Description: 750 characters highlighting subjects, grade levels, approach
- Photos: tutoring space, team, student work (with permission), certificates
- Posts: Weekly updates about test prep seasons, new programs, tips
- Reviews: Systematic collection strategy (ask after every positive milestone)

### Website Content Pages
Create pages targeting local search intent:
- Homepage: "[City] Tutoring Services — [Subject] Tutoring for K-12"
- Subject pages: "Math Tutoring in [City]", "Reading Tutoring in [City]"
- Grade-level pages: "Elementary Tutoring", "Middle School Tutoring", "High School Tutoring"
- Test prep pages: "SAT Prep in [City]", "ACT Tutoring in [City]"
- Blog posts: "How to Choose a Tutor in [City]", "When Does Your Child Need a Tutor?"
- About page: Tutor bios, credentials, philosophy

### Local Content Strategy
- Write about local schools, curriculum standards, and testing schedules
- Create guides tied to local school district calendars
- Partner with local businesses for cross-promotion content
- Sponsor or attend community events and write about them

## Referral Program Design

### Program Structure
- **Referral reward**: $50 credit, free session, or percentage discount
- **New client incentive**: First session free or discounted assessment
- **Tracking method**: Unique referral codes or simple "referred by" field
- **Communication**: Referral cards (physical and digital), email reminders

### Referral Touchpoints
- After a positive progress milestone: "We're so glad [Student] is thriving! Know anyone else who could benefit?"
- At session 10 or end of first month: Include referral card with session materials
- In monthly progress reports: Footer with referral program details
- Seasonal reminders: "Back-to-school is the most popular time for new students"

## Seasonal Campaign Calendar

### August-September: Back to School
- Campaign: "Start Strong" — Free assessment for new students
- Content: "5 Signs Your Child Needs a Tutor This Year"
- Outreach: Flyers at school supply stores, social media countdown

### October-November: First Report Card
- Campaign: "Turn It Around" — Targeted at post-report-card families
- Content: "What Your Child's Report Card Really Means"
- Outreach: Email to past leads, social media testimonials

### January: New Year / Second Semester
- Campaign: "Fresh Start" — New semester, new goals
- Content: "Make This Semester Different: A Parent's Guide"
- Outreach: Re-engagement emails to former clients

### March-April: Test Prep Season
- Campaign: "Test Ready" — Standardized test preparation packages
- Content: "Complete Guide to [State Test/SAT/ACT] Preparation"
- Outreach: Partnerships with school counselors

### May-June: Summer Planning
- Campaign: "Summer Slide Prevention" — Summer tutoring packages
- Content: "Don't Lose 2 Months of Learning This Summer"
- Outreach: End-of-year parent communications, summer program fairs

## Social Media Content

### Content Pillars
1. **Educational tips** (40%): Quick study tips, parent advice, learning hacks
2. **Student wins** (25%): Celebrations, testimonials, grade improvements (with permission)
3. **Behind the scenes** (20%): Tutor introductions, session glimpses, workspace tours
4. **Promotions** (15%): Seasonal offers, referral programs, new services

### Post Formats
- **Tip of the week**: Quick, actionable study or parenting advice
- **Did you know?**: Surprising education statistics or facts
- **Student spotlight**: Celebrate a student achievement (with family permission)
- **Ask me anything**: Invite parents to submit questions
- **Myth vs. fact**: Debunk common tutoring or education misconceptions
