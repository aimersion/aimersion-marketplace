# Student Assessor

Design diagnostic assessments, analyze skill gaps, and create individualized learning plans for tutoring students across K-12 subjects.

## Diagnostic Assessment Design

### Assessment Structure
1. **Pre-Assessment Interview** (5-10 min)
   - Current grade level and school
   - Subjects and specific topics of concern
   - Learning history (previous tutoring, special services, test scores)
   - Student's self-perception of strengths and challenges
   - Parent/guardian goals and expectations
   - Learning preferences and interests

2. **Skills Diagnostic** (20-40 min depending on subject)
   - Start at expected grade level
   - Move backward if student struggles (find the floor)
   - Move forward if student succeeds (find the ceiling)
   - Cover both procedural fluency and conceptual understanding
   - Include application/word problems to test transfer

3. **Learning Style Observation**
   - Note how the student approaches problems
   - Observe persistence, self-correction, and strategy use
   - Identify whether the student prefers visual, verbal, or kinesthetic approaches
   - Note attention span and engagement patterns

### Subject-Specific Diagnostic Areas

**Mathematics (K-12)**
- Number sense and place value
- Operations fluency (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division)
- Fractions, decimals, and percentages
- Algebraic thinking and equations
- Geometry and measurement
- Data analysis and statistics
- Problem-solving strategies

**Reading/ELA (K-12)**
- Phonemic awareness and phonics (K-3)
- Fluency (accuracy, rate, prosody)
- Vocabulary (grade-level and academic)
- Comprehension (literal, inferential, critical)
- Writing mechanics (grammar, punctuation, spelling)
- Writing composition (organization, voice, evidence)

**Science (Middle/High School)**
- Scientific vocabulary and concepts
- Lab skills and scientific method
- Data interpretation and analysis
- Mathematical applications in science
- Content knowledge by unit/topic

## Learning Plan Creation

### Plan Structure
1. **Student Profile Summary**
   - Name, grade, school, subjects
   - Assessment date and results summary
   - Identified strengths (specific skills at or above level)
   - Identified gaps (specific skills below level)

2. **Priority Goals** (3-5 goals, ranked)
   - Each goal is SMART: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound
   - Example: "Increase multiplication fact fluency from 15 to 40 correct facts per minute within 8 weeks"

3. **Session Plan**
   - Recommended session frequency and duration
   - Skill sequence (what to address first, second, third)
   - Instructional approaches for each goal
   - Materials and resources needed
   - Home practice recommendations

4. **Progress Monitoring**
   - How and when to measure progress on each goal
   - Decision rules: "If student achieves X by Y date, move to Z"
   - Re-assessment timeline (typically every 6-8 weeks)

### Goal-Setting Framework
- **Immediate wins** (1-2 weeks): Quick confidence builders
- **Short-term goals** (4-6 weeks): Address foundational gaps
- **Medium-term goals** (2-3 months): Build grade-level competency
- **Long-term goals** (semester/year): Achieve or exceed grade-level expectations

## Assessment Report Template

### Summary Section
- Student name, date, assessor
- Referral reason
- Assessment instruments used
- Overall performance level (X grade level equivalent in Y subject)

### Detailed Findings
For each skill area assessed:
- Current performance level
- Specific strengths observed
- Specific areas for growth
- Recommended interventions

### Recommendations
- Tutoring frequency and focus areas
- Accommodations or modifications suggested
- Resources for home support
- Timeline for re-assessment
