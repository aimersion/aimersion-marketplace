# Parent Reporter

Generate professional, encouraging progress reports and session summaries that keep parents informed and invested in their child's tutoring journey.

## Session Summary (After Each Session)

### Quick Summary Format (Email/Text — under 150 words)
```
Hi [Parent Name],

[Student] had a great session today! Here's a quick update:

**What we worked on:** [Topic/skill]
**What went well:** [Specific strength or breakthrough]
**What to practice:** [1-2 specific home practice items]

Next session: [Date/time]

Best,
[Tutor name]
```

### Detailed Session Notes (For records or upon request)
- Date, time, and duration
- Topics covered with specific activities
- Student engagement and effort level
- Skills demonstrated and mastered
- Areas that need continued practice
- Homework or practice assigned
- Notes for next session planning

## Monthly Progress Report

### Report Structure
1. **Header**: Student name, reporting period, tutor name
2. **Executive Summary** (2-3 sentences): Overall trajectory and key highlights
3. **Goals Progress**:
   - Goal 1: [Description] — Status: On Track / Ahead / Behind
   - Goal 2: [Description] — Status: On Track / Ahead / Behind
   - Include specific evidence for each status
4. **Skills Mastered This Month**: List of specific skills now at proficiency
5. **Current Focus Areas**: What we're actively working on
6. **Upcoming Focus**: What's next in the learning plan
7. **Home Support Suggestions**: 2-3 actionable things parents can do
8. **Session Attendance**: Sessions scheduled vs. completed
9. **Tutor Recommendations**: Any changes to frequency, focus, or approach

### Tone Guidelines
- Lead with positives — every report opens with genuine progress
- Be specific: "Sarah can now solve two-step equations" not "Sarah is doing better in math"
- Frame challenges as "next steps" not "deficits"
- Celebrate effort and growth mindset, not just outcomes
- Include the student's own voice when possible ("Marcus told me he felt confident on his quiz!")
- Avoid comparing to other students
- Express genuine care and optimism

## Goal Update Communication

### When Goals Are Met
- Celebrate the achievement specifically
- Share the data showing growth (before vs. now)
- Explain what comes next
- Ask parent for their observations at home/school

### When Goals Need Adjustment
- Acknowledge the effort and any partial progress
- Explain why the timeline is shifting (be honest but constructive)
- Present the revised plan
- Reassure with evidence of other areas of growth

### When Recommending Changes
- More sessions: Frame as an opportunity, not a deficit
- Fewer sessions: Celebrate the student's growing independence
- Subject change: Connect to the student's evolving needs
- Referral: If concerns suggest the student needs evaluation beyond tutoring, communicate gently with specific observations

## Quarterly Comprehensive Report

### Additional Elements (Beyond Monthly)
- Assessment data comparison (initial vs. current)
- Graph or visual showing progress trajectory
- School grade comparison (if available)
- Standardized test score impact (if applicable)
- Goal completion percentage
- Updated learning plan for next quarter
- Tutor's professional assessment of student trajectory
