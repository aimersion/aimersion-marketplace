Create or streamline new client intake materials. Context: $ARGUMENTS

## Process

1. **Determine Practice Context** — Ask the user for:
   - Practice specialty (general therapy, trauma, couples, family, child/adolescent, substance use, eating disorders, etc.)
   - Licensure type (LCSW, LPC, LMFT, psychologist, psychiatrist)
   - State of practice (for legal requirements)
   - Current intake process (what they already have, what's missing)
   - Whether they see insurance clients, private pay, or both
   - Telehealth vs. in-person vs. hybrid
   - EHR system used (if any)

2. **Generate Intake Packet** — Create the following documents:

   **Welcome Letter / Practice Information**
   - Practice philosophy and approach
   - What to expect in the first session
   - Cancellation and late arrival policy
   - Emergency procedures and crisis resources
   - After-hours contact information
   - Confidentiality overview (in plain language)

   **Informed Consent for Treatment**
   - Nature and scope of treatment
   - Therapist qualifications and approach
   - Confidentiality and its limits (mandatory reporting, danger to self/others, court orders)
   - Telehealth-specific consent (if applicable): technology risks, emergency protocols for remote sessions, recording policy
   - Fee structure, payment expectations, and insurance billing process
   - Cancellation/no-show policy
   - Right to terminate treatment
   - Client rights
   - Signature and date lines
   - Minor consent (if applicable): custodial parent signatures, minor's assent, information sharing between parents

   **Client Information Form**
   - Demographics (name, DOB, address, phone, email, emergency contact)
   - Referral source
   - Insurance information (if applicable)
   - Current medications
   - Medical history relevant to mental health
   - Previous therapy experience
   - Current presenting concern (in their own words)
   - Goals for therapy

   **Clinical Screening Questionnaire**
   - Based on the practice specialty, recommend validated screening tools:
     - General: PHQ-9 (depression), GAD-7 (anxiety)
     - Trauma: PCL-5 (PTSD), ACE questionnaire
     - Substance use: AUDIT, DAST-10
     - Eating disorders: EDE-Q
     - Child/adolescent: SDQ, RCADS
     - Couples: DAS-4 (dyadic adjustment)
   - Safety screening: Columbia Suicide Severity Rating Scale (C-SSRS) or similar
   - Note: Provide guidance on which tools are freely available vs. require licensing

   **HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices**
   - Required elements per federal law
   - Customize to state-specific requirements

   **Release of Information Template**
   - Authorization for release (to/from specific providers)
   - Scope limitations (what information, time period)
   - Revocation rights
   - Expiration date

3. **Telehealth Addendum** (if applicable):
   - Technology requirements
   - Private location expectations for client
   - Emergency plan for remote sessions (local emergency number, client's physical location)
   - Backup plan if technology fails
   - State-specific telehealth regulations

4. **Format Options** — Offer as:
   - Printable packet (PDF/Word)
   - Digital forms (content ready to input into their EHR or form system like IntakeQ, SimplePractice, TherapyNotes)
   - Email-friendly version for pre-session completion

All documents should be written in clear, warm, accessible language. Avoid clinical jargon that might be intimidating to a new client already anxious about starting therapy.
