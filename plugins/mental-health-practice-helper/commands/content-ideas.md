Generate marketing content ideas for a therapy practice. Focus: $ARGUMENTS

## Process

1. **Context** — Ask the user for:
   - Practice specialty and ideal client description
   - Platforms they use (website/blog, Instagram, Psychology Today, LinkedIn, etc.)
   - Comfort level with visibility (some therapists prefer minimal personal exposure)
   - Ethical concerns or boundaries they want to maintain
   - Content they've created before (what worked, what didn't)
   - Geographic focus (for local SEO content)

2. **Generate Content Ideas** organized by type:

   **Blog Posts / Website Content** (SEO-focused, 800-1200 words):
   - Answer the questions potential clients are Googling
   - "What to expect in your first therapy session"
   - "How to know if therapy is working"
   - "[Specialty]-specific educational content" (e.g., "What is EMDR and how does it work?")
   - "How to find the right therapist for you in [city]"
   - Normalize the therapy experience
   - Seasonal mental health content (holiday stress, seasonal depression, back-to-school anxiety, new year goal setting)

   **Social Media Posts** (warm, relatable, boundary-appropriate):
   - Psychoeducation in accessible language (anxiety tips, nervous system regulation, attachment styles)
   - Therapy myth-busting ("Therapy isn't just for people in crisis")
   - Normalizing statements ("It's okay to..." / "You don't have to...")
   - Book/resource recommendations
   - Self-care that goes beyond bubble baths (boundaries, saying no, processing grief)
   - "What I wish people knew about [topic]"
   - Gentle challenges to stigma

   **Psychology Today Profile Optimization:**
   - Profile statement that speaks directly to the ideal client
   - Use "you" language, not "I" language
   - Name the specific struggles clients might be searching for
   - Include what the therapy experience feels like (warm, direct, collaborative, etc.)
   - Mention specific modalities only if clients search for them

   **Email Newsletter** (monthly, for existing and potential clients):
   - One helpful tip or reflection
   - One resource recommendation
   - One practice update (new groups, availability changes)
   - Keep it brief and genuinely useful

3. **Ethical Marketing Boundaries** — All content must:
   - Never promise specific outcomes ("cure," "fix," "guarantee")
   - Never share client information or case details (even anonymized stories need extreme care)
   - Never diagnose the reader
   - Never create urgency or fear ("If you don't get help NOW...")
   - Never disparage other therapists or modalities
   - Comply with state licensing board advertising rules
   - Follow APA/NASW/ACA ethical guidelines for advertising
   - Include appropriate credentials (license number if required by state)
   - Be clear about scope of practice

4. **Content Calendar** — Provide a monthly plan mapping content to platforms with suggested posting frequency:
   - Blog: 2-4 posts per month
   - Instagram: 3-4 posts per week
   - Newsletter: 1 per month
   - Psychology Today: Review and update quarterly

Tone throughout: warm, knowledgeable, empowering, non-pathologizing. The goal is to help potential clients feel seen and think "This person understands what I'm going through."
