# Intake Processor

Streamline new client paperwork, clinical screening, and practice onboarding for therapy practices.

## The Intake Experience Matters

The intake process is a client's first real interaction with your practice. If it's confusing, impersonal, or overwhelming, you risk losing them before the first session. The goal: make paperwork feel supportive, not bureaucratic.

## Required Intake Documents

### Legally Required (varies by state)
1. **Informed Consent for Treatment** — Every practice, every client, every time
2. **HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices** — Federal requirement
3. **Telehealth Consent** — Required if providing any remote sessions
4. **Minor Consent** — Parental/guardian consent for clients under 18
5. **Financial Agreement** — Fee structure, payment expectations, insurance billing

### Clinically Recommended
6. **Client Information Form** — Demographics, contact, emergency contact, referral source
7. **Clinical Screening Measures** — Validated assessment tools for baseline measurement
8. **Safety Screening** — Suicide risk screening (Columbia Protocol or equivalent)
9. **Release of Information** — Templates ready for coordination with other providers
10. **Practice Policies** — Cancellation, communication preferences, emergency procedures

## Writing Informed Consent

The informed consent is the most important document in your practice. It must be legally sound AND understandable to a person who may be anxious, in crisis, or unfamiliar with therapy.

### Essential Elements

**What therapy is:**
- Describe your approach in plain language
- Set expectations: therapy involves discussing difficult topics, progress isn't linear, it requires active participation
- Benefits AND risks (yes, risks: emotional discomfort, relationship changes, things may feel worse before better)

**Confidentiality and its limits:**
- What you will keep private
- Mandatory exceptions (clearly list each one):
  - Suspected child or elder abuse/neglect
  - Imminent danger to self
  - Imminent danger to others (Tarasoff/duty to warn — state-specific)
  - Court order/subpoena
  - Insurance company access to clinical information (if billing insurance)
- Special situations: couples therapy (no-secrets policy or individual confidentiality), group therapy (confidentiality expectations for members), minor clients (what parents can/cannot access)

**Practical matters:**
- Fee per session and accepted payment methods
- Cancellation policy (typically 24-48 hours notice)
- What happens if they don't pay
- Insurance billing process (if applicable)
- Telehealth logistics
- Communication between sessions (email, text, portal — and what's HIPAA-compliant)
- Emergency procedures (what to do in crisis, who to call)
- Social media policy (you won't accept friend/follow requests, won't acknowledge clients publicly)

**Their rights:**
- Right to ask questions about treatment
- Right to refuse any intervention
- Right to terminate therapy at any time
- Right to request records
- Right to file complaints (include licensing board contact)

### Writing Tips
- Use "you" and "I" language, not legalese
- Use headers and short paragraphs (wall of text = unread consent)
- Bold the most important parts (confidentiality limits, fees, cancellation policy)
- Read it out loud — if it sounds like a lawyer wrote it, rewrite it
- Include a section for questions ("Before you sign, please ask about anything that's unclear")

## Clinical Screening Tools

### Choosing the Right Measures

| Presenting Concern | Recommended Screening | Free/Licensed | Time |
|--------------------|-----------------------|---------------|------|
| Depression | PHQ-9 | Free | 2-3 min |
| Anxiety | GAD-7 | Free | 2-3 min |
| PTSD | PCL-5 | Free | 5-7 min |
| General distress | PHQ-9 + GAD-7 combo | Free | 5 min |
| Suicide risk | C-SSRS (Columbia) | Free | 3-5 min |
| Substance use (alcohol) | AUDIT | Free | 2-3 min |
| Substance use (drugs) | DAST-10 | Free | 2-3 min |
| Eating disorders | EDE-Q | Licensed | 10-15 min |
| ADHD | ASRS v1.1 | Free | 2-3 min |
| Childhood trauma | ACE Questionnaire | Free | 2-3 min |
| Couples adjustment | DAS-4 | Free | 2 min |
| Child/adolescent | SDQ | Free | 5-10 min |
| Overall functioning | WHO-DAS 2.0 | Free | 5 min |

Administer at intake for baseline, then periodically (every 4-8 sessions) to track progress. This also satisfies insurance requirements for measurable outcomes.

## Intake Workflow Optimization

### Before the First Session
1. Client completes paperwork online (via EHR portal or secure forms) at least 24-48 hours before
2. Front desk/admin confirms receipt and flags incomplete items
3. Clinician reviews paperwork and screening scores before the session
4. Clinician identifies red flags (safety concerns, high screening scores, complex history)
5. Any insurance verification completed before session

### During the First Session
1. Welcome and rapport building (don't start with paperwork review)
2. Briefly review key consent points verbally (confidentiality limits especially)
3. Discuss presenting concerns, history, and goals
4. Collaboratively develop initial treatment plan
5. Set expectations for therapy process
6. Schedule next session
