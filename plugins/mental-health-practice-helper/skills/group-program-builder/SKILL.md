# Group Program Builder

Design structured group therapy programs, workshops, and psychoeducational series for therapy practices.

## Why Groups Are Good for Practices

- **Revenue efficiency:** 6-8 clients at $40-60/person/session vs. 1 client at $150. Groups generate $240-480/hour.
- **Waitlist solution:** Serve more clients without adding individual hours
- **Clinical effectiveness:** For some conditions (social anxiety, grief, substance use, DBT skills), groups are as effective or more effective than individual therapy
- **Community building:** Groups create connection and reduce the isolation that maintains many mental health conditions
- **Referral generator:** Group members often transition to individual therapy or refer others

## Group Types

### Process Groups
- Unstructured or semi-structured
- Focus on interpersonal dynamics
- Members share and receive feedback
- Typically ongoing (open or closed enrollment)
- Requires advanced group facilitation skills
- Examples: general therapy group, interpersonal process group

### Psychoeducational Groups
- Structured curriculum
- Skill-building focus
- Didactic + experiential
- Time-limited (6-12 weeks)
- Easier to market and fill
- Examples: anxiety management, stress reduction, parenting skills, relationship skills

### Support Groups
- Shared experience focus
- Peer support emphasis
- Facilitator guides but doesn't "teach"
- Can be ongoing
- Examples: grief support, caregiver support, divorce recovery, chronic illness

### Skills Training Groups
- Highly structured
- Specific skill curriculum
- Homework-based
- Time-limited
- Examples: DBT skills group, anger management, social skills, mindfulness-based stress reduction (MBSR)

## Designing a Group Program

### Step 1: Define the Group

- **Population:** Who is this for? Be specific. "Women ages 25-45 processing anxiety related to life transitions" is better than "people with anxiety"
- **Format:** Process, psychoeducational, support, or skills?
- **Duration:** How many sessions? (Typical: 8-12 weeks for structured, ongoing for process)
- **Session length:** 75-90 minutes is standard for most groups
- **Size:** 6-10 members ideal. Below 5 feels sparse; above 10 is hard to facilitate
- **Open vs. closed:** Closed (no new members after start) is easier to manage and builds stronger cohesion. Open allows rolling enrollment but requires more facilitation skill.

### Step 2: Build the Curriculum (for structured groups)

Each session should follow this arc:

1. **Check-in** (10-15 min) — How has the week been? Brief sharing, connect to last session's topic/homework
2. **Psychoeducation** (15-20 min) — Teach the session's concept in accessible language
3. **Experiential exercise** (20-25 min) — Practice the skill, process the topic, role-play, group activity
4. **Discussion** (15-20 min) — Group sharing, application to individual lives, peer support
5. **Wrap-up** (5-10 min) — Key takeaway, homework/practice for the week, preview next session

### Step 3: Create the Materials

For each session:
- Facilitator guide (detailed outline with talking points, timing, activity instructions)
- Participant handout (key concepts, exercises, reflection questions)
- Homework/practice sheet
- Session evaluation (brief feedback form — helps you improve the group and document outcomes)

### Step 4: Handle the Business Side

**Pricing:**
- Per-session: $40-75/person/session (depending on market and group type)
- Package: Full program at slight discount (e.g., 8 sessions for $400 instead of $480)
- Insurance: Bill 90853 (group psychotherapy). Reimbursement varies. Some groups are better run as private-pay.

**Marketing:**
- Start promoting 6-8 weeks before launch
- Require pre-group screening interview (individual meeting to assess fit)
- Set minimum enrollment (usually 4-5) to decide go/no-go
- Create a one-page group description flyer

**Group Agreement:**
- Confidentiality expectations (what's said in group stays in group)
- Attendance policy (how many sessions can be missed)
- Participation expectations
- Respect and safety guidelines
- No social media contact between members (or: discuss boundaries around this)
