# Session Notes Template

Progress note templates for different therapy modalities and documentation formats.

## Documentation Principles

### Why Notes Matter
- Legal protection for the clinician
- Clinical continuity (especially if another provider needs to step in)
- Insurance compliance and audit readiness
- Tracking treatment progress over time
- Ethical requirement of the profession

### The Golden Rule of Clinical Notes
Write as if the client, a judge, an insurance auditor, and a licensing board investigator will all read this note. Because any of them could.

### What to Include
- Enough detail for clinical continuity
- Interventions used (named specifically)
- Client's response to interventions
- Progress toward treatment plan goals
- Risk assessment (even if negative — "Client denied SI/HI")
- Plan for next session

### What NOT to Include
- Verbatim quotes of session content (summarize therapeutically relevant points)
- Your personal reactions or countertransference (put those in supervision notes, not the chart)
- Information the client shared about third parties (keep it clinically relevant and minimal)
- Speculative diagnoses you haven't assessed for
- Anything you wouldn't want read aloud in court

## Note Formats

### DAP (Most Popular for Therapists)

**D — Data:** What happened in the session.
- Client's presenting concern for this session
- Mood and affect as observed
- Key themes discussed
- Interventions used and client's engagement
- Relevant quotes (brief, clinically significant only)

**A — Assessment:** Your clinical interpretation.
- Progress toward treatment plan goals (improving, stable, declining)
- Clinical impressions
- Risk assessment (SI, HI, self-harm, substance use)
- Diagnostic considerations (if relevant to this session)

**P — Plan:** What's next.
- Next session focus
- Homework or between-session tasks
- Referrals made or needed
- Treatment plan modifications
- Next appointment date/time

### SOAP (Common in Medical Settings)

**S — Subjective:** Client's self-report. What they told you.
**O — Objective:** What you observed. Appearance, behavior, affect, cognition.
**A — Assessment:** Clinical analysis. Goal progress. Risk.
**P — Plan:** Next steps. Homework. Referrals. Modifications.

### BIRP (Required by Some State Agencies)

**B — Behavior:** What the client did/said. Observable.
**I — Intervention:** What YOU did. Specific techniques.
**R — Response:** How the client responded to your interventions.
**P — Plan:** Next session plan.

## Modality-Specific Documentation

### CBT Notes Should Include
- Automatic thoughts identified
- Cognitive distortions named
- Behavioral experiments discussed or assigned
- Thought record review (if used)
- Behavioral activation plan
- Homework assigned and homework from last session reviewed

### EMDR Notes Should Include
- Target memory/event
- Negative cognition and positive cognition
- VOC (Validity of Cognition) rating before/after
- SUD (Subjective Units of Disturbance) rating before/after
- Phase of EMDR protocol used this session
- Whether processing was complete or incomplete
- Body scan results
- Closure/containment technique used (if processing incomplete)

### DBT Notes Should Include
- Diary card review
- Skills module being covered (mindfulness, distress tolerance, emotion regulation, interpersonal effectiveness)
- Specific skills taught or practiced
- Chain analysis (if a target behavior occurred)
- Solution analysis
- Phone coaching between sessions (if occurred — note date, duration, skill used)

### Couples/Family Notes Should Include
- Who was present
- Interactional patterns observed
- Each person's perspective (briefly)
- Interventions and how each person responded
- Any safety concerns
- Homework for the system

## Treatment Plan Requirements

Insurance-compliant treatment plans need:

1. **Diagnosis** — DSM-5 with ICD-10 code
2. **Presenting problems** — In behavioral terms (not just "depression" but "reports persistent low mood affecting ability to maintain work attendance")
3. **Goals** — SMART format (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
4. **Objectives** — Smaller steps toward each goal
5. **Interventions** — What the therapist will do (name approaches/techniques)
6. **Frequency and duration** — How often and how long treatment is expected to last
7. **Review date** — When the plan will be reassessed (typically every 90 days)
