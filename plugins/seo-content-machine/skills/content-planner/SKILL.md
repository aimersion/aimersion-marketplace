# Content Planner

Build strategic content calendars that connect keyword research to business outcomes. Not a spreadsheet of titles and dates — a publishing strategy with topic clusters, conversion paths, and content lifecycle management.

## What Makes a Content Calendar Fail

Most content calendars fail because they:
1. **Have no keyword strategy.** Titles are brainstormed, not researched. They target keywords nobody searches for, or keywords impossible to rank for.
2. **Ignore content architecture.** Posts are published as isolated pieces with no cluster structure, so the site never builds topical authority.
3. **Have no conversion path.** Every piece is informational with no clear path to product/service pages.
4. **Never refresh existing content.** The team publishes 100% new content while their best-performing old posts decay.
5. **Overcommit.** The calendar has 20 pieces per month but the team can produce 4 quality ones. Quantity without quality builds nothing.

## Planning Process

### 1. Strategic Inputs

Collect before building:

**Business context:**
- What are the business goals? (Traffic growth, lead generation, brand authority, product launch, customer education)
- What products/services need more visibility?
- Any upcoming launches, events, or campaigns to align with?
- What is the team's realistic publishing capacity? (Be honest — one great post beats four mediocre ones)

**Existing content inventory:**
- What is already published?
- What is currently ranking (positions 1-20)?
- What was ranking but has decayed (positions dropped over last 6 months)?
- What gaps exist in the current topic cluster coverage?

**Keyword intelligence:**
- Pull from existing keyword research or generate new research
- Identify topic clusters to build or expand
- Note which keywords are quick wins (low difficulty, medium+ volume)

### 2. Content Pillar & Cluster Architecture

Before scheduling individual posts, define the content architecture:

**For each topic cluster:**
```
Cluster: [Topic Name]
Pillar page: "[Title]" targeting "[primary keyword]"
Business goal: [What this cluster drives — traffic, leads, authority]

Supporting posts:
1. "[Title]" — [keyword] — [intent] — [format]
2. "[Title]" — [keyword] — [intent] — [format]
3. "[Title]" — [keyword] — [intent] — [format]
4. "[Title]" — [keyword] — [intent] — [format]
5. "[Title]" — [keyword] — [intent] — [format]

Money page: [Product/service page this cluster drives traffic to]
Internal linking plan: All spokes → pillar. Pillar → all spokes. Cross-links between related spokes.
```

**Cluster prioritization:**
- **Cluster A:** Highest business impact, build first
- **Cluster B:** Strong opportunity, build second
- **Cluster C:** Long-term authority play, build over time

### 3. Content Mix by Goal

Adjust the publishing mix based on business goals:

**Growth-focused (maximize organic traffic):**
- 40% informational content targeting medium-volume, medium-difficulty keywords
- 20% quick wins (low-difficulty keywords, content refreshes)
- 20% pillar/comprehensive content for authority building
- 10% commercial investigation content (comparisons, reviews)
- 10% content refreshes on decaying posts

**Conversion-focused (maximize leads/signups):**
- 25% commercial investigation content (comparisons, reviews, alternatives)
- 25% informational content with strong conversion CTAs
- 20% conversion content (case studies, landing pages, demo guides)
- 15% content refreshes adding/improving CTAs on existing posts
- 15% pillar content

**Authority-focused (build brand/expertise):**
- 30% original research, data analysis, industry reports
- 25% pillar content / comprehensive guides
- 20% thought leadership and opinion pieces
- 15% informational supporting posts
- 10% content refreshes

### 4. Content Decay Management

Allocate 15-25% of publishing capacity to content refreshes. This is not optional — it is often the highest-ROI content activity.

**Refresh priority framework:**

| Signal | Priority | Action |
|--------|----------|--------|
| Page ranks 4-10, was previously top 3 | Critical | Full content refresh + competitive re-analysis |
| Page ranks 11-20, traffic declining | High | Update content, add new sections, improve internal links |
| Page older than 12 months, information outdated | High | Update statistics, examples, recommendations |
| Page ranks well but CTR is low | Medium | Rewrite title tag and meta description for better CTR |
| Page in a cluster but not linked properly | Medium | Add internal links to/from cluster posts |
| Page gets traffic but low conversions | Medium | Add/improve CTAs, update conversion path |

**What a real content refresh includes:**
- Updated statistics and examples (not just changing "2024" to "2025")
- New sections addressing topics competitors have added
- Improved heading structure and featured snippet optimization
- Updated internal links to newer content
- Fresh schema markup
- New images or media where appropriate

### 5. Calendar Construction

For each content piece, specify:

| Field | What to Include |
|-------|----------------|
| **Publish Week** | Target week (not specific date — allow flexibility) |
| **Title** | Working title with primary keyword |
| **Primary Keyword** | The specific keyword this piece targets |
| **Intent** | Informational (learn/do), Commercial, Transactional |
| **Format** | How-to, listicle, comparison, guide, case study, landing page, refresh |
| **Cluster** | Which pillar/cluster this belongs to |
| **Target Word Count** | Based on SERP analysis for the keyword |
| **New or Refresh** | New content or refresh of existing URL |
| **CTA** | What should the reader do next? |
| **Internal Links** | Specific pages to link to and from |
| **Schema** | What structured data to implement |
| **Snippet Target** | Featured snippet question to optimize for (if applicable) |
| **Status** | Ideation → Brief → Draft → Review → Published |

### 6. Strategic Sequencing

**Within each cluster:**
1. Publish pillar page first (it is the hub all spokes link to)
2. Publish 2-3 supporting posts per week for the next 3-4 weeks
3. After all posts are live, do an internal linking pass to ensure all connections are made
4. Monitor for 4-6 weeks, then refresh the pillar page with any insights

**Across clusters:**
- Build one cluster at a time (concentrated topical authority > scattered posts)
- Front-load quick wins to build momentum and demonstrate results
- Align conversion content with product launches or campaigns
- Space similar topics to avoid audience fatigue
- Schedule refreshes between new content sprints (prevents burnout)

**Day-of-week guidance:**
- Tuesday-Thursday: Publish educational/informational content (higher B2B engagement)
- Monday or Friday: Publish lighter content, refreshes, or thought leadership
- Avoid publishing important content on major holidays or industry events

### 7. Content Lifecycle Tracking

For each published piece, track:
- **Week 1-2:** Indexed? Crawled? Any technical issues?
- **Month 1:** Initial ranking position for target keyword
- **Month 2-3:** Ranking movement. Is it climbing or stagnant?
- **Month 4-6:** Traffic and conversion performance vs. targets
- **Month 6-12:** Content decay monitoring. Schedule refresh if declining.
- **Year 1+:** Annual content audit. Full refresh or consolidation decision.

## Output Format

1. **Strategy Summary** — Business goals, cluster priorities, content mix rationale (2-3 paragraphs)

2. **Topic Cluster Map** — Visual grouping of all clusters with pillar pages and supporting posts

3. **Monthly Calendar Table**

| Week | Title | Keyword | Intent | Format | Cluster | New/Refresh | CTA | Status |
|------|-------|---------|--------|--------|---------|------------|-----|--------|

4. **Content Refresh Schedule** — Existing pages to update, with priority and specific refresh recommendations

5. **Internal Linking Plan** — Which posts link to which, with anchor text suggestions

6. **KPI Targets**

| Content Type | Primary Metric | 30-Day Target | 90-Day Target |
|-------------|---------------|---------------|---------------|
| Pillar page | Organic sessions | 200-500 | 1,000+ |
| Supporting post | Keyword ranking | Top 30 | Top 10 |
| Comparison post | CTR to product page | 2% | 5% |
| Content refresh | Rank improvement | Stabilize | +5 positions |
| Case study | Demo requests | 1-2 | 3-5 |

7. **Next Month Preview** — 3-5 content ideas already lined up for the following month
