# Board Meeting Preparation Skill

## Overview

This skill assists with board meeting preparation and governance execution. The board-meeting-prep skill enables agenda development, resolution drafting, materials organization, minutes preparation, and governance best practices to ensure effective board meetings, proper authorization of corporate actions, and comprehensive documentation for corporate records and legal compliance.

## Core Capabilities

### 1. Board Governance Framework

**Board Authority and Responsibilities**

The board of directors is the primary decision-making body in a corporation. State law (Delaware General Corporation Law for Delaware corporations, etc.) vests board with authority to:
- Manage business and affairs of corporation
- Authorize major transactions (contracts, acquisitions, financing)
- Appoint officers and establish compensation
- Declare dividends and authorize stock issuances
- Approve annual budget and strategic plans
- Establish board committees and delegate authority
- Evaluate performance; make major staffing decisions

**Board Fiduciary Duties**

Directors owe fiduciary duties to corporation and shareholders:
- **Duty of Care:** Directors must act with reasonable care; make informed decisions; attend meetings; monitor performance
- **Duty of Loyalty:** Directors must avoid conflicts of interest; act in corporation's best interest (not personal interest); disclose conflicts
- **Duty of Good Faith:** Directors must act in good faith; honest discretion in decision-making

**Business Judgment Rule:** Courts defer to board decisions if directors acted: (1) in good faith, (2) with reasonable care, (3) in honest belief that action is in corporation's best interest. This rule protects directors from liability for business decisions that may not turn out well.

**Protecting Directors Through Board Process:**
- Informed decision-making (adequate information, opportunity to ask questions)
- Disclosure of all material conflicts
- Documentation of deliberations and decisions (board minutes)
- Engagement of advisors where appropriate (legal, financial, valuation)
- Conflict of interest policies and procedures

### 2. Board Meeting Types and Frequency

**Annual Shareholder Meeting**

Required once per calendar year. Shareholder meeting has specific functions:
- Elect directors (if board positions up for election)
- Approve executive compensation (if "say on pay" vote required or customary)
- Ratify audit firm
- Approve equity plan amendments (if applicable)
- Address other matters requiring shareholder approval (mergers, charter amendments, etc.)

**Notice Requirements:** Shareholders entitled to notice 10-60 days before meeting (varies by state); notice includes agenda, proxy materials, voting information.

**Voting:** Shareholders vote in person or by proxy (proxy materials typically sent with notice). Quorum required (percentage of shares entitled to vote); typical 33% but varies by company's bylaws.

---

**Regular Board Meetings (Quarterly)**

Typical for public companies and larger private companies. Quarterly meetings coincide with financial reporting (quarterly earnings announcement). Agenda typically includes:
- Financial results review
- Business review and KPI review
- Management presentation on key initiatives
- Committee reports (Audit, Compensation, Nominating)
- Strategic discussion or special topics
- Executive session (board meets without management)

**Notice Requirements:** Directors entitled to notice per bylaws (typically 48-72 hours); less formal than shareholder meetings.

**Quorum:** Majority of directors (or number specified in bylaws) must be present.

---

**Special Meetings**

Called as-needed for specific matters requiring board action between regular meetings. Examples:
- M&A transactions (acquisition/sale of company)
- Financing (debt or equity raise)
- Major contract approval
- Personnel matters (CEO replacement, key officer changes)
- Urgent matters requiring immediate board action

**Notice:** Directors entitled to notice per bylaws; may be called on short notice (24 hours) if urgent.

---

**Written Consent in Lieu of Meeting**

Alternative to meeting: board takes action via written consent (all directors sign written action approving resolution). Faster than meeting; no need to assemble in person. Common for routine matters or when quick action needed.

### 3. Board Composition and Committee Structure

**Board Size and Independence**

Board size varies by company:
- Private companies: 3-7 directors typical
- Public companies: 7-12 directors typical (larger boards for more specialized committees)

**Director Independence:** Public companies and many venture-backed private companies distinguish between:
- **Independent Directors:** Have no material relationship with management; free from management influence
- **Management Directors:** CEO and other executives serving on board
- **Affiliated Directors:** Have relationship to company/shareholders but not management

Typically target for board independence:
- Public companies: Majority independent (often 2/3+)
- Private companies: Mix of independent and management directors; depends on investor preferences
- Venture-backed: Typically founder/CEO + investor representatives + independent director

**Board Committees**

Larger boards typically establish committees:

**Audit Committee:**
- Reviews financial reporting and controls
- Engages/oversees external auditor
- Reviews internal audit and compliance
- Approves audit plan and reviews audit results
- Addresses internal control findings and management audit fees

**Compensation Committee:**
- Reviews executive compensation and benefits
- Approves CEO compensation (salary, bonus, equity)
- Administers stock option/equity plans
- Reviews executive severance agreements
- Addresses compensation and benefits for all executives

**Nominating/Corporate Governance Committee:**
- Recommends director candidates for election
- Reviews board effectiveness and composition
- Addresses corporate governance policies
- Oversees director education and orientation

**Other Committees:** Depending on business, may include:
- Executive Committee (handles urgent matters between full board meetings)
- Finance Committee (oversees financing, capital structure, treasury matters)
- Risk Committee (oversees risk management, compliance, regulatory matters)
- Product/Technology Committee (for tech companies; oversees product strategy, technical roadmap)

### 4. Board Meeting Agenda Structure

**Typical Board Meeting Agenda**

```
BOARD MEETING AGENDA
[Company Name]
[Date], [Time], [Location/Virtual]

Call to Order and Quorum Determination
[Director states number of directors present; confirms quorum]

Approval of Prior Meeting Minutes
[Board approves minutes from prior board meeting]

Executive Session (optional, if needed)
[Board meets without management to discuss compensation, performance, strategic issues]

CEO/President Report
[CEO provides business update, financial review, strategic commentary]

Financial Review
[CFO presents financial statements, budget status, financial metrics]

Committee Reports
[Audit Committee Chair reports on audit matters, financial controls]
[Compensation Committee Chair reports on compensation matters, equity plan]
[Nominating Committee Chair reports on board governance, director candidates]

Special Topics / Business Items
[Presentation on key business initiative, market, product strategy]

Approval of Resolutions/Authorizations
[Board approves specific resolutions as detailed in agenda]

Other Business
[Any other matters raised by board members]

Adjournment
[Director motions to adjourn; meeting concludes]
```

### 5. Board Resolutions and Authorizations

**What Requires Board Resolution**

Board resolutions document formal board decisions. Matters typically requiring resolution:

**Officers and Compensation:**
- Election/appointment of officers (CEO, CFO, General Counsel, etc.)
- Officer removal
- Officer compensation and bonus
- Change of control severance agreements
- Executive employment agreements

**Equity and Capitalization:**
- Authorization to issue stock or options
- Adoption of stock option/equity plan
- Grant of options to specific employees
- Stock splits or recapitalization
- Preferred stock terms and rights

**Corporate Governance:**
- Adoption/amendment of bylaws
- Establishment of board committees
- Committee charter adoption
- Approval of board meeting schedule
- Conflict of interest policy
- Document retention policy
- Equity grant and compensation policies

**Financial and Contracts:**
- Approval of annual budget
- Authorization of debt issuance
- Banking relationships and authorized signers
- Guarantees or pledges of assets
- Approval of material contracts (above certain threshold)
- Related-party transaction approval

**Corporate Actions:**
- Dividend declaration
- Stock repurchase program
- Merger or acquisition
- Sale of company or assets
- Bankruptcy or dissolution
- Material litigation settlement

**Regulatory/Compliance:**
- Appointment of registered agent
- Appointment of corporate secretary
- Tax elections (S-Corp, etc.)
- SEC compliance certifications (if public or SEC filer)
- Audit committee charter and financial expert designation (if public)

### 6. Resolution Drafting Framework

**Resolution Format**

Standard board resolution follows format:

```
RESOLVED, that [substantive action to be taken, with specific details and authorization]

FURTHER RESOLVED, that [officer responsible for implementation, with authority to take actions necessary to implement this resolution]

FURTHER RESOLVED, that the [specific officer or committee] is authorized to take such further actions and execute such documents as may be necessary or advisable to carry out the intent of this resolution.
```

**Key Elements:**

1. **Who acts** — Which officer or party is authorized to perform action
2. **What is authorized** — Specific action, limits (e.g., "up to $X million"), terms, conditions
3. **Effective date** — When authorization takes effect
4. **Implementation authority** — Officer authorized to sign documents, give notices, take actions to implement
5. **Conditions** — Any conditions that must be satisfied (e.g., legal review, financing commitment, third-party approval)

**Example Resolution (Officer Election):**

```
RESOLVED, that [name] is hereby elected Chief Executive Officer of [company] to serve for a term commencing on [date] and ending on [date] or until such earlier time as such person resigns or is removed by the Board of Directors.

FURTHER RESOLVED, that as Chief Executive Officer, [name] shall receive an annual salary of $[amount], payable in accordance with the company's standard payroll practices, together with such employee benefits as are generally provided to executive officers of the company.

FURTHER RESOLVED, that the President/Board Chair is authorized to negotiate, execute, and deliver an employment agreement with [name] incorporating the terms of this resolution and such other terms as the President/Board Chair deems appropriate and consistent with the company's practices.
```

### 7. Minutes and Record-Keeping

**Board Minutes Documentation**

Board minutes are official records of board actions and decisions. Minutes should document:
- **Attendance:** Directors present and absent; presence of officers/advisors
- **Quorum:** Confirmation quorum present
- **Agenda and actions:** Business transacted, resolutions approved, votes taken
- **Discussions:** Summary of key discussion points (not verbatim, but captures material topics)
- **Decisions:** What was decided and how voted
- **Conflicts:** Disclosure of conflicts and recusals
- **Executive session:** Note that held, topics discussed (without disclosure of specific statements)
- **Certifications:** Secretary signature certifying minutes are accurate

**Minutes Format:**

```
MINUTES OF MEETING OF BOARD OF DIRECTORS
[Company Name]

Date: [Date of Meeting]
Time: [Time]
Location: [Location]

ATTENDANCE:
Present: [List directors present]
Absent: [List directors absent, excused/unexcused]
Also present: [CEO, other officers, legal counsel, advisors]

CALL TO ORDER AND QUORUM
The meeting was called to order at [time]. The Secretary confirmed that a quorum of the Board was present.

APPROVAL OF MINUTES
The Board approved the minutes of the meeting held on [prior meeting date].

EXECUTIVE SESSION
The Board held an executive session to discuss [topic] without management present.

[CEO REPORT / OTHER BUSINESS ITEMS WITH SUMMARIES]

RESOLUTIONS APPROVED
1. [Resolution description] - Voted on and approved unanimously [or with voting details]
2. [Resolution description] - Voted on and approved [details]

ADJOURNMENT
The meeting was adjourned at [time].

_________________________
[Secretary Name]
Secretary
```

**Records Retention and Confidentiality:**

- Board minutes are corporate records; maintain permanently
- Minutes are confidential; not shared except with directors, company officers with need-to-know, corporate counsel
- Privilege: Minutes may be protected by attorney-client privilege if prepared with advice of counsel
- Executive session discussions remain confidential
- Secretary maintains official minutes file; provides copies to new directors, corporate counsel as needed

### 8. Board Package Preparation

**Board materials should be prepared and distributed 5-7 days before meeting:**

**Cover Package:**
- Meeting notice (date, time, location, agenda)
- Draft minutes from prior meeting (for approval)
- Detailed agenda with time allocations
- List of attendees

**Financial Materials:**
- Income statement and balance sheet (actual vs. budget, YTD vs. prior year)
- Cash flow statement
- Key financial metrics dashboard (cash, profitability, growth)
- Revenue and customer analysis
- Expense review

**Committee Materials:**
- Audit Committee: audit plan, internal control assessment, audit findings
- Compensation Committee: compensation review, equity grants, executive employment agreements
- Nominating Committee: board composition, director candidates, governance policies

**Business Information:**
- Executive summary or CEO briefing memo
- Strategic initiative updates
- Key business metrics (KPIs, operational metrics)
- Customer/market information

**Resolutions for Action:**
- Draft resolutions in final form, ready for approval
- Supporting documents (employment agreements, equity grant documents, transaction summaries)
- Background on material decisions (valuation opinions for equity, advisability opinion for M&A)

**Governance Documents:**
- Board charter or committee charters (if being reviewed/amended)
- Conflict of interest policy
- Document retention policy

## Application Framework

Use this skill to:

1. **Determine Meeting Requirements** — Identify whether annual, regular, special, or written consent action
2. **Develop Meeting Agenda** — Organize topics logically; allocate time; ensure all material matters covered
3. **Draft Resolutions** — Prepare board-approved resolutions with proper authority grants
4. **Organize Board Materials** — Compile supporting information, financial data, and documentation
5. **Prepare Minutes Template** — Create framework for accurate minutes documentation
6. **Establish Governance Procedures** — Implement board meeting calendar, notice procedures, materials distribution
7. **Track Action Items** — Document board decisions; follow up on implementation
8. **Maintain Records** — Preserve minutes and supporting documentation
