# M&A Deal Tracker Skill

## Overview

This skill equips corporate attorneys with systematic frameworks for managing complex M&A transactions from initial opportunity through post-closing integration. The deal-tracker enables comprehensive timeline management, milestone coordination, due diligence oversight, closing preparation, and post-closing adjustment tracking.

## Core Capabilities

### 1. Deal Lifecycle Management

M&A transactions progress through distinct phases, each with specific legal, financial, and operational objectives:

**Pre-LOI Phase:** Opportunity identification, preliminary valuation, confidentiality agreements, preliminary due diligence, business model review, and competitive positioning. Focus on scope definition and preliminary deal economics.

**Letter of Intent (LOI) Phase:** Negotiation of binding or non-binding LOI covering purchase price, deal structure, financing contingencies, exclusivity, conduct of business covenants, and expense provisions. The LOI establishes framework for definitive agreement negotiation.

**Due Diligence Phase:** Comprehensive review across legal, financial, tax, operational, and industry-specific areas. Responds to information requests, conducts site visits, reviews material contracts, analyzes compliance status, and validates business assumptions. Duration typically 4-12 weeks depending on complexity.

**Definitive Agreement Negotiation:** Drafting and negotiation of purchase agreement, ancillary agreements (disclosure schedules, representations and warranties insurance, third-party consents), and condition satisfaction. This phase establishes closing mechanics, indemnification, earn-out structures, and risk allocation.

**Regulatory and Third-Party Approvals:** Satisfaction of antitrust clearance, foreign investment review (CFIUS), industry-specific approvals, and third-party consents (material contracts, lending agreements, real property leases). Timeline varies significantly by jurisdiction and deal structure.

**Pre-Closing Phase:** Execution of ancillary agreements, final closing conditions verification, closing condition satisfaction, closing statement preparation, insurance finalization, financing documentation execution, and regulatory filing completion.

**Closing:** Execute closing documents, fund transaction, transfer assets or equity, record organizational changes, and announce transaction publicly (if applicable). Typically 1-3 day process.

**Post-Closing:** Adjustment period for purchase price true-up, earn-out administration, integration execution, regulatory approvals monitoring, and dispute resolution procedures.

### 2. Workstream Coordination

Successful M&A execution requires parallel workstream management across multiple functional areas:

**Legal Workstream:** Handles contract review, regulatory compliance, third-party consents, organizational documentation, closing mechanics, and dispute resolution. Key deliverables include disclosure schedules, ancillary agreements, closing legal opinions, and regulatory filings.

**Financial Workstream:** Manages purchase price valuation, valuation adjustments, working capital schedules, earn-out calculations, financial statement schedules, and post-closing accounting. Works with buyer's accounting team on purchase price allocation.

**Tax Workstream:** Analyzes tax structuring, tax opinions, tax representation and warranty insurance, income tax allocation, sales tax compliance, and tax gross-up calculations. Critical for cross-border transactions and complex deal structures.

**Operational Workstream:** Plans business integration, transition service arrangements, personnel matters, systems integration, customer/supplier communication, and operational continuity. Executes 100-day integration plan post-closing.

**Regulatory and Government Affairs:** Monitors antitrust review, CFIUS filing (if required), environmental compliance, industry-specific approvals, and public disclosure obligations. Manages timeline for satisfaction of closing conditions.

**Communications:** Manages announcements, investor updates, employee communications, customer and supplier notices, and media relations. Coordinates timing with legal, regulatory, and transaction requirements.

### 3. Due Diligence Coordination

Due diligence is the information-gathering phase where buyer validates representations, identifies issues, and establishes pricing adjustments. The tracker organizes due diligence by category:

**Corporate Organization:** Certificate of incorporation, bylaws, minute books, shareholder registers, cap table, equity agreements, board composition, related-party transactions, organizational structure in each jurisdiction.

**Financial Analysis:** Historical financial statements (3-5 years), revenue contracts, customer concentration, accounts receivable aging, inventory valuation, capital expenditures, off-balance sheet liabilities, cash management systems, banking arrangements.

**Tax Compliance:** Federal, state, and local returns (3-5 years), transfer pricing documentation (if applicable), tax audit history, deferred tax positions, tax disputes, sales tax compliance, payroll tax filings, property tax assessments.

**Intellectual Property:** Patents and patent prosecution, trademarks and domain registrations, copyrights, licenses in and licenses out, IP assignments, technology stack review, open source software compliance, trade secrets and confidentiality, IP litigation or disputes.

**Employment and Labor:** Employee roster with compensation, equity plans and option agreements, benefit plans, union agreements, OSHA compliance, employment contracts, severance agreements, pending labor disputes, executive compensation, change of control agreements.

**Litigation and Disputes:** Pending litigation, arbitrations, regulatory investigations, threatened claims, material adverse change assessments, insurance coverage, claim history, settlement agreements.

**Environmental Compliance:** Phase I environmental assessment, air and water permits, waste management, contamination history, environmental litigation, compliance certifications, remediation obligations.

**Regulatory and Compliance:** Industry-specific licenses and permits, regulatory compliance history, outstanding notices of violation, pending regulatory matters, consent requirements, compliance policies and training.

**Material Contracts:** Customer agreements, vendor agreements, financing agreements, lease agreements, joint ventures, partnership agreements, key person agreements, non-competes, distribution agreements, technology licenses.

**Real Property:** Owned versus leased facilities, lease terms and options, environmental assessments, title and surveys, liens and encumbrances, zoning compliance, property condition, capital improvement history.

**Insurance and Risk:** General liability, directors and officers, property, cyber, product liability coverage, claims history, loss runs, retention policies, insurance expiring before closing.

**Data and Technology:** Cloud infrastructure and vendor relationships, cybersecurity posture, compliance with GDPR/CCPA, data backup and disaster recovery, technology roadmap, technical debt assessment, vendor dependencies.

### 4. Milestone Management and Timeline Tracking

Effective deal management requires proactive tracking of key milestones and potential bottlenecks:

**Critical Path Milestones:**
- LOI execution (gates due diligence start)
- Initial information request response (indicates due diligence pace)
- Preliminary findings presentation (enables negotiation strategy)
- Purchase agreement draft delivery (starts definitive agreement phase)
- Final due diligence completion (enables final negotiations)
- Regulatory filings submitted (initiates regulatory clock)
- Regulatory approvals received (removes closing conditions)
- Financing commitment confirmed (validates buyer funding)
- Material consents received (satisfies closing conditions)
- Closing condition satisfactions certified (enables closing)
- Closing executed (transaction complete)

**Responsible Party Assignment:** Identify owner for each milestone, establish accountability, and create escalation protocols for delays.

**Risk Indicators:** Changes in timeline, new issues from due diligence, financing uncertainty, regulatory complications, or third-party consent challenges signal need for timeline adjustment.

### 5. Closing Preparation

Closing requires coordination of multiple document packages and condition verifications:

**Purchase Agreement and Ancillary Documents:** Final negotiated purchase agreement with all schedules, disclosure documents, stock purchase agreements (if equity transaction), asset purchase agreements (if asset transaction), assumption agreements, transition service agreements, indemnification and escrow agreements.

**Financing Documentation:** Credit agreements, commitment letters, condition certificates, borrowing bases, and financing closing documents from buyer's lenders.

**Condition Satisfaction Documentation:** Certificates of good standing from each jurisdiction, corporate resolutions authorizing transaction, certificate of officer regarding representations and warranties, no material adverse change certificate, litigation search results, lien searches, and third-party consent evidence.

**Tax Documentation:** Tax certificates from seller, purchase price allocation (Form 8594), tax opinions (if applicable), withholding analysis for foreign sellers, and tax representation and warranty insurance.

**Closing Statement:** Final accounting of purchase price adjustments for working capital, indebtedness, transaction expenses, and other agreed-upon items. Prepared by financial advisor and reviewed by tax team.

**Title and Insurance:** Title insurance commitment (real property), UCC searches to confirm no liens, insurance payoff letters, and new insurance policies (generally obtained by buyer).

**Regulatory Approvals:** Evidence of antitrust clearance, CFIUS approval, environmental certifications, industry-specific approvals, and third-party consents.

**Employee and Benefits Documentation:** WARN Act compliance (if applicable), severance agreement execution, benefit plan amendments, equity acceleration documentation, and new employment agreements (if required).

**Transition Services:** Transition service agreement specifying IT support, accounting, personnel, facility access, customer transition, and vendor transition services during post-closing period.

### 6. Post-Closing Activities

Post-closing work includes purchase price adjustments, integration execution, and dispute avoidance:

**Purchase Price Adjustments:** Review working capital calculations, resolve disputed items, arbitration of purchase price adjustments per agreement mechanics, funding of adjustment payments or refunds.

**Earn-Out Administration:** Track performance metrics, calculate earn-out payments at each milestone, resolve disputes over achievement of conditions, manage time limits for earn-out claims.

**Integration Execution:** Implement business integration plan, manage customer and supplier transitions, consolidate financial systems, align organizational structures, complete personnel transitions, realize identified synergies.

**Indemnification Claims:** Monitor representation and warranty insurance, evaluate potential claims, prepare claim packages, pursue indemnification for breaches, manage coverage disputes, establish escrow or holdback procedures.

**Regulatory Compliance:** Ensure ongoing compliance post-closing, monitor regulatory developments, maintain licenses and permits, execute pending required filings.

**Documentation and Record Retention:** Maintain organized deal records, retain workpapers, index closing documents, establish escrow procedures, and preserve evidence for potential disputes.

## Application Framework

Use this skill to:

1. **Organize Deal Information:** Create centralized repository for deal documents, workstream updates, milestone tracking, and issue logs
2. **Manage Timeline and Accountability:** Assign owners to milestones, track completion, identify delays, and escalate risks
3. **Coordinate Workstreams:** Ensure legal, financial, tax, and operational work progresses in parallel; identify dependencies
4. **Prepare for Closing:** Verify all closing conditions, organize closing document packages, coordinate final negotiations
5. **Track Post-Closing Items:** Manage purchase price adjustments, earn-outs, indemnification claims, and integration activities
6. **Mitigate Risk:** Identify potential deal risks early, develop mitigation strategies, and escalate material issues to deal team

## Common Issues and Solutions

**Timeline Slippage:** Identify bottleneck workstreams (often financing or regulatory), assign dedicated resources, establish weekly milestone reviews, consider parallel path options, and adjust closing timeline expectations.

**Due Diligence Surprises:** Material issues discovered late in process require valuation adjustment, price reduction negotiation, representation and warranty insurance enhancement, or deal restructuring. Establish preliminary findings review at 50% completion point.

**Financing Contingencies:** Confirm financing commitment letters early, establish lender condition satisfaction timeline, manage lender's due diligence separately from buyer's due diligence, and negotiate specific performance rights if applicable.

**Regulatory Complications:** File for antitrust clearance early, establish contingency timeline for extended review, develop mitigation strategies if antitrust issues identified, consider transaction restructuring, and maintain regulatory expert engagement.

**Third-Party Consents:** Identify consent requirements early (material contracts, real property leases, lending agreements, licenses), develop consent request strategy, consider waiver or substitution provisions, and establish consent completion deadline.

**Seller Indemnification:** Consider representation and warranty insurance to shift risk from buyer, negotiate indemnification cap and duration, establish escrow holdback, define specific indemnified matters, and establish dispute resolution procedures.
