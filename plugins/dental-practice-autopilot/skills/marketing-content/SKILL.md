# Marketing Content

Seasonal campaigns, social media content, blog posts, and patient communications for dental practices.

## Dental Marketing Calendar

### Monthly Themes and Hooks

| Month | Health Observance | Campaign Theme Ideas |
|-------|------------------|---------------------|
| January | New Year | "New Year, New Smile" — cosmetic consultations, whitening specials |
| February | Children's Dental Health Month | Pediatric focus, school partnerships, fun kid content |
| March | Oral Cancer Awareness | Screening awareness, early detection education |
| April | Oral Health Month / Facial Protection | Sports guards, general oral health tips, community events |
| May | Mother's Day | "Give Mom a Reason to Smile" — gift certificates, cosmetic packages |
| June | Father's Day / Summer | "Dad Jokes and Dad Smiles" — family fun content |
| July | Summer / Independence Day | Summer smile makeover, teeth-friendly BBQ tips |
| August | Back to School | Sports guards, pediatric exams, braces consultations |
| September | Fall schedule | Catch-up on postponed treatment before holidays |
| October | National Dental Hygiene Month / Halloween | Candy buyback, hygiene education, team appreciation |
| November | Diabetes Awareness Month | Oral-systemic health connection, perio awareness |
| December | Year-End Benefits | "Use it or lose it" insurance push, holiday whitening specials |

## Content Types and Best Practices

### Social Media (Facebook/Instagram)

**Post frequency:** 3-5x per week
**Content mix:**
- 40% Educational (dental tips, myth-busting, procedure explanations)
- 25% Social proof (before/after with consent, patient testimonials, reviews)
- 20% Behind-the-scenes (team spotlights, office culture, community events)
- 15% Promotional (specials, services, new patient offers)

**What works in dental social media:**
- Before/after photos (with written patient consent — HIPAA!)
- Short video clips: office tour, meet-the-team, quick dental tips
- Relatable humor about dental experiences
- Team celebrations and milestones
- Community involvement
- User-generated content (patients who tag the practice)

**What to avoid:**
- Clinical gore (blood, extracted teeth, surgical photos)
- Fear-based messaging ("If you don't floss, THIS happens")
- Stock photos that look obviously fake
- All promotional content with no educational or engaging posts
- Posting without proofreading (dental professionals misspelling dental terms is a bad look)

### Blog Posts

Target 800-1,200 words. Optimize for local SEO:
- Include city/neighborhood name naturally
- Use questions patients actually ask as titles
- Structure with headers for featured snippets
- Include a clear CTA (book an appointment, call for a consultation)

**Evergreen blog topics that drive traffic:**
- "How Much Does a [Procedure] Cost in [City]?"
- "Does a [Procedure] Hurt? What to Actually Expect"
- "How to Choose a Dentist in [City]"
- "Dental Insurance 101: Understanding Your Benefits"
- "[Procedure A] vs. [Procedure B]: Which Is Right for You?"

### Email Newsletters

**Frequency:** Monthly (more than that and unsubscribe rates spike)
**Format:**
- 1 featured article or tip
- 1 practice update or team spotlight
- 1 seasonal offer or reminder
- CTA to book an appointment
- Keep total read time under 3 minutes

### New Patient Offers

Common effective offers:
- Free or discounted new patient exam + X-rays (D0150 + D0210 or D0330)
- Complimentary whitening with completed treatment plan
- Free second opinion
- Discounted cosmetic consultation
- Referral bonuses (gift card, credit toward treatment)

**Legal note:** Check state dental board advertising regulations. Some states restrict:
- Use of the word "free"
- Bait-and-switch pricing
- Guarantees on treatment outcomes
- Testimonials with implied guarantees
