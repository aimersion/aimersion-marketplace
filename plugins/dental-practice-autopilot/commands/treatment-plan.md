Create a patient-friendly treatment plan presentation for: $ARGUMENTS

## Process

1. **Gather Details** — Ask the user for:
   - Patient's diagnosis or condition (cavities, periodontal disease, missing teeth, cracked tooth, etc.)
   - Recommended treatment(s) with CDT codes if available
   - Estimated costs and insurance coverage
   - Number of visits required
   - Any alternatives to discuss
   - Patient's known concerns (cost, anxiety, time, aesthetics)

2. **Generate Patient-Friendly Explanation** — Produce a document that includes:

   **What's Going On (Diagnosis)**
   - Plain-language explanation of the condition (no jargon)
   - Why it matters — what happens if untreated (honest but not fear-based)
   - Use analogies patients understand ("Think of it like a small crack in your windshield — it's easier to fix now before it spreads")

   **What We Recommend (Treatment)**
   - Step-by-step explanation of each procedure in plain language
   - What the patient will experience (what they'll feel, how long it takes, recovery)
   - Common questions and answers for this specific treatment
   - Pain management and comfort options (numbing, sedation options if available)

   **Treatment Options Comparison** (if alternatives exist)
   - Option A (recommended): pros, cons, longevity, cost range
   - Option B (alternative): pros, cons, longevity, cost range
   - Option C (minimum intervention): what it does and doesn't address
   - Present without judgment — let the patient choose

   **Investment and Insurance**
   - Total estimated cost broken down by procedure
   - Estimated insurance coverage (if applicable)
   - Patient's estimated out-of-pocket
   - Payment plan options (if the practice offers them)
   - Financing options (CareCredit, Lending Club, in-house plans)
   - Frame as "investment in your health" not just a cost

   **Timeline**
   - Visual timeline of appointments and recovery
   - How to schedule and what to expect at each visit

3. **Format Options** — Offer to produce as:
   - Printable one-page handout (for chairside presentation)
   - Email-friendly version (for patients who want to "think about it")
   - Detailed version with FAQ (for the thorough researcher patient)

Tone: empathetic, empowering, educational. Never condescending. Help the patient feel informed and confident, not pressured.
