# Compliance Documentation for Financial Advisors

Create thorough, audit-ready compliance documentation including meeting notes, suitability analyses, ADV disclosures, and supervisory records.

## Documentation Types

### Client Meeting Notes
1. **Header** — Date, time, duration, attendees, meeting type (annual review, initial consultation, ad-hoc, phone call).
2. **Client situation update** — Changes in employment, family, health, financial goals, risk tolerance, time horizon.
3. **Topics discussed** — Summarize each topic with enough detail for a regulator to understand the conversation.
4. **Recommendations made** — Document every recommendation with rationale tied to client's stated objectives and risk profile.
5. **Client decisions** — Record what the client agreed to, declined, or deferred. Note any instructions received.
6. **Action items** — Who will do what by when. Distinguish advisor actions from client actions.
7. **Next meeting** — Scheduled date or timeframe for next contact.

### Suitability Documentation
1. **Client profile** — Age, income, net worth, tax bracket, investment experience, time horizon, liquidity needs, risk tolerance (conservative/moderate/aggressive), investment objectives (growth/income/preservation).
2. **Recommendation details** — Specific products or strategies recommended with cost/fee disclosure.
3. **Suitability rationale** — Explicit connection between recommendation and client profile. Why is this suitable? What alternatives were considered?
4. **Risk disclosure** — Material risks of the recommendation disclosed to the client.
5. **Concentration check** — Does this create concentration risk? Document if position exceeds 10% of portfolio.

### ADV Updates
1. **Material changes** — Fee schedule changes, disciplinary events, new conflicts of interest, change in ownership, advisory business changes.
2. **Annual update checklist** — AUM update, fee schedule review, conflict of interest review, business practice changes.
3. **Delivery documentation** — Record of ADV Part 2A/2B delivery to clients with dates and method.

## Best Practices

- Write contemporaneously (same day as meeting when possible)
- Use objective language, avoid editorializing
- Document the "why" behind every recommendation
- Note when a client acts against advice
- Record verbal communications, not just written
- Keep documentation for minimum 5 years (SEC) or longer per state requirements

## Regulatory Framework Notes

- SEC Rule 204-2: Books and records requirement
- SEC Regulation Best Interest (Reg BI) for broker-dealers
- Fiduciary standard for RIAs under Advisers Act
- State-specific requirements vary — note applicable state
