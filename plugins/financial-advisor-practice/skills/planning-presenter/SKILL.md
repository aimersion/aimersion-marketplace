# Financial Plan Presentation

Create clear, compelling presentations of financial plans, retirement projections, estate planning summaries, and other planning deliverables for client delivery.

## Presentation Types

### Comprehensive Financial Plan Summary
1. **Executive summary** — One-page overview: client goals, current financial snapshot, key recommendations, projected outcomes.
2. **Net worth statement** — Assets, liabilities, net worth with year-over-year comparison.
3. **Cash flow analysis** — Income sources, fixed expenses, discretionary spending, savings rate, surplus/deficit.
4. **Retirement projection** — Monte Carlo or deterministic projection showing probability of success, income replacement ratio, Social Security optimization strategy, required savings rate adjustments.
5. **Investment strategy** — Recommended asset allocation, investment policy summary, risk/return expectations.
6. **Insurance review** — Life, disability, LTC, P&C coverage assessment with gap analysis.
7. **Estate planning** — Current documents status, beneficiary review, estate tax exposure, recommended actions.
8. **Tax planning** — Current-year strategies, multi-year Roth conversion analysis, charitable giving opportunities.
9. **Action plan** — Prioritized list of recommended actions with timeline.

### Retirement Income Plan
1. **Income sources timeline** — Visual showing when each income source begins (Social Security, pension, annuity, portfolio withdrawals).
2. **Withdrawal strategy** — Account sequencing (taxable → tax-deferred → tax-free), dynamic withdrawal rate, guardrails approach.
3. **Tax bracket management** — Show projected tax brackets through retirement, Roth conversion opportunities.
4. **Healthcare costs** — Medicare timeline, supplemental insurance, projected out-of-pocket costs.
5. **Longevity scenarios** — Show plan under various life expectancy assumptions.

### Estate Planning Summary
1. **Current estate structure** — Ownership and titling of major assets.
2. **Document inventory** — Wills, trusts, POAs, healthcare directives — status and dates.
3. **Beneficiary audit** — All beneficiary designations across retirement accounts, insurance, TOD/POD accounts.
4. **Estate tax analysis** — Gross estate, applicable exclusions, projected exposure.
5. **Trust structure comparison** — If recommending trusts, compare revocable vs. irrevocable, SLAT, GRAT, ILIT, etc. with plain-language descriptions.
6. **Gifting strategies** — Annual exclusion gifts, 529 superfunding, charitable giving vehicles.

## Presentation Guidelines

- Use visuals over tables where possible (charts, timelines, gauges).
- Lead with the "why" — connect every recommendation to the client's stated goals.
- Use scenario analysis (optimistic/base/pessimistic) to show range of outcomes.
- Include assumptions page with all inputs clearly stated.
- Make it actionable — every section should end with clear next steps.

## Compliance Notes

- Projections are hypothetical and not guarantees of future results.
- Clearly state all assumptions (return rates, inflation, tax rates, life expectancy).
- Note that tax situations are unique and clients should consult their tax advisor.
- Financial plans are based on information provided and should be updated with changes.
