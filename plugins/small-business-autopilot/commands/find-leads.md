Research and qualify potential customers/leads based on the user's criteria.

## Steps

1. **Define the search** — Parse "$ARGUMENTS" for industry and location. If not provided, ask:
   - What industry or type of business are you targeting?
   - What geographic area? (city, state, region)
   - What size businesses? (solo, small team, mid-size)
   - What makes someone an ideal customer for you?

2. **Load the lead-researcher skill** for research methodology and qualification framework.

3. **Research prospects** — Use web search to find businesses matching the criteria:
   - Search for "[industry] [location]" and related terms
   - Look for businesses showing buying signals (growth, hiring, recent changes)
   - Find business owner names and roles where possible
   - Check for online presence quality as a qualification signal

4. **Qualify each prospect** — Score on the 5-dimension framework (Fit, Need, Budget, Timing, Access) on a 1–5 scale each.

5. **Deliver the prospect list** organized by score:

```
🎯 PROSPECT RESEARCH REPORT
Industry: [industry]
Location: [location]
Date: [date]
Prospects found: [n]

🔥 HOT LEADS (Score 20+)

[Business Name] — Score: [n]/25
  Industry: [type] | Location: [city]
  Why they fit: [1-2 sentences]
  Outreach angle: [suggested approach]
  Contact: [name/role if found]
  Website: [URL]

🟡 WARM LEADS (Score 15-19)
[Same format]

🟢 COOL LEADS (Score 10-14)
[Condensed: name, score, one-line note]
```

6. **Recommend outreach** — For the top 3 hot leads, suggest:
   - Which channel to use (email, LinkedIn, phone)
   - What to say in the first message
   - What piece of content or value to lead with

Aim to find at least 10 qualified prospects per search.
