# Email Campaign Builder

Create high-converting email marketing sequences tailored to small business audiences.

## Core Workflow

1. **Define the campaign** — type, audience segment, goal, and number of emails
2. **Outline the sequence** — map each email's purpose, timing, and CTA
3. **Draft each email** — subject line, preview text, body, CTA
4. **Present for review** — show all emails in sequence order with send timing

## Campaign Types

### Welcome Series (3–5 emails)
Goal: Convert new subscribers into engaged customers.

| Email | Timing | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Immediate | Welcome + deliver promised lead magnet + set expectations |
| 2 | Day 2 | Brand story — who you are, why you started, what you believe |
| 3 | Day 4 | Value email — best tip, resource, or insight (no selling) |
| 4 | Day 7 | Social proof — customer story or testimonial |
| 5 | Day 10 | Soft offer — introduce product/service with a new-subscriber incentive |

### Promotional Campaign (3–4 emails)
Goal: Drive sales for a specific product, service, or event.

| Email | Timing | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Launch day | Announce the offer — what, why, and the hook |
| 2 | Day 2–3 | Deeper dive — features, benefits, FAQ, social proof |
| 3 | Day 5–6 | Objection handling — address common hesitations |
| 4 | Final day | Urgency — last chance, scarcity, deadline reminder |

### Re-Engagement Campaign (3 emails)
Goal: Win back inactive subscribers before they churn.

| Email | Timing | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Day 0 | "We miss you" — reminder of value, what they're missing |
| 2 | Day 4 | Special incentive — exclusive offer to come back |
| 3 | Day 8 | Final notice — "Should we remove you?" (triggers loss aversion) |

### Newsletter (recurring, single email)
Goal: Maintain engagement with regular value delivery.

Structure each issue with:
- One primary story or insight (60% of content)
- 2–3 quick links or tips (30%)
- One CTA — product mention, event, or ask (10%)

## Email Writing Guidelines

### Subject Lines
- 6–10 words maximum
- Create curiosity or promise a specific benefit
- Personalize when possible (first name, company, location)
- A/B test format: write 3 subject line options per email
- Avoid spam triggers: ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation, "free", "act now"

### Preview Text
- 40–90 characters
- Complement (don't repeat) the subject line
- Add context that makes the open more compelling

### Body Structure
1. **Hook** (first 2 sentences) — connect to the reader's problem or desire
2. **Value** — the main content, tip, story, or offer
3. **CTA** — one clear, specific action. Use a button-style call to action.
4. **P.S.** — optional secondary CTA or urgency reminder

### Tone
- Write like a helpful friend, not a corporation
- Use "you" more than "we"
- Short paragraphs (1–3 sentences)
- Break up text with subheadings for longer emails

### CTA Best Practices
- One primary CTA per email (secondary in P.S. only)
- Use action verbs: "Get your free guide", "Book your spot", "Shop the sale"
- Create urgency when genuine: deadlines, limited spots, seasonal relevance

## Deliverable Format

Present each email in this format:

```
--- Email [N] of [Total] ---
Send timing: [e.g., Day 2 after signup]
Subject line options:
  A: [option]
  B: [option]
  C: [option]
Preview text: [text]

[Full email body]

CTA: [button text] → [destination description]
```
