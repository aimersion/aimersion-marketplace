# Content Writer

Write content that earns customers — not just traffic. Every word should serve one of 
three jobs: make the reader feel understood, build trust that you're the right choice, 
or move them to take action.

## The Small Business Content Reality

Most small business content is written from the owner's perspective ("We offer high-quality 
services with 20 years of experience"). Customers don't care about that. They care about 
their problem, their hesitation, and whether you're the right person to solve it.

Shift every piece from "here's what we do" to "here's what changes for you."

## Required Inputs

Before writing, establish:
- **Business type and location**: What do they do, who do they serve, what geography?
- **Target customer**: Who specifically — homeowner, business owner, parent, etc.?
- **Customer's top problem/pain**: What's the situation that makes them search for this?
- **Unique differentiator**: Why this business over every competitor nearby?
- **Target keyword** (for SEO content): The specific phrase people search for
- **Desired action**: What should the reader do after reading?

## Blog Post Writing

### Structure for Local/Small Business SEO Posts

```
Title: [Primary keyword + local modifier + compelling angle]
Example: "How Much Does a Kitchen Remodel Cost in [City]? Real Numbers from 2024-2025"

Meta description: [155 characters — includes keyword, answers the question, implies benefit]

Opening paragraph (75-100 words):
- Open by naming the exact situation the reader is in
- Validate their question/concern as smart and legitimate  
- Preview specifically what this post will answer
- Include primary keyword naturally

H2: [First major subtopic — usually the most-searched question]
[200-400 words per section — specific, practical, scannable]

H2: [Second major subtopic]

H2: [Third major subtopic]

H2: Common questions answered (FAQ format — captures "People Also Ask" rankings)

H2: Next steps / How to get started
- Specific action the reader can take today
- Soft CTA: link to contact, quote form, or phone number
```

### SEO Rules for Small Business Content
- Primary keyword in: title, first 100 words, one H2, meta description
- Local modifier in title and first paragraph for local businesses
- Length: 800-1,200 words for most local topics; 1,500-2,500 for competitive national keywords
- Internal links: 1-2 links to your services pages or other blog posts
- Include real specifics: local landmarks, price ranges, typical timelines — generic content ranks nowhere
- Every post should end with a low-friction CTA (free estimate, free consultation, call us)

### Blog Topics That Drive Leads (not just traffic)
- "How much does [service] cost in [city]?" → High purchase intent
- "[Service] near me: what to look for" → High purchase intent
- "[Problem] — DIY vs hiring a professional" → Catches fence-sitters
- "How long does [service] take?" → Addresses a common hesitation
- "[Service] checklist / what to expect" → Builds trust before they buy
- "[Competitor comparison] vs [your service type]" → Captures active shoppers

## Landing Page Copy

### The Only Structure That Converts

```
HERO SECTION
Headline: [Outcome they want, in their words — not your service name]
Good: "No More [Pain] — [Outcome] in [Timeframe]"
Good: "Finally, [City]'s [Category] that [Specific Differentiator]"
Bad: "Welcome to [Company Name]" 
Bad: "[Service] Services in [City]"

Subheadline: [One sentence explaining who you help and the key benefit]

CTA button: [Specific action — "Get My Free Estimate" not "Contact Us"]

PROBLEM SECTION (50-100 words)
"If you've ever [specific frustrating situation], you know that feeling of..."
Name their exact pain. Make them feel seen. Don't sell yet.

SOLUTION SECTION (75-125 words)
Introduce your service as the answer — but frame it by the transformation, not the features.
"That's why we built [service] around [key principle that solves the problem]."

BENEFITS (3-5 items, each with a headline + 1 sentence)
Format: [Outcome] — [How/Why]
Good: "Done in one day — Our crew works [X hours] straight so you're not living in chaos for weeks"
Bad: "Fast Service — We work quickly"

TRUST BUILDERS
- Number of customers served / years in business / specific stat
- 2-3 customer quotes (specific, outcome-focused, real names if possible)
- Logos, certifications, or recognizable clients

FAQ (3-5 questions)
Use actual questions from your customers, not what you wish they'd ask.

FINAL CTA
Repeat the offer with clear next step.
Add any urgency that's genuine (limited slots, seasonal pricing, etc.)
```

## Product/Service Descriptions

```
Opening line: [The transformation or outcome — what changes after using this?]
How it works: [3 simple steps or features — keep it scannable]
For whom: [Specific customer type this is right for]
What's included: [Specific deliverables — not vague "comprehensive service"]
CTA: [Specific next step]
```

Never describe a service without answering: "What specifically happens and what does the 
customer have at the end?" Vague copy kills conversions.

## About Page (The Most Misunderstood Page)

Most about pages are written FOR the business owner, not the customer.

```
Opening: Start with THEM, not you.
"If you're looking for [what they want], and you're tired of [what they've experienced before]..."

Origin: Why this business exists (the real story — frustration, gap in market, personal 
experience that drove you to start it)

Mission: What you actually stand for (specific — not "excellence" or "quality")

How you're different: ONE specific differentiator with proof
Not: "We care about our customers"
Yes: "Every project gets [specific thing] that most [competitors] skip"

Team/Owner: Brief, with personality — a photo makes a 40% conversion difference

Trust: Years in business, certifications, community involvement

CTA: "Here's how to work with us" → link to contact/service page
```

## Google Business Profile Posts

Post weekly for local SEO benefit. Each post:
- 150-300 words
- Include primary service keyword + city name naturally
- Lead with customer benefit, not business announcement
- Clear CTA with phone number or link
- Rotate between: tips, before/after, seasonal reminders, promotions, answers to common questions

## Quality Check

Before delivering any piece:
- [ ] Written from the customer's perspective, not the owner's?
- [ ] Does it name the specific problem or hesitation customers have?
- [ ] Are all claims specific enough to be credible? (no "best" or "highest quality" without proof)
- [ ] Is there exactly one clear CTA?
- [ ] Would a competitor's customer read this and feel understood?
- [ ] Is the reading level accessible? (aim for 7th-8th grade)
