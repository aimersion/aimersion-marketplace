# Marketing Campaigns

Flu shot drives, clinical service promotion, wellness events, and community marketing for independent pharmacies.

## The Independent Pharmacy Advantage

Independent pharmacies win on relationships, not volume. Marketing should emphasize:
- Personal relationships (patients know the pharmacist by name)
- Clinical expertise (pharmacists are the most accessible healthcare provider)
- Community presence (local ownership, community investment)
- Services chains don't offer (compounding, delivery, packaging, extended counseling)

## Service-Based Campaign Frameworks

### Immunization Campaigns

**Flu Season (September-March)**
Target: every patient picking up a prescription during flu season

Marketing tactics:
- Ask at every pickup: "Have you gotten your flu shot yet?"
- Bag stuffers starting September 1
- Window signage: "Walk-In Flu Shots Available — No Appointment Needed"
- Social media: post weekly during season
- Partner with local businesses: offer on-site flu clinics
- Senior-targeted outreach: high-dose/adjuvanted options for 65+
- Text/email blast to patient database
- Google Business Profile: update with flu shot availability

Track: shots given per week, revenue per shot, patient acquisition (new patients who came for flu shot)

**Other Immunization Opportunities:**
- COVID boosters (ongoing)
- Shingrix (shingles — lucrative, 2-dose series, target 50+)
- Pneumococcal (PCV20/PPSV23 for 65+ and at-risk)
- Tdap (every 10 years, pregnant women each pregnancy)
- HPV (catch-up through age 45)
- Travel vaccines (if stocked and trained)
- Back-to-school required immunizations

### MTM (Medication Therapy Management) Services
Target: Medicare Part D patients who qualify for CMR (Comprehensive Medication Review)

Marketing:
- Identify eligible patients through plan outreach lists
- Phone call from pharmacist: "Medicare has approved a free medication review for you"
- Frame as a benefit they've already paid for, not a sales pitch
- In-store signage: "Ask about your FREE Medicare medication review"
- Partner with prescribers: send postcards co-branded with local physician offices

Revenue: $50-150 per CMR, plus improved star ratings and DIR fee savings

### Point-of-Care Testing
If offering CLIA-waived testing (rapid strep, flu, COVID, A1C, lipid panel, INR):

Marketing:
- "Skip the waiting room" messaging
- Walk-in convenience
- Competitive pricing displayed
- Partner with local employers for workplace wellness
- Social media: "Got a sore throat? Get tested here in 15 minutes"

### Wellness Events
Monthly or quarterly community health events:

- Free blood pressure screening day
- Diabetes risk assessment
- Medication clean-out (bring unused/expired medications for safe disposal)
- Heart health month screening (February)
- Bone density screening
- Smoking cessation counseling sign-ups
- Senior health fair (partner with aging services, home health, durable medical equipment)

## Marketing Channels for Pharmacies

### In-Store (Highest ROI — Captive Audience)
- Counter signage and shelf talkers
- Prescription bag inserts
- Waiting area displays and educational materials
- Staff verbal promotion during dispensing ("Did you know we also offer...")
- Checkout counter product displays

### Digital
- Google Business Profile (keep updated with hours, services, photos, and posts)
- Facebook (primary social platform for pharmacy demographic)
- Email/text blasts to patient database
- Website with service pages optimized for local SEO
- Online prescription refill/transfer tools

### Community
- Sponsorship of local events (health fairs, 5K runs, school events)
- Partnerships with physician offices, senior centers, churches
- Local newspaper columns or health tips
- Radio spots (effective in smaller markets)
- Chamber of commerce involvement
