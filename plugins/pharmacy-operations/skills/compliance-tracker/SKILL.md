# Compliance Tracker

DEA, state board of pharmacy, and HIPAA regulatory compliance tracking for independent pharmacies.

## DEA Compliance (Controlled Substances Act)

### Registration Requirements
- DEA registration must be renewed every 3 years (Form DEA-224a)
- Registration must be prominently displayed
- Any change of address, name, or schedule authority requires notification
- Pharmacist-in-charge change must be reported

### Controlled Substance Schedules

| Schedule | Characteristics | Pharmacy Rules | Examples |
|----------|----------------|---------------|---------|
| II | High abuse potential, accepted medical use | DEA-222 for ordering, perpetual inventory recommended, no refills | Oxycodone, hydromorphone, fentanyl, methylphenidate, amphetamines |
| III | Moderate abuse potential | Standard ordering, 5 refills in 6 months | Testosterone, buprenorphine, ketamine, acetaminophen w/ codeine |
| IV | Lower abuse potential | Standard ordering, 5 refills in 6 months | Benzodiazepines, zolpidem, tramadol (federal), carisoprodol |
| V | Lowest abuse potential | Standard ordering, 5 refills in 6 months, some OTC in some states | Pregabalin, lacosamide, some cough preparations |

### Record-Keeping Requirements
- **Biennial inventory:** Physical count of all controlled substances every 2 years from initial inventory date. Schedule II: exact count. Schedule III-V: estimated count allowed (unless container holds > 1,000 — then exact count).
- **Perpetual inventory (recommended):** Running tally of Schedule II receipts and dispensing. Many states require this.
- **Invoices:** Schedule II invoices filed separately or readily retrievable. Schedule III-V invoices must be readily retrievable.
- **Prescriptions:** Schedule II prescriptions filed separately. Schedule III-V filed separately or stamped with red "C" if intermingled with non-controlled.
- **Retention:** All records minimum 2 years (many states require longer — check your state).

### Theft and Loss Reporting
- Discover theft/significant loss → Report to DEA on DEA-106 form
- Report to local law enforcement
- Document circumstances and corrective actions
- Report to state board of pharmacy per state requirements

### PDMP (Prescription Drug Monitoring Program)
- Check PDMP per state requirements (before dispensing all controlled substances in most states)
- Document PDMP checks in pharmacy records
- Many states now require check before EVERY controlled substance dispensing
- Interstate PDMP data sharing — check neighboring states for patients near borders

## State Board of Pharmacy Compliance

State requirements vary significantly. Key universal areas:

### Pharmacist-in-Charge (PIC) Responsibilities
- Overall compliance responsibility for the pharmacy
- Ensure proper staffing levels
- Maintain records and inventory
- Report changes to the board
- Ensure staff competency and proper supervision

### Technician Supervision
- Know your state's technician-to-pharmacist ratio (commonly 3:1 or 4:1)
- Ensure all technicians are registered/certified per state requirements
- Verify continuing education compliance
- Document training and competency assessments

### Dispensing Standards
- Prescription labeling requirements (state-specific elements)
- Patient counseling requirements (OBRA '90 mandates offer to counsel)
- Prospective drug utilization review (DUR) on every prescription
- Generic substitution rules (state-specific: may vs. shall substitute, DAW codes)
- Transfer rules (number of transfers allowed, documentation requirements)
- Partial fill rules (especially for controlled substances — state and federal)
- Emergency supply provisions and documentation

## HIPAA Compliance

### Required Documentation
1. **Notice of Privacy Practices** — Displayed and available to all patients
2. **Business Associate Agreements** — With every vendor that handles PHI
3. **Risk Assessment** — Annual security risk assessment
4. **Breach Notification Policy** — Procedures for identifying and reporting breaches
5. **Training Documentation** — Annual HIPAA training for all staff
6. **Policies and Procedures** — Written privacy and security policies

### Physical Safeguards
- Prescription bins and bags not visible/accessible to unauthorized persons
- Computer screens positioned away from public view
- Fax machine in secure area
- Prescription labels and paperwork not visible on counters
- Consultation area with reasonable privacy for counseling
- Secure shredding/destruction of PHI documents

### Technical Safeguards
- Unique user IDs for all system users
- Automatic logoff on unattended workstations
- Encrypted electronic PHI transmission
- Audit trail capabilities on pharmacy management system
- Regular password changes
- Anti-virus and firewall protection
