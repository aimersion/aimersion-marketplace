# Clinical Services Builder

Design MTM, immunization, point-of-care testing, and other clinical service programs for independent pharmacies.

## The Clinical Services Opportunity

Independent pharmacies that add clinical services see 15-30% higher gross margins on those services compared to dispensing. Clinical services also:
- Differentiate from chains and mail-order
- Build patient loyalty (services create relationships, not just transactions)
- Generate new revenue streams independent of PBM reimbursement
- Position the pharmacy for value-based care models
- Leverage the pharmacist's clinical training

## Service Types and Requirements

### Medication Therapy Management (MTM)

**What it is:** Comprehensive medication review and optimization by a pharmacist. Includes identifying drug therapy problems, resolving issues, and providing ongoing monitoring.

**Revenue model:**
- Medicare Part D CMR (Comprehensive Medication Review): $50-150 per review
- Targeted Medication Reviews (TMRs): $25-50 each
- Private payer MTM (some commercial plans now reimburse)
- Self-pay MTM ($75-150 per session for uninsured)
- Platform-based MTM (OutcomesMTM, Mirixa/Tabula Rasa)

**Requirements:**
- Pharmacist training (APHA MTM certificate or equivalent)
- MTM platform registration (OutcomesMTM, Mirixa)
- Documentation system for MAP (Medication Action Plan) and PMR (Personal Medication Record)
- Quiet consultation area
- Dedicated time blocks (30-60 min per CMR)

**Implementation steps:**
1. Train pharmacists in MTM delivery and documentation
2. Register with MTM platforms
3. Identify eligible patients (Medicare Part D with multiple medications and conditions)
4. Schedule CMR appointments
5. Conduct reviews, document findings, and send recommendations to prescribers
6. Bill through MTM platform
7. Follow up on accepted recommendations

### Immunization Services

**Revenue:** $15-40 administration fee per vaccine (above drug cost), plus the professional service builds traffic and loyalty.

**Requirements:**
- Pharmacist immunization training (APhA Immunization Certificate)
- State protocol or collaborative practice agreement (depending on state)
- Proper vaccine storage (VFC-compliant refrigerator with data logger, or at minimum monitored refrigerator)
- Emergency kit (epinephrine, diphenhydramine, blood pressure monitoring)
- VAERS reporting capability
- State immunization registry reporting
- Liability insurance covering immunization services
- CLIA waiver (if performing pre-immunization screening tests)

**Vaccines commonly offered by pharmacies:**
- Influenza (all formulations)
- COVID-19
- Shingrix (shingles)
- Pneumococcal (PCV20, PPSV23)
- Tdap/Td
- Hepatitis A and B
- HPV (expanding to pharmacies in more states)
- Meningococcal
- RSV (for 60+ and pregnant women)

### Point-of-Care Testing (POCT)

**What it is:** CLIA-waived diagnostic tests performed in the pharmacy with immediate results.

**Requirements:**
- CLIA Certificate of Waiver ($180 biennial fee)
- State authorization for pharmacist testing
- Collaborative practice agreement (for test-and-treat in states that allow it)
- Staff training on each test method
- Quality control procedures
- Proper specimen handling and biohazard waste disposal

**Common pharmacy POCT:**

| Test | Revenue/Test | Use Case | Turnaround |
|------|-------------|----------|-----------|
| Rapid Influenza | $20-40 | Flu season test-and-treat | 15 min |
| Rapid Strep | $20-40 | Sore throat test-and-treat | 5-10 min |
| COVID-19 Rapid Antigen | $20-40 | Symptomatic screening | 15 min |
| A1C | $25-50 | Diabetes monitoring/screening | 5 min |
| Lipid Panel | $25-50 | Cardiovascular risk screening | 5 min |
| INR | $15-25 | Warfarin monitoring | 1 min |
| Blood Glucose | $10-15 | Diabetes screening | 30 sec |
| Blood Pressure | Free-$10 | Hypertension screening | 5 min |

### Smoking Cessation Program

**Revenue:** Counseling fees ($25-50/session), NRT product sales, prescription smoking cessation medications, some state Medicaid programs reimburse pharmacist cessation counseling.

**Program structure:**
1. Brief intervention at dispensing (for all patients with smoking-related prescriptions)
2. Individual counseling sessions (4-8 sessions over 12 weeks)
3. Medication management (NRT selection, varenicline/bupropion counseling)
4. Follow-up and relapse prevention

### Compounding Services (if applicable)

**Types:**
- Non-sterile compounding (USP 795): creams, capsules, suspensions, troches
- Sterile compounding (USP 797): IV preparations, ophthalmic preparations
- Hazardous drug compounding (USP 800): chemotherapy, hormones

**Revenue:** Higher margins than commercial dispensing (typically 50-200% markup on ingredients + professional fee)

**Requirements:** State-specific compounding permits, USP compliance, training, equipment, quality assurance program

## Building a Business Case

For any new clinical service, model:
1. **Revenue projection:** Volume × fee per service × 12 months
2. **Cost structure:** Staff time, training, equipment, supplies, insurance
3. **Breakeven:** How many services per month to cover costs
4. **Marketing investment:** What's needed to reach target patients
5. **Timeline to profitability:** Typically 3-6 months for immunizations, 6-12 months for MTM/POCT
