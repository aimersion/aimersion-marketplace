# Customer Communicator

Draft clear, professional customer communications for pool service operations. All messages should be friendly, informative, and build trust.

## Communication Types

### 1. Service Completion Report
Send after each visit to document what was done.

**Include:**
- Date and technician name
- Services performed (brief bullet list)
- Water chemistry readings (before/after if chemicals were adjusted)
- Any issues found or observations
- Recommendations (if any)
- Next scheduled visit date

**Tone:** Professional, concise. Avoid jargon — translate technical terms for homeowners.

**Template structure:**
```
Subject: Pool Service Complete — [Date]

Hi [Customer Name],

[Tech name] completed your pool service today. Here's a summary:

SERVICES PERFORMED:
• [Service 1]
• [Service 2]

WATER CHEMISTRY:
[Reading summary — keep it simple: "All levels are in the ideal range" or note specific adjustments]

[OBSERVATIONS/RECOMMENDATIONS if any]

Your next service is scheduled for [date]. Let us know if you need anything before then!

[Company name]
```

### 2. Appointment Reminder
Send 24-48 hours before a scheduled visit.

**Include:**
- Date, time window, and service type
- Any prep the customer should do (e.g., unlock gate, remove pool toys)
- Contact info for rescheduling

**Keep it:** Short — 3-4 sentences max.

### 3. Follow-Up Messages
Send after repairs, estimates, or seasonal services.

**Repair follow-up (2-3 days after):**
- Ask if the repair is working properly
- Remind of any warranty period
- Offer to schedule a check if there are concerns

**Estimate follow-up (3-5 days after):**
- Gentle check-in on the estimate
- Offer to answer questions
- Do not be pushy — one follow-up is sufficient

### 4. Seasonal Notifications
Send to customer base at seasonal transition points.

- **Pre-winter**: Offer winterization scheduling (send 4-6 weeks before typical closing)
- **Pre-spring**: Offer opening scheduling (send 4-6 weeks before swim season)
- **Mid-summer**: Remind about filter maintenance, offer mid-season checkup

### 5. Upsell / Recommendation Messages
When a tech identifies an upgrade opportunity, draft a message that:
- Explains the problem or opportunity clearly
- Describes the recommended solution and its benefit
- Provides a rough cost range
- Includes a clear call-to-action (reply, call, schedule)

**Never be:** High-pressure. Frame upgrades as recommendations, not urgencies (unless there's a safety issue).

## Writing Guidelines

- **First name basis**: Use the customer's first name
- **Plain language**: "Your chlorine is a bit low" not "FC reading was 0.8 ppm below target"
- **Actionable**: End with a clear next step or offer to help
- **Branded sign-off**: Use the business name consistently
- **Short paragraphs**: 1-3 sentences each; use bullet lists for multiple items
- **Proofread**: No typos, no technical shorthand (ppm, TA, CYA — spell these out or skip them)

## Handling Sensitive Situations

- **Equipment failure**: Be honest, explain the issue, provide options with costs
- **Missed appointment**: Apologize briefly, offer prompt rescheduling, don't over-explain
- **Price increase**: Lead with value, explain the reason simply, give advance notice
- **Customer complaint**: Acknowledge, take ownership, offer a concrete resolution
