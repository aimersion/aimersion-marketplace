# Estate Review Command

Develop a detailed estate planning review with document checklists, trust recommendations, and tax optimization strategies.

## Purpose

Conduct thorough estate planning reviews that ensure clients have coordinated, tax-efficient wealth transfer plans that protect family interests and achieve philanthropic goals.

## Inputs

The user should provide:
- **Client Information**: Name, age, marital status, dependents
- **Estate Composition**: Major assets and approximate values
- **Estate Plan Status**: Current documents or gaps
- **Family Situation**: Spouse, children, business interests
- **Goals**: Wealth transfer objectives, charitable intent
- **Tax Situation**: Income level, estate tax concerns
- **Previous Planning**: What's been done, what needs updating

## Usage Pattern

```
Complete an estate plan review for [CLIENT NAME].
Situation: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]
Focus on: [SPECIFIC CONCERNS - e.g., liquidity, tax efficiency, business succession]
Beneficiaries: [KEY DETAILS]
```

## Output Includes

1. **Current Estate Plan Assessment**
   - Will review: Executor, beneficiaries, provisions
   - Living trust review: Funding, administration, distribution
   - Power of attorney review: Scope and succession
   - Healthcare directives: Current and adequate
   - HIPAA and guardianship: Status review

2. **Estimated Estate Tax Analysis**
   - Gross estate calculation (current market values)
   - Estimated estate value at death (growth scenarios)
   - Federal estate tax exposure
   - State estate/inheritance tax exposure
   - Beneficiary inheritance projection

3. **Trust Structure Recommendations**
   - Comparison of options (revocable, irrevocable, SLAT, GRAT, etc.)
   - Recommendation rationale
   - Tax implications by option
   - Administration complexity assessment
   - Cost-benefit analysis

4. **Gifting Strategy Analysis**
   - Annual exclusion planning
   - Lifetime exemption utilization
   - 529 superfunding opportunities
   - FLP or holding company structures
   - Discounting opportunities

5. **Wealth Transfer Framework**
   - Primary beneficiary distribution plan
   - Secondary/contingent beneficiary provisions
   - Generation-skipping transfer tax planning
   - Asset-specific disposition strategy
   - Timing and sequencing

6. **Business Succession Plan** (if applicable)
   - Ownership succession plan
   - Management succession plan
   - Buy-sell agreement review/recommendation
   - Valuation method
   - Liquidity strategy for buy-out

7. **Charitable Planning** (if applicable)
   - Charitable giving history/intent
   - Vehicle recommendations (DAF, CRT, CLT, bequest)
   - Tax benefit quantification
   - Impact on family wealth transfer

8. **Action Items and Timeline**
   - Document preparation/updates needed
   - Prioritized by urgency
   - Timeline for completion
   - Responsible parties
   - Coordination with attorneys/CPAs

## Key Components

**Document Checklist**
- [ ] Revocable living trust (if applicable)
- [ ] Pour-over will
- [ ] Durable financial power of attorney
- [ ] Healthcare power of attorney
- [ ] HIPAA authorization
- [ ] Beneficiary designations (life insurance, retirement)
- [ ] ILIT documents (if applicable)
- [ ] Buy-sell agreements (if business owner)

**Trust Comparison Matrix**
- Structure options presented side-by-side
- Tax implications compared
- Administrative complexity assessed
- Cost-benefit analysis

**Tax Projection Scenarios**
- Conservative growth assumption
- Moderate growth assumption
- Aggressive growth assumption
- Impact analysis for each

## Customization Options

- **Tax-Focused**: Emphasize exemption use and mitigation strategies
- **Family-Focused**: Emphasize family dynamics and relationship preservation
- **Business-Focused**: Emphasize succession and liquidity
- **Charitable-Focused**: Emphasize philanthropy integration
- **Multi-Generational**: Emphasize dynasty planning

## Integration with Other Skills

- **Tax Strategist**: Coordinate for tax optimization
- **Client Communicator**: Use for family meeting facilitation
- **Compliance Tracker**: Ensure fiduciary documentation

## Notes

- Estate plans should be reviewed every 3-5 years or after significant life changes
- Coordinate with clients' estate planning attorneys
- Understand state-specific laws and considerations
- Consider marital dynamics and family relationships
- Charitable intent should be explored openly
- Business interests require special attention and valuation
- Life insurance needs reassessed during planning
