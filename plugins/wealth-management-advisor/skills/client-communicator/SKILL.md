# Client Communicator Skill

Create compelling client communications that build relationships, reinforce investment strategy, and address market concerns. This skill helps advisors navigate difficult conversations and celebrate client milestones.

## Quarterly Portfolio Review Letters

### Letter Structure and Content

**Opening Section (1 paragraph)**
- Warm greeting addressing client(s) by name
- Reference specific period covered
- One key takeaway about their portfolio or market
- Acknowledgment of current conditions (market environment)

**Market Context (2-3 paragraphs)**
- Summary of major market events during quarter
- Performance of key asset classes they own
- Economic data relevant to their situation
- No unnecessary jargon; explain terms as needed

**Portfolio Performance (3-4 paragraphs)**
- Return summary for quarter and year-to-date
- Comparison to benchmark (if applicable)
- Explanation of why return occurred (attribution story)
- How different pieces of portfolio contributed differently
- Not an apology if down; context of strategy
- Callout if rebalancing occurred and why

**Strategic Commentary (2-3 paragraphs)**
- Reinforcement of investment approach alignment
- How portfolio positioning suited the environment
- Risk management elements that worked (or didn't)
- Why diversification matters (show example)
- Long-term perspective in context of short-term moves

**Action Items (1-2 paragraphs)**
- Any rebalancing or allocation changes made
- Upcoming planning items to discuss
- Questions answered or decisions made
- "Let's talk about" invitations for next meeting

**Closing (1 paragraph)**
- Thank you for their trust
- Invitation to discuss or ask questions
- Contact information
- Personal signature

### Quarterly Letter Tone Principles

- **Honest not Cheerful**: Acknowledge concerns; don't minimize difficulties
- **Clear not Clinical**: Explain investments without financial jargon
- **Personal not Robotic**: Show that you know their situation and goals
- **Confident not Cocky**: Reinforce strategy without predicting markets
- **Perspective not Prediction**: Help them understand what happened, not predict future

### Customization by Client Type

**Conservative/Income-Focused Clients**
- Emphasize capital preservation accomplishments
- Explain volatility in equity portion
- Highlight income generation
- Address inflation/purchasing power concerns

**Growth-Focused Clients**
- Celebrate gains, contextualize pullbacks
- Emphasize long-term compounding
- Show how diversification tempered downside
- Reference time horizon and staying invested

**High-Net-Worth/Complex Situation**
- Reference estate, tax, or business planning impact
- Show attribution at deeper level
- Discuss alternative asset performance
- Address concentrated position management

**Newly Retired Clients**
- Emphasize withdrawal sustainability
- Show income coverage from portfolio
- Address sequence of returns risk management
- Reinforce spending discipline

## Market Volatility Response Communications

### Volatility Letter Framework

**Immediate Reaction (1 paragraph)**
- Name the event/market movement
- Acknowledge client concern is natural
- Reassurance without dismissing concern
- Statement of readiness to discuss

**Historical Context (2-3 paragraphs)**
- Show that volatility is normal: historical examples
- Chart or table of past corrections/recoveries
- Timeline to recovery after past events
- Reminder that their strategy accounts for this
- No "this time is different" message

**Portfolio-Specific Explanation (2-3 paragraphs)**
- How their diversification provides cushion
- Which holdings benefited (flight-to-quality, etc.)
- Why some portions declined (they should have)
- Whether rebalancing opportunities emerged
- Specific dollar or percentage impact on their account

**Long-Term Reinforcement (2 paragraphs)**
- Investment policy statement reminder
- Why they chose their allocation
- What staying invested means over time
- Sequence of returns risk for near-retirees

**Action Guidance (1-2 paragraphs)**
- "What we're doing" (rebalancing, reviewing, monitoring)
- "What we recommend" (stay the course, consider opportunities, etc.)
- "What we suggest you avoid" (panic selling, market timing, etc.)
- Next meeting or check-in timing

### Market Volatility Talking Points

- **Market Correction (5-20% decline)**: Normal, healthy, opportunity
- **Bear Market (>20% decline)**: Painful but recoverable, historically temporary
- **Recession Fears**: Diversified portfolios can cushion earnings impact
- **Interest Rate Concerns**: Fixed income adjusts; opportunity to reinvest higher
- **Inflation Concerns**: Some assets inflation hedges; rebalancing helps
- **Geopolitical Events**: Markets price quickly; diversification matters

## Milestone Celebrations and Acknowledgments

### Retirement Milestones

**Retirement Launch Letter**
- Congratulations with genuine warmth
- Acknowledgment of achievement
- Transition from accumulation to distribution
- Withdrawal plan confirmation
- Portfolio rebalancing for retirement
- First withdrawal process and timing
- Healthcare planning checklist
- Social Security timing discussion
- Tax strategy adjustments
- Invitation for quarterly check-in

**First Year Retirement Check-in**
- How is retirement feeling?
- Actual spending vs planned spending
- Any lifestyle or expense surprises
- Portfolio performance and withdrawals
- Healthcare insurance transitions (Medicare)
- Tax return outcomes
- Rebalancing results
- Next year adjustments

### Life Event Communications

**Major Purchase (Home, Vehicle)**
- Reference their goal we discussed
- Celebration of milestone achievement
- Impact on overall plan
- Tax implications if relevant
- Insurance review needs
- Remaining goals and priorities

**Significant Inheritance or Windfall**
- Congratulations/sympathy as appropriate
- Integration into overall plan
- Tax planning needs
- Liquidity timeline
- Investment policy for funds
- Charitable or family giving intentions

**Birth of Grandchild**
- Warmth and celebration
- 529 education planning opportunity
- Dynasty/trust planning consideration
- Future goal impact on plan
- Gift funding options
- Family values discussion

**Business Sale or Liquidity Event**
- Major congratulations and acknowledgment
- Concentration risk assessment
- Diversification strategy
- Tax deferral or acceleration analysis
- Charitable giving opportunity
- Lifestyle and retirement impact
- Legacy planning alignment

**Significant Birthday**
- Recognize milestone personally
- Reflect on progress made
- Legacy planning discussion opening
- Intergenerational planning
- Review of goals and timeline
- Estate and trust review

**Year in Review (2-3 paragraphs)**
- Portfolio performance recap
- Market environment context
- What worked/what didn't
- Rebalancing activity
- Fee or cost review

**Tax Planning Opportunities (2-3 paragraphs)**
- Tax-loss harvesting results
- Charitable giving strategies
- Roth conversion analysis
- Asset location review
- Estimated tax adjustment needs

**2026 Planning Focus (2-3 paragraphs)**
- Key planning opportunities
- Estate plan review timing
- Insurance coverage check
- Beneficiary designation review
- Goals review and adjustment

**Action Items (1 paragraph)**
- Meetings to schedule
- Documents to gather
- Decisions needed

## Welcome Letters for New Clients

### First Communication

**Introduction Section**
- Warm welcome to firm
- Acknowledgment of trust in advisor
- Brief advisor background
- Team introduction

**Our Approach (2-3 paragraphs)**
- Fiduciary commitment
- Long-term relationship focus
- Comprehensive planning mindset
- Communication philosophy
- Fee transparency

**Relationship Expectations (2-3 paragraphs)**
- Frequency of communication
- Quarterly letters and reviews
- Annual planning meetings
- Rebalancing approach
- Availability and responsiveness

**Next Steps (1 paragraph)**
- First meeting agenda
- Documents needed
- Contact for questions
- Welcome gift/benefit

## Estate Planning Nudge Communications

### Annual Estate Plan Review Prompt

- **Timing**: Near birthday or anniversary
- **Message**: "It's been [X years] since we last reviewed your estate plan"
- **Focus**: Life changes that trigger updates
- **Call to Action**: Schedule 30-minute review meeting
- **Benefit**: "Ensure your wishes are reflected and protect your family"

### Specific Trigger Events

- **Children/Grandchildren Born**: Update beneficiaries and guardianship
- **Marriage/Divorce**: Comprehensive estate plan overhaul
- **Business Changes**: Buy-sell agreement and succession integration
- **Significant Portfolio Growth**: Review exemption usage
- **Move to New State**: State law differences require review
- **Major Asset Purchase**: Real property ownership structure

## Next-Generation Engagement Outreach

### Content for Young Adult Children/Heirs

**Structured Introduction Letter**
- Parent's endorsement and introduction
- Your role as advisor to family
- Core planning principles
- Financial literacy emphasis
- Invitation to financial planning conversation

**Financial Literacy Topics**
- Investment principles and time horizon
- Diversification and risk management
- Tax-efficient investing basics
- Estate plan overview
- Their role in family financial plan

**Gradual Engagement**
- First meeting: Overview and questions
- Second: Deep dive on their assets/goals
- Third: Coordination with parents' plan
- Ongoing: Regular check-ins as financial situation evolves

### Messaging for Wealth Transfer

- **Before transfer**: Education and expectations setting
- **During transfer**: Clear communication on structure
- **After transfer**: Integration into ongoing planning
- **Long-term**: Mentoring and guidance
