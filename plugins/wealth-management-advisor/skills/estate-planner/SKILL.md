# Estate Planner Skill

Help advisors and clients develop comprehensive estate plans aligned with wealth transfer objectives, tax efficiency, and family dynamics. This skill provides actionable frameworks for trusts, gifting, and succession planning.

## Estate Planning Document Review Checklists

Systematic review of foundational estate documents:

- **Will Review**: Executor named, beneficiaries clear, guardianship provisions, residuary clause, specific bequests, no ambiguous language
- **Revocable Living Trust**: Assets properly funded, trustee succession clear, distribution provisions, amendment ability, tax identification
- **Durable Power of Attorney**: Agent named with successor agents, scope of powers defined, health vs financial separated, springing vs immediate
- **Healthcare Proxy/HIPAA**: Health decisions delegated, advance directives clear, organ donation preferences documented
- **QTIP Trust**: Surviving spouse income rights, trustee powers, principal distribution, marital deduction qualification
- **Charitable Documents**: Charity selection, distribution mechanics, CRT terms, CLT payout, advisor oversight provisions

## Trust Structure Comparison Matrix

Detailed comparison of trust types for different situations:

### Revocable Trusts
- **Best For**: Avoiding probate, privacy, incapacity planning, complex family situations
- **Advantages**: Flexible, can be amended, no income tax burden, maintains control
- **Disadvantages**: No creditor protection, no estate tax savings, revocable at will
- **Creator Control**: Full control during lifetime

### Irrevocable Life Insurance Trusts (ILITs)
- **Best For**: Estate tax mitigation, life insurance benefits outside estate
- **Advantages**: Removes insurance proceeds from taxable estate, trustee controls cash, provides liquidity
- **Disadvantages**: No longer revocable, requires formal administration, trustee duties
- **Tax Impact**: Significant estate tax savings for large estates

### Spousal Lifetime Access Trusts (SLATs)
- **Best For**: Married couples wanting to gift but maintain access
- **Advantages**: Leverages gift tax exemption, surviving spouse has access, removes appreciation
- **Disadvantages**: Couple must remain married, requires gift tax return, trustee duties
- **Timing Consideration**: Often done in lower-value years for married couple maximization

### Grantor Retained Annuity Trusts (GRATs)
- **Best For**: Transferring appreciating assets, short-term tax strategy
- **Advantages**: Fixed annuity received, remainder to beneficiary at discount, minimizes gift tax
- **Disadvantages**: Grantor must survive term, variable return rates matter, moderate complexity
- **Duration**: Typically 2-10 years depending on mortality assumptions

### Intentional Defective Grantor Trusts (IDGTs)
- **Best For**: Leveraging grantor trust tax treatment for growth assets
- **Advantages**: Income taxed to grantor (not trust), allows loan strategy, removes growth from estate
- **Disadvantages**: Grantor pays income tax (estate freeze benefit), requires promissory note
- **Strategy Integration**: Often combined with GRAT or other structures

### Charitable Remainder Trusts (CRTs)
- **Best For**: Charitable intent with income needs, concentrated stock monetization
- **Advantages**: Charitable deduction, income stream, capital gains deferral, social impact
- **Disadvantages**: Irrevocable, charitable remainder, actuarial costs
- **Structure**: Unitrust (% of assets) vs Annuity Trust ($ amount)

### Charitable Lead Trusts (CLTs)
- **Best For**: Large gifts with charitable motivation, younger beneficiaries
- **Advantages**: Reduces taxable gifts, achieves charitable purpose, returns wealth to family
- **Disadvantages**: Family doesn't receive income, timing risk for appreciation
- **Tax Benefit**: Charitable deduction for present value of lead payments

### Donor Advised Funds (DAFs)
- **Best For**: Immediate charitable deduction with flexible grants, bunching strategy
- **Advantages**: Immediate tax deduction, flexible timing of grants, professional management
- **Disadvantages**: Irreversible charitable intent, account fees, limited control
- **Usage**: Ideal for alternating high/low income years

### Qualified Personal Residence Trusts (QPRTs)
- **Best For**: Primary residence transfer with continued enjoyment
- **Advantages**: Removes appreciation from estate, retains residence use, modest gift tax cost
- **Disadvantages**: Residence reverts to beneficiaries, irrevocable, timing critical
- **Term Selection**: Based on life expectancy and market outlook

## Gifting Strategies

### Annual Exclusion Strategy
- **2026 Limit**: $19,000 per recipient per donor (indexed annually)
- **Married Couple**: $38,000 per recipient with gift splitting
- **Application**: No gift tax return required, reduces estate
- **Timing**: December gifts for current year; January gifts for following year

### Lifetime Exemption Planning
- **2026 Exemption**: $13.61 million per person (indexed annually)
- **Married Couple**: $27.22 million combined with portability election
- **Sunset Risk**: Exemption scheduled to drop to ~$7 million in 2026
- **Strategy**: Consider using in lower-exemption years

### 529 Superfunding
- **Timing**: Contribute 5 years of exclusions at once ($95,000 per recipient)
- **Administration**: File gift tax return showing 5-year election
- **Growth**: All in-plan growth escapes estate and generation-skipping tax
- **Flexibility**: Unused funds can be redirected to other qualified education beneficiaries

### Family Limited Partnerships (FLPs)
- **Structure**: Limited partners (family members) + general partner (donor control)
- **Valuation**: Typical 25-40% discounts for lack of control, marketability
- **Strategy**: Leverage discounts to transfer more wealth at lower gift tax cost
- **Administration**: Required partnership accounting, annual returns, formalities

### Discounted Gifts
- **Valuation Discounts**: Lack of control (20-40%), lack of marketability (25-35%)
- **Valuation Methods**: Comparable company analysis, net asset value, cash flow analysis
- **Documentation**: Professional appraisals required for IRS support
- **Risk**: IRS challenges for aggressive discounts; prior cases matter

## Estate Tax Projection and Mitigation

### Projection Framework
- **Current Estate Value**: Liquid + illiquid assets + life insurance
- **Growth Rate Assumptions**: Conservative, moderate, aggressive scenarios
- **Federal Exemption**: Account for potential changes (current sunset 2026)
- **State Taxes**: Consider state-level exemptions (many states much lower)
- **Tax Liability**: Estimated federal + state taxes at death

### Mitigation Strategies
- **Exemption Utilization**: Use both spouses' exemptions; portability election
- **Freeze Strategies**: Lock current value, transfer appreciation via GRATs/QPRTs
- **Discounting**: FLPs, LLCs, or holding company structures
- **Insurance Planning**: Life insurance trust for liquidity and tax payment
- **Charitable Integration**: CRTs/CLTs for both philanthropy and reduction

## Generation-Skipping Transfer Tax Planning

### GST Tax Overview
- **Rate**: 40% (same as estate tax) on amounts transferred to generation skippers
- **Exemption**: Same amount as estate tax exemption ($13.61M in 2026)
- **Trigger**: Transfers more than one generation below grantor

### GST Planning Techniques
- **Exemption Allocation**: Strategically allocate exemption to maximize dynasty trust growth
- **Inclusion Ratio Calculation**: Determines GST tax exposure for dynasty trusts
- **Dynasty Trusts**: Multi-generational structures for states allowing perpetual trusts
- **Skip Person Avoidance**: Ensure beneficiaries are in same generation to avoid tax

## Business Succession Integration

### Planning Dimensions
- **Ownership Succession**: Who inherits the business?
- **Management Succession**: Who runs the business?
- **Valuation**: What is the business worth for estate tax?
- **Liquidity**: How to fund buy-out or estate taxes?

### Succession Documents
- **Buy-Sell Agreement**: Cross-purchase or entity-redemption mechanics
- **Operating Agreement**: Successor management and decision authority
- **Key Person Insurance**: Funds for transition and stability
- **Transition Plan**: Timeline and responsibilities

### Tax-Efficient Structures
- **ESOP (Employee Stock Ownership Plan)**: Tax deferral through reinvestment
- **Installment Sale to IDGT**: Freeze value, finance with promissory note
- **Recapitalization**: Separate voting and non-voting stock classes
- **Partnership Interest Transfer**: Leverage valuation discounts
