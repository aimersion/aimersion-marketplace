# Compliance Tracker Skill

Maintain comprehensive compliance documentation and processes to meet regulatory requirements, fiduciary obligations, and industry best practices. This skill helps advisors create defensible compliance programs.

## Best Execution Review and Documentation

### Best Execution Framework

Advisors must demonstrate reasonable efforts to execute trades at most favorable terms:

- **Venue Selection**: Document trading venues used (exchanges, market makers, dark pools)
- **Price Analysis**: Capture execution price vs market quotes at time of trade
- **Timing**: Document order timing and how quickly filled
- **Liquidity Impact**: Size of order relative to typical daily volume
- **Cost Analysis**: Transaction costs relative to alternatives

### Trade Execution Documentation

**Order Records Must Capture**
- Client identification
- Security: CUSIP, name, ticker
- Quantity and price
- Buy/sell indication
- Order time and execution time
- Execution venue
- Executed price and amount
- Comparison to best bid/offer
- Any special instructions or limitations
- Client authorization level

**Best Execution Testing (Quarterly/Annual)**
- Sample trades across securities, sizes, venues
- Compare execution to alternative venues
- Identify if patterns suggest suboptimal execution
- Document findings and any corrective actions
- Retain test results for regulatory review

### Exception Handling

- **Unusual Pricing**: Flag trades >X% away from market
- **Latency Issues**: Monitor execution delays
- **Venue Changes**: Document reason for switching venues
- **Client Requests**: Honor requests even if suboptimal execution; document
- **Small Accounts**: May use different venues for efficiency
- **Illiquid Securities**: Document challenges and execution rationale

### Regulatory Requirement Alignment

- **Reg SHO**: Proper locate for short sales
- **Reg F**: Market access controls
- **Reg Systems Compliance**: Technology governance
- **SEC Rule 10b-5**: Anti-fraud compliance in communications

## Fiduciary Duty Documentation Requirements

### Core Fiduciary Obligations

**Duty of Loyalty**
- Document: Procedures to identify and manage conflicts
- Evidence: Conflicts disclosures to clients
- Tracking: Personal trading restrictions
- Proof: Client interests prioritized over firm interests

**Duty of Care**
- Document: Due diligence on investments and advisors
- Evidence: Research memos, manager reviews, fund analyses
- Tracking: Manager changes with documented reasons
- Proof: Reasonable investigations before recommendations

**Duty of Disclosure**
- Document: All material facts disclosed to clients
- Evidence: Signed acknowledgments of disclosures
- Tracking: Fee disclosures and comparisons
- Proof: Form ADV updates and delivery

### Fiduciary Documentation Template

**For Each Investment Decision:**
1. Investment Policy Statement reference
2. Why this investment aligns with client goals
3. Due diligence performed
4. Conflicts of interest identified
5. Client suitability assessment
6. Approval by compliance/portfolio manager
7. Client communication and acknowledgment

**For Each Manager/Advisor Recommendation:**
1. Background check and registration verification
2. Philosophy and process review
3. Performance analysis (if applicable)
4. Cost assessment
5. Peer comparison
6. Conflicts check
7. Ongoing monitoring plan

## Trade Allocation and Fairness Reviews

### Fair Allocation Framework

**The Challenge**
When same trade executed for multiple clients, allocation must be fair, not favoring some clients over others.

**Allocation Methods**
- **Equal Weight**: Same dollars per account
- **Proportional**: Based on account size
- **Parity**: Based on time in program
- **Rotation**: Different clients benefit first on alternating trades

**Documentation Requirements**
- Allocation method documented in writing
- Applied consistently across accounts
- Exceptions documented with rationale
- Manager approval for allocation decisions

### Allocation Test and Monitoring

**Quarterly Allocation Review**
- Select sample of trades (15-30 trades)
- Verify allocation was made before execution
- Check fairness of allocation across accounts
- Identify any patterns of preference
- Document findings and any corrective actions

**Red Flags**
- Favored accounts receive better fills
- Smallest accounts always receive worst allocation
- Allocation decisions made after execution
- Undisclosed discretion in allocation
- Preferential treatment based on account relationship

## Soft Dollar Disclosure

### What Are Soft Dollars?

Using client commissions to pay for services/research rather than cash:

- **Research**: Third-party equity research reports
- **Software**: Portfolio analysis, risk analytics
- **Data**: Market data, reference data
- **Conferences**: Industry conferences and seminars
- **Execution Services**: Order routing, trade execution
- **Clearing/Settlement**: Trade clearing, settlement services

### Disclosure Obligations

- **In Form ADV**: List major soft dollar arrangements
- **To Clients**: Explain how soft dollars benefit client
- **In Policies**: Document soft dollar approval process
- **Quantify Impact**: Estimate percentage of commissions used

### Prohibited Soft Dollar Uses

- **Office Rent**: Cannot pay for facility costs
- **Salaries**: Cannot pay for staff compensation
- **Marketing**: Cannot pay for client communications
- **Travel**: Only conference/educational travel allowed
- **Technology**: Only if enhances client service

## Fee Reasonableness Analysis

### Fee Structure Documentation

**At Program Launch:**
- Document rationale for fee structure
- Benchmark against peer advisors
- Show alignment with services provided
- Retention decision and approval

**Annual Review:**
- Compare fees to industry surveys
- Assess peer group median
- Review investment performance
- Consider assets under management (AUM)

### Benchmark Comparison Sources

- Cerulli Associates peer analysis
- Morningstar fee studies
- SEC examination findings
- Advisory board industry surveys
- Published compensation surveys

### Fee Reasonableness Factors

- **Complexity of Services**: More complex services justify higher fees
- **Assets Under Management**: Larger AUM may support lower fees
- **Services Included**: Broader scope supports higher fees
- **Discretionary vs Non-Discretionary**: Discretionary typically higher
- **Performance Guarantees**: Enhanced guarantees justify premium
- **Technology/Tools**: Access to proprietary tools justifies fees
- **Talent/Experience**: Senior advisor fees typically higher

## Conflict of Interest Register

### Conflict Types to Register

**Revenue-Based Conflicts**
- Managed accounts vs mutual funds (fee difference)
- Proprietary products vs competitor products
- High-fee vs low-fee investment options
- Alternative investments (higher fees)
- Firm-affiliated service providers

**Time-Based Conflicts**
- New business prioritized over existing client service
- Advisor compensation structure (individual vs firm)
- Tenure at firm (experienced vs junior)

**Client-Selection Conflicts**
- Minimum account size creating barriers
- Asset class focus limiting opportunity
- Geographic focus limiting client base
- Conflict between different client groups

### Mitigation Strategies

- **Disclosure**: Clearly communicate conflicts
- **Policies**: Document decision-making process
- **Monitoring**: Track advisor compensation
- **Controls**: Implement checks on recommendations
- **Training**: Ensure all staff understand conflicts

### Conflict Register Maintenance

**Annual Review:**
- Update conflict inventory
- Assess effectiveness of controls
- Document any new conflicts
- Consider additional mitigations
- Board/management approval

## Personal Trading Compliance

### Prohibited Activities

- **Front-Running**: Trading personally before client trades
- **Reverse Churning**: Placing client trades after personal benefit trades
- **Influencing Prices**: Substantial trading to move prices
- **Research Abuse**: Using client research for personal gain
- **IPO Allocation**: Avoiding restricted persons in IPO allocations

### Personal Trading Policies

**Pre-Clearance**
- Advisors must clear trades before execution
- Exceptions: Automatic investment plans, mutual funds
- Restricted persons list maintained
- Real-time compliance monitoring

**Blackout Periods**
- No personal trading while portfolios under development
- Blackout during research announcement periods
- Blackout around recommendation changes
- Blackout timed to client transaction periods

**Reporting Requirements**
- Monthly personal trading reports
- Portfolio reviews for conflicts
- Compensation verification
- Outside account monitoring

## Advertising and Marketing Compliance

### Regulatory Framework

**SEC Rule 206(4)-1**: Investment advisers cannot make false/misleading statements

**Key Compliance Areas**
- **Performance Claims**: Must be accurate and complete
- **Benchmarking**: Appropriate indices used
- **Comparisons**: Fair presentation vs competitors
- **Track Records**: Include all accounts in composite
- **Disclaimers**: Proper risk warnings included

### Marketing Materials Review

**Before Distribution:**
1. Verify all factual claims
2. Check performance calculations
3. Compare benchmarks appropriately
4. Include required disclaimers
5. Avoid superlatives ("best," "guaranteed")
6. Document source of data/claims
7. Manager/compliance approval

**Ongoing Monitoring:**
1. Test claims annually
2. Verify performance updates quarterly
3. Monitor competitor claims
4. Update dated materials
5. Archive all versions (1-year minimum, typically 6-year standard)

## Cybersecurity and Privacy Compliance

### Data Protection Requirements

**Regulatory Frameworks**
- **SEC Rule 30(c)(1)**: Data security rule
- **GLBA Safeguards Rule**: Reasonable security measures
- **State Privacy Laws**: CCPA, VAMSL, others

**Implementation Areas**
- Encryption of client data
- Access controls and authentication
- Employee training and background checks
- Vendor management and oversight
- Incident response procedures
- Business continuity planning

### Privacy Policy Maintenance

- Disclosed how client information used
- Opt-out rights (where applicable)
- Third-party sharing restrictions
- Data retention procedures
- Breach notification procedures
- Regular policy updates
