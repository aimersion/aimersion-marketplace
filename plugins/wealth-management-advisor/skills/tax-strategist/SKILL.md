# Tax Strategist Skill

Help wealth advisors develop year-round tax strategies that optimize after-tax returns through harvesting, conversions, charitable giving, and strategic asset placement. This skill combines tactical and strategic tax planning.

## Tax-Loss Harvesting Identification and Execution

### Harvesting Process

Systematic approach to capturing tax losses:

- **Loss Identification**: Scan all holdings quarterly for positions down from cost basis
- **Magnitude Assessment**: Prioritize by loss size; smallest losses may not warrant execution
- **Tax Rate Impact**: Consider client's marginal tax rate (higher rates, more valuable loss)
- **Realizability**: Assess liquidity and transaction costs vs loss benefit

### Wash Sale Rules Compliance

- **Definition**: Cannot repurchase substantially identical security within 30 days before or after sale
- **Tracking Period**: 60-day window (30 before, sale date, 30 after)
- **Substantially Identical**: Same security, not sufficiently different funds in same asset class
- **Documentation**: Maintain records of harvest dates and repurchase dates
- **Replacement Strategies**: Use similar but different funds/securities for reinvestment

### Multi-Year Harvest Planning

- **Deductibility Limits**: Net capital losses limit to $3,000 per year (excess carries forward indefinitely)
- **Carryforward Optimization**: Plan multi-year harvests to efficiently use carryforwards
- **Ordinary Income Offset**: Prioritize offsetting ordinary income with net capital losses
- **Tax Rate Heterogeneity**: Consider future tax rate changes when harvesting

### Loss Harvesting Vehicles

- **Individual Accounts**: Losses offset gains in same year or carry forward
- **Couples**: Each spouse harvests separately; losses not transferable
- **Partnership/S-Corp**: Losses flow to owners; verify basis for utilization
- **IRA/401k Accounts**: No harvesting benefit (gains/losses non-taxable)
- **Donor Advised Fund**: Harvest before contribution for maximum benefit

## Roth Conversion Analysis and Execution

### Conversion Mechanics

- **Source Accounts**: Can convert from Traditional IRA, SEP IRA, Simple IRA, or pre-tax 401k
- **Tax Recognition**: Entire converted amount is taxable income in year of conversion
- **No Income Limits**: Unlike direct Roth contributions, no MAGI limits apply
- **Pro-Rata Rule**: Applies if client has other pre-tax IRAs (conversion taxes proportionally)
- **Reversal Option**: No longer available; conversions are permanent (as of 2018)

### Bracket Management Strategy

- **Identify Target Bracket**: Determine optimal conversion amount to stay in current bracket
- **Income Projection**: Consider other income sources (wages, dividends, capital gains)
- **Marginal vs Average**: Conversion creates marginal income; understand impact on total taxes
- **Surtax Thresholds**: Be aware of 3.8% NIIT, 0.9% Medicare HI, other phase-outs
- **Multi-Year Plan**: Coordinate conversions across multiple years for tax efficiency

### Conversion Year Considerations

- **Low Income Years**: Sabbaticals, retirement transition, business losses are ideal
- **Capital Loss Years**: Harvest losses to offset conversion income
- **Market Downturns**: Convert at lower valuations to minimize tax on higher-basis future growth
- **Tax Law Changes**: Consider conversions before anticipated rate increases

### Post-Conversion Planning

- **Qualified Distribution Rules**: Five-year holding period per conversion year
- **Earnings Distribution**: First conversions must satisfy pro-rata rule before earnings withdrawal
- **Account Segregation**: Keep conversion IRAs separate if beneficial
- **Required Distributions**: Roth IRAs have no RMDs during account owner's lifetime

## Charitable Giving Vehicles Comparison

### Donor Advised Fund (DAF)

- **Mechanism**: Contribute to fund, take immediate deduction, recommend grants over time
- **Contribution Types**: Cash, appreciated securities, real estate, private company stock
- **Charitable Deduction**: Available immediately regardless of grant timing
- **Grant Flexibility**: Recommend distributions to qualified charities over many years
- **Bunching Strategy**: Combine multiple years' giving in high-income year
- **Investment Growth**: Tax-free growth on contributed assets
- **Fees**: Typically 0.50-1.00% annually on account balance

### Charitable Remainder Trust (CRT)

- **Structure**: Irrevocable trust providing income to donor, remainder to charity
- **Deduction**: Charitable deduction for present value of remainder interest
- **Income Options**: Fixed annuity payment or unitrust percentage (4-8% typical)
- **Use Case**: Concentrated stock, real estate, monetization with income stream
- **Tax Deferral**: Capital gains on contributed assets not immediately taxable
- **Longevity Risk**: Longer life means less value transferred to charity
- **Administration**: Trust must file annual returns, calculate income carefully

### Charitable Lead Trust (CLT)

- **Structure**: Trust makes annual payments to charity, remainder to family
- **Deduction**: Immediate charitable deduction for present value of lead payments
- **Benefit**: Assets appreciate tax-free, lower-valued gifts to family
- **Use Case**: High-growth assets, multi-generational transfer
- **Taxable Income**: Grantor must pay tax on retained income (grantor CLT)
- **Remainder Risk**: If assets underperform, family receives less
- **Term Options**: Fixed term or life-based term

### Charitable Bequest

- **Estate Tax Deduction**: Full fair market value deductible in estate
- **Simplicity**: Named in will, no complex administration during lifetime
- **Flexibility**: Can be changed through updated will/trust
- **Income Tax**: No income tax benefit during life
- **Probate**: Bequest may go through probate (unless in trust)
- **Timing**: Charity receives assets after death, no lifetime income

### Qualified Charitable Distribution (QCD)

- **Requirement**: Age 70.5+ with IRA
- **Mechanics**: IRA trustee pays directly to qualified charity
- **Benefit**: Distributions satisfy RMD without creating taxable income
- **Limit**: Up to $100,000 per year per person
- **Qualification**: Charity must be qualified (not DAF or private foundation)
- **Simplicity**: Reduces reportable income without requiring charitable deduction

### Bargain Sale to Charity

- **Mechanism**: Sell appreciated asset to charity at below fair market value
- **Deduction**: Charitable deduction for difference between FMV and sale price
- **Capital Gain**: Pro-rata capital gain tax on proceeds received
- **Use Case**: Property (real estate, art), when some proceeds needed
- **Valuation**: Independent appraisal required for deduction substantiation
- **Timing**: Must qualify as charitable organization (not all do)

## Asset Location Optimization

### Strategic Placement Principles

Place investments to minimize after-tax returns:

- **Tax-Inefficient to Taxable**: Long-term capital gains investments (growth stocks)
- **Tax-Efficient to Taxable**: Municipal bonds, index funds, tax-managed funds
- **Taxable Bonds to Retirement**: High-coupon bonds, real estate, high-turnover strategies
- **Alternatives to Retirement**: Private equity, hedge funds (deferred distributions)
- **Match Duration**: Align account types with investment time horizons

### Asset Location by Account Type

**Taxable Accounts (Brokerage)**
- Individual stocks with long holding periods
- Tax-managed mutual funds
- Low-turnover index funds
- Municipal bonds (tax-free income)
- Growth stocks with unrealized gains

**Tax-Deferred (Traditional IRA, 401k)**
- High-coupon bonds
- Bond funds
- Real estate investment trusts (REITs)
- High-turnover active management
- Taxable income-generating securities

**Tax-Free (Roth IRA, 401k)**
- Highest growth potential assets
- Volatile or high-turnover strategies
- Long-duration bonds
- Emerging market equities
- Alternative investments with high appreciation potential

### Tax-Loss Harvesting in Location

- **Harvest in Taxable**: Generate losses in brokerage accounts
- **Maintain Exposure**: Use tax-deferred accounts for immediate reinvestment
- **Avoid Wash Sales**: Ensure brokerage and retirement accounts don't violate rules
- **Coordinate Locations**: Harvest bonds from taxable while holding in tax-deferred

## Capital Gains Management

### Short-Term vs Long-Term Strategy

- **Holding Period**: 12+ months = long-term rate; <12 months = ordinary rate
- **Tax Rates**: Long-term 0/15/20%; ordinary rates up to 37%
- **Planning**: Consider timing of sales to optimize holding periods
- **Losses**: Can offset long-term or short-term gains (short-term first)

### Concentrated Stock Strategies

### Collar Strategy
- **Structure**: Buy put (downside protection) + sell call (capped upside)
- **Cost**: Collar can be zero-cost if strike levels balance
- **Benefit**: Retain downside risk protection without immediate sale
- **Tax**: Deferred gain, structured around put/call dates
- **Complexity**: Requires options knowledge; may have assignment risk

### Prepaid Variable Forward
- **Mechanism**: Agree to deliver shares in future at forward price
- **Proceeds**: Receive cash today against future delivery
- **Deferral**: Gain recognized when shares delivered (typically 12+ months later)
- **Flexibility**: Can adjust shares delivered based on future price
- **Tax Planning**: Structures gain recognition into lower-income future year

### Exchange Funds
- **Participation**: Contribute concentrated position to fund with others
- **Diversification**: Receive pro-rata interests in diversified portfolio
- **Tax Deferral**: Exchange treated as like-kind (if structured as partnership)
- **Time Frame**: Typically 5-7 year holding periods
- **Registration**: Typically via private fund, not SEC-registered

## Net Unrealized Appreciation (NUA) Strategy

### Employer Stock Benefit

- **Benefit**: NUA (unrealized appreciation on employer stock) taxed as long-term gain
- **Cost Basis**: Only employee contributions taxed as ordinary income
- **Tax Timing**: Substantially appreciated company stock can defer gain recognition
- **Withdrawal**: Requires lump-sum distribution from plan
- **Trigger**: NUA benefit realized when shares sold in taxable account

### Implementation Steps

1. Separate company stock from other plan assets during distribution
2. Take lump-sum distribution of company stock (defer taxation)
3. Distribute other plan assets to IRA (defer taxation)
4. Company stock cost basis taxed immediately as ordinary income
5. Hold stock to realize long-term gains on appreciation

### Conditions and Limitations

- Plan must permit in-kind distributions
- Employee must take complete distribution (one-time chance)
- Tax on cost basis immediate (cannot defer)
- NUA taxed as long-term when sold (must hold in taxable account)
- Best when NUA exceeds 50% of total stock value
