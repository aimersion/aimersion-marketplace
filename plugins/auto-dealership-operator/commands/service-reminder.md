description: Create service retention messages and campaigns
argument-hint: [reminder type: declined-service, maintenance-due, recall, or win-back]
---

Create service department retention communications based on the scenario provided.

Ask the user what type of retention outreach they need:
- **Declined service follow-up**: Customer visited but declined a recommended repair/service
- **Maintenance reminder**: Proactive reminder based on mileage or time interval
- **Recall notification**: Alert customer about an open recall on their vehicle
- **Win-back campaign**: Re-engage customers who haven't visited in 6+ months

Based on the scenario, generate:

1. **Multi-channel message sequence**: Texts, emails, and phone scripts for a complete follow-up cadence with specific timing
2. **Personalization fields**: Clearly mark where customer name, vehicle info, and service details should be inserted
3. **Incentive recommendation**: Suggest an appropriate offer or discount to motivate action
4. **Urgency framework**: How to create appropriate urgency without being pushy (especially for safety items)

Use the service-retention skill for cadence timing, prioritization by service type, and seasonal context. All messages should be professional, helpful, and focused on the customer's benefit — not salesy.

If the user wants a bulk campaign (e.g., "send a reminder to all customers due for oil changes"), create a campaign plan with segmentation, send schedule, and expected response rates.
