# Lead Nurture

Create compelling follow-up sequences and phone scripts to convert internet leads into showroom appointments and sales.

## Lead Response Framework

### Speed-to-Lead Priority

The #1 factor in lead conversion is response time:

- **Under 5 minutes**: 8x higher contact rate — always aim for immediate response
- **5-30 minutes**: Strong contact rate, still competitive
- **30-60 minutes**: Significant drop-off, but recoverable with strong messaging
- **1+ hours**: Lead has likely contacted competitors — urgency required

### Initial Response Template Structure

Every first response must include:
1. **Personalization** — Use the customer's name and reference their specific vehicle of interest
2. **Value statement** — Why this vehicle/dealership is the right choice
3. **Availability confirmation** — Confirm the vehicle is in stock (or offer alternatives)
4. **Soft call-to-action** — Invite to schedule a VIP appointment, not "come in today"
5. **Contact options** — Phone, text, email — let them choose their preferred channel

### Follow-Up Cadence (Internet Leads)

Day-by-day sequence for unconverted leads:

| Day | Channel | Purpose |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Day 0 | Email + Text + Call | Immediate response — confirm interest, answer questions |
| Day 1 | Phone call | Personal follow-up, gauge interest level |
| Day 2 | Email | Value-add content (vehicle features, comparison, incentives) |
| Day 3 | Text | Casual check-in, offer to answer questions |
| Day 5 | Email | New angle — similar vehicles, price updates, limited-time offer |
| Day 7 | Phone call | Re-engagement attempt, ask about timeline |
| Day 10 | Email | Market update or new incentive announcement |
| Day 14 | Text | Final active follow-up, keep door open |
| Day 21 | Email | Long-term nurture — monthly market updates |
| Day 30+ | Email (monthly) | Drip campaign — new inventory, seasonal specials |

### Phone Script Structure

Build phone scripts with these sections:

1. **Opening**: Introduce yourself, reference their inquiry, be enthusiastic but not pushy
2. **Discovery questions**: Budget range, timeline, trade-in situation, must-have features
3. **Value bridge**: Connect their needs to specific inventory
4. **Appointment set**: Offer specific times, create urgency with availability
5. **Objection handling**: Prepare responses for common objections (see references)
6. **Close**: Confirm appointment details, send calendar invite, set expectations

### Lead Scoring

Classify leads by engagement signals:

- **Hot (A)**: Responded to outreach, asked specific questions, mentioned timeline
- **Warm (B)**: Opened emails, clicked links, but hasn't responded directly
- **Cool (C)**: No engagement after 3+ touchpoints — move to long-term nurture
- **Dead (D)**: Bounced email, wrong number, or explicit "not interested" — archive

## Message Tone Guidelines

- Professional but conversational — avoid car-salesman cliches
- Never pressure — create value and let interest build
- Always reference specific vehicles, not generic inventory
- Include pricing transparency when possible — builds trust
- Sign with a real person's name, direct phone number, and photo link
