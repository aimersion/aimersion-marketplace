# Reputation Recovery

Assess the severity of reputation damage and provide a structured, time-bound recovery playbook to rebuild trust and improve ratings.

## Why This Matters

A drop from 4 stars to 3.5 stars can reduce revenue by 5-9%. Review extortion is one of the fastest-growing threats to small businesses in 2025-2026, and 70% of consumers look at how brands respond to negative reviews before buying. A systematic recovery plan can reverse reputation damage in 60-90 days.

## Core Workflow

1. **Crisis Assessment** — quantify the damage and identify the cause
2. **Triage** — determine which reviews need immediate response vs. reporting
3. **Response Blitz** — address all unanswered negative reviews within 48 hours
4. **Content Strategy** — create positive content to push down negative results
5. **Review Generation** — accelerate legitimate positive review collection
6. **Monitor & Adjust** — track recovery progress weekly

## Crisis Assessment Framework

### Step 1: Quantify the Damage

Gather these data points:
- Current average rating vs. 30/60/90 days ago
- Number of negative reviews in the crisis period
- Percentage of reviews that are negative (current month vs. typical)
- Which platforms are affected
- Whether reviews are appearing in Google search results

### Step 2: Classify the Crisis Type

| Crisis Type | Description | Severity | Recovery Timeline |
|------------|-------------|----------|-------------------|
| **Service Failure** | A real quality issue caused several bad reviews | MEDIUM | 30-60 days |
| **Single Viral Incident** | One bad experience went viral/shared widely | HIGH | 60-90 days |
| **Review Bombing** | Coordinated attack, often fake | HIGH | 30-45 days (with platform intervention) |
| **Competitor Attack** | Fake negative reviews from competitors | HIGH | 30-60 days |
| **Employee Issue** | A specific employee repeatedly causing bad reviews | MEDIUM | 30 days (once addressed) |
| **Systemic Decline** | Gradual quality erosion over months | HIGH | 90-180 days |
| **PR/Media Crisis** | Negative news coverage or social media backlash | CRITICAL | 90-180 days |

### Step 3: Assess Business Impact

- Is the crisis affecting new customer acquisition?
- Are existing customers mentioning it?
- Is it appearing in search results?
- Are competitors benefiting?

## Recovery Playbook by Crisis Type

### Service Failure Recovery

**Week 1: Stop the Bleeding**
- Identify and fix the root cause of the service issue
- Respond to all unanswered negative reviews within 48 hours
- Train or retrain staff if applicable
- Communicate internally about the issue and resolution

**Week 2-4: Rebuild**
- Launch a review solicitation campaign targeting recent satisfied customers
- Create social media content showing improvements made
- Personally reach out to the most impactful negative reviewers to offer resolution
- Monitor for new negative reviews daily

**Week 5-8: Strengthen**
- Continue review solicitation at a steady pace
- Share positive customer stories on social media
- Consider a "We listened" campaign showing changes made
- Track rating recovery weekly

### Review Bombing / Fake Review Attack

**Immediate (Day 1-3):**
- Document all suspicious reviews (screenshots, timestamps, patterns)
- Report to the platform with evidence (Google, Yelp, etc.)
- Respond professionally to each review without accusing anyone of being fake
- Alert your loyal customers that you're under attack (optional, via email/social)

**Week 1-2:**
- Follow up with the platform on reported reviews
- Accelerate legitimate review collection from real customers
- Publish a factual statement on your website/social media if appropriate
- Consider legal action if the source is identifiable (consult an attorney)

**Week 3-4:**
- Continue monitoring for new suspicious activity
- Follow up again with platforms on unresolved reports
- Track which reported reviews have been removed
- Assess remaining impact and adjust strategy

### Systemic Decline Recovery

**Month 1: Diagnose**
- Analyze the last 6 months of reviews for recurring themes
- Identify the top 3 issues driving negative sentiment
- Survey existing customers for candid feedback
- Benchmark against competitors

**Month 2: Fix**
- Address the root causes (staffing, training, process, product)
- Make visible changes customers will notice
- Respond to all unanswered reviews from the past 90 days
- Begin modest review solicitation

**Month 3-6: Rebuild**
- Ramp up review solicitation as improvements take effect
- Track sentiment shift month-over-month
- Celebrate improvements publicly (social media, email newsletter)
- Set up ongoing monitoring to prevent future decline

## Content Suppression Strategy

When negative content appears in search results, push it down with positive content:

1. **Optimize your Google Business Profile** — complete every field, add photos weekly, post Google Business updates
2. **Create positive content** — blog posts, customer success stories, community involvement
3. **Build social profiles** — active, optimized profiles on all major platforms rank well
4. **Encourage reviews on multiple platforms** — diversified positive reviews across platforms
5. **Local citations** — ensure your business info is consistent across all directories
6. **Press coverage** — even small local press mentions rank well and push down negatives

## Monitoring During Recovery

Track these metrics weekly during active recovery:

- Rating average (trending up?)
- Review volume (new positives coming in?)
- Response rate (all reviews answered?)
- Negative review frequency (declining?)
- Search result snapshot (what shows on page 1?)
- Competitor comparison (closing the gap?)
