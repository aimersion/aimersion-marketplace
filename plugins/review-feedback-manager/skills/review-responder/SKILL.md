# Review Responder

Draft professional, empathetic, and platform-appropriate responses to customer reviews that protect the business's reputation and turn negative experiences into recovery opportunities.

## Why This Matters

86% of consumers check how businesses respond to negative reviews before deciding to buy. 53% expect a response within one week. Every unanswered review — positive or negative — is a missed opportunity to strengthen customer relationships and influence future buyers reading those reviews.

## Core Workflow

1. **Classify the review** — determine sentiment (positive/negative/neutral/mixed), identify the core issue(s), and assess urgency
2. **Select the response framework** — match to the right template category from `references/response-templates.md`
3. **Draft the response** — personalize the template with specifics from the review
4. **Adapt for platform** — adjust tone and length for Google, Yelp, Facebook, or other platforms
5. **Present for approval** — show the draft with explanation of the approach

## Review Classification

### Sentiment Categories

| Category | Signals | Response Priority |
|----------|---------|-------------------|
| **Negative (1-2 stars)** | Complaints, frustration, threats to leave | URGENT — respond within 24 hours |
| **Mixed (3 stars)** | Both positive and negative feedback | HIGH — respond within 48 hours |
| **Positive (4-5 stars)** | Praise, gratitude, recommendations | STANDARD — respond within 1 week |
| **Neutral** | Factual, no strong emotion | STANDARD — respond within 1 week |
| **Fake/Spam** | No purchase evidence, competitor language, bulk patterns | FLAG — advise on reporting |

### Issue Categories

When analyzing negative or mixed reviews, identify which category applies:

- **Service quality** — slow, rude, unprofessional, mistakes
- **Product quality** — defective, not as described, poor materials
- **Pricing** — overcharged, hidden fees, not worth the money
- **Wait times** — long wait, scheduling issues, delayed delivery
- **Communication** — unresponsive, poor follow-up, confusing information
- **Cleanliness/Environment** — dirty, uncomfortable, poorly maintained
- **Billing/Payment** — incorrect charges, refund issues, payment problems

## Response Principles

Every response, regardless of sentiment, should follow these principles:

1. **Acknowledge specifically** — reference something unique from their review so it doesn't feel copy-pasted
2. **Show empathy first, solutions second** — validate their experience before offering to fix it
3. **Take responsibility without oversharing** — don't make excuses or air internal problems publicly
4. **Move sensitive issues offline** — provide a direct contact method for complaints
5. **Keep it concise** — 3-5 sentences for positive reviews, 4-7 sentences for negative reviews
6. **Never argue publicly** — even if the customer is factually wrong, remain professional
7. **Include the business name naturally** — helps with SEO on Google reviews

## Platform-Specific Adaptations

### Google Reviews
- Responses are public and permanent — write for future readers, not just the reviewer
- Include business name and relevant service keywords naturally for SEO value
- Keep responses between 50-150 words
- Professional but warm tone

### Yelp Reviews
- Slightly more conversational tone than Google
- Yelp allows private messaging in addition to public response — use private messages for sensitive details
- Don't ask reviewers to update their review (Yelp penalizes this)
- Focus on showing future readers how you handle feedback

### Facebook Reviews
- More casual, community-oriented tone
- Can be longer since Facebook users expect more engagement
- Tag the reviewer's name when responding
- Use the response as an opportunity to showcase brand personality

### Industry-Specific Platforms
- Match the tone of the platform (professional on Healthgrades, casual on TripAdvisor)
- Reference industry-specific standards when relevant
- Address compliance or safety concerns directly and factually

## Handling Difficult Scenarios

### The Angry Reviewer Who Won't Stop
If a reviewer continues posting or updating with escalating negativity:
1. Respond once professionally and invite offline resolution
2. Do not engage in back-and-forth publicly
3. If they update after your response, respond once more briefly acknowledging you've reached out
4. Document the timeline in case you need to report to the platform

### The Factually Incorrect Review
When a reviewer states something objectively false:
1. Gently correct the record without being confrontational
2. Focus on facts, not opinions — "Our records show..." rather than "You're wrong about..."
3. Still express empathy for their experience
4. Offer to discuss further offline

### Reviews from Competitors or Non-Customers
1. Respond politely noting you can't find their transaction in your records
2. Invite them to contact you directly with details
3. Report to the platform with evidence (no matching transaction, suspicious patterns)
4. Do not accuse them publicly of being fake

## Quality Checklist

Before presenting a response, verify:
- [ ] Personalized with details from the reviewer's specific experience
- [ ] Empathetic tone (not defensive, not robotic)
- [ ] Takes responsibility where appropriate
- [ ] Offers a clear next step or resolution path
- [ ] Includes offline contact info for negative reviews
- [ ] Appropriate length for the platform
- [ ] Business name included naturally (for Google SEO)
- [ ] No sensitive internal information disclosed
- [ ] Professional even if the review is unfair
