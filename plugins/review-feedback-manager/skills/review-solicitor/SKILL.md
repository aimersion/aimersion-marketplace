# Review Solicitor

Design and execute review solicitation campaigns that consistently generate authentic customer reviews through email, SMS, and in-person strategies with research-backed timing.

## Why This Matters

Businesses with fewer than 3 reviews per month have critically low social proof. SMS review requests have a 45% higher response rate than email, and 90% of texts are read within 3 minutes. A well-timed, multi-channel approach can increase review volume by 200-400%.

## Core Workflow

1. **Assess current state** — review volume, rating average, platform distribution
2. **Identify the target audience** — recent customers, loyal customers, or specific segments
3. **Select channels** — email, SMS, in-person, QR codes, or combination
4. **Design the campaign** — messaging, timing, follow-up sequence
5. **Create the assets** — email templates, SMS scripts, QR code instructions
6. **Plan the follow-up** — reminder sequence and response tracking

## Timing Science

### When to Ask — The Research

| Channel | Optimal Timing | Why |
|---------|---------------|-----|
| **Email** | 24-48 hours after service | Fresh memory, not rushed |
| **SMS** | 1-2 hours after appointment | Highest engagement window |
| **In-person** | At checkout / service completion | Face-to-face warmth |
| **Follow-up reminder** | 3-5 days after initial request | Second touch without nagging |

### Best Days and Times

- **Email**: Mid-week (Tuesday-Thursday), sent at 9-10 AM when inboxes are being checked
- **SMS**: Same day as service, around 6 PM (41.4% higher engagement than noon sends)
- **Avoid**: Monday mornings (inbox overload), Friday afternoons (weekend mindset), weekends (lower open rates)

## Channel-Specific Strategies

### Email Review Requests

**Subject line best practices:**
- Include the customer's name: "[Name], how was your experience?"
- Keep it short: under 50 characters
- Create mild urgency: "Quick question about your visit"
- Avoid spam triggers: no ALL CAPS, no "FREE", no excessive punctuation

**Email body framework:**
1. Personal greeting with their name
2. Reference the specific service/product/visit (1 sentence)
3. Express that their opinion matters (1 sentence)
4. Direct link to the review platform (prominent button)
5. Mention how long it takes: "It only takes 30 seconds"
6. Thank them regardless

**Key rules:**
- One review link per email (don't offer multiple platforms — pick the one that matters most)
- Make the link/button the most prominent element
- Keep the email under 100 words
- Mobile-optimized (70%+ will open on phone)

### SMS Review Requests

**SMS framework:**
```
Hi [Name]! Thanks for choosing [Business]. We'd love to hear about your experience — it only takes 30 seconds. [Direct link] Thank you! — [Business Name]
```

**Key rules:**
- Under 160 characters if possible (single SMS)
- Include the direct link — no asking them to search
- Don't ask for a specific star rating (platforms penalize this)
- Include business name for context
- One follow-up max, 3-5 days later

### In-Person / At Point of Sale

**Script for staff:**
```
"Thank you for coming in today! If you enjoyed your experience, we'd really appreciate a Google review — it helps other people find us. I can text you a direct link right now if you'd like?"
```

**Supporting materials:**
- Table cards or counter signs with QR codes
- Receipt with review link and QR code
- Business cards with review QR code on the back

### QR Code Strategy

Place QR codes that link directly to the review page:
- On receipts and invoices
- At checkout counters / waiting areas
- On packaging or product inserts
- On business cards
- In follow-up thank-you cards

## Follow-Up Strategy

### Optimal Follow-Up Sequence

1. **Initial request**: 24-48 hours post-service (email or SMS)
2. **First reminder**: 3-5 days later (switch channel if possible — if email first, try SMS)
3. **Stop**: Maximum 2 touches total. More than that feels pushy and can backfire.

### What NOT to Do

- Never offer incentives for reviews (violates Google, Yelp, and FTC guidelines)
- Never ask for a specific star rating ("Give us 5 stars!")
- Never gate reviews (only sending unhappy customers to a feedback form and happy ones to reviews)
- Never buy reviews or use review farms
- Never ask employees or family to post reviews
- Never post fake reviews of competitors
- Never send unsolicited review requests to people who aren't customers

## Platform-Specific Review Links

Help the user generate direct review links for:
- **Google**: Search for "Google Place ID" to find the direct review URL format
- **Yelp**: Business page URL + "/writeareview" or use Yelp's review link generator
- **Facebook**: Page URL + "/reviews"
- **Industry-specific**: Guide the user to their platform's direct review link

## Measuring Success

Track these metrics monthly:
- **Review volume**: Total new reviews per month (target: 6+ for small business)
- **Average rating**: Trend over time (target: above industry average)
- **Response rate**: What percentage of solicitations result in reviews (benchmark: 5-15%)
- **Platform distribution**: Are reviews going to the platforms that matter most?
- **Conversion impact**: Are more reviews leading to more leads/customers?
