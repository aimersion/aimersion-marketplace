Conduct a comprehensive cybersecurity risk assessment based on the NIST Cybersecurity Framework.

Gather:
- Organization name, industry, and size
- Scope of assessment (entire org, specific division, specific system)
- Regulatory requirements (HIPAA, PCI, CMMC, SOX, etc.)
- Previous assessment results (if any)
- Known security concerns or recent incidents
- Current security tools and controls

Produce a risk assessment report:

## 1. Executive Summary
- Overall risk rating (Critical / High / Moderate / Low)
- Key findings count by severity
- Top 5 risks requiring immediate attention
- Estimated remediation investment

## 2. NIST CSF Assessment
Evaluate against all 5 core functions (6 in CSF 2.0 with Govern):

### Govern (CSF 2.0)
- Organizational context and risk strategy
- Cybersecurity supply chain risk management
- Roles, responsibilities, and authorities

### Identify
- Asset management maturity
- Business environment understanding
- Governance framework
- Risk assessment capabilities
- Risk management strategy

### Protect
- Identity management and access control
- Awareness and training
- Data security
- Information protection processes
- Maintenance procedures
- Protective technology

### Detect
- Anomalies and events detection
- Continuous security monitoring
- Detection processes

### Respond
- Response planning
- Communications
- Analysis capabilities
- Mitigation actions
- Improvements process

### Recover
- Recovery planning
- Improvements
- Communications

## 3. Risk Register
For each identified risk:
| Risk ID | Description | Likelihood | Impact | Risk Score | Current Controls | Recommended Controls |
|---------|-------------|-----------|--------|-----------|-----------------|---------------------|

## 4. Maturity Assessment
Score each NIST CSF category on the capability maturity scale:
- Tier 1: Partial — ad hoc, reactive
- Tier 2: Risk Informed — aware but inconsistent
- Tier 3: Repeatable — formal policies, consistent implementation
- Tier 4: Adaptive — continuous improvement, lessons learned

## 5. Gap Analysis
Map current state vs. target state for each function area.

## 6. Remediation Roadmap
Prioritized action plan:
- Immediate (0-30 days): Critical vulnerabilities and quick wins
- Short-term (1-3 months): High-priority gaps
- Medium-term (3-6 months): Strategic improvements
- Long-term (6-12 months): Maturity advancement

## 7. Investment Estimate
| Priority | Initiative | Estimated Cost | Risk Reduced |
|----------|-----------|---------------|-------------|
