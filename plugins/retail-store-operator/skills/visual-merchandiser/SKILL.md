# Visual Merchandiser

Create compelling in-store visual merchandising plans that drive traffic flow, increase dwell time, and maximize sales per square foot.

## Core Principles

Apply the **AIDA framework** to every display: Attention (stop shoppers), Interest (engage them), Desire (create want), Action (drive purchase).

### Zone Planning

Divide the store into four merchandising zones:

- **Decompression zone** (first 5–15 feet): Transition area — use bold signage, seasonal hero displays. Never place high-margin items here; shoppers walk past them.
- **Power wall** (right-hand wall): First natural destination. Place new arrivals, trending items, or highest-margin categories here.
- **Speed bumps** (mid-floor fixtures): Interrupt the browsing path every 15–20 feet with feature displays, cross-merchandising opportunities, or promotional endcaps.
- **Destination zone** (back of store): Place essential or high-demand items here to pull traffic through the entire space.

### Planogram Guidelines

When creating planograms:

1. **Eye-level is buy-level** — Place highest-margin items at 48–67 inches from the floor (adult eye level). Place kids' products at their eye level (36–48 inches).
2. **Block by brand, then by size** — Vertical brand blocking helps shoppers find what they want; horizontal size blocking encourages trade-up.
3. **Rule of three** — Group products in odd numbers for visual appeal.
4. **Color flow** — Arrange by color gradient (light to dark, left to right) within a category.
5. **Cap shelf capacity** — Never fill more than 80% of shelf capacity; crowded shelves feel cheap.

### Seasonal Display Calendar

Follow a seasonal rotation cadence:

- **Major resets** (4/year): Align with seasons or major retail events (Back-to-School, Holiday, Spring, Summer).
- **Feature refreshes** (every 2–4 weeks): Rotate endcaps, window displays, and promotional tables.
- **Micro-adjustments** (weekly): Move slow-movers, restock hero items, adjust signage.

### Window Display Design

For window displays, follow the **focal point method**:

- Establish one primary focal point at eye level, slightly off-center.
- Use lighting to direct attention — 3:1 ratio of accent to ambient light.
- Limit to 3 colors maximum; use contrast to pop.
- Include a clear call-to-action (price point, "New Arrival," event date).
- Change windows every 2 weeks minimum — repeat visitors stop noticing static displays.

### Cross-Merchandising Strategies

Increase average basket size by placing complementary products together:

- Adjacent placement: Related items side-by-side (shirts near ties).
- Solution bundling: Create "complete look" or "project kit" displays.
- Impulse adjacency: Place small add-ons near high-traffic decision points and checkout.

## Output Formats

When creating merchandising plans, deliver:

- **Planogram**: ASCII or tabular grid showing product placement by shelf/fixture.
- **Display brief**: Theme, color palette, key products, signage copy, setup instructions.
- **Seasonal calendar**: Week-by-week rotation schedule with display themes and product focuses.
