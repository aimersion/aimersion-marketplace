# Expansion Planner

Design and execute strategies to grow revenue from existing SaaS customers.

## The Expansion Revenue Framework

Expansion revenue is the most efficient growth lever — it costs 5-7x less to expand an existing customer than to acquire a new one, and it drives NRR above 100%.

### Expansion Revenue Types

1. **Upsell**: Customer moves to a higher-priced plan
2. **Cross-sell**: Customer buys additional products
3. **Seat expansion**: More users added within the organization
4. **Usage expansion**: Customer exceeds usage tier and pays more
5. **Add-on purchases**: Premium features, services, or integrations

### Expansion Triggers

Identify when a customer is ready for expansion by monitoring these signals:

**Usage-based triggers:**
- Approaching plan limits (>80% of tier capacity)
- High DAU/MAU ratio (>60%)
- Using features only available on higher tiers (feature wall hits)
- Frequent API calls or data exports

**Organization-based triggers:**
- New team members onboarded
- New department or use case adopted
- Company announced growth (funding, hiring, expansion)
- Approaching contract renewal (90-day window)

**Engagement-based triggers:**
- Executive sponsor engagement increase
- Attending webinars or training sessions
- Requesting case studies or ROI data
- Asking about premium features in support

### Expansion Playbooks

**Playbook 1: The Natural Upgrade**
Trigger: Customer hits plan limits
Action sequence:
1. In-app notification: "You've used 85% of your [resource]"
2. Show comparison of current vs. next plan
3. Offer seamless 1-click upgrade
4. For high-value accounts, CSM proactive outreach

**Playbook 2: The Team Growth Play**
Trigger: Customer adding users or multiple logins on single seat
Action sequence:
1. Detect multi-user behavior or invite requests
2. Highlight team collaboration features
3. Offer team plan trial or volume discount
4. Provide team onboarding resources

**Playbook 3: The Power User Upgrade**
Trigger: Power user behaviors (advanced feature usage, API access, custom integrations)
Action sequence:
1. Identify power user patterns
2. Offer beta access to advanced features
3. Present ROI case for premium tier
4. Connect with technical success team

**Playbook 4: The QBR Expansion**
Trigger: Quarterly business review meeting
Action sequence:
1. Prepare ROI report showing value delivered
2. Identify unused capabilities that solve known pain points
3. Present expansion proposal aligned with customer goals
4. Offer bundle pricing for multi-product

**Playbook 5: The Renewal Expansion**
Trigger: 90 days before contract renewal
Action sequence:
1. Prepare usage and value summary
2. Identify feature gaps vs. higher tier
3. Present multi-year discount with upgrade
4. Negotiate expansion as part of renewal terms

For detailed playbook templates and campaign messaging, read `references/expansion-playbooks.md`.

## Measuring Expansion Success

**Key metrics:**
- Expansion MRR as % of starting MRR (target: >5% monthly for growth stage)
- Expansion rate by account segment
- Time from trigger to expansion close
- Expansion conversion rate by playbook
- NRR contribution breakdown

## Output Guidelines

When designing expansion strategies:
- Identify specific trigger conditions for each play
- Provide messaging templates for outreach
- Design in-app prompts and notification copy
- Calculate expected revenue impact
- Include rollout timeline and measurement plan
