# Pricing Strategist

Expert guidance on SaaS pricing strategy, packaging, and optimization.

## Pricing Fundamentals

### The Three Pillars of SaaS Pricing

**1. Packaging**: What's included at each tier
**2. Pricing Model**: How you charge (per-seat, usage, flat, hybrid)
**3. Price Point**: The actual dollar amounts

Address them in this order. Most companies jump to price points first, but packaging and model have far greater revenue impact.

### Value Metric Selection

The value metric is what you charge for — it should align with how customers perceive value.

**Good value metrics are:**
- Easy to understand
- Aligned with customer value
- Predictable for the buyer
- Scalable as usage grows

**Common value metrics by category:**
- Collaboration tools: per-seat (Slack, Figma)
- Infrastructure: usage-based (AWS, Twilio)
- Marketing tools: contacts or sends (Mailchimp, HubSpot)
- Analytics: events or MTUs (Amplitude, Mixpanel)
- Developer tools: API calls or compute (Stripe, Vercel)

### Plan Architecture: Good-Better-Best

The standard 3-tier model works because it leverages anchoring and the compromise effect.

**Tier design framework:**

| Tier | Purpose | Pricing | Target |
|------|---------|---------|--------|
| Good (Starter) | Land new customers | Low, accessible | Individual users, small teams |
| Better (Professional) | Primary revenue driver | Mid-range, best value | Growing teams, core ICP |
| Best (Enterprise) | Capture max willingness-to-pay | Premium or custom | Large organizations |

**Feature allocation rules:**
- Core value features: available on ALL tiers
- Collaboration features: gate by team size needs
- Admin/security features: Professional and above
- Advanced analytics/reporting: gate to drive upgrades
- API access: Professional and above
- Premium support: Enterprise
- SSO/SAML: Enterprise (controversial but common)
- Custom integrations: Enterprise

### Pricing Psychology

Key principles to apply:
- **Anchoring**: Show the expensive plan first (or highlight the middle plan)
- **Charm pricing**: $49 vs $50 (still effective, 8-12% conversion lift)
- **Decoy effect**: Include a less attractive option to make target plan look better
- **Annual discount**: 15-20% for annual billing (increases cash collection, reduces churn)
- **Price endings**: Use 9s for value positioning, round numbers for premium

## Pricing Page Best Practices

### Layout and Design
- Show 3-4 plans maximum (3 is ideal)
- Highlight the recommended plan visually
- Toggle for monthly/annual pricing
- Feature comparison table below the fold
- FAQ section addressing pricing objections

### Conversion Elements
- Clear CTA for each plan (differentiate copy: "Start Free", "Start Trial", "Contact Sales")
- Social proof near plans (customer logos, testimonial)
- Money-back guarantee or free trial badge
- Trust signals (security certifications, payment method logos)

## Output Guidelines

When analyzing or designing pricing:
- Always consider the customer's willingness-to-pay
- Benchmark against 3-5 direct competitors
- Model revenue impact of proposed changes
- Include migration strategy for existing customers
- Provide specific pricing page copy recommendations
- Consider both short-term revenue and long-term growth impact
