# Content Creator

Write platform-native social content that stops the scroll, earns engagement, and builds 
an audience that actually buys.

## Before Writing Anything

Establish these inputs first — every post fails without them:

- **Brand voice**: How does your brand sound? (formal/casual, witty/serious, educational/entertaining)
- **Audience**: Who specifically — job title, industry, pain point, aspiration
- **Goal**: Awareness, engagement, traffic, leads, or community?
- **Topic/angle**: The specific insight, story, or value this post delivers
- **Platform(s)**: Each platform needs native copy — never copy-paste across platforms

## Platform Specifications & Formulas

### LinkedIn
**Character limit**: 3,000 characters  
**Optimal length**: 150–300 words for feed posts  
**What works**: B2B authority, personal stories, data-backed insights, contrarian takes  

**High-performing formats:**

*Hook-first:*
```
[Line that makes them stop — a surprising stat, a controversial claim, or a specific 
named pain. Never start with "I" or "We"]

[Expand the tension — 2-3 sentences]

[The insight most people miss]

[3-5 bullet points or numbered steps with specific detail]

[Question or CTA that invites engagement]
```

*Story-led:*
```
[Specific moment — time, place, real detail that grounds the story]
[The conflict or realization]
[What changed]
[The lesson for the reader — universalized from the specific]
[CTA: question, reflection, or link]
```

**Rules:**
- First line must work standalone — it appears before "see more"
- Use line breaks every 2-3 sentences
- Specific numbers beat vague claims every time ("47% of managers" not "most managers")
- End with a question that has a real answer — not "what do you think?"

### X (Twitter)
**Character limit**: 280 characters  
**Optimal length**: 200–260 characters for singles; threads 5–15 tweets  

**High-performing formats:**

*Single tweet:*
```
[One sharp observation or insight — punchy, specific, opinionated]
[One supporting line or example]
[Optional: question, link, or thread tease]
```

*Thread:*
```
Tweet 1: [Bold claim or surprising premise — the hook]
Tweet 2-N: [One point per tweet, in order of logical flow]
Last tweet: [Summary + CTA — "Save this," "Share if helpful," or link]
```

**Rules:**
- Be more opinionated than feels comfortable
- One idea per tweet
- No more than 2 hashtags (none is fine)
- Thread openers should be self-contained — someone who doesn't read on should still get value

### Facebook
**Optimal length**: 100–250 words (longer gets collapsed)  
**What works**: Community-oriented, friendly, storytelling, local business news  

**Formula:**
```
[First 2 lines must hook — they appear before "See More"]
[Value: tip, story, or insight]
[Question to drive comments — make it easy to answer]
[CTA with link if applicable]
```

**Rules:**
- Never lead with "Click here" or "Check out"
- Emojis sparingly — 1-2 max, only if authentic to brand voice
- Photos and videos dramatically outperform text-only
- Tag relevant people/places for organic reach boost

### Instagram
**Caption length**: 2,200 characters max; 125–150 words optimal  
**What works**: Visual-first, aspirational, storytelling, behind-the-scenes  

**Formula:**
```
[First line hooks — this is all they see before "more"]
[Story or value in 3-5 short paragraphs]
[CTA: "comment below", "link in bio", "save this"]
.
.
[Hashtags — 5-15 relevant ones, in first comment or below dots]
```

**Rules:**
- Caption is secondary to the visual — it should explain or add context, not stand alone
- Line breaks improve readability dramatically (use periods on blank lines)
- Hashtags go at the very bottom or in the first comment

## Content Pillars Framework

Structure your content calendar around 3-5 pillars — not just "posts about our product":

| Pillar | % of Content | Purpose |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Education | 30-40% | Build authority, give value first |
| Perspective | 20-30% | POV, industry commentary, contrarian takes |
| Behind the scenes | 15-20% | Build trust and humanize the brand |
| Social proof | 10-15% | Customer results, testimonials, case studies |
| Offers/CTAs | 5-10% | Direct conversion — earn this with the other 90% |

## Quality Check

Before delivering any post:
- [ ] Platform-native? (Would this look weird on this specific platform?)
- [ ] Does it deliver value even if they never click anything?
- [ ] Specific enough? (Vague posts get no engagement)
- [ ] Written for the AUDIENCE, not the brand?
- [ ] One clear point — not three okay points?
- [ ] Would the reader share this or save it?
