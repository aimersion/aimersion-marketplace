# Workshop Builder

Design and facilitate engaging, interactive workshops that drive participation, retention, and application.

## Workshop Agenda Design

Create timed agendas that balance content delivery with engagement and energy.

**Agenda Components:**

**Opening & Framing (10-15% of time)**
- Welcome and housekeeping
- Learning objectives and outcomes
- Relevance and context (why this matters)
- Icebreaker or energizer
- Agenda overview and timing

**Content Delivery (40-50% of time)**
- Key concepts presented in short chunks (10-15 minutes maximum)
- Immediately followed by interaction or application
- Story-based or scenario-based examples
- Visual aids and multimedia
- Check for understanding

**Interactive Activities (30-40% of time)**
- Breakout discussions
- Small group problem-solving
- Role-plays and simulations
- Case study analysis
- Hands-on practice
- Peer teaching or knowledge sharing
- Q&A and discussion

**Closing (5-10% of time)**
- Key takeaways reinforcement
- Commitment to action
- Next steps and resources
- Evaluation/feedback collection
- Appreciation and closing remarks

**Energy Management Principles:**
- Start high energy with opening/icebreaker
- Alternate between presentation and interaction
- Lower-energy activities (reflection, writing) mid-day before lunch dip
- High-energy activities post-lunch
- Build to a strong close
- Schedule breaks strategically (every 60-90 minutes)
- Vary activity types to maintain engagement
- Monitor for fatigue and adjust pacing

**Time Blocking Template:**
```
9:00-9:15   Opening & Icebreaker (15 min)
9:15-9:35   Content Block 1 (20 min)
9:35-10:00  Activity 1 (25 min)
10:00-10:10 Break (10 min)
10:10-10:35 Content Block 2 (25 min)
10:35-11:00 Activity 2 (25 min)
11:00-12:00 Extended Activity/Discussion (60 min)
12:00-1:00  Lunch Break
1:00-1:20   Content Block 3 (20 min)
1:20-2:00   High-Energy Activity (40 min)
2:00-2:10   Break (10 min)
2:10-2:40   Small Group Work (30 min)
2:40-3:00   Report-Out & Debrief (20 min)
3:00-3:15   Action Planning & Close (15 min)
```

## Facilitation Techniques

Master the skills of engaging, responsive facilitation.

**Powerful Facilitation Moves:**

**1. Asking Powerful Questions**
- Start with open-ended questions (what, how, why)
- Avoid yes/no questions
- Ask follow-up questions to deepen thinking
- Use "What else?" to generate additional ideas
- Pause and wait for responses
- Never answer your own questions
- Questions to deepen thinking: "What made you say that?" "How does this connect to...?" "What's another way to look at this?"

**2. Active Listening**
- Give full attention without planning your response
- Take notes on key ideas participants share
- Reflect back what you hear to confirm understanding
- Notice non-verbal cues and emotions
- Validate participant contributions
- Use listening as data for understanding

**3. Creating Psychological Safety**
- Invite diverse perspectives and genuinely seek them
- Thank people for candid contributions
- Acknowledge vulnerability in sharing
- Normalize mistakes and learning
- Protect confidentiality
- Don't shame or criticize responses

**4. Managing Dominant Voices**
- Gently redirect: "Thank you. Let's hear from someone else."
- Use go-around format where everyone speaks once
- Track who hasn't participated and invite them
- Redirect side conversations
- Use written responses first, then verbal
- Establish norms about listening and equal airtime

**5. Drawing Out Quiet Participants**
- Direct questions to individuals respectfully
- Give thinking time before asking for responses
- Use small groups before full group share-out
- Offer multiple ways to contribute (chat, written, verbal)
- Pair quiet people with a partner
- Offer "no pass" option respectfully

**6. Handling Difficult Moments**
- **Off-topic tangents** — "That's interesting. Let's capture that for later and stay focused on our main question."
- **Conflict/tension** — "I notice we have different perspectives here. Let's understand both viewpoints."
- **Dominating participant** — "Thanks for that insight. I want to make sure we hear from others too."
- **Emotional reactions** — "I can see this matters to you. Let's pause and understand what's behind that feeling."
- **Silence** — Resist filling it. Wait 10 seconds. Then prompt or move on.
- **Misinformation** — "I appreciate that perspective. Here's what research shows..." or "Let's verify that assumption."

**7. Keeping Energy High**
- Match and mirror participant energy
- Use vocal variety (pace, volume, tone)
- Move around the room
- Make eye contact
- Use hand gestures
- Show genuine enthusiasm
- Celebrate good contributions

**8. Reading the Room**
- Notice engagement levels (body language, participation)
- Assess whether activities are landing
- Monitor time and adjust if needed
- Watch for confusion and clarify
- Gauge energy and adjust pacing
- Observe group dynamics

## Breakout Activity Formats

Vary activity structures to maintain engagement and achieve different learning goals.

**Structured Discussion**
- Assign a discussion question
- Provide 5-10 minutes for small groups to discuss
- Each group reports out one key insight
- Facilitator synthesizes across groups

**Fish Bowl**
- Small group role-plays or discusses in center while others observe
- Observers watch and take notes
- Pause and debrief: What did you notice?
- Switch and repeat with different scenario

**Fishbowl Variation:**
- Inner circle discusses while outer circle listens
- Rotate positions so everyone participates

**Gallery Walk**
- Post large papers or visual prompts around room
- Groups rotate through stations, adding thoughts/examples
- Synthesize and share what emerged

**Case Study Analysis**
- Provide written case study or scenario
- Small groups discuss guiding questions
- Present findings and recommendations
- Compare to actual outcomes or expert recommendations

**Role-Play & Simulation**
- Assign roles for realistic scenario
- Groups act out the situation
- Observers note what happened and how it felt
- Debrief: What worked? What would you do differently?
- Discuss applications to real workplace situations

**Problem-Solving Exercise**
- Present a business problem or challenge
- Groups brainstorm solutions within constraints
- Present recommendations with reasoning
- Evaluate solutions together
- Discuss implementation considerations

**Peer Teaching**
- Assign topic or case to each group
- Group becomes "experts" and teaches others
- Teaches retention, active learning, and peer connection
- Works well for larger workshops with multiple topics

**One-Minute Paper**
- Pose a reflection question
- Each person writes for 1-2 minutes (individual)
- Pairs share their response
- Volunteers share with full group
- Excellent for reflection and individual processing

**Think-Pair-Share**
- Individual thinking time (1-2 minutes)
- Pair discussion (2-3 minutes)
- Share with larger group
- Ensures everyone thinks, not just talkative people

**Speed Networking**
- Pairs are assigned
- Ask a question; each person has 2 minutes to answer
- Rotate to new partner
- Great for building connections and energy

**Jigsaw Activity**
- Divide complex topic into subtopics
- Groups become experts on one subtopic
- Regroup so each new group has one expert per topic
- Experts teach their subtopic to their new group

## Icebreakers and Energizers

Start strong and maintain energy throughout the workshop.

**Icebreakers (Opening—5-10 minutes)**

**Pair Introductions**
- Pairs interview each other for 3 minutes
- Introduce partner to the group with one interesting fact
- Quick, inclusive, low-pressure

**Desert Island**
- Everyone writes down three items they'd bring to a desert island
- Share in small groups
- Discuss why those items matter
- Reveals values and interests

**Human Bingo**
- Create bingo card with traits (has traveled to 3+ countries, speaks 2+ languages, etc.)
- Find people who match each trait
- First to complete a row wins
- Gets people moving and talking

**Two Truths & A Lie**
- Everyone shares three statements, two true and one false
- Others guess which is the lie
- Creates conversation and laughter

**Timeline**
- Arrange yourself by birthday, tenure, or number of countries visited (no talking)
- Requires communication without words
- Fun and energizing

**Ball Toss Icebreaker**
- Toss a ball; person who catches it introduces themselves and answers a question
- Can toss back to same person or throw to new person
- Maintains energy, can go quickly

**Energizers (Mid-workshop—3-5 minutes)**

**Stretch & Shake**
- Everyone stands and does 30-second full-body stretch
- Shake out arms, legs, and energy
- Simple and refreshing

**Movement**
- Everyone stand, sit, or move to different location
- Get blood flowing after sitting
- Can play music while moving

**Think-Feel-Audience**
- Transition pose: make specific face (surprised, confused, energized)
- Exaggerate facial expression
- Brief, silly, gets energy up

**Brain Dump**
- Everyone write down one random thought, task, or distraction on a piece of paper
- Crumple it and toss across room
- Clears mental clutter before refocusing

**Speed Walk**
- Pairs walk around room for 1 minute while talking
- Allows movement and connection
- Refreshing transition

## Discussion Facilitation

Guide discussions that generate insight without dominating.

**Setting Up Discussion:**
- Provide clear question or prompt
- Explain any context needed
- Be specific about what you're looking for
- Set ground rules if needed (listen without interrupting, build on others' ideas, stay on topic)

**Guiding the Discussion:**
- Start with silence—let people think
- Ask follow-up questions to deepen thinking
- Reflect back what you hear
- Connect comments to broader themes
- Invite different viewpoints
- Don't assume you have the right answer
- Use "I'm curious..." and "What if..." language

**Balancing Perspectives:**
- Make space for minority viewpoints
- Ask "What might the other side say?"
- Seek to understand, not win
- Highlight common ground where it exists
- Hold tension without forcing resolution

**Synthesizing Discussion:**
- Identify themes that emerged
- Name the different perspectives
- Connect back to learning objectives
- Extract practical implications
- Capture action items or commitments

## Room Setup & Environment

Create physical space that supports the desired interaction.

**Room Configurations:**

**Classroom/Theater**
- Rows of chairs facing front
- Efficient for large groups
- Limiting for interaction
- Best for: lectures, presentations to large groups

**U-Shape**
- Chairs in open U around facilitator
- Good sightlines for all
- Supports discussion
- Limiting for breakout groups
- Best for: 15-30 people, discussion-focused

**Banquet/Rounds**
- Tables of 4-6 facing inward
- Builds small group connection
- Good for meals and networking
- Works for interaction
- Best for: multi-course meals, extended workshops

**Pods/Small Groups**
- Multiple tables of 4-5 people
- Excellent for breakout work
- Harder to see presenter
- Great for activity-based learning
- Best for: hands-on, collaborative workshops

**Classroom Pods**
- Rows of tables facing front with pairs
- Supports presentation and group work
- Efficient space use
- Best for: mixed lecture and activity

**Environmental Considerations:**
- Lighting (bright but not glaring)
- Temperature control
- Sound quality and acoustics
- Comfortable seating
- Access to water and restrooms
- Technology visibility from all seats
- Wall space for posting visuals
- Traffic flow (no bottlenecks)
- Visual appeal and energy

## Virtual Workshop Adaptations

Design for engagement in online environments.

**Virtual Facilitation Principles:**
- Shorter sessions (60-90 minutes maximum)
- More frequent breaks
- Explicit turn-taking (no one multitasking)
- Use breakout rooms for small group work
- Increase use of chat and polls
- More individual reflection and writing
- Visual variety and screen changes
- Technology check and troubleshooting time

**Technology Setup:**
- Test all video, audio, and sharing before start
- Have backup tech (two screens, headset backup)
- Use good lighting (front/side light on face)
- Minimize distracting background
- Use quality microphone
- Have technical support person on standby

**Engagement Strategies:**
- Camera on policy for participants
- Use polls and quizzes frequently
- Chat for questions and reactions
- Breakout rooms for small group work
- Visual hand-raising
- Unmute to speak (with mute-all override)
- Screen sharing from participants

**Breakout Room Management:**
- Assign people to rooms if needed
- Give clear instructions before groups go to breakout
- Set timer and give warnings
- Have backup questions if groups finish early
- Capture key insights from groups

Ensure you have everything needed for smooth facilitation.

**Physical Materials:**

- Name tents/name badges

- Post-it notes

## Participant Engagement Strategies

Keep people actively involved throughout.

**Pre-Workshop Engagement:**
- Send welcome message with pre-work (optional)
- Share agenda and learning outcomes
- Ask pre-assessment questions
- Invite questions in advance
- Build anticipation

**During Workshop:**
- Use varied activity types
- Ask for input on pacing and topics
- Use storytelling and examples
- Make learning personal and relevant
- Celebrate contributions
- Use humor appropriately
- Build in reflection time
- Connect to participants' roles and challenges

**Post-Workshop Follow-Up:**
- Share resources and references
- Provide opportunity to give feedback
- Offer continued learning or coaching
- Create accountability for application
- Celebrate successes from the workshop
- Build community (online group, monthly calls)
