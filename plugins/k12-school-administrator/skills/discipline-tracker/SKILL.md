# Discipline Tracker

Create professional incident documentation, behavior intervention plans, restorative justice frameworks, and parent communication related to student discipline. All documentation must be objective, factual, legally defensible, and focused on student growth.

## Incident Documentation

### Required Elements
Every incident report must include:
1. **Student information** — Name, grade, ID (provided by user)
2. **Date, time, and location** of the incident
3. **Referring staff member** name and role
4. **Objective description** — What happened, in factual, observable terms
5. **Witnesses** — Names and roles of anyone present
6. **Student statement** — What the student said (quoted where possible)
7. **Prior interventions** — What was tried before the referral
8. **Action taken** — Consequence assigned and rationale
9. **Parent/guardian notification** — Date, time, method, and who was contacted
10. **Follow-up plan** — Next steps and timeline

### Documentation Standards
- Use objective, behavioral language: "Student threw a chair" not "Student was angry"
- Avoid subjective interpretations or emotional language
- Do not include diagnostic labels or disability status in general referrals
- Record direct quotes in quotation marks
- Note de-escalation attempts and their results
- Document any injuries, property damage, or safety concerns
- Include time stamps for key events during extended incidents

## Behavior Intervention Plans (BIP)

### Plan Structure
1. **Student Profile** — Grade, relevant history, strengths
2. **Target Behavior** — Operationally defined (observable, measurable)
3. **Baseline Data** — Current frequency, duration, or intensity
4. **Hypothesis / Function of Behavior** — What need is the behavior meeting?
   - Attention seeking
   - Escape/avoidance
   - Access to tangible items
   - Sensory stimulation
5. **Replacement Behavior** — What should the student do instead?
6. **Prevention Strategies** — Environmental or instructional changes
7. **Teaching Strategies** — How to explicitly teach the replacement behavior
8. **Reinforcement Plan** — How positive behavior is acknowledged
9. **Response to Target Behavior** — What staff do when the behavior occurs
10. **Data Collection Method** — How progress is tracked
11. **Review Date** — When the team will reconvene

### PBIS Tier Alignment
- **Tier 1** (Universal): Classroom expectations, positive reinforcement systems
- **Tier 2** (Targeted): Check-in/check-out, social skills groups, mentoring
- **Tier 3** (Intensive): Individual BIP, functional behavior assessment, wraparound services

## Restorative Justice Frameworks

### Restorative Conference Structure
1. **Pre-conference preparation**
   - Meet individually with the person who caused harm and the person harmed
   - Assess readiness and willingness to participate
   - Explain the process and set expectations
2. **Opening circle**
   - Establish community agreements (respect, listening, honesty)
   - Facilitator explains the purpose
3. **Storytelling round**
   - Person harmed shares their experience and impact
   - Person who caused harm shares their perspective
   - Community members share how they were affected
4. **Understanding round**
   - Explore the root causes and needs behind the behavior
   - Build empathy and understanding
5. **Agreement round**
   - Collaboratively develop a repair plan
   - Set specific, achievable commitments
   - Establish follow-up timeline
6. **Closing**
   - Summarize agreements
   - Express appreciation for participation
   - Schedule check-in

### Restorative Questions
For the person who caused harm:
- What happened?
- What were you thinking at the time?
- Who has been affected by what you did? In what way?
- What do you think you need to do to make things right?

For the person who was harmed:
- What did you think when you realized what happened?
- What impact has this had on you and others?
- What has been the hardest thing for you?
- What do you think needs to happen to make things right?

## Parent Communication for Discipline

### Initial Notification
- State the facts calmly and briefly
- Describe what the school observed and what action was taken
- Invite the parent to share their perspective
- Express commitment to the student's success
- Provide next steps and contact information

### Suspension Notification Letter Elements
- Date and student name
- Description of the incident (factual, brief)
- Policy or code of conduct violated (with specific reference)
- Consequence: type and duration of suspension
- Conditions for return (conferences, plans)
- Appeal process and timeline
- Contact information for questions

## Legal Considerations
- Ensure all documentation aligns with district policy and state law
- Note any special education status that triggers additional procedural requirements (manifestation determination)
- Maintain confidentiality — share only with those who have a legitimate educational interest
- Preserve due process rights — document notification, opportunity to be heard, and appeal options
- Keep records of all communications with families
