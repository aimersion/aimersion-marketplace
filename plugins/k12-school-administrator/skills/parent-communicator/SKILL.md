# Parent Communicator

Draft clear, warm, and professional communications from school administrators to parents and families. Every message must balance authority with approachability, convey essential information efficiently, and respect the diverse backgrounds of school communities.

## Communication Types

### Newsletters
- **Weekly/monthly digests** covering academics, events, achievements, and reminders
- Open with a brief, warm greeting from the principal
- Use scannable sections with headers — parents skim, not read
- Include 1-2 student/staff spotlights per issue
- Close with key dates in a calendar-style list
- Keep total length under 800 words for email; adjust for print

### Emergency & Safety Alerts
- Lead with the essential facts: what happened, what action is needed
- Use direct, calm language — avoid panic-inducing phrasing
- State what the school is doing to address the situation
- Provide clear next steps for parents (pickup procedures, lockdown info)
- Include contact information for questions
- Follow up within 24 hours with an update

### Event Announcements
- State the event name, date, time, location in the first 2 sentences
- Explain what the event is and why families should attend
- Include logistics: parking, childcare, food, accessibility
- Provide RSVP or sign-up instructions
- Mention volunteer opportunities if applicable

### Policy & Procedure Updates
- Lead with what is changing and when
- Explain why the change is happening (briefly)
- Describe how it affects families
- Provide resources or contacts for questions

## Tone Guidelines

- **Warm but professional**: Write as a trusted school leader, not a corporate memo
- **Inclusive language**: Use "families" alongside "parents"; avoid assumptions about household structure
- **Accessible reading level**: Target 6th-8th grade reading level for broad accessibility
- **Multilingual awareness**: Note when translation is needed; keep sentence structures simple for easier translation
- **Action-oriented**: Every communication should make clear what parents need to do (if anything)

## Formatting Standards

- Subject lines: specific and action-oriented ("Math Night This Thursday — RSVP by Tuesday")
- Use bullet points for logistics and dates
- Bold key dates, deadlines, and action items
- Include school name, logo placement notes, and contact info in templates
- Add "En Español" or translation notes where appropriate
